Imperial Pool · Millennia

Huaqing Palace lies at the northern foot of Mount Li in Lintong District, Xi'an, just 6 km from the Qin Terracotta Warriors Museum. Its hot spring history spans over three thousand years, with the 'Li Palace' built here as early as the Western Zhou dynasty, and expanded through Qin, Han, and Sui dynasties.

During the Tianbao era of Emperor Xuanzong's reign in the Tang dynasty, it was massively expanded — 'hot springs were turned into pools, palaces lined the mountains' — creating a grand imperial retreat. The Lotus Pool, Begonia Pool, Star Pool and other imperial baths bear witness to the ultimate luxury of the High Tang.

Everlasting Sorrow

Emperor Xuanzong Li Longji and Yang Guifei spent their most blissful days here. By the Begonia Pool (the Imperial Consort's Bath), at the foot of Mount Li, their legendary romance was born.

"On the seventh night of the seventh month in the Hall of Eternal Life, they whispered in the dead of night with no one else around: 'In heaven let us be lovebirds flying side by side, on earth let us be intertwined branches.'" Bai Juyi's "Song of Everlasting Sorrow" has forever etched itself into the historical memory of this hot spring imperial garden.

Mount Li · Scenic Splendor

Huaqing Palace nestles against Mount Li with breathtaking scenery. 'Mount Li Sunset Glow' is one of the 'Eight Sights of Guanzhong'. As the sun sets, Mount Li is bathed in gold, casting its glow over the ancient palaces and hot springs — an unforgettable beauty.

Today, Huaqing Palace and Mount Li form a unified scenic area, encompassing the Tang Huaqing Palace Imperial Hot Spring Site Museum, the Xi'an Incident historic site (Five-Pillar Hall), and the Mount Li Admonition Pavilion. The 'Song of Everlasting Sorrow' large-scale outdoor historical dance show here is notoriously hard to get tickets for.
