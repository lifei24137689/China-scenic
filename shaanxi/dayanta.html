Buddhist Light Pagoda

The Giant Wild Goose Pagoda was first built in 652 AD (Tang Dynasty) by Master Xuanzang to house the scriptures and statues he brought back from India. The seven-story pagoda stands 64.5 meters tall and is a symbol of the ancient capital Xi'an.

In 2014, the pagoda was inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List as part of the "Silk Roads: the Routes Network of Chang'an-Tianshan Corridor." Inside are treasured scriptures, Buddhist statues, and relics brought back by Xuanzang from India.

Xuanzang's Journey West

The Giant Wild Goose Pagoda was built by Master Xuanzang, who spent seventeen years traveling to India and brought back 657 volumes of Sanskrit Buddhist scriptures. To translate these texts, Xuanzang founded the renowned Ci'en Temple Translation Hall, a historic milestone in Chinese Buddhist history.

At the southern square in front of the pagoda stands a bronze statue of Master Xuanzang, holding a monk's staff with a dignified bearing. The music fountain on the north and south squares is the largest matrix fountain plaza in Asia, attracting countless visitors every evening.

Tang Paradise

Tang Paradise is China's first large-scale royal garden-style cultural theme park that comprehensively showcases the splendor of the Tang Dynasty. Built on the site of the Tang royal Lotus Garden, it covers an area of 1,000 mu (about 66.7 hectares).

Within the park, magnificent Tang-style buildings such as Ziyun Tower, Ladies' Pavilion, and Imperial Banquet Hall recreate the grandeur of the Tang era. Spectacular shows like the water screen film "The Monkey King" and the grand dance performance "Dream Back to Tang" captivate visitors. This is a recreation of the Tang Dynasty's golden age.
