/// 01

Yellow River Roar

Hukou Waterfall is a midstream waterfall on the Yellow River, 30 meters wide with a drop of over 50 meters. The rolling Yellow River water suddenly narrows from a 300-meter-wide riverbed to just 30 meters, like water pouring from a teapot, surging and roaring downward. It is most spectacular from late spring to early summer when the water flow is at its peak.

"From Kunlun Mountains springs the mighty stream, through nine turns at Yumen Pass, one pot collects it all." Hukou Waterfall is a National Scenic Area and National Geopark, known as the "Heart of the Yellow River, Soul of the Nation." It was rated a 5A Scenic Area in 2022.

/// 02

Geological Wonder

Hukou Waterfall recedes backward year after year due to erosion, making it an important geological site for studying the evolution of the Yellow River channel. The "Ten-Li Dragon Trough" is a deep channel carved over millions of years by the waterfall, flanked by sheer cliffs, creating a magnificent spectacle.

The scenery of Hukou Waterfall changes with the seasons: spring brings the "Peach Blossom Flood," summer brings "Thunderous Might," autumn brings "Rainbow Arches Across," and winter brings "Icy Jade Vase." Especially in winter, ice waterfalls, jade-like icicles, and exquisite ice sculptures create the unique "Winter Charm of Hukou."

National Spirit

Hukou Waterfall is not only a natural wonder but also a symbol of the national spirit. The "Yellow River Cantata" was inspired by Hukou — the surging, roaring Yellow River waters symbolize the indomitable and forward-charging spirit of the Chinese nation.

The Yellow River is the mother river of the Chinese nation, and Hukou Waterfall is its heart. Whether it is the thunderous crash of the falling water or the rainbow rising through the mist, all tell the story of the vastness and grandeur of the Chinese land. This is a spiritual totem for the Chinese people.
