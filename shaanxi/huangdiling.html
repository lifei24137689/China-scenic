First Ancestor

"The Yellow Emperor died and was buried at Qiaoshan." — These six words from Sima Qian's "Records of the Grand Historian" established the supreme status of this mausoleum. The Yellow Emperor, Xuanyuan, was the first tribal leader in Chinese history to unify the Huaxia tribes. He created clothing and crowns, built boats and chariots, taught sericulture and agriculture, and is revered as the First Ancestor of the Chinese nation.

The Mausoleum of the Yellow Emperor sits atop Qiaoshan Mountain north of Huangling County, Yan'an, Shaanxi. Qiaoshan covers 566.7 hectares, with the Ju River flowing around three sides and over 80,000 millennium-old cypress trees reaching skyward. In 1961, the State Council designated it as "Ancient Tomb No.1" — a National Key Cultural Relics Protection Site.

Xuanyuan Temple

The Xuanyuan Temple was first built in the Han Dynasty, located at the eastern foot of Qiaoshan. The temple grounds are filled with towering ancient cypresses. The "Cypress Hand-planted by the Yellow Emperor" stands 19.4 meters tall with a girth exceeding 11 meters. At over 5,000 years old, it is hailed as the "Father of Cypress Trees in the World."

The temple features a main hall, stele pavilions, and the Sincerity Pavilion, housing precious artifacts and stone inscriptions from the Han Dynasty to the present. Every year during Qingming Festival, the public memorial ceremony for Xuanyuan, the Yellow Emperor, is held here — a ritual now inscribed on the National Intangible Cultural Heritage list.

Millennium Rituals

Since 422 BCE, when the Qin court established a dedicated altar for imperial sacrifices to the Yellow Emperor, the tradition has continued for over 2,400 years. Emperor Wu of Han, Liu Che, personally led an army of 180,000 to perform sacrificial rites at Qiaoshan, built the Qixian Platform, and left the Armor-hanging Cypress.

Since 1980, the Qingming public memorial system for the Yellow Emperor has been established. Every Qingming Festival, state leaders and representatives of Chinese descendants from home and abroad gather at Qiaoshan to honor the First Ancestor with the most solemn rites. The Mausoleum of the Yellow Emperor has become a spiritual sanctuary uniting Chinese people worldwide.
