Sacred Buddha Relic

Famen Temple was first built during the reign of Emperor Huan and Emperor Ling of the Eastern Han Dynasty (147–189 AD), originally named Ashoka Temple. During the Tang Dynasty, eight emperors welcomed and venerated the Buddha finger relic six times, making Famen Temple a nationally revered royal temple.

In 1987, during the restoration of the temple pagoda, the Tang Dynasty underground palace was unexpectedly discovered beneath it. After 1,113 years in seclusion, Sakyamuni's true finger bone relic and 2,499 Tang dynasty national treasures were brought to light — an unprecedented collection in terms of grade and variety.

Underground Treasures

The precious artifacts unearthed from the Famen Temple underground palace include: Sakyamuni's true finger bone relic, a bronze pagoda, a gilded silver casket with four heavenly kings, a gilded five-legged silver incense burner, and more. The Buddha finger relic enshrined within the eight-fold casket is the supreme sacred object of the Buddhist world.

The excavated secret-color porcelain bowls and plates are the earliest known examples of mi-se (secret color) ceramics with a clear date. The complete set of Tang dynasty imperial tea wares significantly advanced the study of Chinese tea culture.

Contemporary Grandeur

In 2009, the Famen Temple Cultural Scenic Area was fully completed. The newly built Joined Palms Relic Stupa stands 148 meters tall, resembling two hands clasped in prayer, solemn and majestic. Grand Buddhist ceremonies are held annually on the eighth day of the fourth lunar month (Buddha's Birthday).

Famen Temple and the Joined Palms Relic Stupa together form a sacred site for Buddhist cultural tourism. The Buddha finger relic is opened for public veneration periodically, drawing a continuous stream of devotees and tourists from home and abroad. In 2004, UNESCO recognized it as the "Ninth Wonder of the World."
