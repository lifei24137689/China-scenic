/// 01

Qinling Summit

Mount Taibai, at 3,771.2 meters above sea level, is the highest peak of the Qinling Mountains and the tallest mountain in mainland China east of the Tibetan Plateau. It borders the Weihe Plain to the north and sits deep within the Qinling range to the south, magnificent and vast.

The wonder of "Taibai Snow in June" has been renowned since ancient times. The summit is bitterly cold; even in midsummer June, snow still blankets the area near Baxian Peak. This sacred Taoist mountain is also the legendary site where Jiang Ziya conferred the gods in the classic novel Creation of the Gods.

/// 02

Cirque Lakes

Mount Taibai preserves the most complete Quaternary glacial relics in eastern China. Above 3,000 meters, cirque lakes such as Daye Lake, Erye Lake, and San Ye Lake display crystal-clear blue waters, earning them the nickname "Pearls of the Heavenly Lake."

Baxian Peak is the highest point of Mount Taibai. From the summit, one can overlook the vast Qinchuan region. The mountain features a remarkable climate where "one day spans four seasons, and every ten li offers a different sky," with mist-shrouded peaks like a fairyland and spectacular sunrises over the sea of clouds.

/// 03

Ecological Treasure

Mount Taibai is a national nature reserve and national forest park, featuring a complete vertical vegetation spectrum. From the foothills to the summit, one can find temperate deciduous forests, mixed coniferous and broad-leaved forests, alpine shrubs, and alpine meadows.

This area is a natural gene bank for flora and fauna, home to rare plants such as the Taibai larch and the single-leaf grass. Giant pandas, golden snub-nosed monkeys, and takins all inhabit this region, making it one of the most biodiverse areas in China.
