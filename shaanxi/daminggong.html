◈ Palace of Palaces · Tang GrandeurDaming Palace was the most magnificent palace complex of the Tang Dynasty, covering 3.2 km² — 4.5 times the size of the Forbidden City. Construction began under Emperor Taizong, and it served as the political heart of the Tang Dynasty.

/// 01

Danfeng Greeting the Sun

Danfeng Gate was the main southern gate of Daming Palace, known as the "First Gate of Sui and Tang Dynasties." Its five gateways embodied the openness and inclusiveness of the Tang golden age. From atop Danfeng Gate, one looked out over a broad imperial avenue leading straight to Hanyuan Hall.

In 2014, the Daming Palace ruins were inscribed as part of the "Silk Roads: the Routes Network of Chang'an-Tianshan Corridor" World Heritage site. The Danfeng Gate Ruins Museum showcases archaeological discoveries and the restored appearance of this thousand-year-old gate.

/// 02

Hanyuan Grand Hall

Hanyuan Hall was the main hall of Daming Palace, built in the second year of the Linde era under Emperor Gaozong of Tang. It was where Tang emperors held grand audiences, received foreign envoys, and conducted major ceremonies. Its elevated foundation afforded a panoramic view of the entire city.

Daming Palace was arranged along a central axis of three great halls: Hanyuan, Xuanzheng, and Zichen. The Taiye Pool rippled serenely, music drifted from the Pear Garden, and Linde Hall could banquet three thousand guests — it was the largest and most magnificent palace complex in the world at the time.

Ruins Reborn

In 2010, Daming Palace National Heritage Park officially opened. Adopting the principle of "on-site preservation," the site uses scientific exhibition and digital restoration to present the thousand-year-old palace in a new light. A miniature model recreates the Tang palace complex at a 1:15 scale.

Today, the heritage park integrates archaeological research, cultural exhibitions, and public recreation. An IMAX theater screens "Legend of Daming Palace," while the Archaeological Discovery Center lets visitors experience excavations firsthand. It is the finest window into the glory of the Tang Dynasty.
