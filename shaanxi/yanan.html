◈ Red Sacred Land · Cradle of RevolutionYan'an is the sacred land of the Chinese revolution. From 1935 to 1948, the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China led the War of Resistance Against Japanese Aggression and the War of Liberation from Yan'an. Revolutionary sites such as Yangjialing, Zaoyuan, and Wangjiaping tell the story of those eventful years.

The Pagoda Towering

Pagoda Hill is the symbol and landmark of Yan'an. Built during the Tang Dynasty, this nine-story octagonal pagoda stands 44 meters tall on the banks of the Yan River, witnessing the great journey of the Chinese revolution from Yan'an to nationwide victory.

Climbing Pagoda Hill offers a panoramic view of Yan'an city, with the Yan River winding like a ribbon. The beacon tower and Star-Picking Pavilion on the hill complement the pagoda beautifully. Pagoda Hill is not only a geographic landmark of Yan'an but also a spiritual beacon in the hearts of the Chinese people.

Revolutionary Sites

The Yan'an Revolutionary Memorial includes Pagoda Hill, Yangjialing Revolutionary Site, Zaoyuan Revolutionary Site, Wangjiaping Revolutionary Site, and the Yan'an Revolutionary Memorial Hall. Yangjialing was the seat of the CPC Central Committee, where the Seventh National Congress was held.

Zaoyuan was where Mao Zedong, Zhou Enlai and other leaders lived and worked. Those rows of loess cave dwellings witnessed the birth of immortal works such as "On Protracted War". The Yan'an Spirit spread from here to the whole nation.

The Yan'an Spirit

The Yan'an Spirit is a precious spiritual treasure of the CPC and the Chinese nation — upholding the correct political direction, the ideological line of emancipating the mind and seeking truth from facts, the fundamental purpose of serving the people wholeheartedly, and the entrepreneurial spirit of self-reliance and hard struggle.

Today, the Yan'an Revolutionary Memorial is a national demonstration base for patriotism education. Millions of people come here every year to relive those passionate years. Yan'an is not only a witness to history but also a source of strength for generations of Chinese people.
