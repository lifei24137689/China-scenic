The World's Only Red Coast Wonder

Located on the coast of Liaodong Bay in Panjin City, Liaoning Province, the Red Beach is one of the best-preserved coastal wetland ecosystems in the world. From April to October each year, Suaeda grass turns from green to crimson, dyeing the shoreline into an endless blaze — a rare natural spectacle known as the "Red Beach."

This magnificent wetland spans over 18 kilometers and is a national 5A-level tourist attraction as well as a national nature reserve. Vast reed marshes intertwine with the red Suaeda grass, creating a stunning natural tapestry.

Liaodong Bay Biodiversity Treasure

The Red Beach is not only a visual wonder but also a vital stopover for global bird migration. Each spring and autumn, over 260 species of migratory birds rest and feed here — red-crowned cranes, Saunders's gulls, Siberian cranes, and other rare species use this area as an important midway station.

The reed marshes within the scenic area stretch endlessly, making it one of China's largest reed production bases. Glide by boat through the reed maze, listen to the birdsong, watch the wings in flight, and feel the pulse of nature.

/// 03

Red Suaeda Symphony with Reeds

The Red Beach scenic area comprises three main zones: the Red Beach Pier, the Reed Sea Crane Viewing Area, and the Crescent Bay Wetland Park. Wooden boardwalks wind through the crimson Suaeda grass, offering distant views of Bohai Bay where sky and sea meet — red, green, and blue intertwine like a painter's palette.

July to September is the peak viewing season, when the Suaeda grass displays its most vibrant red. Winter brings a different charm — snow blankets the red meadows, and the tranquil stillness after the birds have flown south is captivating.
