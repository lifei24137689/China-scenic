/// 01

Goguryeo Founding Capital

Wunü Mountain is located in Huanren Manchu Autonomous County, Benxi, Liaoning Province. It is the site of the first capital of the Goguryeo kingdom — the ancient city of Hyeondo (Gae-seong-gol-seong). In 2004, the Wunü Mountain Fortress was inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List as a key component of the "Goguryeo Capital Cities, Tombs and Noble Burials."

Built atop a steep mountain peak, the fortress is surrounded by sheer cliffs on three sides, making it easily defensible and difficult to conquer. Its walls wind along the mountain ridges, and within the city, remains of palaces, watchtowers, and reservoirs still stand, bearing witness to over 700 years of Goguryeo's glorious history.

/// 02

Above the Sheer Cliffs

Wunü Mountain Fortress takes full advantage of the perilous natural terrain — its walls were built along ridges and cliffs, with three sides consisting of vertical precipices dozens of meters high and only one side accessible for climbing. This "natural moat as city wall" design fully demonstrates the military ingenuity of the ancient Goguryeo people.

The mountain fortress covers an area of approximately 600,000 square meters, divided into palace, military, and residential zones. After more than two thousand years of wind and rain, the foundations of city walls, palace platforms, and reservoirs remain clearly distinguishable — a frozen chronicle of ancient Northeast Asian history.

/// 03

Huanren Landscape Panorama

Wunü Mountain sits adjacent to Huanlong Lake, where mountain and water complement each other — from the summit, the Hun River winds like a silver ribbon along its banks, with panoramic views of the lake and surrounding peaks. The scenic area also preserves Goguryeo-era tomb clusters and a wealth of excavated artifacts, providing invaluable material evidence for the study of ancient Northeast Asian history.

Hiking up Wunü Mountain, winding through ancient pines and oddly-shaped rocks, ascending the Eighteen-Bend Ancient Path, every step carries the weight of history. Standing before the ancient city wall ruins and overlooking the land of Huanren, one can almost hear the echoes of war horns from two thousand years ago.
