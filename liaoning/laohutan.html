/// 01

By the Yellow Sea, an Ocean Kingdom

Laohutan Ocean Park is located on the southern coast of Dalian and is China's largest modern ocean theme park. Facing the vast Yellow Sea and backed by green hills, it integrates marine sightseeing, science education, and cultural entertainment, making it the most internationally influential coastal tourist destination in Northeast China.

The park's most renowned Polar Ocean Animal Pavilion showcases rare marine creatures from the Arctic and Antarctic, including beluga whales, polar bears, and emperor penguins. Here, visitors can experience the magical charm of the polar world up close.

/// 02

Spectacular Ocean Performances

Dolphin Bay is one of the most popular areas in the park. Smart dolphins leap and tumble through the air, performing in perfect sync with their trainers to create breathtaking aquatic ballets. Each show is a heartfelt dialogue between humans and ocean creatures.

The Beluga Theater is the park's crown jewel — pure white belugas glide elegantly through the water, singing melodic calls like angels from the deep. The Polar Bear Pavilion and Penguin House also transport visitors into a polar wonderland.

Where Mountains Meet the Sea: A Natural Panorama

Laohutan (Tiger Beach) gets its name from its shape, which resembles a crouching tiger. Backed by mountains and facing the sea, the park boasts a 4-kilometer golden coastline. With the sky blending into the sea and rugged reefs scattered along the shore, taking the aerial cable car offers a panoramic view of the majestic Yellow Sea.

The Coral Hall, Bird Forest, and Marine Animal Performance Arena offer diverse attractions, making Laohutan one of Dalian's most dazzling urban landmarks. Millions of visitors come each year to experience the unique charm of this romantic city by the sea.
