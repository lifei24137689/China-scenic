/// 01

Green Heart of Northeast China

Also known as Shenyang Expo Garden, Shenyang Botanical Garden was the venue for the 2006 Shenyang World Horticultural Expo. Covering an area of 2.1 km², it gathers botanical treasures from around the world, making it the largest and most diverse plant sanctuary in Northeast China.

The 100-meter-tall Lily Tower stands at the heart of the garden, an iconic landmark of the Shenyang Expo. Climb to the top for a panoramic view of lush greenery — the Rose Garden, Peony Garden, International Pavilions, and other themed areas unfold like a green scroll beneath you.

/// 02

A Grand Floral Pantheon

Shenyang Botanical Garden features dozens of specialized plant zones, including the Rose Garden, Peony Garden, Azalea Garden, and Iris Garden. In late spring and early summer, a dazzling symphony of colors unfolds — the most spectacular floral feast in Northeast China.

The garden hosts rare plants from five continents — from Japanese red pine to Siberian iris, from Korean mountain magnolia to Chinese peony. Each plant is a green emissary that has crossed mountains and rivers to find its home here.

/// 03

Harmony Between People and Nature

Shenyang Botanical Garden is more than a botanical museum — it is an ecological paradise. Forest adventure zones, wetland landscapes, and lake systems weave together, offering visitors a green sanctuary to get close to nature and unwind.

Peonies in spring, lotuses in summer, crimson leaves in autumn, and snow-draped pines in winter — as the seasons turn, the garden reveals ever-changing natural vistas. The international pavilions allow visitors to experience garden art from around the world in a single day.
