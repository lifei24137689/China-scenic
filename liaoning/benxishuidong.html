/// 01

World's Longest Underground Water-Filled Cave

Located in Benxi City, Liaoning Province, Benxi Water Cave is the longest explored underground water-filled cave in the world. The underground river stretches 5,800 meters, flowing year-round with crystal-clear water. Millions of years of dissolution have created a spectacular array of stalactites in countless forms within the cave.

The cave maintains a constant temperature of 12°C, cool in summer and warm in winter. Taking a small boat through the underground river, visitors are surrounded by stalactites and stalagmites of all shapes, illuminated by colorful lights — a dreamlike experience known as the "Treasure of the North."

/// 02

Millennia of Limestone Carving

The cave is filled with a dazzling array of stalactites, stalagmites, and stone pillars in countless forms. Some cascade like the Milky Way, others burst forth like ten thousand arrows, and still others stand like divine pillars — a subterranean art gallery meticulously sculpted by nature over millions of years.

The water cave consists of two parts: the water section, explored by boat, and the dry section, explored on foot. The "Nine-Bend Galaxy" scenic area within the cave is famous for its winding underground river, with surprising delights around every turn.

/// 03

Light & Water in Perfect Harmony

The cave features an advanced LED lighting system with rich, varied colors that remain soft and natural. Blue, purple, and golden lights dance among the stalactites, casting reflections of water onto the cave ceiling, creating an ethereal atmosphere.

The scenic area also includes supporting facilities such as a Geology Museum, Petrified Wood Kingdom, and Hot Spring Temple. Combining cave sightseeing, geological education, and leisure vacations, it is a unique karst landscape destination in Northeast China.
