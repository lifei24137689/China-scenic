/// 01

Foremost Mountain of Eastern Liaoning A Thousand Peaks in Splendor

Located southeast of Anshan City, Liaoning Province, Qianshan's highest peak, Immortal Terrace, reaches 708.3 meters. With overlapping peaks rising in majestic layers, it resembles a thousand lotus blossoms about to bloom — hence its poetic name "Thousand Lotus Mountain," and it is one of the three great mountains of Northeast China.

Qianshan combines natural scenery with cultural heritage. Ancient temples dot the mountains — Longquan Temple, Zuyue Temple, Da'an Temple, Zhonghui Temple, and Xiangyan Temple form the five great Buddhist monasteries with long histories, making it the most important Buddhist sacred site in Northeast China.

/// 02

Morning Bells, Evening Drums A Millennium of Zen

Buddhism in Qianshan began during the Northern Wei dynasty and flourished in the Ming and Qing dynasties. Each of the five great temples has its own character: Longquan Temple with its sweet spring water, Zuyue Temple with its majestic halls, Da'an Temple preserving Tang dynasty charm, Zhonghui Temple hidden in deep valleys, and Xiangyan Temple in serene seclusion. The Maitreya Great Buddha stands in solemn majesty, blessing the land.

Beyond Buddhist heritage, Qianshan's natural scenery is equally breathtaking — "Thread of Sky" with its narrow cliffs, "Squeeze Rock" allowing only one person to pass sideways, and "Heaven Above" where one can climb high and survey all the lesser mountains below.

/// 03

Conquer the Heights Among the Sea of Clouds

Qianshan offers beauty in every season: azaleas in spring bloom, pine waves rustle in summer, red leaves blanket the mountains in autumn, and snow cloaks everything in winter. Climbing Immortal Terrace, one overlooks peaks surging like ocean waves, as clouds roll and ancient temple roofs appear and disappear.

Beyond natural and cultural attractions, Qianshan has well-developed visitor facilities. Cable cars reach the summit, winding trails meander through the mountains, attracting millions of visitors each year for pilgrimage, hiking, and sightseeing.
