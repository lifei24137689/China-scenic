I · Location

Dabie Mountains Three Provinces Junction

Tiantangzhai Scenic Area is located in the southwestern part of Jinzhai County, Lu'an City, Anhui Province, deep in the Dabie Mountains at the junction of Hubei, Henan and Anhui provinces. Known as "The No.1 Pass of Southeast Wu and Chu", the scenic area covers 120 km². Its main peak, Tiantangzhai, rises 1,729.13 m above sea level, making it the second highest peak in the Dabie Mountains.

Tiantangzhai was historically called "Cloudy Mountain" for its year-round misty summit. Designated a National 5A Scenic Area in 2012, it is also a National Nature Reserve, National Forest Park, and National Geopark, renowned as "the last virgin forest in East China" and a "natural oxygen bar".

II · Waterfall Cluster

Cascading Torrents Awe-Inspiring

Tiantangzhai boasts the largest waterfall cluster in East China, with a total drop of 310 meters. Along a 1.5 km canyon, five major waterfalls cascade year-round: Jiuying (Nine Shadows), Xieyu (Jade Pouring), Shunv (Lady), Yingong (Silver Bow), and Qingren (Lovers) — all thunderous and magnificent.

Among them, Jiuying Waterfall plummets 71 meters as the crown of the cluster, surging like a silver dragon; Xieyu Waterfall spans about 20 meters wide, pouring like white jade with a roar that shakes the valley. Precipitous cliffs, lush vegetation, flying waterfalls and virgin forest intertwine to form a natural landscape painting.

III · Virgin Forest

Natural Oxygen Bar Green Treasure

Tiantangzhai boasts a forest coverage rate of 96.5%, making it the last well-preserved virgin forest in East China. It hosts 1,881 species of higher plants, including over 30 nationally protected species such as ginkgo, fragrant tree, and Chinese tulip tree.

Negative oxygen ion levels in the area are extremely high, peaking at 120,000 ions per cubic centimeter, earning it the reputation of "natural oxygen bar". The Baima Peak (White Horse Peak) plank road winds through primeval forests, and Yaochi (Jade Pool) is embedded like emerald amid the peaks — a perfect destination for hiking adventures and ecological study. In 2024, it received approximately 3 million visitors.
