I · Water Town of Northern Anhui

Where Huai & Ying Rivers Meet Nature's Aquatic Grace

Balihe Scenic Area is located in Yingshang County, Fuyang City, Anhui Province, at the confluence of the Huai River and Ying River. Covering an area of 3,600 mu (about 2.4 km²), with a water area of approximately 2,200 mu, it features a dense network of rivers and lakes, making it a rare large-scale wetland ecological scenic area in northern Anhui, renowned as the "Water Town of Northern Anhui."

The construction history of Balihe is nothing short of a miracle. Once a barren floodplain and low-lying marshland, the people of Yingshang transformed it with extraordinary wisdom and perseverance in the 1990s into a national 5A-level tourist attraction integrating ecological sightseeing, leisure vacations, and cultural experiences. Hailed as the "World's First Farmers' Park," it is the most iconic tourism landmark of Guan Zhong's hometown.

II · Five Themed Parks

One Park, One World Changing Views at Every Step

Balihe Scenic Area is renowned for its five themed parks: World Scenery, Splendid China, Twelve Flower Islands, Bird Forest, and Blue Wave Excursion Area. World Scenery Park features miniature replicas of world-famous landmarks including the Greek Temple of Zeus, the French Arc de Triomphe, and the German Bundestag, while Splendid China Park showcases the essence of Chinese garden art.

The Twelve Flower Islands are scattered across the blue waves, each with its own charm. Bird Forest is home to over a hundred species of rare birds, making it the largest bird-watching base in northern Anhui. In the Blue Wave Excursion Area, visitors can boat on the water, gliding through reed marshes and lotus clusters, experiencing the poetic sentiment of "sailing on blue waves, wandering in a painting."

III · Ecological Paradise

Azure Waves Stretching Far Harmony of All Life

Balihe boasts 2,200 mu of open water with clear water quality and abundant aquatic plants, forming a complete wetland ecosystem. It is home to dozens of waterbird species such as egrets, grey cranes, and wild ducks. The lake teems with various fish and shrimp, while the shores are shaded by willow groves and swaying reeds, making it one of the most biodiverse wetlands in northern Anhui.

Adhering to an ecology-first development philosophy, the scenic area now receives approximately 4 million visitors annually. In late spring and early summer each year, the Twelve Flower Islands burst into bloom and the Bird Forest echoes with birdsong, creating a vibrant ecological tableau. This is not only a wonderful destination for sightseeing and leisure but also a precious ecological treasure bestowed by nature upon northern Anhui.
