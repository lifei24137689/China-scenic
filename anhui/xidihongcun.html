Xidi & Hongcun

Peach Blossom Paradise · Village in Chinese Painting
World Heritage
2000 UNESCO

With white walls, black tiles, horse-head walls, green hills, and clear waters, Xidi and Hongcun are masterpieces of Huizhou architecture, renowned as a "Village in Chinese Painting" and a "Peach Blossom Paradise." These thousand-year-old villages carry the glorious memories of the Huizhou merchants' heyday and stand as a brilliant pearl among World Cultural Heritage sites.

/// 01

Thousand-Year Huizhou Charm

Xidi and Hongcun were first built during the Northern Song Dynasty, with a history of nearly a thousand years. They are important cradles of Huizhou merchant culture, preserving a complete layout of ancient Ming and Qing villages. Whitewashed walls, black tiles, and layered horse-head walls form a three-dimensional ink painting.

In 2000, the ancient villages in southern Anhui were inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List, becoming the first World Heritage site in Anhui Province. The villages preserve over 140 Ming and Qing residential houses, renowned as a "museum of ancient residential architecture."

/// 02

Ox-shaped Water System

Hongcun is world-famous for its unique ox-shaped water system. The entire village resembles an ox: Leigang Mountain as the head, ancient trees at the entrance as horns, the Moon Pond as the stomach, South Lake as the belly, and winding channels as the intestines — a masterpiece of ancient Chinese village water engineering.

The South Lake Painting Bridge and the reflection of the Moon Pond are Hongcun's most iconic sights. The Oscar-winning film "Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon" shot scenes here, where Li Mubai leads his horse across South Lake Painting Bridge, bringing Hongcun's beauty to the world.

/// 03

Picturesque Countryside

Xidi is renowned for its exquisite Huizhou three carvings (wood, brick, and stone carvings) and its complete clan culture. The Hu Wenguang Archway at the village entrance is a masterpiece of Huizhou stone carving. Ancestral halls such as Zhuimu Hall and Jing'ai Hall showcase the Huizhou tradition of respecting culture, education, and ancestors.

Spring and autumn are the best seasons to visit Xidi and Hongcun. In spring, rapeseed flower fields contrast beautifully with white walls and black tiles; in autumn, forests are painted in vibrant colors, creating a pastoral idyll. In 2025, Hongcun received 4.59 million visitors, with ticket revenue exceeding 200 million yuan.
