I · Lake & Mountain Views

Pearl of W. Anhui Clear Waters Mirroring the Sky

Wanfo Lake Scenic Area is located in Shucheng County, Lu'an City, Anhui Province, at the northeastern foot of the Dabie Mountains. Covering a total area of 135 km² with a lake surface of 50 km² and a storage capacity of 903 million m³, it is one of the top ten reservoirs in Anhui Province and an important water control hub and eco-tourism destination in western Anhui.

The lake water is crystal clear, consistently meeting the National Class II Surface Water Standard, with a forest coverage rate of over 95%. Sixty-six islands of various sizes are scattered across the lake like emeralds, creating a unique landscape of "islands within the lake and lakes within the islands." It is renowned as "Anhui's Beidaihe and Hefei's Back Garden," and was rated a National 5A Tourist Attraction in 2016.

II · Man-Made Wonder

World's Longest Man-Made Earth Dam

Wanfo Lake, originally named Longhekou Reservoir, was first built in 1958. Its core project — the Longhekou Reservoir Dam, stretching approximately 1,000 meters — is the longest man-made earth dam in the world, hailed as the "World's Longest Man-Made Earth Dam." In an era dominated by manual labor, hundreds of thousands of Shucheng people carried loads on their shoulders, building this magnificent hydraulic monument through the most basic of methods.

The dam not only serves important functions such as flood control, irrigation, and water supply, but has also become the most breathtaking cultural landscape of Wanfo Lake. Standing atop the dam and looking out, on one side lies the vast expanse of emerald waters, and on the other, lush green mountains — a perfect fusion of human engineering and nature that inspires awe at the power of human determination.

III · Island Cruise

Sixty-Six Islands Scattered Like Stars

Sixty-six islands of various sizes are scattered across Wanfo Lake, like sixty-six emeralds embedded in the blue waters. Zhou Yu Island is named after the famous Three Kingdoms general Zhou Yu (a native of Shucheng), featuring cultural attractions such as Zhou Gong Pavilion and a drilling ground. Swallow Island resembles a swallow spreading its wings, with lush forests and fragrant flowers. Peach Blossom Island is covered with peach trees that bloom like rosy clouds in spring. Wanfo Island, themed around Guanyin culture, is the core of the "Buddhas Paying Homage to Guanyin" landscape.

Traveling by boat among the islands, with rippling blue waves and a gentle breeze on your face, distant mountains appear ink-washed and nearby waters veil in mist. Each island has its unique ecological and cultural story — some offer panoramic views from their heights, others invite tea by the water's edge or explorations of ancient relics, allowing visitors to experience the dual charm of nature and culture amidst the lake and mountain scenery.
