I · Three Rocks of the Yangtze

By the Yangtze River A Timeless Wonder

Caishiji is located on the east bank of the Yangtze River, about 5 km southwest of Ma'anshan, Anhui Province. Along with Chenglingji in Yueyang and Yanziji in Nanjing, it is known as one of the "Three Great Rocks of the Yangtze," with Caishiji ranking first, hailed as "A Timeless Wonder." Covering a total area of 3.92 km², it integrates natural landscapes with historical and cultural heritage.

Originally called Niuzhuji, Caishiji earned its name from the local legend that colorful stones were abundantly found here. With steep cliffs rising dramatically above the Yangtze River, it boasts magnificent scenery and has long been praised as the "Phoenix Terrace of the East." In 2020, it was officially designated as a National 5A Scenic Area.

II · Li Bai, the Immortal Poet

Seven Visits in His Life An Eternal Poetic Soul

Li Bai visited Caishiji seven times throughout his life, composing over fifty magnificent poems. The breathtaking river views of Caishiji provided the Immortal Poet with endless inspiration, giving birth to masterpieces such as "Viewing Mount Tianmen," "Mooring at Niuzhu at Night," and "Song of Hengjiang." Li Bai ultimately chose Dangtu in Ma'anshan as his final resting place, forging an everlasting bond with Caishiji.

To honor Li Bai, the Taibai Tower was built at Caishiji during the Yuanhe period of the Tang Dynasty. It is renowned alongside the Yellow Crane Tower, Yueyang Tower, and Prince Teng Pavilion as the famed "Three Towers and One Pavilion" of the Yangtze. Over the millennia, Taibai Tower has weathered many vicissitudes, being destroyed and rebuilt multiple times. Now a National Key Cultural Heritage Site, it serves as a sacred place to pay homage to the Immortal Poet and experience the grandeur of the Tang Dynasty.

III · Historic Wonders

A Thousand Years of Tides Scenic Beauty

The Caishiji Cultural Eco-Tourism Area comprises five functional zones: the Caishiji Sightseeing Zone, the Hebao Mountain War Relics Zone, the Wangfu Mountain Cultural Tourism Zone, the Yangtze Wetland Tourism Zone, and the Caishiji Ancient Town Leisure Experience Zone. It seamlessly blends natural scenery, historical sites, and cultural experiences.

Sanyuan Cave is the most famous natural cave at Caishiji, consisting of two levels with interconnected chambers. Legend has it that three scholars once studied here and later ranked as the top three candidates in the provincial examination, hence the name "Sanyuan Cave" (Cave of Three Top Scholars). From within the cave, visitors can overlook the mighty Yangtze River flowing eastward and feel the vastness of nature. Approximately 1.5 million visitors come here each year.
