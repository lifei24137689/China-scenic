I · Millennia-old Village

Origin of Huizhou Style Millennia-old Longchuan

Longchuan Village was founded in 337 AD during the Eastern Jin Dynasty, with a history of over 1,600 years. Nestled in Yingzhou Town, Jixi County, the village is traversed by the winding Dengyuan River. Its layout resembles a boat, earning it the name "Boat-shaped Village," a testament to the profound wisdom of ancient Feng Shui in site selection.

Longchuan Scenic Area centers on Longchuan Water Street, encompassing landmark sites such as the Hu Clan Ancestral Hall, Yishi Shangshu Archway, and Hu Zongxian Shaobao Mansion. The core area covers approximately 3 km². In 2012, Longchuan was recognized as a National 5A Tourist Attraction, becoming a vital window for Huizhou culture research and Huizhou-style architecture appreciation. It is also the ancestral home of former Chinese President Hu Jintao.

II · Hu Clan Ancestral Hall

Top Ancestral Hall South of the Yangtze Woodcarving Masterpiece

The Longchuan Hu Clan Ancestral Hall was first built in the Song Dynasty and underwent a major renovation during the Jiajing reign of the Ming Dynasty under the direction of Minister of War Hu Zongxian. It is a National Key Cultural Relics Protection Unit. Facing south with a three-hall, seven-bay layout, the hall covers an area of 1,729 m² and is hailed as the "Top Ancestral Hall South of the Yangtze."

Renowned worldwide for its woodcarving, the ancestral hall represents the pinnacle of Huizhou woodcarving art and is celebrated as a "Woodcarving Art Palace." The main hall, rear hall, and other spaces are adorned with exquisite woodcarvings depicting themes such as the Twenty-Four Filial Exemplars, Hundred Deer, and Mandarin Ducks among Lotuses, featuring meticulous knife work and rich layering. Together with brick carving and stone carving, these woodcarvings are known as the "Three Wonders of Longchuan," representing the pinnacle of ancient Chinese folk carving art.

III · Water Street

Dragon Embankment & Phoenix Street Enduring Huizhou Charm

Longchuan Water Street is the most charming Huizhou-style landscape belt within the scenic area, stretching approximately 500 meters along both banks of the Dengyuan River. The two sides of the water street consist of "Dragon Embankment" and "Phoenix Street," with the dragon running through the center and the phoenix winding alongside, embodying the "Dragon and Phoenix Bringing Prosperity" motif and reflecting the Huizhou philosophy of "harmony between heaven and humanity."

Standing on the water street is the Yishi Shangshu Archway, built in 1562 during the Ming Dynasty to honor two Ministry Chiefs — Hu Fu and Hu Zongxian. This four-pillar, three-gate, five-story stone archway features exquisite carvings and grand scale, and is a National Key Cultural Relics Protection Unit. Additionally, the Hu Zongxian Shaobao Mansion, Xiangxian Shrine, and Chengxin Hall Paper Workshop are scattered along the water street, creating a vivid cultural tapestry of Huizhou.
