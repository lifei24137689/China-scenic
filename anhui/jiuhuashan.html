I

Sacred Buddhist Land

Foremost Mountain of the Southeast · Lotus Buddha Land

Kṣitigarbha Bodhimanda

Jiuhua Mountain is located in Qingyang County, Chizhou City, Anhui Province, and is one of China's Four Sacred Buddhist Mountains, renowned as the "Lotus Buddha Land". As the manifested bodhimanda of Kṣitigarbha Bodhisattva, Jiuhua Mountain is world-famous for its profound Buddhist culture, solemn ancient temples, and beautiful natural scenery.

The scenic area covers a planned area of 120 km², with a protected area of 174 km². The highest peak, Shiwang Peak, rises to an elevation of 1,342 m. In 2007, it was rated a National 5A Tourist Attraction, and in 2019 it was inscribed on the UNESCO Global Geopark list, making it a world-class destination that combines a sacred Buddhist site, natural wonders, and geological heritage.

Jiuhua Mountain is home to 99 existing temples, with approximately 1,000 resident monks and nuns, housing over 10,000 Buddha statues and more than 2,000 cultural relics. Among them, 5 mummy Bodhisattvas can be venerated — a unique sacred wonder in the Buddhist world.

II

Millennia of Buddhist Chanting

Ancient Temple Bells · Mummy Sacred Relics

Ancient Temples

Jiuhua Mountain is home to 99 existing temples, among which Tiantai Temple (also known as Dizang Chan Temple) is located atop Tiantai Peak at an elevation of 1,306 m. It is the highest temple on Jiuhua Mountain, and there is a saying: "A visit is incomplete without reaching Tiantai." Baisui Palace was built in memory of Chan Master Wuxia, enshrining his mummified body from the Ming dynasty, and is a renowned site for venerating mummy Bodhisattvas.

Huacheng Temple, the founding temple of Jiuhua Mountain, was first built in the Eastern Jin dynasty and is the oldest monastery on the mountain. Flesh-body Treasure Hall enshrines the mummified body of Kim Gyo-gak (Kṣitigarbha's incarnation), making it the core sacred site of Jiuhua Mountain. Qiyuan Temple is one of the four great monasteries of Jiuhua Mountain, renowned for its grand scale and exquisite architecture.

Jiuhua Mountain is world-famous for its mummy Bodhisattvas. Since the Tang dynasty, 15 mummy Bodhisattvas have appeared, of which 5 (including Chan Master Wuxia, Monk Daxing, Monk Ciming, etc.) can still be venerated today, holding an extremely high status and influence in the Buddhist world.

III

Natural Wonders

Sea of Clouds & Sunrise · Strange Peaks & Odd Rocks

Landscape Wonders

Jiuhua Mountain is a branch of the Huangshan mountain range, composed of granite, forming rich geological landscapes such as strange peaks, odd rocks, secluded caves, and cascading waterfalls. 99 peaks including Shiwang Peak, Tiantai Peak, Lotus Peak, and Arhat Peak are steep and elegant, each with its own charm, creating a magnificent scene of "every peak like a lotus flower."

The sea of clouds and sunrise at Jiuhua Mountain are both unparalleled wonders. Climbing to the top of Tiantai Peak to watch the sunrise, the morning light emerges, clouds surge, and ancient temple bells echo in the distance — it feels as if you are in a Buddhist paradise. The best viewing seasons are spring and autumn (March–May, September–November), when the climate is pleasant and the probability of sea of clouds is highest, making it the ideal time for pilgrimage and sightseeing.

The great Tang dynasty poet Li Bai visited Jiuhua three times and left behind an immortal verse: "Once on the River Nine I did espy / The Nine Lotus Peaks in distance high." Since then, Jiuhua Mountain has been famous throughout the world, becoming a sacred mountain longed for by scholars and writers.
