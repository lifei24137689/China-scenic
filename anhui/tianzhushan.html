Ancient Southern Mountain

Located in Qianshan, Anqing, Anhui, Tianzhu Mountain was known as Wan Mountain in ancient times. In 106 BC, Emperor Wu of Han (Liu Che) ascended Tianzhu Mountain and named it the "Southern Mountain." The abbreviation "Wan" for Anhui Province derives from this mountain, which is regarded as the origin of Anhui.

Renowned for its grandeur, uniqueness, spirituality, and elegance, Tianzhu Mountain ranks among Anhui's three most famous mountains alongside Huangshan and Jiuhua Mountain. Its main peak, Tianzhu Peak, stands at 1,489.8 meters, soaring into the sky like a solitary pillar. Since ancient times, it has been praised as "No mountain is worth seeing after returning from Tianzhu."

Pillars of Heaven · Timeless Majesty

Majestic Peaks & Mystic Caves

Tianzhu Mountain boasts 42 famous peaks over 1,000 meters, along with 18 ridges, 53 caves, 16 rock formations, and 17 cliffs, creating a magnificent mountain panorama. The Mystic Valley is known as the "Finest Granite Cave Wonderland," with caves within caves spanning over 450 meters in length.

Alchemy Lake, at an elevation of 1,100 meters, is the third largest alpine artificial lake in China. Penglai Peak, Green Dragon Ridge, Yixian Tian (A Thread of Sky), and Feilai Peak each offer unique features — masterpieces of nature's extraordinary craftsmanship.

Ecological Paradise

With a forest coverage rate of over 97%, Tianzhu Mountain is a natural oxygen bar and green treasure. In 2011, it was designated a UNESCO Global Geopark, becoming China's 25th such geopark.

Covering a total area of 102.72 km², the scenic region enjoys a mild and humid climate with distinct seasons. The best time to visit is from March to November, with spring and autumn being particularly pleasant. It is not only a natural museum of granite landforms but also a habitat for numerous rare plants and animals.
