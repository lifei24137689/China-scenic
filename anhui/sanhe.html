I · Millennium Town

Ancient Que'an Famous Town of Jianghuai

Sanhe Ancient Town is located in Feixi County, Hefei City, Anhui Province, at the junction of Hefei, Lu'an, and Chaohu. It is named after the three rivers that flow through it — Fengle River, Hangbu River, and Xiaonan River. With a history dating back to the "Que'an" site of the Spring and Autumn period, the town boasts over 2,550 years of heritage.

Covering an administrative area of 72 km², the ancient town's scenic zone spans 4.71 km². In 2015, it was recognized as a National 5A Tourist Attraction, the only one of its kind in Hefei. Sanhe is renowned across the Jianghuai region for its four iconic titles: "Millennium Ancient Town, Historic Battlefield, Hometown of Celebrities, and Food Paradise."

II · Hometown of Celebrities

Land of Scholars Cradle of Heroes

Sanhe Ancient Town is a place of exceptional talent. It is the hometown of renowned physicist and Nobel laureate Chen-Ning Yang, celebrated Resistance general Sun Liren, and prominent patriotic overseas Chinese leader Dong Yinchu. The town preserves the former residences of Yang Zhenning, Sun Liren, and the Dong Yinchu Life Exhibition Hall, embodying a rich historical and cultural legacy.

Strolling through the ancient streets, you will find these historic homes scattered throughout, with every brick and tile telling stories of a glorious past. Sanhe was also an important birthplace of the Huai Army during the late Qing Dynasty. The famous "Battle of Sanhe" during the Taiping Rebellion also took place here, adding a profound layer of military history to the town.

III · Water Town Charm

Bridges & Streams Timeless Hui Charm

Water is the soul of Sanhe Ancient Town. The Xiaonan River flows through the town, flanked by traditional Hui-style architecture with white walls and black tiles. Ancient bridges, alleys, and docks dot the landscape. The old streets paved with bluestone slabs wind their way through the town, weaving together a thousand years of memories. A ride on a wupeng boat along the river offers picturesque views on both banks, as if drifting through a classic Chinese ink painting.

Sanhe is also a renowned food paradise. Local delicacies such as Sanhe rice dumplings, Sanhe dried tea tofu, and Sanhe crispy duck are famous far and wide. In 2025, the ancient town received 4.8364 million visitors. This millennium-old town continues to attract guests from all over with its unique charm.
