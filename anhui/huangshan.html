I · Geography

Southern Scenic Wonder The World's Most Marvelous Mountain

Mount Huangshan is located in Huangshan City, Anhui Province, deep in the mountainous region of southern Anhui. Its geographic coordinates are 118°01′—118°17′E, 30°01′—30°18′N. The scenic area covers 160.6 km², with a total mountain area of approximately 1,200 km². It is the only mountain scenic area among China's Top Ten Scenic Spots.

Originally named "Yishan" for its dark green peaks, it was later renamed "Huangshan" (Yellow Mountain) after the legendary Yellow Emperor was said to have made elixirs here. In 2007, it was rated a national 5A tourist attraction and is a UNESCO World Cultural and Natural Heritage site, a Global Geopark, and a member of the World Biosphere Reserve Network.

II · Cloud Sea & Peaks

Competing Peaks Raging Sea of Clouds

Huangshan has 88 peaks exceeding 1,000 meters in elevation, with Lotus Peak (1,864 m), Bright Summit (1,841 m), and Heavenly Capital Peak (1,810 m) being the three main peaks. The cluster of peaks stands majestically, with uniquely shaped rocks scattered throughout, creating Huangshan's distinctive peak forest landscape.

The sea of clouds is known as the foremost of the "Four Wonders of Huangshan," with over 200 days of cloud and mist each year. At sunrise or after rain, the scenery is misty and magnificent, with peaks appearing and disappearing in the sea of clouds like a fairyland. Bright Summit, Jade Screen Pavilion, and Cloud Dispersing Pavilion offer the best views.

III · Ecological Treasure

All Life Thrives Cathedral of Nature

Huangshan's forest coverage rate reaches 98.29%, with distinct vertical vegetation zones. From the foot to the summit, evergreen broad-leaved forests, deciduous broad-leaved mixed forests, coniferous and broad-leaved mixed forests, mountain dwarf forests, and alpine meadows appear in succession. It is home to 1,805 species of higher plants, including 31 nationally protected species.

Huangshan is an important biological gene bank in East China and a UNESCO-designated World Biosphere Reserve. It hosts a variety of rare animals, including the Huangshan short-tailed macaque, White-necked long-tailed pheasant, and Black muntjac. The representative plant, Huangshan Pine, is famous for its unique postures, and the Welcoming Pine is one of China's most iconic natural landscapes.
