I · Cultural Mountain

Millennium Heritage Scenic Wonders

Langya Mountain is located about 5 km southwest of Chuzhou City, Anhui Province. Its main peak, Xiaofeng Mountain, stands at 317 meters above sea level, with the highest peak reaching 321 meters. The scenic area covers a total of 115 km², with a core area of 4.98 km². Langya Mountain has long been a destination for literati and scholars, renowned for its "Eight Great Wonders" — famous people, famous pavilion, famous literature, famous inscriptions, famous springs, famous caves, famous forests, and famous gardens.

Langya Mountain gained its name during the Eastern Jin Dynasty, when Sima Rui (Prince of Langya) sought refuge here. During the Song Dynasty, the great literary master Ouyang Xiu served as the prefect of Chuzhou and wrote the timeless masterpiece "The Story of Zuiweng Pavilion" here, cementing Langya Mountain's reputation as an important landmark in Chinese literary history. In 2024, Langya Mountain was officially designated as a National 5A Tourist Attraction.

II · Zuiweng Pavilion

Top Pavilion Under Heaven Eternal Masterpiece

Zuiweng Pavilion was first built in 1046 during the Northern Song Dynasty by the monk Zhixian for Ouyang Xiu. Ouyang Xiu styled himself "The Old Tippler" and often came here with guests to drink wine and compose poetry, writing the timeless essay "The Story of Zuiweng Pavilion." The opening line — "All around Chuzhou lie mountains" — paints a panoramic landscape in just five characters, while the closing sentiment — "Drunk, I share the joy with others; sober, I record it in words" — embodies the literati spirit, earning the pavilion the title "Top Pavilion Under Heaven."

Having undergone centuries of restoration, Zuiweng Pavilion now stands as the centerpiece of Langya Mountain Scenic Area. Ancient trees tower around the pavilion, springs babble nearby, and inscriptions by generations of scholars adorn its interior. Zuiweng Pavilion, together with Beijing's Taoran Pavilion, Changsha's Aiwan Pavilion, and Hangzhou's Huxin Pavilion, is known as one of China's "Four Great Pavilions." Among them, Zuiweng Pavilion ranks first in cultural depth, embodying the ideal of "harmony between landscape and humanity" in traditional Chinese culture.

III · Ecological Sanctuary

Lush Langya Harmony of Life

Langya Mountain boasts a forest coverage rate of over 85%, serving as a vital "urban green lung" and natural oxygen bar for the Jianghuai region. Ancient trees tower, vines entwine, and streams babble through the mountain. With four distinct seasons, the scenery transforms dramatically — spring blossoms carpet the hills, summer foliage forms a canopy of green, autumn paints the forest in layers of color, and winter snow blankets the ancient pavilion. The seasons change, and so does the joy they bring.

Langya Mountain is home to an exceptionally rich variety of plant life, including the Langya Elm and Zuiweng Elm — rare tree species found exclusively here, holding immense scientific research and conservation value. A wide range of wildlife inhabits the mountain, forming a complete forest ecosystem. Through the unique fusion of pristine natural ecology and millennia-old culture, Langya Mountain stands as an outstanding representative of China's landscape culture.
