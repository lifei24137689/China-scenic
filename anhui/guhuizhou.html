I · Huizhou Ancient City

Millennium City Huizhou's Legacy

Huizhou Ancient City is one of the four best-preserved ancient cities in China. Located in She County, Huangshan City, Anhui Province, it is the core area of a National Historic and Cultural City. First built in the Qin Dynasty, the city has weathered over two thousand years of history, still retaining its complete ancient layout and a large number of Ming and Qing dynasty buildings.

The ancient city features a maze of streets and lanes, whitewashed walls and black-tiled roofs, and layered horse-head walls. The XuGuo Stone Archway (Octagonal Archway) is a rare Ming Dynasty stone archway masterpiece nationwide. Historic streets such as Doushan Street and Dagujing Street are well preserved. Led by this ancient city, the Ancient Huizhou Cultural Tourism Zone connects core scenic spots including Tangyue Archway Group, Tangmo, Chengkan, and Qiankou Folk Houses, covering a total area of approximately 35 km².

II · Tangyue Archways

Seven Archways Loyalty, Filial Piety, Virtue

Located in Tangyue Village, Zhengcun Town, She County, the Tangyue Archway Group consists of seven stone archways built by the Bao family during the Ming and Qing dynasties. Lined up along the village's stone-paved path with majestic grandeur, they are renowned as "the finest archways in China." Arranged in the order of "loyalty, filial piety, chastity, and righteousness," these archways encapsulate centuries of the Bao family's moral ideals and family honor.

The archway group is a Major National Heritage Protection Site. Together with the adjacent Bao's Garden, they form the Tangyue Archway Group · Bao's Garden Scenic Area. Bao's Garden is one of China's largest private gardens, a masterpiece of Huizhou-style bonsai, known as the "Mother of Oriental Gardens." The archways and the garden complement each other, forming the most magnificent architectural complex within the Ancient Huizhou Cultural Tourism Zone.

III · Huizhou Architecture

White Walls, Black Tiles Harmony of Heaven and Man

Huizhou architecture is one of the most important schools of traditional Chinese architecture, characterized by whitewashed walls, black-tiled roofs, horse-head walls, and courtyard compounds. Chengkan Village is known as "China's No. 1 Fengshui Village," with the entire village laid out according to the Eight Trigrams — a perfect fusion of Huizhou architecture and fengshui theory.

Tangmo Village is called "China's No. 1 Watermouth Garden Village," centered around the Tan'gan Garden, with a water-street, covered bridge, ancient trees, and archways arranged in picturesque harmony. Qiankou Folk Houses showcase the finest Ming Dynasty Huizhou residential architecture, earning the title "Ming Dynasty Residential Museum." Each of the five scenic areas has its own distinctive character, together forming the most complete and concentrated display of Huizhou architecture.
