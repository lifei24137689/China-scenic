Garden on the Sea Romantic Island

Gulangyu lies off the southwest coast of Xiamen Island, facing it across the sea. The island enjoys a pleasant climate with spring-like weather year-round, blooming flowers and singing birds, earning it the reputation of a "Garden on the Sea." It is one of China's earliest modern resort areas, preserving a wealth of architectural heritage blending Chinese and Western styles.

In 2017, Gulangyu was inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List, becoming China's 52nd World Heritage site. No motor vehicles are allowed on the island; strolling through it, every corner offers scenery and stories.

World Architecture Century of Elegance

Gulangyu preserves many buildings in diverse Chinese and foreign architectural styles, earning it the name "World Architecture Exhibition." It once housed consulates of various nations, with British, American, French, and Japanese consular buildings still well-preserved today.

The island boasts over a thousand heritage villas, featuring traditional Southern Fujian red-brick buildings, European classical architecture, and unique Sino-Western fusion styles. Each building carries a piece of history, recounting Gulangyu's century of change.

Island of Pianos Music Sanctuary

Gulangyu is known as the "Island of Pianos," once home to over 500 pianos — an average of one for every two households. Many renowned musicians were born here, including pianists Yin Chengzong and Zhou Shu'an.

The Piano Museum, located within Shuzhuang Garden, houses antique pianos from around the world and is the only museum showcasing historic pianos from various countries. As you wander the island, melodious piano tunes often drift from the heritage villas.
