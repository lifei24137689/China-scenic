The Gutian Conference The Party Commands the Gun

The Gutian Conference site is located in Gutian Town, Shanghang County, housed in a ancestral hall built in the late Qing Dynasty. In December 1929, the Fourth Red Army of the Chinese Workers' and Peasants' Red Army held the famous Gutian Conference here, establishing the principle of "the Party commands the gun," a vital milestone in the history of the Communist Party of China and the Red Army's development.

The conference adopted the "Gutian Conference Resolution," which clarified the nature, purpose, and mission of the Red Army, emphasized the Party's absolute leadership over the military, and laid a solid foundation for the victory of the Chinese Revolution.

Revolutionary History Red Memories

The Gutian Tourism Area not only features the Gutian Conference site but also the Xiecheng Shop — where Comrade Mao Zedong wrote "A Single Spark Can Start a Prairie Fire" — as well as the Red Army training ground, Red Army hospital, and other revolutionary relics. These historic sites bear witness to the arduous journey and great achievements of the Chinese Revolution.

Visitors can tour the Gutian Conference Memorial Hall to learn about the historical background and significance of the conference, and to feel the noble spirit and unwavering faith of the revolutionary forebears.

Red Education Carrying Forward the Spirit

The Gutian Tourism Area is a national model base for patriotic education, attracting millions of visitors each year for study and reflection. This site is not only a witness to history but also a vessel for passing on the spirit, allowing future generations to remember the revolutionary forebears' struggles and carry forward the red heritage.

The scenic area offers dedicated red education programs, using on-site visits and scenario-based teaching to help visitors deeply understand the essence of the Gutian Conference spirit and strengthen their patriotic sentiment.
