Mazu Ancestral Temple Millennium Sanctuary

The Meizhou Mazu Ancestral Temple is the first Mazu temple in the world, built in the fourth year of Yongxi of the Northern Song Dynasty (987 AD). It is the birthplace of Mazu culture. After a thousand years, the temple complex is grand in scale and magnificent in stature, earning the title "Forbidden City of the Sea."

In 2006, the Meizhou Mazu Ancestral Temple was listed as a National Key Cultural Relics Protection Unit, making it a sacred site for over 300 million Mazu devotees worldwide. Grand ceremonial activities are held annually on the 23rd day of the third lunar month (Mazu's birthday) and the 9th day of the ninth lunar month (Mazu's ascension day).

World Heritage Mazu Belief & Customs

On September 30, 2009, UNESCO officially inscribed "Mazu Belief and Customs" on the Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity, making it China's first belief-type world intangible cultural heritage.

Mazu Belief and Customs encompass rich content including sacrificial ceremonies, folk legends, and festival activities, embodying the traditional Chinese virtues of "cultivating virtue, doing good, and practicing great love." It serves as an important spiritual bond connecting Chinese people at home and abroad.

Island Scenery Fairyland of the South

Meizhou Island boasts 13 golden sand beaches totaling 20 km in length and 5 km of spectacular sea-eroded landforms, where mountains meet the sea in picturesque scenery. Attractions such as the Goose Tail Sacred Stone Park, Meizhou Tidal Sound, and Golden Sand Beach each have their unique characteristics, blending azure seas, golden sands, lush forests, and unique rock formations.

The island's forest coverage rate reaches 63.66%, with a beautiful ecological environment and an annual average temperature of 21°C, making it an ideal coastal resort. In 2025, the island received 3.2323 million tourists, setting a new historical record.
