Jin Lake Danxia on Water

Jin Lake is the most iconic attraction in Taining, covering an area of 36 square kilometers. The lake water is crystal clear, surrounded by spectacular Danxia peaks. A boat ride on Jin Lake offers a unique view of "Danxia on Water," experiencing the perfect fusion of mountains and water.

The Danxia landforms along the shores of Jin Lake take various shapes — some resemble an eagle spreading its wings, others a roaring lion. Each scene is breathtaking. Especially at dawn or dusk, the lake is shrouded in mist, creating a fairy-tale atmosphere.

Danxia Landform Red Cliffs & Walls

Taining's Danxia landform is a quintessential representative of China's Danxia landscapes, formed during the Late Cretaceous period 65 million years ago. The steep and rugged Danxia peaks, with their colorful red cliffs and walls, showcase the marvelous craftsmanship of nature.

Taining's Danxia is renowned for the densest valley network, the most developed cliff caves, the best-preserved ancient planation surface, the richest cave culture, and the grandest Danxia on water. It is the core area of the Global Geopark.

Mysterious Caves Cave Culture

Taining is home to numerous mysterious caves, the most famous being the Ganlu Rock Temple. This extraordinary temple is built inside a natural cave, with "a single pillar supporting the structure, not a single tile used," showcasing the superb craftsmanship of ancient builders.

Other attractions include the Zhaixia Grand Canyon and the Nine Dragon Lake, each featuring unique geological wonders and beautiful legends. Visitors can enjoy the natural scenery while immersing themselves in a rich historical and cultural atmosphere.
