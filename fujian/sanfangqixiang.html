Lane & Alley Layout The Weaver's Grid

Three Lanes and Seven Alleys is located in the heart of Fuzhou, serving as the core of the city's historic and cultural district. The "Three Lanes" refer to Yijin Lane, Wenru Lane, and Guanglu Lane; the "Seven Alleys" refer to Yangqiao Alley, Langguan Alley, Ta Alley, Huang Alley, Anmin Alley, Gong Alley, and Jibi Alley. Covering approximately 0.38 square kilometers, this area preserves over 200 ancient buildings from the Ming and Qing dynasties.

The layout of Three Lanes and Seven Alleys was formed during the late Tang and Five Dynasties periods. After thousands of years of change, it still retains the ward-and-alley pattern from the Tang and Song dynasties. It is one of the largest and most well-preserved historic cultural districts in China, renowned as the "Museum of Ming-Qing Architecture" and the "Living Fossil of the Urban Ward System."

Notable Figures A Cradle of Luminaries

Three Lanes and Seven Alleys is the cultural cradle of Fuzhou. Since the Tang and Song dynasties, it has produced numerous figures who shaped the course of Chinese history. National hero Lin Zexu, modern enlightenment thinker Yan Fu, literary figures Bing Xin and Lin Juemin all lived or grew up here.

This is not only a treasure trove of architectural art but also a hall of human spirit. The cobblestone paths and ancient courtyards carry profound historical memories and cultural heritage, telling the thousand-year cultural lineage of the city of Fuzhou.

Traditional Crafts Intangible Heritage

Three Lanes and Seven Alleys is not just a museum of historical architecture but also a living heritage site for traditional crafts. It brings together many of Fuzhou's intangible cultural heritages, such as Fuzhou wood carving, bodiless lacquerware, cork painting, and Shoushan stone carving. These traditional skills are well preserved and passed down here.

Strolling through the lanes and alleys, you can see many time-honored shops and handicraft workshops, experiencing the unique charm of Fuzhou's traditional crafts. Each piece of handicraft embodies the craftsmen's dedication and wisdom, forming an important part of Fuzhou culture.
