Baishuiyang World Wonder

Baishuiyang, located in Pingnan County, Ningde City, is the world's largest shallow water plaza. The flat rock riverbed stretches about 2 km long, up to 182 meters wide, with water only ankle-deep, making it a natural water park. The water is crystal clear, shimmering under the sunlight like a giant piece of white silk spread across the mountains.

Every summer, thousands of visitors come here to play in the water and cool off, enjoying a unique aquatic experience. The wonder of Baishuiyang lies in its formation — this massive rock riverbed was created by volcanic eruptions over 100 million years ago, evolving through long geological processes into the extraordinary landscape seen today.

Mandarin Duck Creek Creek of Love

Mandarin Duck Creek is China's only Mandarin duck nature reserve. Every autumn and winter, thousands of pairs of mandarin ducks migrate here to winter, creating a unique ecological spectacle. The creek valley is secluded and deep, dotted with waterfalls, among which Baizhangji Waterfall stands at 150 meters tall, one of the tallest waterfalls in Fujian.

The ecological environment of Mandarin Duck Creek is exceptionally rich, with a forest coverage rate of over 95%, fresh air, and pure water quality. It is not only a paradise for mandarin ducks but also a habitat for numerous rare animals and plants, serving as a model of harmonious coexistence between humans and nature.

Geological Wonder Hundred Million Years

Baishuiyang · Mandarin Duck Creek is an important part of the World Geopark, rich in geological relics. The landform was formed by volcanic eruptions over 100 million years ago, evolving through long geological processes into unique volcanic rock landscapes, deep-cut canyons, and clustered waterfalls.

Liugong Rock within the scenic area is a classic volcanic rock columnar joint landscape, with stone columns neatly arranged as if carved by hand. These geological relics not only hold great scientific research value but also offer visitors an excellent opportunity to appreciate the wonders of nature.
