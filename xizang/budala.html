Millennium Fortress

The Potala Palace was first built in 631 AD (15th year of Tang Zhenguan) by King Songtsen Gampo of the Tubo Kingdom to welcome his brides Princess Wencheng of the Tang court and Princess Bhrikuti of Nepal. It has stood for nearly 1,400 years. In the 17th century, the 5th Dalai Lama undertook a large-scale reconstruction, giving it its current form. In 1994, it was inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List.

The main building rises 117 meters, with 13 stories stretching over 360 meters from east to west, built into the mountainside with breathtaking grandeur. The walls are predominantly white and red — the White Palace served as the Dalai Lama's winter residence, while the Red Palace contains Buddha halls and the stupa tombs of successive Dalai Lamas, their golden roofs gleaming brilliantly in the sunlight.

Splendor of the Golden Roof

The Red Palace stands at the center of Potala and is its core area, enshrining the stupa tombs of the 5th through 13th Dalai Lamas. The 5th Dalai Lama's stupa is the most lavish — 12.6 meters tall, clad in 3,721 kilograms of gold and inlaid with tens of thousands of precious gems, earning it the title "the world's most ornate ornament."

The golden roof complex comprises seven gilded copper-tiled rooftops, resplendent in their brilliance. The halls house nearly 10,000 Ming and Qing dynasty scroll paintings, vast collections of Pattra-leaf scriptures, thangkas, gold and silverware, cloisonné, jade and other priceless relics — a true artistic treasury of Tibetan culture.

Highland Sanctuary

Perched on Marpo Ri (Red Mountain) at 3,767 meters above sea level in central Lhasa, the Potala Palace is the highest ancient palace complex in the world. The entire structure covers over 360,000 square meters, with a building area of approximately 130,000 square meters, making it Tibet's largest architectural ensemble.

At night, the Potala appears even more sacred and majestic, its silhouette traced by lights and reflected in the waters of Dzongyab Lukang Park — one of Lhasa's most enchanting sights. Each year, millions of visitors come on pilgrimage and sightseeing, making it Tibet's most brilliant cultural icon.
