Pearl of Eastern Tibet

Basum Lake — meaning "jade green water" in Tibetan — lies in a deep alpine gorge along the Ba River in Gongbo'gyamda County, Nyingchi, Tibet, at an elevation of approximately 3,480 meters. The lake covers a total area of 37.5 square kilometers, with a water surface of about 27 square kilometers. Its narrow crescent shape stretches roughly 15 kilometers in length, reaching a maximum depth of 120 meters.

Designated a National Scenic Area in 1994 and a 4A scenic area in 2000, Basum Lake was officially approved as a National 5A Tourist Attraction in August 2017 — the first natural scenery 5A site in Tibet. Integrating snow-capped mountains, a pristine lake, forests, waterfalls, pastures, and cultural relics, it is often called "Little Switzerland."

Lake Island Paradise

Tashi Island at the center of the lake is Basum Lake's crown jewel. Legend calls it a "hollow island" — not connected to the lakebed but floating on the water. The island is home to the Tsodzong Gompa monastery of the Nyingma school, built during the Tang dynasty over 1,300 years ago. Inside, a statue of Guru Rinpoche (Padmasambhava) is enshrined, with incense burning continuously.

Basum Lake is a sacred site for the Nyingma (Red Hat) school of Tibetan Buddhism. Every year on the 15th day of the fourth lunar month, local Tibetans come to circumambulate the lake in pilgrimage. The nearby Gyeba Village preserves the traditional architecture of the Kongpo Tibetan people, offering visitors a glimpse into their unique folk culture.

Outdoor Paradise

Basum Lake offers a wealth of outdoor experiences: a 41-kilometer cycling route around the lake with stunning reflections of snowy peaks, a 4-hour hike to Xin Co through primeval forests and alpine meadows, plus hot air balloon rides, boat cruises, horseback riding and more. In 2021, it was recognized as a National Sports Tourism Demonstration Base — the only scenic area in Tibet to earn this honor.

The area is rich in biodiversity, with a high forest coverage rate and abundant oxygen — making it one of Tibet's most comfortable destinations for high-altitude travel. Whether lush green in spring and summer or ablaze with autumn colors, Basum Lake displays nature's masterwork in every season.
