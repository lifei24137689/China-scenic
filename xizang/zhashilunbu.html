Famed Gelug Monastery

Tashilhunpo Monastery — meaning "Auspicious Mount Sumeru" in Tibetan — was founded in 1447 AD (12th year of Ming Zhengtong) by Gendun Drup, a disciple of Tsongkhapa who later became the 1st Dalai Lama. It is one of the six great monasteries of the Gelug (Yellow Hat) school of Tibetan Buddhism, sharing equal renown with Lhasa's三大寺 (three great monasteries).

The monastery sits at the foot of Mount Niseri, south of Shigatse city, at an elevation of approximately 3,900 meters. It covers about 150,000 square meters, with a building area of 460,000 square meters. Built into the mountainside with winding walls and magnificent scale, it is the largest temple complex in the Shigatse region.

World's Largest Copper Buddha

The most striking feature of the monastery is the Maitreya Buddha Hall (Great Maitreya Hall), standing 30 meters tall and housing the world's largest copper-cast seated Maitreya statue, completed in 1914. The statue measures 26.2 meters in height, 11.5 meters across the shoulders, with ears 2.8 meters long and hands 3.2 meters in length. It used 115 tonnes of copper and over 250 kilograms of gold — a pinnacle of Buddhist sculptural art.

Since the 4th Panchen Lama, Losang Chökyi Gyaltsen, Tashilhunpo has been the traditional seat of successive Panchen Lamas. The monastery houses the stupa tombs of successive Panchen Lamas, including the combined stupa of the 5th through 9th Panchen Lamas — "Panchen Dongling Tashinamje" — and the 10th Panchen Lama's stupa hall, both funded by the central government, bearing witness to the state's reverence for Tibetan Buddhist culture.

Architectural Treasure

The Tashilhunpo complex comprises the Tsokchen Hall, four Dratsang (colleges), the Panchen Labrang (residence), stupa halls, and Buddha halls. The Tsokchen Assembly Hall can accommodate 2,000 monks chanting in unison, making it the monastery's most magnificent structure. It also houses a Chinese-style Buddha hall (Gyanak Lhakang), displaying gifts from Qing emperors and testifying to Han-Tibetan cultural exchange.

In 1961, Tashilhunpo Monastery was declared by the State Council as one of the first national-level key cultural heritage protection sites. In 2008, the national government and Tibet Autonomous Region invested 118 million yuan in a large-scale renovation of its main halls. On August 28, 2017, it was rated a National 5A Tourist Attraction — the first 5A scenic area in Shigatse.
