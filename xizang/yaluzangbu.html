World's Greatest

The Yarlung Tsangpo Grand Canyon stretches 504.6 kilometers, reaching a maximum depth of 6,009 meters with an average depth of 2,268 meters. It begins at Daduka Village in Mainling County, Nyingchi, and ends at Baxika Village in Medog County. It far surpasses the Grand Canyon of Colorado (1,833 m deep, 370 km long) and Peru's Colca Canyon (3,203 m deep), making it the undisputed largest canyon on Earth.

In 1994, China's State Bureau of Surveying and Mapping confirmed its status as the world's number one canyon. On November 9, 2020, it was officially designated a National 5A Tourist Attraction, standing alongside the Potala Palace and Mount Everest as one of Tibet's three world-class tourism resources.

Father of Snow Mountains

Towering at 7,782 meters on the canyon's south side stands Mount Namjagbarwa, whose Tibetan name means "the spear piercing the sky." It was ranked first among "China's Ten Most Beautiful Mountains" by Chinese National Geography magazine. To the north rises Mount Gyala Peri at 7,257 meters. The Yarlung Tsangpo River encircles Namjagbarwa in a spectacular horseshoe-shaped great bend.

The canyon hosts the world's most complete spectrum of nine vertical vegetation zones — from alpine snow belt to tropical monsoon rainforest in the lower valley. It harbors two-thirds of the known higher plant species on the Tibetan Plateau, half of its known mammals, and four-fifths of its known insects, earning it the titles "Natural Geological Museum" and "Biological Gene Bank."

Mystery Canyon

The Yarlung Tsangpo Grand Canyon National Nature Reserve covers 916,800 hectares, with a core zone of 320,000 hectares. The narrowest point of the canyon is just 35 meters wide, with a relative elevation difference of 2,885 meters. Starting from Pai Town in Nyingchi, visitors can experience the canyon's magnificent scenery up close and feel the thundering power of the river.

Vast areas of the canyon region remain unexplored by humans, making it one of the most mysterious natural wonders on Earth. Opened to the public in July 2008, it has become an ultimate destination for adventurers and nature lovers worldwide. The area features multiple viewing platforms and hiking trails, allowing visitors to experience the awe-inspiring beauty of the world's greatest canyon.
