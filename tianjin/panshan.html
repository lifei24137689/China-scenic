/// 01

East of Beijing's First Mountain

Mount Pan is located in Jizhou District, Tianjin, just 90 km from Beijing, the brightest jewel in the Yan Mountain range. First recorded in the Han Dynasty, flourishing in the Tang, and reaching its zenith in the Qing, it has been a Buddhist sacred site renowned for both its natural landscapes and historical relics since ancient times.

Emperor Qianlong of the Qing Dynasty had a special affection for this mountain, visiting it 32 times and leaving behind over a thousand poems and essays. His immortal exclamation "Had I known Mount Pan, why go south of the Yangtze" made Mount Pan famous throughout the land.

/// 02

Three Plates Wonder

Mount Pan is renowned for its "Three Plates" — the upper plate famed for twisted pines that canopy the sky; the middle plate for bizarre rocks of myriad forms; the lower plate for flowing springs that never cease. Five peaks and eight rocks, layered peaks and ridges, compose the most magnificent landscape east of Beijing.

Guayue Peak is the main summit of Mount Pan at 864 meters. At its top stands the Dingguang Buddha Relic Pagoda from the Tang Dynasty, housing 60 Buddha tooth relics. From the summit, you can see the Great Wall meandering to the west and the misty waves of Bohai Sea to the east — a scene of majestic grandeur.

/// 03

Thousand-Year Buddhist Light

Mount Pan's Buddhist culture has a long history, with temples built since the Eastern Han Dynasty. At its peak during the Ming and Qing dynasties, there were as many as 72 temples of various sizes on the mountain, with incense burning fervently. Ancient monasteries such as Tiancheng Temple, Wansong Temple, and Yunzhao Temple still stand majestically today.

Tiancheng Temple was first built in the Tang Dynasty, where Emperor Qianlong once practiced meditation as a substitute monk. The thousand-year-old ginkgo tree and ancient pagoda in front of the temple create a picturesque scene. In autumn, the golden leaves carpet the ground, as if entering a pure land of Buddha. The pagoda forest composed of 99 stupas of past eminent monks is the most serene soul of Mount Pan.
