Jinmen Ancient Home

Tianjin Ancient Culture Street is located in Nankai District, Tianjin, on the west bank of the Haihe River, with a total length of 687 meters. Its history dates back to the third year of the Taiding era of the Yuan Dynasty (1326). With the construction of Tianhou Temple, the South and North Streets gradually formed, making it the birthplace of Tianjin's urban culture.

Before Tianjin was established as a garrison in the second year of Yongle of the Ming Dynasty (1404), this area was already a hub of culture, religion, and commerce at the Sanchakou junction. The saying "First came Tianhou Temple, then came Tianjin" speaks to its history that predates the city itself.

Folk Grand Garden

Ancient Culture Street brings together Tianjin's most renowned intangible cultural heritage — Clay Figure Zhang's painted sculptures, Yangliuqing New Year paintings, and Kite Wei's kites, known as the "Three Wonders of Tianjin Folk Crafts." Nearly a hundred time-honored shops line the street, with gray bricks, black tiles, and upturned eaves evoking the charm of the Ming and Qing dynasties.

Tianhou Temple (Mazu Temple) sits at the heart of the street, one of the earliest existing Mazu temples in China, with incense burning continuously for over seven hundred years. Every year, folk activities such as the Spring Lantern Festival and Imperial Procession take place here, featuring dragon lanterns, stilt walking, and lion dances in a lively spectacle.
