/// 01

The Heavenly Sanctuary of the Capital

Located in Beijing's Dongcheng District, the Temple of Heaven was the sacred site where Ming and Qing emperors worshipped heaven and prayed for good harvests. Construction began in the 18th year of the Yongle reign (1420 AD), contemporaneous with the Forbidden City. Covering approximately 273 hectares, it is China's largest and best-preserved ancient sacrificial architectural complex.

As a perfect embodiment of the ancient Chinese philosophy of "Unity of Heaven and Man," the Temple of Heaven's layout is exquisitely conceived — the inner and outer enclosures form a concentric "回" pattern, with the south side square and the north side circular, symbolizing the cosmology of "round heaven and square earth." In 1998, it was inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List.

/// 02

Exquisite Architectural Acoustics

The Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests is the iconic structure of the Temple of Heaven. Its triple-tiered circular roof, clad in blue glazed tiles and crowned with a golden spire, reaches 38 meters in height. The entire structure is supported by 28 massive nanmu pillars without a single nail. The caisson ceiling inside is intricately carved with golden dragons spiraling in resplendent splendor.

The three core architectural complexes — the Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests, the Imperial Vault of Heaven, and the Circular Mound Altar — are not only unparalleled in architectural beauty but also astonishing in their acoustic design: the Echo Wall allows a whisper to travel tens of meters along its surface, while the Triple Sound Stones produce varying numbers of echoes depending on one's position, fully demonstrating the extraordinary ingenuity of ancient Chinese craftsmen.

/// 03

The Serene Ancient Cypress Forest

The Temple of Heaven park is home to over three thousand ancient cypress trees, some dating back more than a century. The most famous among them, the "Nine-Dragon Cypress," has stood for over five hundred years, its gnarled trunk twisting like nine coiling dragons. This ancient cypress grove is not only a precious natural heritage but also the very soul of the Temple of Heaven's solemn atmosphere.

Strolling slowly along the Danbi Bridge, flanked by verdant pines and cypresses, the blue glazed tiles of the Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests glisten in the sunlight in the distance. Whether it is the tai chi practitioners in the morning mist or the circling pigeons at dusk, the Temple of Heaven exudes a timeless dignity and tranquility that allows every visitor to feel the profound depth of Chinese civilization.
