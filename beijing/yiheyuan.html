/// 01

Between Lake and Mountain

The Summer Palace is located in the western suburbs of Beijing. Built as a royal garden during the Qing Dynasty, it was originally known as the Garden of Clear Ripples and was first constructed in the 15th year of Emperor Qianlong's reign (1750). Covering an area of approximately 301 hectares, with Kunming Lake and Longevity Hill as its main features, water accounts for about three-quarters of the total area, making it the largest and best-preserved imperial garden in China.

Modeled after West Lake in Hangzhou, the Summer Palace incorporates design techniques from Jiangnan gardens, perfectly blending the grandeur of northern imperial gardens with the refined elegance of southern private gardens. It represents the pinnacle of traditional Chinese garden art and is hailed as the "Museum of Royal Gardens." In 1998, it was inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List.

/// 02

The Seventeen-Arch Bridge Spanning the Blue Waves

The Seventeen-Arch Bridge is the largest stone bridge in the Summer Palace, measuring 150 meters in length and 8 meters in width. It consists of 17 arches and spans between the east embankment of Kunming Lake and South Lake Island. The balustrade pillars are carved with 544 stone lions in various poses, each exquisitely crafted. Around the winter solstice each year, the setting sun shines through the arches, creating a spectacular "golden light piercing the arches" phenomenon that attracts countless visitors.

The Tower of Buddhist Incense is the iconic structure of the Summer Palace, perched on the front slope of Longevity Hill. Standing 41 meters tall, it is an octagonal, three-story, four-eaved pavilion. Ascending to the top offers a panoramic view of Kunming Lake and the distant Western Hills, capturing the magnificent scenery of "lake and mountain in one pavilion." Together with the Seventeen-Arch Bridge, the Long Corridor, and the Marble Boat, it forms an unparalleled architectural tableau.

/// 03

Ingenious Garden Art

The Long Corridor is one of the most breathtaking structures in the Summer Palace. Stretching 728 meters with 273 bays, it is the longest covered corridor in Chinese classical gardens. Every beam is adorned with exquisite painted scenes, totaling over 14,000 panels, featuring landscapes, flowers, birds, and historical tales, earning it the title of the "Longest Gallery in the World."

The Marble Boat (Qingyan Boat) is the only Western-style structure in the Summer Palace. Built from massive stone blocks on the shore of Kunming Lake, it measures 36 meters in length. Suzhou Street recreates the bustling marketplace of the Jiangnan water towns, with bridges, flowing water, and rows of shops, bringing a touch of earthly life to the imperial garden. From the Tower of Buddhist Incense to Suzhou Street, every scene in the Summer Palace tells the profound story of Chinese garden art.
