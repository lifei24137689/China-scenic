OVERVIEW · INTRODUCTION

Garden of Gardens
The Sigh of a Lost Civilization

Yuanmingyuan, consisting of three gardens — Yuanming, Changchun, and Qichun, covers 350 hectares. Construction began in the 46th year of Kangxi's reign (1707), and continued through five reigns — Yongzheng, Qianlong, Jiaqing, Daoguang, and Xianfeng — over more than 150 years. It was hailed as "the paragon of garden artistry".

In 1860, a great fire set by the Anglo-French forces reduced this magnificent imperial garden — a masterpiece combining Chinese and Western landscaping — to ashes. Today, the Ruins Park preserves the weathered texture of history while rising from the rubble, serving as a patriotic education base and a UNESCO World Heritage site.

XI YANG LOU · Western Pavilion

Amid the Ruins
Baroque Meets the Orient

The Xiyang Lou (Western Pavilion) scenic area, designed by the missionary Giuseppe Castiglione (Lang Shining) and others, was China's first imperial garden to extensively introduce European Baroque architecture. The Dashuifa (Grand Waterworks) is the most iconic ruin of Yuanmingyuan — massive stone fountain columns stand isolated amid the rubble, silent witnesses to that chapter of history.

Before Haiyantang, the Twelve Bronze Animal Heads once spouted water in turn according to the traditional Chinese timekeeping system (two-hour periods), showcasing astonishing craftsmanship. Some of these animal heads have since been returned to China, becoming a global symbol of cultural heritage preservation.

FU HAI · Blessing Sea

Vast Emerald Waters
Jiangnan Amid the Lotuses

Fuhai is the largest water body in Yuanmingyuan, covering approximately 28 hectares. Modeled after Hangzhou's West Lake, it embodies the essence of Chinese classical garden design. Over a dozen gardens ring the lake, each with its own character, while Penglai Yaotai pavilion sits at the center, evoking the mythical immortal islands.

Each summer, the Yuanmingyuan Lotus Festival arrives on schedule. Hundreds of lotus varieties burst into bloom — a boundless expanse of green leaves and vibrant flowers. Visitors glide across Fuhai by boat, weaving through the lotuses, sensing the flow of time and nature's healing power between ruins and renewal.
