Olympic Legacy
New City Landmark

Beijing Olympic Park is located at the northern end of the city's central axis, covering approximately 11.6 square kilometers. It served as the main venue area for the 2008 Beijing Olympic Games. In 2022, the Beijing Winter Olympics once again focused the world's attention here, making it the world's only "Dual Olympics" core area.

The park integrates sports competitions, conferences and exhibitions, cultural entertainment, and leisure shopping. It is not only a showcase of modern architectural marvels but also an eternal symbol of the Olympic spirit in China.

NATIONAL STADIUM · BIRD'S NEST

Steel Woven
Eastern Nest

The National Stadium — "Bird's Nest," was designed by the Swiss firm Herzog & de Meuron in collaboration with Chinese architects. The exterior is woven from 24 portal trusses, resembling a cradle of life and a giant bird's nest, interpreting the round beauty of traditional Chinese artifacts through the most modern steel structure language.

The Bird's Nest measures 333 meters long, 298 meters wide, and 69 meters high, with a capacity of 91,000 spectators. The opening and closing ceremonies of the 2008 Olympics were held here — a spectacular audio-visual feast that shook the world and remains China's most thrilling collective memory.

NATIONAL AQUATICS CENTER · WATER CUBE

Water Magic
Bubble Fantasy

The National Aquatics Center — "Water Cube," features an ETFE membrane structure with over 3,000 irregular bubble-like forms, inspired by the natural beauty of water molecule arrangements and soap bubbles. Crystalline and translucent by day, the building transforms with dazzling lighting effects at night.

The Water Cube covers nearly 80,000 square meters, housing competition pools, diving pools, and warm-up pools. During the 2008 Olympics, 25 world records were set here. For the 2022 Winter Olympics, the Water Cube transformed into the "Ice Cube," successfully hosting the curling competitions and continuing its legendary legacy.

OLYMPIC TOWER

Top of the City
Overlooking the Axis

Beijing Olympic Tower stands 246.8 meters tall, consisting of five independent towers of varying heights, topped with a permanent Olympic Rings emblem. The observation deck at the top is the highest point in northern Beijing, offering a 360-degree panoramic view of the Olympic Park and the Beijing central axis.

At the foot of the tower lies the Sunken Garden and the Dragon-shaped Waterway, where traditional garden aesthetics and modern landscape design blend seamlessly. During major events and festivals, this area becomes a vibrant urban living room for the public.
