Three Millennia of Vigil

One of only three remaining primitive populus euphratica forests in the world, the Ejina Populus Euphratica Forest is renowned for its legendary "three millennia" life cycle — alive for a thousand years, standing for a thousand years after death, and enduring for a thousand years after falling. Every October, hundreds of thousands of acres of populus euphratica burst into brilliant golden yellow, transforming the entire desert into a sea of gold.

Located in Ejina Banner, Alxa League, Inner Mongolia, the Populus Euphratica Forest Tourist Area is one of only three remaining primitive populus euphratica forests in the world and China's most spectacular populus euphratica landscape. During the golden autumn season, photography enthusiasts and travelers from around the world flock here to witness this soul-stirring golden feast.

Eight-Bridge Scenery

From Bridge One to Bridge Eight in the Ejina Populus Euphratica Forest, each bridge offers its own unique scenery. Bridge One has the densest populus trees, with golden leaves forming a canopy; Bridge Two features the enchanting Reflection Forest, where populus reflections on the calm river surface create a dreamlike scene; Bridge Three's Red Willow Sea interweaves with golden populus to form a colorful autumn tableau.

Bridge Four's Hero Forest is the filming location for the movie "Hero." The populus trees here are robust and powerful, with twisted branches resembling dragons, appearing even more majestic against the golden leaves. Bridge Seven's Dream Forest and Bridge Eight's Desert Forest showcase the magnificent spectacle of populus and desert coexisting in harmony, leaving visitors in awe.

Ghost Forest & Black City

The Ghost Forest is one of the most awe-inspiring attractions in the populus euphratica scenic area. The populus trees here have withered, yet still stand tall, assuming bizarre and varied postures across the desert. The dead trunks display countless strange forms — some like soaring dragons, others like roaring beasts — appearing increasingly desolate and magnificent under the setting sun, as if telling stories of millennia of vicissitudes.

The Black City ruins were once a frontier stronghold of the Western Xia Kingdom. Though only crumbling walls remain, one can still feel the prosperity and glory of those bygone days. The ancient walls have stood in the wind and sand for a thousand years, witnessing the changes of history alongside the surrounding populus forest. At dusk, golden afterglow spills over the ancient city walls, blending with the distant populus forest to create a timelessly beautiful scene.
