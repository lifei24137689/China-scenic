Desert Echoes — The Singing Sands Marvel

Xiangshawan is located at the eastern end of the Kubuqi Desert in Dalad Banner, Ordos City. It is China's first 5A-rated scenic area themed around the desert. Its most renowned feature is the "Singing Sands" — under specific climatic conditions, as you slide down the dunes, the sand emits a rumbling sound akin to an airplane engine. This natural phenomenon remains an unsolved mystery to this day.

At an elevation of approximately 1,100 meters and covering an area of 24 square kilometers, Xiangshawan is a comprehensive tourist destination integrating desert sightseeing, leisure vacations, and outdoor adventure. In 2011, it was officially designated a National 5A Scenic Area, becoming the iconic symbol of Inner Mongolia's desert tourism. A solitary smoke straight in the vast desert, the setting sun round over the long river — every grain of sand here tells an ancient grassland story.

Five Sand Islands — A Desert Paradise

Xiangshawan is world-famous for its "One Sand Belt, Five Islands" layout. The Xiangsha (Resonant Sand) Belt is the core experience area, where visitors can take a desert cable car to overlook the vast sea of sand and marvel at nature's breathtaking craftsmanship. Xiansha (Fairy Sand) Island is themed around leisure and entertainment, offering desert off-roading, sand sledding, camel riding, and a wealth of outdoor activities for every visitor to find their own desert delight.

Yuesha (Moon Sand) Island is a water park — playing in the water amidst the desert is a truly unique experience. Liansha (Lotus Sand) Resort Island is renowned for its luxurious Lotus Hotel, whose distinctive architecture resembles a white lotus blooming in the desert, making it an iconic landmark of Xiangshawan. Fusha (Fortune Sand) Island preserves the purest Mongolian folk culture, allowing visitors to experience the warmth and boldness of nomadic life deep in the desert.

Ethnic Charm — Where Grassland Meets Desert

Xiangshawan is not only a natural wonder but also a vibrant stage for Mongolian culture. Ordos is the birthplace of the traditional Mongolian song-and-dance "Ordos Dance." At the desert theater of Xiangshawan, visitors can enjoy authentic performances of Mongolian long-tune folk songs, horse-head fiddle playing, and bowl-balancing dances.

In addition, the scenic area features a traditional Mongolian cuisine experience zone, where specialties such as hand-grilled lamb, milk tea, and fried rice leave a lasting impression. The evening bonfire parties in the desert fully showcase the passion of the grassland people. Every summer, Xiangshawan hosts a Desert Culture Festival, attracting tourists from around the world to experience the magic of this remarkable land.
