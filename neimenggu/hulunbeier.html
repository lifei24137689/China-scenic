The First Winding River Under Heaven

The Morigele River, meaning "winding river" in Mongolian, originates from the Greater Khingan Range. It stretches approximately 290 kilometers, meandering across the Hulunbuir Grassland. With its extremely winding course forming countless "Ω"-shaped bends, it is renowned as the "First Winding River Under Heaven." From above, it looks like a silver ribbon dancing upon a green carpet.

This river is the lifeblood of the grassland. Its banks are lush with abundant water and grazing livestock. In midsummer, the river reflects the blue sky and white clouds, complementing the endless prairie in a magnificent natural tableau. The Morigele River is not only the ecological artery of the grassland but also a paradise for photographers.

Golden Horde Mongol Tribe

Located along the Morigele River, the Golden Horde Mongol Tribe is the most representative site on the Hulunbuir Grassland for experiencing Mongolian culture. It recreates the historical grandeur of the Golden Horde Khanate from the era of Genghis Khan. Mongolian yurts scatter across the prairie like pearls, their white felt walls contrasting beautifully with the blue sky and green grass.

Visitors can experience authentic Mongolian nomadic life here: savor the richness of hand-grabbed mutton and milk tea, watch thrilling horse races and Bokh (Mongolian wrestling) performances, and listen to the melodious strains of the horsehead fiddle. As night falls, a bonfire party ignites the prairie night, allowing you to feel the passion and hospitality of the nomadic people under the stars.

Ecological Wonderland

The Hulunbuir Grassland is one of the world's four great grasslands, boasting an extraordinarily rich ecosystem. Hoh Nur Lake lies like a gem embedded in the prairie, with crystal-clear waters and flocks of waterbirds. In the birch forest area, silvery-white trunks stand elegantly, turning a brilliant gold in autumn — a paradise for photographers.

The Erguna Wetland is one of the largest wetlands in Asia, known as the "Number One Wetland of Asia." Rivers crisscross the wetland, and dense vegetation provides a habitat for numerous rare bird species. From the observation deck, the Genhe River winds through the wetland, creating a magnificent natural landscape. Across the four seasons, the grassland takes on different hues: lush green like a sea in spring and summer, brilliant gold in autumn, and a silver blanket of snow in winter.
