Volcanic Crater Lake — Jade Jewel Deep in the Greater Khingan

Located in the northwestern part of Hinggan League, Inner Mongolia, at the southwestern foot of the Greater Khingan Mountains, the Arshan-Chaihe Scenic Area covers a total area of 740 km². It is home to China's largest volcanic crater lake cluster, with dozens of volcanic barrier lakes — including Arshan Tianchi, Camel Peak Tianchi, and Azalea Lake — scattered like pearls across the vast forest sea. The crystal-clear waters reflect the blue sky and white clouds, creating an endlessly captivating landscape.

Arshan Tianchi sits at approximately 1,100 meters above sea level and is elliptical in shape, formed by water accumulating in a volcanic crater after an eruption. The lake is surrounded by dense primeval forest. Every autumn, the forest is painted in vibrant hues — golden birch trees contrast with the azure lake water, creating a richly colored oil painting. In 2017, Arshan-Chaihe was officially rated a National 5A Tourist Attraction.

Sacred Spring Snow City — A Rare Wonder of Hot Springs and Ice & Snow

Known as the "Sacred Spring Snow City," Arshan is home to one of China's largest mineral spring clusters. The Hot Spring Museum houses dozens of natural mineral springs with varying temperatures and therapeutic benefits. Arshan's hot springs are volcanic in origin, with water temperatures ranging from 2°C to 48°C, rich in minerals and trace elements that offer significant health and wellness benefits for a variety of conditions.

In winter, Arshan transforms into a breathtaking fairy-tale world. Snow-covered volcanic crater lakes, rime-covered birch trees, and steaming hot spring pools create a stunning tableau of fire and ice. The snow quality is excellent, with a snow season lasting up to seven months each year, making it a paradise for skiing enthusiasts. Here, you can experience the ultimate pleasure of "bathing in hot springs amid the snow."

Forest Sea Wonderland — A Fantastical Journey through Stone Forest and Three Pool Gorge

The Stone Forest is one of Arshan's most extraordinary geological wonders, covered in lava remnants formed after volcanic eruptions. Black volcanic rock interwoven with green moss creates an otherworldly landscape that feels like stepping onto an alien planet. The Stone Forest is home to many rare plants, the most striking of which is the dwarf pine — a unique pine tree that grows in volcanic rock crevices, with twisted branches and countless distinctive forms.

Three Pool Gorge is another spectacular attraction in Arshan, where the Halaha River cuts through volcanic rock to form three interconnected deep pools. The gorge is flanked by sheer cliffs, with clear, rushing waters and cascading waterfalls visible throughout. Azalea Lake is one of Arshan's most romantic spots — every May, azaleas bloom in profusion along the lakeshore, their pink blossoms creating a stunning contrast with the blue lake waters, making visitors reluctant to leave.
