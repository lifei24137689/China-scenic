Great Wall Meets Yellow River — Crossroads of Chinese Civilization

Laoniuwan is located at the border between Qingshuihe County, Hohhot, Inner Mongolia, and Pianguan County, Shanxi Province. It is the only place where the Great Wall meets the Yellow River, known as "the first bend of the Yellow River entering Shanxi." Here, the winding Ming Great Wall and the surging Yellow River converge, creating an epic junction of two great symbols of Chinese civilization.

The name "Laoniuwan" (Old Ox Bend) comes from a beautiful legend: in ancient times, a divine ox, wanting to relieve the suffering of the people, plowed through the mountains with its horns, forming this magnificent river bend. Today, Laoniuwan attracts countless visitors with its majestic natural scenery and profound historical and cultural heritage.

Gorge Wonderland — A Magnificent Tapestry Carved by the Yellow River

The Yellow River Gorge is the most spectacular section of the river within the Jin-Shaan Grand Canyon. For hundreds of millions of years, the ceaseless flow of the Yellow River has carved a deep gorge into the Loess Plateau, with sheer cliffs and crisscrossing ravines on both sides, creating a breathtaking natural landscape. The narrowest part of the gorge is only a few dozen meters wide, while the widest reaches several hundred meters.

Looking down from the cliffs of Laoniuwan, the Yellow River takes an almost 180-degree turn here, with emerald green waters flowing quietly through the gorge. In spring and summer, the cliffs are covered with lush trees and blooming mountain flowers; in autumn and winter, the forests turn golden or are blanketed in snow — each season offering its own unique grandeur.

Ancient Fortress — Laoniuwan Fortress & Ming Great Wall Ruins

Laoniuwan Fortress was a key component of the Ming Dynasty's Great Wall defense system, built during the Chenghua era (1465–1487). It is a military fortress constructed into the mountainside. The fortress walls, made of rammed earth, have withstood over 500 years of wind and rain and still stand proudly. From atop the walls, you can overlook the magnificent views of the Yellow River Gorge.

The scenic area also includes important sites such as the Wanjiazhai Water Control Project and the Qiankun Bend. Wanjiazhai is a large-scale water conservancy project on the middle reaches of the Yellow River, creating a stunning landscape of high gorges and placid lakes. The Qiankun Bend, named for the "S"-shaped turn the Yellow River makes here, is hailed as the "First Bend of the Yellow River."
