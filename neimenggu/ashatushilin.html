Geological Wonder — Granite Carved by Eons

Ashatu Stone Forest is located in Hexigten Banner, Chifeng City, Inner Mongolia, and is one of the world's most typical granite stone forest landforms. "Ashatu" means "steep rock" in the Mongolian language. Formed over approximately 150 million years, the stone forest was shaped by weathering, glacial erosion, and other natural forces, resulting in a spectacular array of unique formations.

The stone forest spreads across the prairie at an elevation of about 1700 meters, coexisting with the vast Gongger Prairie. From a distance, the stone pillars stand like knife-carved sculptures — a giant sculptural complex carved by nature over eons. In 2005, Ashatu Stone Forest was recognized as a Global Geopark by UNESCO.

Prairie Stone Array — A Dialogue of Stone & Prairie

What makes Ashatu Stone Forest unique is that it does not stand alone — together with the surrounding prairie, forests, and rivers, it forms a complete ecological landscape. The stark contrast between the stone forest and the prairie creates a striking visual experience — a dialogue across time between hard granite and soft grassland.

Key attractions include Moon Castle, Seven Fairies Peak, Kunpeng Alights on the Prairie, and Prairie Sky Road. Moon Castle is a group of stone pillars resembling an ancient castle, appearing more mysterious under moonlight. Seven Fairies Peak features seven uniquely shaped stone peaks standing side by side, said to be the embodiment of seven fairies descending to earth. Kunpeng Alights on the Prairie resembles a giant mythical bird spreading its wings over the grassland.

Kunpeng Alights on Prairie — A Symphony of Myth & Nature

Among the many remarkable rock formations of Ashatu Stone Forest, "Kunpeng Alights on the Prairie" is one of the most iconic. A massive granite rock resembles the legendary Kunpeng divine bird, spreading its wings as it descends upon the vast prairie. Local Mongolian herders have passed down the legend that this is the embodiment of a sacred bird bestowed upon the grassland by the heavens.

Each year at the turn of summer and autumn, hundreds of flowers bloom across the prairie, the green grass forming a vivid contrast with the gray-white stone forest. Visitors can stroll along the Prairie Sky Road and admire these stone pillars up close, experiencing nature's masterful craftsmanship. After winter snowfall, the stone forest is cloaked in silver, resembling a fairyland.
