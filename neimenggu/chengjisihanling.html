✦ 01 ✦

Land of the Legendary Khan

The cenotaph of Genghis Khan, the legendary conqueror — with a sacred flame that has burned for eight hundred years, this is the spiritual sanctuary of the Mongolian people. Located on the Gandel Grassland in Yijinhuoluo Banner, Ordos City, the Mausoleum of Genghis Khan is the holiest pilgrimage site in the hearts of Mongolians. It houses the relics and personal effects of Genghis Khan, preserving the immortal faith and glory of the Mongolian nation.

The Genghis Khan Mausoleum is not only the spiritual home of the Mongolian people but also a symbol of the integration of China's diverse ethnic cultures. The grand main hall stands majestic with its golden roof shining brilliantly. The Sulde Altar is solemn and dignified, attracting countless visitors from home and abroad each year who come to pay homage and experience the heroic spirit of the grassland warrior.

✦ 02 ✦

Main Hall

The main structure of the Genghis Khan Mausoleum consists of three interconnected yurt-shaped halls. The central main hall rises 26 meters high with a golden roof and blue eaves, majestic in scale. Inside the hall stands a statue of Genghis Khan, and the walls are adorned with exquisite murals that vividly depict the epic unification of the Mongolian tribes and the glorious history of the Mongol Empire.

The Sulde Altar, located in front of the mausoleum, is an important site for Mongolian ritual ceremonies. The Sulde is a symbol of the Mongolian war god. A tall Sulde flagpole stands on the altar. During grand ritual ceremonies, Mongolian compatriots, dressed in magnificent attire, gather here from all directions to hold solemn rituals, expressing their boundless reverence for their ancestors.

✦ 03 ✦

Grassland Culture

The Genghis Khan Mausoleum Scenic Area houses the Mongolian History and Culture Museum, with a rich collection showcasing the long history of the Mongolian people from their origins to their glorious era. The museum displays precious artifacts from Genghis Khan's time, including weapons, costumes, and daily utensils, as well as various exhibits on traditional Mongolian nomadic culture — an excellent window into the history and culture of the Mongolian people.

Naadam Square is a venue within the scenic area for traditional Mongolian sports events. Every summer, a grand Naadam Festival is held here. Traditional competitive events such as horse racing, wrestling, and archery are spectacular. The melodious sound of the horsehead fiddle and the heroic Mongolian long songs echo across the grassland, allowing visitors to deeply feel the boldness and passion of the grassland people.
