Millennia-old Ice Caves

The Yunqiu Mountain ice caves are one of the world's three greatest ice cave wonders, formed during the Quaternary glaciation 3 million years ago. To date, 14 ice caves have been discovered, with 5 open to the public. The total length reaches 199 meters, with a maximum width of 12 meters and height of 15 meters, maintaining a year-round temperature of -10°C inside.

The caves feature a dazzling array of ice stalagmites, ice pillars, ice waterfalls, and ice stalactites, resembling an underground crystal palace. Three million years of time have condensed into a translucent crystalline world, a true marvel of geological wonders.

Taoist Ancestral Temple

Yunqiu Mountain is the founding ancestral temple of the Quanzhen Longmen Sect, renowned as "Yunqiu in the North, Wudang in the South." Taoist culture runs deep here, with temples dotting the mountainside where incense has burned for millennia without cease.

The Ta'erpo Ancient Village boasts a history of 2,500 years. Legend says it was the lodging place of Laozi himself. With 43 preserved courtyards, it is hailed as a "living fossil of millennia-old residential architecture." Built into the mountainside with winding stone paths, the village carries the profound memories of Yellow River civilization.

Ecological Treasure

The scenic area spans 210 km², with a main developed zone of 35 km². Forest coverage reaches an impressive 89%, hosting over 2,300 tree species and more than 2,000 medicinal plants.

Karst landforms create magnificent landscapes of spectacular peaks, strange rocks, deep valleys, and secluded ravines. The concentration of negative oxygen ions reaches up to 25,000 per cm³, making it a true natural oxygen bar. The "Zhonghe Festival" was listed as a national intangible cultural heritage in 2011.
