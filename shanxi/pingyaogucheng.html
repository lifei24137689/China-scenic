/// 01

World Heritage

In December 1997, Pingyao Ancient City (including Shuanglin Temple and Zhenguo Temple) was inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List. First built during the reign of King Xuan of Western Zhou (c. 800 BC), it dates back over 2,700 years and is the best-preserved Ming-Qing county town in China.

The ancient city covers 2.25 km², with a city wall perimeter of 6,142.63 m, standing about 10–12 m high, featuring 6 gates. The wall has 72 watchtowers and 3,000 crenellations, symbolically representing Confucius' 72 disciples and 3,000 followers — a masterful design.

Shanxi Merchant Homeland

Pingyao is the birthplace of modern Chinese finance. In the third year of Daoguang's reign (1823), China's first draft bank — Rishengchang — was founded on West Street in Pingyao, ushering in a financial era of "a single paper connecting the world." At its peak, Pingyao housed 22 of the nation's 51 draft banks.

Rishengchang had branches in 35 cities across China, reaching as far as Europe, America, and Southeast Asia, renowned for "connecting the world through remittances." The Shanxi merchants spanned 9,000 miles across Eurasia and dominated commerce for 500 years, with Pingyao as the heart of this commercial legend.

/// 03

Living City

Pingyao Ancient City is not an empty museum. Its 199 streets and alleys (4 main streets, 8 minor streets, and 72 winding lanes) are still inhabited today. Some 3,798 traditional Ming-Qing courtyard residences are scattered throughout, with over 10,000 residents continuing their centuries-old way of life.

The city contains 1,075 immovable cultural relics and 143 protected heritage sites at various levels (including 20 at the national level). Shuanglin Temple is renowned as the "Treasure House of Oriental Painted Sculpture," while the Ten Thousand Buddha Hall at Zhenguo Temple is one of the oldest surviving wooden structures in China. In 2023, the site received 9.09 million visitors.
