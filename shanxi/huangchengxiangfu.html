/// 01

Famous Minister's Hometown

Chen Tingjing (1639–1712), courtesy name Ziduan, art name Shuoyan, also known as Wuting in his later years, was born in Zezhou, Shanxi (present-day Yangcheng County, Jincheng). A Jinshi scholar of the fifteenth year of Shunzhi reign, he served successively as Minister of Works, Revenue, Justice, and Personnel, and ultimately as Grand Secretary of the Wenyuan Pavilion.

He was the personal tutor of Emperor Kangxi and the chief editor of the Kangxi Dictionary. Serving the Kangxi Emperor for half a century, he was renowned for his integrity and erudition, hailed as "a premier minister of the era." Emperor Kangxi praised him as "magnanimous and seasoned, nearly perfect in virtue."

/// 02

Castle Marvel

Huangcheng Xiangfu consists of two parts: the inner city "Douzhuju" and the outer city "Zhongdaozhuang". The inner city was built in the fifth year of Chongzhen reign of Ming Dynasty (1632) by Chen Tingjing's uncle Chen Changyan to escape war; the outer city was built in the forty-second year of Kangxi reign of Qing Dynasty (1703) after Chen Tingjing became Grand Secretary of the Wenyuan Pavilion.

The city walls stand 12 meters high with a circumference of nearly one kilometer, encompassing 16 courtyards and over 640 rooms. The entire complex masterfully blends the ritual norms of official residences with military defense. The seven-story Heshan Tower stands majestically, making this an outstanding representative of castle-style official dwellings in northern China.

/// 03

Cultural Clan

The Chen clan was one of the most illustrious cultural families in northern China during the Ming and Qing dynasties. From the Wanli reign of Ming to the Qianlong reign of Qing, spanning over two hundred years, the family produced 41 Gongsheng, 19 Juren, 9 Jinshi, and 6 Hanlin scholars, resounding through court and society.

Emperor Kangxi visited Huangcheng Xiangfu twice and bestowed the imperial plaque "Wuting Mountain Village," a testament to his high esteem for Chen Tingjing. The Chen clan upheld the tradition of passing down poetry and scholarship through generations, and their family ethos continues to exert profound influence to this day — a paragon of ancient Chinese family culture.
