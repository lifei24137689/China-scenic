/// 01

First Grand Canyon of Taihang

Baquan Gorge is named after eight springs converging at one source. It stretches 13 km in length, with a basin area of 60.33 km². The canyon's lowest elevation is over 600 m, the highest over 1400 m, with a maximum drop of approximately 1100 m. It is acclaimed as the 'First Grand Canyon of Taihang'.

Within the scenic area, there are over 300 spring sources and over 30 waterfalls. Winding gorges and babbling brooks make this the most exquisite section of the Taihang Mountain Grand Canyon. After their inspection, UNESCO experts praised it as a 'world-class premium tourism resource'.

/// 02

Sky City

Baquan Gorge boasts multiple world-class tourism facilities. The 208 m sightseeing elevator — 'Sky City' — ascends from the canyon floor to the summit, offering visitors a 360-degree panorama of the Taihang peaks. The 3000 m cableway spans the canyon with a vertical drop of 369 m, providing an aerial view of the gorge's magnificent landscape.

The 760 m 'Time Tunnel' cuts through the mountain, connecting both sides of the canyon. The scenic area is divided into four touring zones: Gate Tower Landscape Area, Canyon Sightseeing Area, Cableway Sightseeing Area, and Trail Viewing Area, encompassing 36 major attractions for a rich and diverse experience.

/// 03

Taihang Eco-Treasure

Baquan Gorge is not only a geological wonder but also an ecological treasure. Covering a total area of 24.11 km², the scenery is lush with vegetation and fresh air, making it a National Forest Park and National Geopark. Streams within the canyon flow year-round with clear, sweet water.

In January 2020, Baquan Gorge was officially rated as a National 5A Tourist Attraction, becoming a new landmark of Shanxi tourism. Here, spring brings blooming wildflowers, summer offers clear springs and plunging waterfalls, autumn paints the mountains in red leaves, and winter showcases spectacular ice formations — each season presents a unique landscape, making it the most rewarding wonderland to explore in the Taihang Mountains.
