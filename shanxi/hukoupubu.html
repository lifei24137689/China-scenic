/// 01

A Thousand Li of Yellow River Gathered in One Pot

Hukou Waterfall is the world's largest yellow waterfall and China's second-largest waterfall. As the Yellow River surges to this point, the towering cliffs on both banks compress the upstream river — 300 meters wide — to just 20-30 meters within a mere 500 meters, cascading down a 30-meter sheer cliff, creating the awe-inspiring spectacle known as "A Thousand Li of Yellow River, Gathered in One Pot."

The thunderous roar of the waterfall and the rising mist often create soaring rainbows on sunny days, forming the wondrous scene of "smoke rising from water, rainbows bridging the sky." Located in the middle section of the Jin-Sha Grand Canyon, the scenic area covers 116 square kilometers — the most spectacular and breathtaking stretch of the Yellow River.

/// 02

Four Seasons at Hukou

Hukou Waterfall offers breathtakingly diverse scenery across the seasons. In spring, the "March Peach Blossom Flood" arrives as ice and snow melt, dramatically increasing water volume as the waterfall thunders like ten thousand horses galloping. In summer, the water flow is abundant, with the main and secondary falls merging into a majestic display, rainbows arching across both banks on sunny days.

Autumn's "Hukou Autumn Wind" brings crisp skies and light clouds, the waterfall roaring like a yellow dragon, with spray refracting countless rainbows in the sunlight. In winter, ice and snow seal the landscape, the waterfall freezes into stunning ice curtains and pillars, creating a rare frozen waterfall wonder that leaves all who see it in awe.

/// 03

Heart of the Yellow River · Soul of the Nation

Hukou Waterfall is not only a natural wonder but also a symbol of the Chinese national spirit. The Yellow River is the mother river of Chinese civilization, and Hukou is its most passionate and magnificent movement. In 1988, it was designated as a National Key Scenic Area, and in 2002, it was recognized as a National Geopark.

On July 15, 2022, Hukou Waterfall was officially rated as a National 5A Tourist Attraction. This place carries the profound memories of Yellow River civilization and bears witness to the enduring spirit of the Chinese people. Standing by the banks of Hukou, listen to the roar of the Yellow River and feel the surging power of the nation's soul.
