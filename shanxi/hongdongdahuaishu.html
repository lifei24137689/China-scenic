/// 01

Migration Epic

During the Hongwu and Yongle reigns of the Ming Dynasty (1368–1417), over nearly half a century, the Ming government organized 18 large-scale official migrations under the Great Pagoda Tree of Hongdong. Millions of people set out from beneath this ancient locust tree, relocating to 18 provinces and over 500 counties.

This was the largest, longest, and most extensive official migration movement in Chinese history. The Great Pagoda Tree thus became the most profound cultural symbol of the migration era, carrying the bloodline memories and homeland nostalgia of countless families.

/// 02

Root Sanctuary

The Ancestor Worship Hall enshrines 1,230 ancestral tablets of migrant surnames, making it the largest surname worship site in China. Every Qingming Festival, Zhongyuan Festival, and Winter Clothing Festival, tens of thousands of Chinese from home and abroad gather here to seek their roots and pay homage to their ancestors.

In 2008, the "Great Pagoda Tree Ancestor Worship Custom" was inscribed on the National Intangible Cultural Heritage list. This folk worship tradition, spanning over 600 years, has become an important cultural heritage and spiritual bond for the Chinese nation.

Chinese Homeland

"Where do my ancestors dwell? Shanxi's Hongdong Great Pagoda Tree" — this folk rhyme, sung for over 600 years, is the tenderest memory of home in the hearts of hundreds of millions of migrant descendants worldwide. The Great Pagoda Tree is far more than an ordinary tree; it has become the spiritual symbol of "home" for the Chinese nation.

The scenic area comprises five major sections: Migration Heritage Zone, Ancestor Worship Activity Zone, Folk Customs Zone, Fenhe River Ecological Zone, and Root Culture Plaza, featuring over 60 cultural attractions — from the Root Character Screen Wall to the Stele Pavilion, from the Ancestor Worship Hall to Guangji Temple, unfolding a complete root-seeking cultural lineage.
