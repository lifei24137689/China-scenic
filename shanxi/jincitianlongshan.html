Millennia-old Garden

Jinci is the earliest and largest existing Tang-Song classical garden in China, first built during the Northern Wei dynasty and flourishing in the Tang and Song dynasties. Three national treasure buildings — the Sacred Mother Hall, the Fish Pond Flying Bridge, and the Offering Hall — have stood for a thousand years, earning Jinci the title of "Museum of Ancient Chinese Architecture."

Set against mountains and waters, the garden features towering ancient trees and pavilions arranged in exquisite order. The Nanlao Spring, source of the Jin River, flows endlessly throughout the year. Jinci's three wonders — the Song-dynasty painted maidens in the Sacred Mother Hall, the Zhou Dynasty cypress, and the Nanlao Spring — embody the profound cultural heritage of Sanjin.

Grotto Art

The Tianlongshan Grottoes were first carved during the Eastern Wei period and continued through the Northern Qi, Sui, and Tang dynasties. Today, 29 caves with over 500 statues remain. The grotto complex is distributed across the eastern and western peaks, featuring steep terrain and lush forests, renowned as the "Tianlongshan Style."

A total of 1,144 apsaras, caisson ceilings, and murals showcase exquisite carving techniques and elegant forms. The Tianlongshan Grottoes serve as an important testament to the transition of Chinese grotto art from the Northern Wei to the Tang dynasty, hailed as a treasure house of world sculptural art.

Ancient Trees

The Jinci Tianlongshan Scenic Area boasts a vegetation coverage rate of over 90%, with 31 ancient trees over a thousand years old. The most famous among them, the Zhou Dynasty cypress, is approximately 2,990 years old and still flourishes after three millennia, standing as one of Jinci's three wonders.

The Panlong (Coiling Dragon) Pine at Tianlongshan is hailed as "China's Most Extraordinary Pine," with a shape like a giant coiled dragon, radiating majestic grandeur. The ancient groves and historic architecture complement each other, forming a unique historical and ecological landscape.
