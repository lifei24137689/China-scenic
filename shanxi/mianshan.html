Origin of Hanshi

Hermitage of Jie Zitui in Spring & Autumn Period · Birthplace of Hanshi & Qingming Festival · Over 2,640 Years of History

Mianshan, located 20 km southeast of Jiexiu City, Jinzhong, Shanxi Province, is a famous historical and cultural mountain in China and the birthplace of the oldest commemorative festival of the Chinese nation — Hanshi (Cold Food) and Qingming Festival.

Over 2,640 years ago, during the Spring and Autumn Period, the renowned Jin minister Jie Zitui followed Duke Wen of Jin (Chong'er) into exile for 19 years. When Chong'er was starving, Jie Zitui "cut flesh from his own thigh to make soup" to save him. After Chong'er ascended the throne, Jie Zitui sought no reward and retreated with his mother into seclusion in Mianshan.

To force Jie Zitui out, Duke Wen ordered the mountain to be set on fire. Unexpectedly, Jie Zitui and his mother perished while embracing a willow tree. Stricken with remorse, Duke Wen decreed that no fires be lit and only cold food be eaten on this day each year — thus the Hanshi Festival was born, 350 years earlier than the Dragon Boat Festival. Mianshan has since become a sacred site of eternal loyalty and filial piety.

Fairyland Landscape

294.75km² · 98% Forest Cover · Natural Oxygen Bar

Mianshan covers a total area of 294.75 square kilometers, with a scenic area of 54.1 km² and a highest peak of 2,566.6 meters. The forest coverage rate reaches 98%, the average annual temperature is 10°C, and the concentration of negative oxygen ions reaches 15,000–18,000 per cm³, making it a true "natural oxygen bar."

The scenic area features 14 major sites and over 360 attractions, integrating mountain scenery, cultural relics, Buddhist temples, and revolutionary ruins. Towering cliffs, winding perilous paths, murmuring streams, and ancient cypresses reaching the sky — its cliff-hanging architecture is awe-inspiring, making Mianshan a rare tourism destination in northern China that blends nature and culture.

Cliff-Hanging Wonders

Daluo Palace — Foremost Taoist Temple · Baofu Cliff · Twelve Mummified Bodhisattva Statues

Mianshan's most breathtaking cliff-hanging architectural complex is built against the precipice, steep and magnificent. Among them, Daluo Palace is known as the "foremost Taoist temple under heaven," suspended on a hundred-meter cliff with multi-story stacked pavilions, forming one of the largest Taoist architectural complexes in China.

Baofu Cliff (also known as Baofo Cliff) is Mianshan's greatest spectacle — a naturally formed giant cave that can accommodate over 200 halls and chambers. Numerous temples and shrines have been built within the cave against the mountain, making it a perfect fusion of nature and human craftsmanship.

Zhengguo Temple enshrines 12 mummified Bodhisattva statues wrapped in clay that have remained incorrupt for over a thousand years — an extremely rare miracle in Buddhist history with profound religious and archaeological value.
