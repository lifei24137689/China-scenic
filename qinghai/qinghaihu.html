Plateau Pearl

Qinghai Lake, located in the northeastern part of Qinghai Province, is China's largest inland saltwater lake, known as the "Plateau Sapphire". With a surface elevation of 3,196 meters and an area of 4,540 square kilometers, it stretches as vast as the sea, where water and sky merge into one. In 2005, it was ranked first among "China's Five Most Beautiful Lakes" by Chinese National Geography.

The formation of Qinghai Lake originated from geological movements about 200,000 years ago. The uplift of the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau created this plateau basin, where meltwater from surrounding snow-capped mountains converges to form this magnificent plateau lake. The lake water is deep blue with extremely high transparency, displaying captivating color changes under sunlight.

Ecological Treasure

Qinghai Lake is an internationally important wetland and a crucial stopover for bird migration. Every spring and summer, tens of thousands of migratory birds inhabit and breed here, the most famous being bar-headed geese, fish gulls, and cormorants. Bird Island, as a core protected area, is a paradise for birdwatchers.

The lake's ecosystem is unique and fragile, hosting a variety of rare plants and animals. The Przewalski's gazelle, endemic to the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau, runs freely here. On the lakeside grasslands, plateau flowers such as Potentilla fruticosa and Potentilla glabra bloom in abundance. The Qinghai Lake naked carp (Gymnocypris przewalskii) is the only fish species in the lake, known as the "giant panda of the water".

Scenery of Four Seasons

Qinghai Lake is beautiful in all four seasons, each with its own charm. From July to August each year, thousands of acres of rapeseed flowers bloom along the lakeshore, creating a stunning picture with golden flower seas reflecting off the blue lake water. In winter, the lake surface freezes over, resembling a giant white jade mirror, crystal clear.

The lake-ring road stretches about 360 kilometers, making it a mecca for cycling enthusiasts. The annual Tour of Qinghai Lake International Road Cycling Race has become one of Asia's top events. In 2011, Qinghai Lake Scenic Area was officially rated as a National 5A Tourist Attraction, receiving over 3.1 million visitors annually.
