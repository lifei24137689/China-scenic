Culture

Rainbow Tribe · Tu Culture

Step into the Homeland of Rainbows

Located in Huzhu Tu Autonomous County, Haidong City, Qinghai Province, the Huzhu Tu Ethnic Homeland sits at the southeastern foot of the Qilian Mountains on the northern bank of the Huangshui River basin, at an average elevation of approximately 2,500 meters. This is the only Tu autonomous county in China and the region where Tu culture is best preserved, earning it the reputation of being the "Homeland of Rainbows."

Covering a total area of 6.8 km², it is a national 5A tourist attraction that integrates Tu folk culture, natural ecology, and leisure vacations. The ancestors of the Tu people were descendants of the ancient Tuyuhun, and after millennia of heritage, they have formed a distinctive ethnic cultural system.

— A thousand years of heritage, one continuous legacy

Folk Custom

Folk Customs · Intangible Heritage Treasures

Experience the Charm of Intangible Heritage

The park features several core attractions including the Rainbow Tribe Tu Garden, Nadun Manor, Western Tu Folk Culture Village, and the Origin of Chinese Highland Barley Wine at Tianyoude, offering a comprehensive display of Tu architectural art, costume culture, dietary customs, and festive traditions.

Multiple intangible cultural heritage practices are preserved here, including Tu pan-embroidery, Tu wedding ceremonies, the Anzhao dance, and the "Nadun" festival. Tu women, dressed in seven-color sleeved gowns, dance gracefully on the wheel swing, creating the most captivating scene in the Homeland.

— Seven-color sleeved gowns, dancing through millennia
Gastronomy

Barley Wine Town · A Feast for the Senses

Savor the Highland Aroma

Huzhu is renowned as the "Origin of Chinese Highland Barley Wine," with Tianyoude's barley wine brewing techniques passed down through centuries. The Origin of Barley Wine attraction within the park traces the complete lineage from traditional brewing methods to modern techniques, with the rich aroma of barley wine wafting through every corner of the Homeland.

Beyond the fine wine, Tu specialty cuisine is equally captivating — Goujiao Niang Bing (pan-fried chive cake), Bei Kou Dai (stuffed pocket bread), yogurt, hand-grabbed lamb — each dish embodies the Tu people's love for life and wisdom. At Nadun Manor, you can also savor the most authentic Tu hospitality feast.

— Fine wine wafts, with a lingering aftertaste
