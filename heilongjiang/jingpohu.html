5A Scenic Area Jingpo Lake ★★★★★

Volcanic Dammed Lake · UNESCO Global Geopark

Located in Mudanjiang City, Heilongjiang Province, Jingpo Lake is China's largest volcanic lava dammed lake. The lake boasts crystal-clear waters, a winding shoreline, and is embraced by verdant mountains. The spectacular Diaoshuilou Waterfall within the scenic area is the landmark attraction of Jingpo Lake.

01

Dammed Lake

Jingpo Lake is China's largest volcanic lava dammed lake, formed approximately ten thousand years ago by volcanic eruptions. Lava flows blocked the Mudanjiang River, creating this beautiful lake. The lake sits at an altitude of 350 meters, stretching 45 kilometers from north to south and up to 6 kilometers at its widest, covering a water area of 95 square kilometers.

The lake water is so clear you can see the bottom, with an average depth of 40 meters and a maximum depth of 70 meters. The shoreline is winding with numerous islands, earning it the name "Hundred-Li Long Lake." The area experiences four distinct seasons: blooming mountain flowers in spring, gentle blue waves in summer, layered autumn foliage, and magnificent winter ice and snow.

02

Diaoshuilou Waterfall

Diaoshuilou Waterfall is the iconic landmark of Jingpo Lake and the largest basalt waterfall in China. The waterfall is approximately 40 meters wide with a drop of about 20 meters. During the summer when water flow is abundant, the waterfall cascades down like a Milky Way, thundering through the valley with magnificent force.

In winter, Diaoshuilou Waterfall presents an entirely different spectacle. The frozen waterfall forms a massive ice curtain, crystal clear and translucent, like a jade belt suspended between the mountains. This unique ice waterfall attracts numerous visitors and is a highlight of winter tourism at Jingpo Lake.

03

Geological Wonder

Jingpo Lake is not only a scenic wonder but also a UNESCO Global Geopark. It preserves complete volcanic geological relics, including volcanic craters, lava platforms, and lava tunnels. These geological remains record the entire process of volcanic activity, serving as a natural museum for the study of volcanic geology.

Within the scenic area, Maogong Mountain is named for its striking resemblance to the recumbent figure of Chairman Mao Zedong, making it one of Jingpo Lake's important attractions. Additionally, cultural landmarks such as the Yaoshi Ancient Temple, Rainbow Bridge, and Hongluonu Cultural Park complement the natural scenery beautifully.
