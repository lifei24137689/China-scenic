Homeland of Red-Crowned Cranes World Great Wetland

Zhalong Wetland is China's largest nature reserve centered on rare birds and wetland ecosystems, with cranes and other large waterfowl as its main inhabitants. It is renowned as the "World's Great Wetland, China's Crane Homeland."

01

WetlandWonder

Zhalong Wetland, located in the western Songnen Plain, covers a total area of 2,100 km² and is the best-preserved and most pristine wetland ecosystem in northern China. With swaying reeds and lush aquatic plants, it serves as an ideal habitat for numerous rare waterfowl.

Crisscrossed by rivers and dotted with lakes, the wetland creates a unique landscape. Every spring and autumn, tens of thousands of migratory birds stop here to rest and breed, painting a vibrant picture of nature.

Red-Crowned CraneHomeland

Zhalong Wetland is a world-renowned breeding ground and migratory stopover for red-crowned cranes. Each year, over 300 wild red-crowned cranes breed and thrive here. Elegant in posture and clear in call, the red-crowned crane is revered as the "Immortal Crane," a symbol of grace and longevity.

The scenic area features a crane-watching platform and a crane rewilding and training zone, allowing visitors to observe the graceful postures and flight of red-crowned cranes up close. The daily crane release performance is a major highlight of the area.

03

EcologicalTreasure

Zhalong Wetland is not only the home of red-crowned cranes, but also a habitat for many rare bird species. Over 260 bird species have been recorded here, including 8 species under national first-class protection and more than 40 under second-class protection, making it a true bird paradise.

The wetland ecosystem is complete and rich in biodiversity. In addition to birds, it hosts fish, amphibians, reptiles, mammals, and a variety of aquatic plants and wetland vegetation, forming a complete ecological chain.
