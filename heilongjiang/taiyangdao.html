Pearl of the Ice City · European Charm

Sun Island Park is located on the north bank of the Songhua River in Harbin. It is a national-level scenic area that blends ice and snow culture, folk culture, European charm, and suburban landscapes, renowned as Harbin's "city living room."

01

Island Scenery

Sun Island is located on the north bank of the Songhua River, covering a total area of 38 km². It is the largest riverside ecological zone in Harbin. The island is lush with green trees and fragrant flowers, truly earning its reputation as a "natural oxygen bar" and "city lung."

The scenic area is divided into the East District, Central District, and West District. The East District is the core landscape area, the Central District is a cultural leisure zone, and the West District is a natural ecological area. The four seasons here are distinct: spring brings a riot of flowers, summer offers lush greenery, autumn displays a tapestry of colorful foliage, and winter presents spectacular ice and snow wonders.

02

Ice & Snow Art

Sun Island is the main venue for the Harbin International Snow Sculpture Expo. Every winter, a grand snow sculpture art exhibition is held here. Artists from around the world create exquisite snow sculptures, perfectly blending ice and snow with art.

The Snow Sculpture Expo is a vital part of the Harbin Ice and Snow Festival, standing alongside the Ice and Snow World and the Ice Lantern Garden Party as Harbin's three major ice and snow attractions. The snow sculptures here are grand in scale and beautifully crafted, making it a must-visit destination for winter tourism.

European Charm

Sun Island is deeply influenced by Russian culture, with buildings on the island exuding strong European charm. The Rain Shelter Corridor, Russian-style wooden houses, and European gardens showcase Harbin's unique urban character and cultural heritage.

This place is not only a haven of natural beauty but also a vessel of culture. Sun Island has witnessed a century of Harbin's historical changes, blending the essence of Chinese and Russian cultures to form a distinctive urban cultural landscape.
