China's Northernmost

Beiji Village is located in Beiji Town, Mohe City — the northernmost village in China, known as the "Arctic Outpost of China." It faces Russia across the river, with the Heilong River flowing past the village, creating a unique border landscape.

Beiji Village is not only China's northernmost geographic point but also a cultural symbol of the Arctic. The village features iconic landmarks such as the "Arctic Outpost of China" stele and Beiji Village Square, attracting countless visitors who come to experience the thrill of "finding the North."

Spectacular Aurora

Beiji Village is the only place in China where the aurora borealis can be observed. The aurora is a dazzling natural phenomenon caused by the interaction of solar wind with the Earth's magnetic field, displaying a spectrum of colors including green, purple, and red.

The best time to view the aurora is during winter nights, especially from December to February. Standing by the Heilong River on a clear, moonless night, gazing at the starry sky and waiting for the aurora to appear, is an unforgettable experience.

Unique Climate

Beiji Village lies in a cold temperate continental climate zone, with long, frigid winters where temperatures can drop below -40°C, and cool, short summers with average temperatures around 20°C. It is one of the coldest places in China.

The Beiji Village National Weather Observation Station is the northernmost meteorological station in China, recording the village's climate data. With distinct four seasons and pronounced climatic features, it serves as an important base for climate change research and meteorological science.
