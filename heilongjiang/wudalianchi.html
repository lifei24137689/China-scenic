5A Scenic Area Wudalianchi ★★★★★

Volcanic Geology Wonder · UNESCO Global Geopark

Wudalianchi Scenic Area is located in Heihe City, Heilongjiang Province. It is a well-preserved volcanic geology museum, featuring 14 volcanoes, 5 interconnected lakes, abundant mineral spring resources, and unique volcanic landforms. It is known as the "Natural Volcanic Museum."

01

Geological Wonder

Wudalianchi Scenic Area covers a total area of 1,060 km² and is one of the best-preserved volcanic geological sites in the world. Its landscape was formed by volcanic eruptions between 1719 and 1721, when lava flows blocked the Bai River, a tributary of the Nemoer River, creating five interconnected lakes.

The scenic area features a complete range of volcanic landforms, including craters, lava plateaus, lava tunnels, and lava waterfalls, making it a natural laboratory for volcanic research. In 2004, Wudalianchi was designated a UNESCO Global Geopark.

Volcanic Cluster

The Wudalianchi volcanic cluster consists of 14 volcanoes, among which Laohei Mountain (Black Mountain) and Huoshao Mountain (Burn Mountain) are the youngest, formed by the 1719鈥?721 eruptions. Laohei Mountain stands at 515.9 meters above sea level, making it the highest volcano in the area, with a well-preserved crater approximately 350 meters in diameter.

The volcanic cluster includes both young and old volcanoes. The young ones are represented by Laohei Mountain and Huoshao Mountain, while the older ones include Nan Gelaqiu Mountain and Xi Longmen Mountain. These volcanoes create a unique volcanic landscape and serve as an important base for studying volcanic activity.

Lake Scenery

Wudalianchi consists of five interconnected lakes: from north to south, they are Lianhua Lake, Yanshan Lake, Bailong Lake, Heming Lake, and Ruyi Lake. The total lake area is approximately 18.47 km², with a maximum depth of 10 meters. The water is crystal clear and the scenery is stunning.

The five lakes are connected by natural waterways, creating a unique beaded-lake landscape. The lake water is rich in minerals and has distinctive therapeutic value. With lush trees lining the shores, it is an ideal destination for leisure and vacation.
