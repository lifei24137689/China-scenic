Border Fortress

Hutou Scenic Area is located in Hutou Town, Hulin City, Jixi, Heilongjiang Province, along the banks of the Wusuli River, facing Russia across the river. This area has a unique geographical position and is one of the important border port cities between China and Russia.

Hutou Town has a long history and has been an important military stronghold since ancient times. During World War II, the Japanese Kwantung Army built the Hutou Fortress here, making it one of the largest military fortresses in Asia. Today, the Hutou Fortress site has become an important patriotic education base and tourist attraction.

Historic Fortress

The Hutou Fortress is a military fortress built by the Japanese Kwantung Army during World War II, located within Hutou Town. The fortress consists of multiple underground structures, including command posts, barracks, warehouses, hospitals, and other facilities, making it one of the largest military fortresses in Asia.

In August 1945, the Soviet Red Army attacked the Hutou Fortress, and after fierce fighting, ultimately captured it. The Battle of Hutou Fortress was the final battle of World War II and holds great historical significance. Today, the Hutou Fortress site has become an important patriotic education base, attracting numerous visitors.

Natural Scenery

Hutou Scenic Area not only has profound historical and cultural heritage but also boasts beautiful natural scenery. The Wusuli River flows past the scenic area, with clear waters and picturesque banks, making it an excellent place to enjoy border scenery.

Within the scenic area, there are also natural landscapes such as Xingkai Lake, with stunning lake and mountain views. Xingkai Lake is a boundary lake between China and Russia, with clear waters and rich wetland ecology along its shores, making it a great place for birdwatching and leisure vacations.
