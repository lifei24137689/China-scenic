The Last Boat Trackers · The Most Moving Sight of the Three Gorges

Shennongxi is world-renowned for its boat tracker culture. The trackers here are the last of their kind in the world. Their bare-backed towing silhouettes have become the most touching scenery of the Three Gorges. The waters of Shennongxi are crystal clear, flanked by towering cliffs, and the primitive, rustic canyon landscape offers a refreshing and uplifting experience.

The boat trackers struggle against the rapids using the most primitive methods, chanting resonant work songs as they forge ahead through treacherous shoals. This ancient mode of river transport has persisted for over a thousand years, now standing as Shennongxi's most unique cultural heritage, a testament to the resilience and wisdom of working people.

Primitive Drifting · A Thrilling Journey Through the Canyon

Shennongxi drifting is the most distinctive experience. Riding an ancient "Peapod" wooden boat through the rushing stream, with sheer cliffs on both sides and a sliver of sky overhead, the boatmen chant work songs and pole the boat forward, offering a truly primitive drifting thrill.

The "Peapod" wooden boat is a unique watercraft exclusive to Shennongxi — long and narrow, shaped like a pea pod, lightweight and agile. Navigating through rapids and shoals, the boatmen steer with masterful skill, allowing visitors to enjoy the majestic primitive canyon scenery amidst the excitement and adventure.

Mystery of the Hanging Coffins · An Ancient Unsolved Cultural Code

On the cliffs along both sides of Shennongxi, ancient hanging coffins left by the Ba people can be seen. These coffins are perched tens of meters above the water, and how the ancients managed to place heavy coffins onto the cliffs remains an unsolved mystery to this day. The presence of these hanging coffins adds an air of mystery to Shennongxi.

Hanging coffin burial was a unique funerary custom of the ancient Ba people, with coffins placed in natural caves or man-made niches on sheer cliffs. This burial practice reflects the ancient Ba people's belief in "burying near water, honoring height." The hanging coffin clusters at Shennongxi are important physical materials for the study of ancient Ba culture.
