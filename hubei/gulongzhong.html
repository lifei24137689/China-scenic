Three Visits to the Cottage · Cradle of the Tripartite Strategy

Gulongzhong is where Zhuge Liang lived in seclusion and farmed during the Three Kingdoms period. The story of Liu Bei's "Three Visits to the Cottage" took place here. Moved by Liu Bei's sincerity, Zhuge Liang proposed the famous "Longzhong Plan," outlining the strategic blueprint for dividing the empire into three.

In 207 AD, Liu Bei personally visited Zhuge Liang at Gulongzhong three times, failing to meet him on the first two attempts. On the third visit, they finally met, and Zhuge Liang analyzed the overall situation of the realm for Liu Bei, proposing the strategy of allying with Wu against Cao and occupying Jing and Yi provinces. From then on, Zhuge Liang left his secluded life to assist Liu Bei, ushering in the historical chapter of the Three Kingdoms tripartite division.

Wuhou Temple · A Sacred Site Honoring Zhuge Liang Through the Ages

The Wuhou Temple houses a statue of Zhuge Liang, with numerous poems and inscriptions left by literati through the ages. The temple grounds are solemn and dignified, with ancient cypresses reaching skyward. Every year, countless visitors come to pay homage to this illustrious prime minister who "gave his all until his heart ceased to beat."

The Wuhou Temple was first built in the Jin Dynasty and has been well-preserved through millennia of renovations. The temple is filled with inscriptions, gathering poems and writings from famous figures across dynasties. Among the most renowned is Yue Fei's handwritten copy of the "Memorial on Sending Out the Troops," with its vigorous brushwork and majestic momentum — a treasured artifact within the temple.

Thatched Cottage Chronicle · Zhuge Liang's Rural Life of Farming in Seclusion

Gulongzhong boasts a tranquil environment where Zhuge Liang spent ten years in seclusion. Attractions like the Thatched Cottage Pavilion, Farming Field, and Embracing Knees Pavilion recreate scenes of Zhuge Liang's life "farming in Nanyang." Here, you can experience Zhuge Liang's spiritual realm of serene ambition and tranquil far-sightedness.

Zhuge Liang's decade in Gulongzhong was an important period of accumulation in his life. He studied and farmed here, befriended renowned scholars, and observed the overall situation of the realm, laying a solid foundation for his later role in assisting Liu Bei. The mountains and waters of Gulongzhong witnessed the growth and transformation of a generation's wisest strategist.
