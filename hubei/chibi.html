The River Flows East The Historic Site of the Battle of Red Cliffs

The Red Cliffs Ancient Battlefield is the site of the "Battle of Red Cliffs" during the Three Kingdoms period. In 208 AD, the allied forces of Sun Quan and Liu Bei defeated Cao Cao's million-strong army here, laying the foundation for the tripartite division of the Three Kingdoms. "The mighty river flows east, its waves have swept away all the great heroes of the ages" — Su Shi's "Remembering the Past at Red Cliffs" (Nian Nu Jiao) made Red Cliffs famous for eternity.

Standing on the Red Cliffs bluff, gazing at the rolling Yangtze River flowing east, the scene of thousands of war ships racing and flames soaring to the sky from that year seems right before your eyes. This is one of the most famous battle sites in Chinese history.

Cliff Inscriptions Zhou Yu's Eternal Imprint

The cliff inscription of "Red Cliffs" (赤壁) on the river bluff is said to have been written by Zhou Yu. Each character is 150 cm tall and 100 cm wide, with vigorous strokes that remain clearly visible after a thousand years of wind and rain. Standing on the Red Cliffs bluff, you can almost hear the thunder of war drums from that era.

The cliff inscriptions are among the most precious cultural relics of the Red Cliffs Ancient Battlefield. These carvings record the odes to Red Cliffs by literati through the ages, serving as important physical materials for studying Three Kingdoms history.

Wind Worship Platform The Legend of Zhuge Liang Borrowing the East Wind

The Wind Worship Platform is said to be where Zhuge Liang set up an altar to borrow the east wind. In that year, Zhuge Liang "borrowed" the east wind here to assist Zhou Yu's fire attack on Cao Cao's warships, achieving the brilliant victory of the Battle of Red Cliffs. The Wind Worship Platform is simple and solemn, the most legendary structure at the Red Cliffs Ancient Battlefield.

The Wind Worship Platform sits atop Red Cliffs Mountain. Ascending the platform and looking into the distance, the Yangtze River is like a silk ribbon and the mountains are like dark eyebrows. Legend has it that Zhuge Liang observed the celestial phenomena and prayed for the east wind here, leaving behind the eternal allusion "everything is ready, all we need is the east wind."
