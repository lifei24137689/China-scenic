Roof of Central China · A Green Treasure of World Natural Heritage

Shennongjia is named after the legendary Yan Emperor Shennong, who is said to have climbed cliffs and gathered herbs here. It is the only administrative region in China named as a "Forest District." Shennong Peak, at 3,106 meters, is the highest peak in Central China. In 2016, it was inscribed as a UNESCO World Natural Heritage site, boasting a pristine ecosystem.

With a forest coverage rate of over 96%, Shennongjia is home to more than 3,000 species of plants and over 1,800 species of wildlife, including precious animals such as golden snub-nosed monkeys and South China tigers. It is truly a "green treasure trove" and a "species gene bank."

Wild Man Mystery · The Enigmatic Veil of Shennongjia

The most legendary aspect of Shennongjia is the "Wild Man" mystery. For centuries, reports of sightings of the Shennongjia Wild Man have never ceased. Although no conclusive evidence has been found to this day, this mysterious legend has added endless allure to Shennongjia, drawing countless adventurers and scientists to explore.

Since the 1970s, the Chinese Academy of Sciences has repeatedly organized scientific expeditions into Shennongjia to investigate the Wild Man. While the existence of the Wild Man has not been confirmed, a large number of suspected traces have been discovered, including strange footprints, hair, and fecal samples, making the mystery even more intriguing.

Dajiuhu Wetlands · A Dreamlike Meadow on the Alpine Wetland

Dajiuhu is the most beautiful alpine wetland in Shennongjia, formed by a chain of nine lakes of varying sizes. With abundant water and lush grass, herds of cattle and sheep roam freely, surrounded by rolling mountains. It is often called "Hubei's Hulunbuir." In the early morning, mist shrouds the lake surface like a mirror, resembling a celestial jade pool.

Dajiuhu Wetland is the largest and best-preserved alpine wetland in Central China, with an elevation of approximately 1,700 meters. Its wetland ecosystem holds tremendous scientific research and ornamental value. Spring and autumn are particularly enchanting, while summer is cool and pleasant, making it an ideal summer retreat.
