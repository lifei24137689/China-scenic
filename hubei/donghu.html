China's Largest Urban Lake

East Lake is the largest urban lake in China, with a water area six times that of West Lake in Hangzhou. Centered around Chu culture, it comprises four main scenic areas: Tingtao, Moshan, Luoyan, and Chuidi. Mao Zedong stayed at East Lake 48 times, calling it "the place of white clouds and yellow cranes".

With its vast waters, winding shoreline, hills within the lake, and lakes nestled among the hills, East Lake offers a mesmerizing blend of mountain and water scenery. Whether boating on the lake or strolling along its shores, you can experience the unique charm of this urban lake.

East Lake Greenway World-Class Urban Greenway

Stretching 101 kilometers, the East Lake Greenway is the longest urban greenway in the world. It connects the various scenic areas of East Lake, offering breathtaking lake and mountain views along the way. Whether cycling or strolling, the lake breeze and rippling blue waters make it a favorite leisure activity for Wuhan residents.

The greenway is divided into six sections: Tingtao, Moshan, Luoyan, Baima, Forest, and Countryside, each with its own distinct scenery and experience. Morning jogs, afternoon bike rides, and evening walks — the East Lake Greenway embodies the good life for the people of Wuhan.

Moshan Chu City A Journey Through Time to the Chu Kingdom

The Moshan scenic area is centered around Chu culture, featuring attractions such as Chu City, Chu Sky Tower, and Chu Ci Garden. Chu Sky Tower is an iconic landmark of East Lake — from its summit, a panoramic view of the entire lake unfolds. Each spring, when cherry blossoms are in full bloom, the Moshan Cherry Blossom Garden is a breathtaking sight.

Moshan Chu City recreates the grandeur of the ancient Chu capital. At Chu Sky Tower, the melodies of bronze bell and dance performances linger in the air, while in Chu Ci Garden, Qu Yuan's poetry has been celebrated for millennia. Here, you can experience the profound depth of Chu culture.
