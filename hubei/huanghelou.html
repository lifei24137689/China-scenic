/// 01

Millennium Tower · The Finest Tower Under Heaven

The Yellow Crane Tower was first built during the Three Kingdoms period. Destroyed and rebuilt many times through the dynasties, the current tower was reconstructed in 1985. Cui Hao's poem "The sage has ridden the yellow crane away, Only the Yellow Crane Tower remains here" and Li Bai's admiration made the tower famous for millennia. Ascending the tower, you can see the Yangtze River flowing to the horizon and the three towns of Wuhan spreading out beneath your feet.

Poetic Legacy · A Pilgrimage for Literati

Since ancient times, the Yellow Crane Tower has been a gathering place for scholars and poets. Literary giants such as Cui Hao, Li Bai, Bai Juyi, and Su Shi left behind hundreds of timeless poems. Sites like Baiyun Pavilion, Plum Pavilion, and the Poetry Stele Corridor are steeped in a rich poetic and cultural atmosphere.

/// 03

Pearl of Jingchu · A Cultural Landmark by the Yangtze

Together with Yueyang Tower and Prince Teng's Pavilion, the Yellow Crane Tower is known as one of the "Three Great Towers of Jiangnan." As an urban landmark of Wuhan and a national 5A-level tourist attraction, the tower embodies the essence of millennia of Jingchu culture and serves as an important window into traditional Chinese culture.
