The No.1 Celestial Mountain · Architectural Marvel of Taoism

Wudang Mountain is a renowned Taoist mountain and the birthplace of Wudang martial arts, hailed as "an unparalleled sacred land, the No.1 celestial mountain under heaven." The ancient architectural complex of Wudang Mountain is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, grand in scale and magnificent in style, representing the pinnacle of ancient Chinese architectural art.

The Wudang Mountain architectural complex was first built in the Tang Dynasty and reached its peak during the Ming Dynasty. Emperor Yongle of the Ming Dynasty undertook a massive renovation of Wudang, spending 12 years to build 33 palace and temple complexes, creating a spectacular scene of "a nunnery every five li, a palace every ten li, with red walls and green tiles glittering in the distance." These buildings follow the mountain's contours, harmoniously blending with the natural environment.

Golden Hall · Bronze-Gilt Miracle atop Tianzhu Peak

The Golden Hall, built atop Tianzhu Peak, is the largest existing bronze-gilt hall in China. Entirely constructed from bronze-gilt components, it has remained brilliantly golden for over 600 years despite wind and rain. The statue of Emperor Zhenwu inside is solemn and majestic, revered as the treasure of Wudang Mountain.

Built in the 14th year of Yongle of the Ming Dynasty (1416), the Golden Hall stands 5.5 meters high, 4.4 meters wide, and 3.15 meters deep. All components were cast in Beijing and then transported to Wudang Mountain for assembly. The casting craftsmanship represents the highest level of Ming Dynasty metalwork, and has remained dazzlingly golden for centuries — a true marvel in architectural history.

Wudang Martial Arts · The Tao of Softness Overcoming Strength

Founded by Zhang Sanfeng, Wudang martial arts are renowned for "softness overcoming strength and leveraging four ounces to deflect a thousand pounds," making them a major school of Chinese martial arts. Wudang Mountain is home to numerous martial arts academies, where every morning you can see Taoists and students practicing Tai Chi in the morning light.

Based on Taoist philosophy, Wudang martial arts emphasize "using stillness to control motion, softness to overcome hardness, and striking after the opponent strikes." Tai Chi, Xingyi Quan, and Bagua Zhang — these Wudang boxing styles are widely practiced worldwide. More than just martial arts, they are a path of self-cultivation, attracting martial arts enthusiasts from around the globe to study here.
