/// 01

Dragon Vein Imperial Mausoleum

The Eastern Qing Tombs are located in Zunhua City, Hebei Province. They are the largest and most complete imperial mausoleum complex in China. Construction began in the 18th year of Shunzhi (1661) and lasted 247 years. The tombs house five emperors Shunzhi, Kangxi, Qianlong, Xianfeng, and Tongzhi along with 161 royal members including Empress Dowager Xiaozhuang.

In 2000, the Eastern Qing Tombs were inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List. Bounded by Changrui Mountain, the complex is divided into the front enclosure and the rear dragon section, covering a total area of 80 square kilometers. It represents the culmination of Chinese feudal imperial mausoleum architecture and stands as a pinnacle of ancient Chinese architectural art.

/// 02

Sacred Way Grand Spectacle

The Sacred Way of the Eastern Qing Tombs stretches an impressive 5 kilometers, lined with 18 pairs of stone statues including civil officials, military generals, horses, qilins, elephants, camels, xiezhi, and lions. Each statue is carved from a single massive stone block, featuring vivid forms and exquisite craftsmanship outstanding representatives of ancient Chinese stone carving art.

Built along the mountain terrain, the Sacred Way winds gracefully with stone statues arranged symmetrically on both sides, creating a solemn and majestic atmosphere. The Xiaoling Sacred Way is the largest, with a stone archway, grand red gate, changing hall, dragon-phoenix gate and other structures progressively unfolding, showcasing the grand layout of Qing imperial mausoleum architecture.

/// 03

Underground Palace Historical Witness

The most famous underground palace in the Eastern Qing Tombs is the Yuling Underground Palace of Emperor Qianlong. Built of stone, it extends 54 meters deep with an area of 372 square meters. The walls and vaulted ceiling are adorned with exquisite Buddhist stone carvings, including the Eight Great Bodhisattvas, Four Heavenly Kings, and Five Directional Buddhas. The intricate craftsmanship has earned it the reputation of a "treasure house of stone carving art."

Equally renowned is the Dingdongling Tomb of Empress Dowager Cixi. Its Long'en Hall and eastern/western side halls feature precious huanghuali wood structures, with golden dragon and color paintings still shimmering today. The floor of Cixi's tomb is paved with speckled stones transported from Yunnan, each piece meticulously polished.
