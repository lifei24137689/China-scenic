/// Vast Blue Waves

Pearl of North China · Vast Blue Waves

Located in Anxin County, Baoding City, Hebei Province, Baiyangdian is the largest freshwater lake on the North China Plain. Covering a total area of 366 km², with water area accounting for about half, it is a brilliant pearl embedded in the land of North China.

Baiyangdian is rich in natural resources, with reed weaving, fishing, and tourism as its main industries. With shimmering waters, red lotus flowers, and green reeds, it has long been praised as the "Jiangnan of the North." Major attractions include Yuanfei Lotus Garden, Grand Lotus Viewing Garden, and Wangjiazhai Folk Village, each carrying a rich water-town culture.

This area was once the anti-Japanese base of the Yanling Team, where red culture and natural ecology blend perfectly, making Baiyangdian a comprehensive scenic area integrating ecological sightseeing, red tourism, and leisure vacations.

/// Lotus World

Lotus World · Reed Maze

Baiyangdian features 143 lakes and ponds of various sizes, connected by over 3,700 crisscrossing waterways, forming a unique aquatic maze unparalleled in the world. Gliding through in a small boat feels like entering a green water wonderland.

The lotus is the soul of Baiyangdian. Every summer, thousands of acres of lotus flowers bloom in succession, with a rich variety from single-petal to multi-petal, from pale pink to deep red. Amidst the layered lotus leaves, the flowers stand tall and graceful, with a gentle breeze carrying the fragrance of lotus — an overwhelming feast for the senses.

The vast reed marshes stretch endlessly, with waves of reeds swaying and birds darting through, creating a natural ink-wash painting. Reeds are not only an excellent raw material for papermaking but also a vital component of Baiyangdian's ecological environment.

/// Ecological Home

Water Town Charm · Ecological Home

Baiyangdian is a vital wetland ecosystem in North China, providing multiple ecological functions such as water conservation, climate regulation, and water purification. It is hailed as the "Kidney of North China." The establishment of Xiong'an New Area in 2017 elevated Baiyangdian's ecological protection to a national strategic level.

This is a paradise for birds. Baiyangdian is home to over 200 species of birds, including national first-class protected species such as red-crowned cranes, white cranes, and great bustards. Every spring and autumn, flocks of migratory birds stop here to rest and feed, creating a vibrant natural tableau.

Generations of local fishermen have lived on the water, forming a unique water-town folk culture. Intangible cultural heritages such as evening fishing songs, reed weaving techniques, and water-town wedding customs add profound cultural depth to Baiyangdian.
