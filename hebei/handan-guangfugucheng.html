/// 001_HISTORY

Millennium City · Ancient Times

With a history of over 2,600 years, Guangfu Ancient City is located in Yongnian District, Handan City. Built during the Spring and Autumn Period, it became the capital of the Daxia Kingdom founded by Dou Jiande in the late Sui Dynasty. Within the city are numerous historical relics including Qinghui Academy, the Confucian Temple, and the Former Residence of Wu Yuxiang — every brick and tile tells stories spanning millennia.

Guangfu Ancient City is the only dry-land water city in northern China, surrounded by a wide moat. The city wall, approximately 4.5 km in length, is well-preserved. Gazing from the city wall, you'll see rippling waters and lush reeds outside, while inside, blue bricks and gray tiles exude ancient charm, creating a unique water-city panorama.

The streets within the city are laid out in an orderly pattern, with traditional residences, shops, and temples scattered in harmony, preserving the style of the Ming and Qing dynasties. Strolling through the ancient city feels like traveling through time, returning to an era of slow carriages and leisurely days.

/// 002_TAICHI

Water City Tai Chi · Strength in Gentleness

Guangfu Ancient City is surrounded by water on all four sides, with a moat tens of meters wide embraced by green waves and swaying reeds, forming a unique wetland water city landscape. This body of water not only endows Guangfu with a graceful and refined temperament but also nurtured the two most influential schools of Tai Chi in Chinese martial arts history.

Guangfu Ancient City is the birthplace of Yang-style and Wu-style Tai Chi. During the Daoguang period of the Qing Dynasty, Yang Luchan founded Yang-style Tai Chi here, which was later carried forward by his grandson Yang Chengfu. Around the same time, Wu Yuxiang also founded Wu-style Tai Chi in Guangfu. Like the waters of the moat, these two schools embody strength within gentleness, with a long and enduring legacy.

Today, the former residences of Yang Luchan and Wu Yuxiang remain well-preserved in Guangfu Ancient City. Tai Chi enthusiasts from around the world gather here to practice beneath the ancient city walls and along the moat, experiencing the charm of this treasure of Chinese martial arts.

/// 003_ARCHITECTURE

Ancient Architecture · Imprint of Time

The city wall of Guangfu Ancient City was originally an earthen wall before the Yuan Dynasty. The existing wall was built during the Ming Dynasty, stretching approximately 4.5 kilometers, 12 meters high and 8 meters wide, making it one of the best-preserved ancient city fortifications in northern China. Each of the four gates has a barbican, forming a complete defense system with magnificent and imposing gate towers.

Qinghui Academy within the city was built during the Ming Dynasty and is one of the earliest officially-run academies in the Handan area, nurturing countless scholars and literati. The grand hall of the Confucian Temple is solemn and majestic, with intricate carvings and painted beams showcasing the exquisite craftsmanship of ancient artisans. The former residences of Wu Yuxiang and Yang Luchan, along with other Tai Chi cultural sites, are well-preserved, bearing witness to the development of Chinese martial arts.

Inside and outside the ancient city, there are numerous residential houses, shops, and temples from the Ming and Qing dynasties. Each site is an imprint of time, carrying millennia of cultural heritage, serving as precious physical materials for studying ancient Chinese urban planning, architectural art, and social life.
