/// Jinshanling Beauty

Jinshanling Beauty · Essence of the Great Wall

Jinshanling Great Wall is located at the junction of Luanping County, Chengde City, Hebei Province and Miyun District, Beijing. It stretches 10.5 km from Longyukou in the west to Wangjinglou in the east, and is the finest section of the Ming Dynasty Great Wall under the Ji Garrison.

With expansive views, dense watchtowers, exquisite construction, and diverse forms, this section offers a breathtaking panorama. Standing atop the wall, the mountains surge like waves and the Great Wall coils like a dragon winding through the towering peaks a sight of spectacular grandeur.

Jinshanling is hailed as a "photographer's paradise." With ever-changing seasonal scenery and shifting light at dawn and dusk, it is one of the most photographed sections of the Great Wall.

/// Dense Watchtowers

Dense Watchtowers · Continuous Barrier Walls

The most distinctive feature of Jinshanling is its dense watchtowers. Along the 10.5 km wall, there are 67 watchtowers an average of one every 150 meters, the highest density of any Ming Dynasty Great Wall section.

The watchtowers come in various forms brick-and-wood, brick-and-stone, single-story and double-story. Their roofs range from flat and domed to boat-shaped and four-cornered pyramidal, earning Jinshanling the title of "Military Architecture Museum on the Great Wall."

Barrier walls, horse-blocking walls, and support walls form layered defenses truly "one man can hold the pass against ten thousand." During General Qi Jiguang's garrison here, he undertook large-scale reinforcement and renovation of the wall.

/// Four Seasons

Four Seasons · Endless Change

The four seasons at Jinshanling each have their own charm. In spring, mountain apricots burst into bloom, their pinkish-white flowers dotting the gray stone walls a perfect blend of strength and grace.

Summer brings lush greenery as the wall snakes through verdant mountains beneath a clear blue sky. Golden autumn transforms the landscape with red leaves contrasting against the ancient wall the prime season for photographers.

When winter snow falls, the wall is draped in silver white, resembling a giant white dragon coiled atop the mountain peaks a solemn northern spectacle. Each season offers its unique allure, drawing countless visitors who linger in wonder.
