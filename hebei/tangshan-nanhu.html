/// 01

Industrial Memory · Urban Rebirth

Located in the heart of Tangshan, the Nanhu·Kailuan Scenic Area consists of two major sections: the Kailuan National Mine Park and the Tangshan Nanhu Scenic Area, covering a total area of 5.8 km² (5.4 km² for Nanhu + 0.4 km² for Kailuan Mine Park). Once a coal mining subsidence area and a century-old industrial site, it has now become an exemplary model showcasing Tangshan's magnificent transformation from industrial civilization to ecological civilization.

Centered on "industrial heritage protection + ecological restoration," the scenic area integrates mining culture, eco-tourism, leisure vacations and other diverse formats. It is a benchmark for resource-based city transformation in Hebei Province and across China, and was rated a National 5A Scenic Area in 2024.

/// 02

Nanhu Waters · City Green Lung

Once a subsidence area formed after coal mining at Kailuan, the Nanhu Scenic Area has undergone years of ecological restoration. Today, with its sparkling waters and lush greenery, it has become a central city park for Tangshan residents to enjoy leisure and recreation. With over 130 scenic spots, vast waters, and verdant trees, it stands as a model of ecological restoration in North China.

The scenic area features a lakeside greenway, water sports center, wetland science museum, music fountain plaza and other facilities. In spring, visitors can admire cherry blossoms; in summer, go boating on the lake; in autumn, enjoy the red leaves; and in winter, go ice skating and play in the snow — suitable for visiting all year round. It is a exemplary work of Tangshan's urban ecological development.

/// 03

Mine Heritage · Industrial Heritage

The Kailuan National Mine Park centers on the century-long history of the Kailuan Coal Mine, showcasing the glorious journey of modern Chinese industry. Founded in 1878, the Kailuan Coal Mine was one of the earliest large-scale coal mines in China to use machine mining, and is hailed as the "Cradle of China's Modern Coal Industry."

The park preserves unique industrial relics including century-old mining pit sites, vintage steam locomotives, and a mine experience area. It also houses a Coal Museum and an underground exploration experience. Visitors can go deep underground to personally experience the working conditions of miners a century ago and appreciate the profound heritage of China's industrial civilization.
