/// 01

Strategic Pass · Between Mountain & Sea

Located in the northeast of Qinhuangdao, Hebei Province, Shanhaiguan is the first fortress at the eastern end of the Ming Great Wall, and the only place where the Great Wall meets the sea. Backed by the Yanshan Mountain range to the north and facing Bohai Bay to the south, it guards the vital corridor connecting North China and the Northeast.

In 1381 (the 14th year of Hongwu reign of Ming Dynasty), General Xu Da was ordered to build the fortress here. Because it backs against the mountain and faces the sea, it was named “Shanhaiguan” (Mountain-Sea Pass). For over six hundred years, this grand pass has witnessed countless historical events, carrying the collective memory of the Chinese nation.

In 1987, as an important part of the Great Wall, Shanhaiguan was inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List, becoming one of the most renowned ancient passes in China and the world.

/// 02

Military Fortress · Contested by All Armies

Since ancient times, Shanhaiguan has been a strategic military stronghold contested by all armies. Guarding the Liaoxi Corridor, it served as the vital junction connecting North China and the Northeast. During the Ming Dynasty, Shanhai Guard was established here; in the Qing Dynasty, Linyu County was set up with garrison troops, forming a complete military defense system.

The fortress is square-shaped, with a circumference of about 4 km. The city wall is 14 meters high and 7 meters thick, with four gates: Zhendong (Suppress the East), Ying’en (Welcome Grace), Wangyang (Gazing at the Sea), and Weiyuan (Awe-inspiring Distance). Arrow towers, barbicans, and outer fortifications on the walls formed an impregnable defensive layout.

Historically, major events such as Wu Sangui leading Qing forces through the pass and the Ji Shen Incident took place here. Every time the gates of Shanhaiguan opened or closed, it influenced the fate of the Chinese nation.

/// 03

Ancient City Charm · Historical Legacy

The Shanhaiguan fortress is well-preserved, with crisscrossing ancient streets and alleys inside, and blue-brick gray-tiled folk houses scattered in an orderly manner, maintaining the ancient city style of the Ming and Qing dynasties. Walking through the city feels like traveling back in time to the border stronghold of six hundred years ago.

Laolongtou (Old Dragon’s Head) is the only place where the Ming Great Wall enters the sea. The Great Wall winds into the ocean like a giant dragon, creating a magnificent vista. Jiaoshan Great Wall meanders over the Yanshan Mountains; climbing up for a distant view, one can take in the fortress, mountains, and sea in one sweeping panorama.

Historical sites such as the Meng Jiangnu Temple and Wang Family Courtyard are scattered throughout the area. Rich cultural landscapes and grand military architecture complement each other, together forming the unique cultural tourism appeal of Shanhaiguan.
