/// 001_GEOLOGY

Marble Peak Forest · Global Geological Wonder

Located in Laiyuan County, Baoding City, Baishishan is named for its white marble mountains. It features China's one-of-a-kind marble peak forest landscape, formed through a billion years of geological evolution—a masterpiece of nature's extraordinary craftsmanship.

Baishishan is known for its "Three Summits, Six Terraces, Nine Valleys, and Eighty-One Peaks." The main peak rises to 2,096 meters, serving as the dragon head rising from the northwest of the North China Plain. From the summit, vast peaks stretch like an ocean with rolling clouds and mists—a breathtaking spectacle.

In 2006, Baishishan was designated a UNESCO Global Geopark, becoming one of northern China's most renowned geological research and science education bases.

/// 002_SKYWALK

Glass Skywalk · Walking Among the Clouds

Baishishan features China's longest and widest suspended glass skywalk, approximately 95 meters long, 2 meters wide, at an elevation of 1,900 meters. The skywalk is cantilevered over a vertical cliff, with a sheer abyss below and endless peaks all around—a truly exhilarating experience.

The glass skywalk is constructed with triple-laminated tempered glass, bearing up to 1,000 kg per square meter with nationally leading safety standards. Walking on the transparent glass feels like strolling among the clouds, offering an unprecedented thrill and wonder.

Looking back from the end of the skywalk, the fantastical peak forest of Baishishan unfolds before your eyes. When clouds and mist swirl, the entire skywalk seems to float in a celestial realm, making it a must-visit for photography enthusiasts and thrill-seekers.

/// 003_SEASONS

Changing Seasons · Marvel of Cloud Seas

Each season at Baishishan has its own charm: spring brings blooming mountain flowers, summer offers rolling seas of clouds, autumn paints the forests in vibrant colors, and winter cloaks the peaks in snow. This celebrated northern Chinese mountain, with its rich vertical climate zones, presents an ever-changing tapestry of seasons.

The sea of clouds at Baishishan is truly spectacular. Due to its high elevation and location at the junction of the Taihang Mountains and the North China Plain, warm and cold air currents converge here, creating a magnificent, ever-present sea of clouds. As the rising sun's golden rays pierce through the clouds and illuminate the white marble peak forest, the scene is incomparably splendid.

Autumn is one of the most beautiful seasons at Baishishan. The vibrant red leaves blanketing the mountains create a striking visual contrast with the white marble peak forest, drawing hordes of photography enthusiasts to capture the scenery.
