/// 001_CANYON

Hundred-Mile Gorge · A Wonder Like No Other

Yesanpo is located in Laishui County, Baoding City. It is a rare scenic area in northern China that integrates majestic mountains, emerald waters, unique canyons, and strange springs. Covering approximately 498 square kilometers, it features five core scenic zones: Hundred-Mile Gorge, Juma River, Longmen Tianmen Pass, Baicaopan, and Yugu Cave, each a masterpiece of nature.

Hundred-Mile Gorge is world-famous for its "majesty, peril, wonder, and serenity." The three gorges stretch over a hundred miles in total, with the narrowest point allowing only one person to pass through. The cliffs on both sides rise a thousand feet, earning it the title of North China's Geological Museum. Inside the gorge, clear springs and waterfalls cascade, vegetation thrives, and the average summer temperature is only 22°C, making it an ideal summer retreat.

Yesanpo was recognized as a UNESCO Global Geopark in 2006 and rated as a National 5A Tourist Attraction in 2011, becoming a brilliant gem in northern China's tourism landscape.

/// 002_RAFTING

Juma Rapids · Rafting Paradise

The Juma River is the mother river of Yesanpo. Its waters are crystal clear, flanked by layered mountains with picturesque scenery. Juma River rafting is renowned as the "Best Rafting in the North," stretching about 10 kilometers with thrilling rapids, deep pools, and serene gorges along the way, allowing visitors to experience nature's mighty power through excitement and adventure.

The Juma River area offers not only rafting but also numerous natural bathing spots and leisure campsites along its banks. In summer, the emerald waters and green mountains create a delightful contrast, where visitors can play in the water, fish, and camp, enjoying a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle. The karst landforms on both sides complement the clear river, forming a unique gorge landscape.

In addition, the Juma River basin is home to many rare flora and fauna. National protected birds such as the black stork and white crane frequently appear here, adding an unexpected delight for visitors.

/// 003_GEOLOGY

Geological Wonder · Nature's Classroom

As a UNESCO Global Geopark, Yesanpo's geological formations hold immense scientific research value. The area exposes strata from the Archean to the Cenozoic eras, with diverse lithologies and rich structural features, serving as a natural laboratory for studying the geological evolution of the North China Plate.

The Longmen Tianmen Pass area preserves well-preserved Ming and Qing dynasty cliff inscriptions and ancient pass ruins, where granite peak forest landforms intertwine with historical culture. Baicaopan, at 1,983 meters, is the highest peak of Yesanpo. Its summit alpine meadow stretches for dozens of miles, bursting into a sea of blooming flowers from spring to summer, a breathtaking sight.

Yugu Cave is a natural karst cave with spectacular stalactites and an underground river running through it. Every year during the Grain Rain solar term, vast numbers of fish surge out of the cave with the spring water, creating a unique natural wonder that attracts countless visitors and researchers.
