/// Revolutionary Sacred Land

Revolutionary Sacred Land · Historic Monument

Xibaipo is located in Pingshan County, Shijiazhuang City, Hebei Province, at the eastern foot of the Taihang Mountains and on the north bank of the Hutuo River. From May 1948 to March 1949, it served as the seat of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China and the headquarters of the People's Liberation Army — the last rural command post of the Chinese revolution.

From here, the Party Central Committee and Chairman Mao commanded the Liaoshen, Huaihai, and Pingjin campaigns, leading the People's Liberation Army to a decisive victory in the War of Liberation. In March 1949, the historic Second Plenary Session of the Seventh Central Committee was convened here.

Though the Xibaipo period lasted only ten months, it marked a historic turning point for the Chinese revolution — from the countryside to the cities, from war to peace, and from revolution to construction — holding a milestone status in the history of the Chinese revolution.

/// Strategic Command

Strategic Command · Decisive Victory

The former site of the CPC Central Committee is the core of the Xibaipo Scenic Area, preserving the former residences of revolutionary leaders including Mao Zedong, Zhu De, Liu Shaoqi, Zhou Enlai, and Ren Bishi, as well as historic buildings such as the Central Military Commission Operations Room and the venue of the Second Plenary Session of the Seventh Central Committee.

The Central Military Commission Operations Room was a modest adobe building of less than 30 square meters, with large military maps on the walls and telephones and documents on the tables. It was under such humble conditions that the Party Central Committee commanded the three major campaigns, creating a miracle in the history of warfare.

Zhou Enlai once said wryly: "This operations room of ours — it issued no guns, no grain, only telegrams — and yet it defeated the Kuomintang." Every brick and tile, every table and chair here tells the story of those arduous yet glorious years.

/// Red Memories

Red Memories · Passing the Torch

The Xibaipo Memorial Hall is a national-level patriotic education demonstration base, housing more than 2,000 cultural relics. Through historical photographs, artifacts, sculptures, and multimedia displays, it comprehensively recreates the great revolutionary practices of the CPC Central Committee in Xibaipo.

In front of the memorial hall stands the bronze statue of the "Five Secretaries," vividly capturing the historic moment of Mao Zedong, Zhu De, Liu Shaoqi, Zhou Enlai, and Ren Bishi striding forward — a landmark scene where visitors pay tribute to their predecessors and take commemorative photos.

Xibaipo is not only a witness to revolutionary history but also the birthplace of the "Examination" spirit. The "Two Musts" proposed by Chairman Mao at the Second Plenary Session of the Seventh Central Committee still resonate powerfully today, inspiring generation after generation to stay true to their original aspirations and forge ahead.
