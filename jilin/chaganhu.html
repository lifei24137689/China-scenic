The Millennial Wonder of Ice Fishing

Chagan Lake is located in Qian Gorlos Mongolian Autonomous County, Songyuan, Jilin Province. It is one of China's top ten freshwater lakes, covering a total area of 420 km². "Chagan Nur" means "white sacred lake" in Mongolian, and has been the source of life for northern fishing tribes since ancient times.

From mid-December to mid-January each year, the Chagan Lake winter fishing season begins. Nearly a thousand people work on the ice, with dozens of vehicles transporting day and night. The highest single-net catch reached 210,000 kg, setting a Guinness World Record. This spectacular underwater ice fishing scene is hailed as "China's Last Fishing Tribe."

Thousands of Mu of Lotus in Full Summer Bloom

Chagan Lake is not only a winter fishing sanctuary but also an ideal summer retreat. From July to August each year, thousands of mu of lotus flowers bloom in competition, covering the lake with pink blossoms and green leaves, creating a magnificent vista of endless greenery stretching to the horizon.

Deep in the lakeshore, reeds sway and water birds gather. The lake is home to 68 fish species from 15 families, making it a bird paradise and natural botanical garden. At the turn of summer and autumn, take a boat ride on the lake to watch lotus flowers sway and water birds dance, experiencing the unique charm of a Jiangnan-style landscape in northern China.

The Ancient Ritual of Offering and Net-Waking

Chagan Lake winter fishing is not just a harvesting feast — it carries the ancient Mongolian ritual of "Offering to the Lake and Waking the Nets." The fishing leader guides the fishermen in lighting incense, reciting prayers, and offering hada scarves and milk wine to the lake, praying to the lake god for a bountiful and safe harvest.

The scenic area features cultural sites such as Miaoyin Temple, Genghis Khan Shrine, and the Fishing Culture Museum. The yurt-style architecture at Nabo Square recreates the historical scene of Liao Dynasty emperors' "Spring Nabo" (spring hunt). In 2008, the "Chagan Nur Winter Fishing Custom" was inscribed on the National Intangible Cultural Heritage list.
