Jilin's Buddhist Sacred Land

Liuding Mountain Cultural Tourism Zone is located in the southern outskirts of Dunhua City, Jilin Province. It is a comprehensive 5A scenic area integrating Buddhist culture, Bohai historical culture, and Manchu Qing ancestral culture. Key attractions include the Golden Ding Buddha, Zhengjue Temple, Qing Ancestral Shrine, and the Bohai Ancient Tombs.

The scenic area nestles against mountains and rivers, surrounded by peaks. Zhengjue Temple is the world's largest Bhikkhuni practice center, with five grand halls built along the mountainside. Climbing the twenty-tier Prajna Steps leads to the Golden Ding Buddha viewing platform.

Bohai Kingdom Millennium Legacy

The Liuding Mountain ancient tomb complex is the early royal mausoleum of the Bohai Kingdom. In 698 AD, Dae Jo-yeong, chief of the Mohe Sumo tribe, established the Zhen Kingdom (later known as Bohai Kingdom) in Dunhua, which lasted over 200 years. The tombs are simple in structure, blending seamlessly with the mountain landscape.

Today, the tomb complex comprises two sections with over a hundred tombs of various sizes. Among them, the stone lions and epitaphs unearthed from the tomb of Princess Zhenhui are particularly precious, serving as important archaeological materials for studying Bohai Kingdom history and culture.

Ancestral Homeland

Dunhua is recognized as the hometown of Bukūri Yongšon, the founding ancestor of the Qing dynasty. According to the "Draft History of Qing," "Dunhua is where the Qing ancestor resided in Oduoli City." The Qing Ancestral Shrine houses a seated statue of Bukūri Yongšon, with the Aisin Gioro genealogy behind him, flanked by seated statues of the twelve Qing emperors.

The walls feature large murals such as "The Three Goddesses Bathing" and "The Qing Ancestor Crossing the River," vividly recreating the legendary tales of the Manchu founding ancestor. The plaque of the Qing Ancestral Shrine was inscribed by Aisin Gioro Yuchan, a nephew of the last emperor Puyi.
