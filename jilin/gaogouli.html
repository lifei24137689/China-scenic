Millennia-Old Capital Hidden in the Changbai Mountains

The Goguryeo Historic Relics are located in Ji'an, Tonghua, Jilin Province — the capital of the Goguryeo dynasty (37 BC – 668 AD). In 2004, the "Goguryeo Royal City, Royal Tombs, and Noble Burials" were inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List.

Wandu Mountain Fortress was built along the mountain ridge, its walls winding along the crest. Within the fortress are ruins of palaces, watchtowers, and reservoirs. The Gwanggaeto Stele stands 6.39 m tall with 1,775 characters, recording the founding myth of Goguryeo and the conquests of King Gwanggaeto.

General's Tomb: The Pyramid of the East

The General's Tomb is the mausoleum of King Jangsu, the 20th ruler of Goguryeo. Built in a stepped pyramid form with over 1,100 finely hewn granite blocks, it is known as the "Pyramid of the East." The base measures about 31 m per side and stands approximately 13 m tall.

The tomb has seven tiers, each built with three layers of stone blocks. The burial chamber sits at the center of the fifth tier, containing two stone coffin platforms. The blocks fit together perfectly, and after over 1,500 years of wind and rain, the structure still stands firm.

King Gwanggaeto: The Empire-Building Conqueror

King Gwanggaeto (r. 374–412) was the 19th king of Goguryeo. During his reign, he expanded Goguryeo's territory to its zenith through campaigns in all directions. His tomb and the 6.39 m commemorative stele one kilometer away are core components of the site.

The Gwanggaeto Stele is one of the earliest surviving steles with the most characters. The inscription details the military campaigns and tomb-guarding system of King Gwanggaeto's era. Protected by a pavilion, the stele is inscribed on all four sides, featuring bold and primitive calligraphy of great historical and artistic value.
