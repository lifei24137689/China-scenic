/// 01

The Northeast Roofridge

Changbaishan is located in southeastern Jilin Province, straddling the border between China and North Korea. It is one of China's top ten famous mountains. Its main peak, Baiyun Peak, rises 2,691 meters above sea level, making it the highest peak in Northeast China and earning it the title "Roofridge of the Northeast."

The Changbaishan range stretches over a thousand kilometers, serving as the boundary mountain between China and North Korea. Its unique geological structure has formed a volcanic landscape centered around Heavenly Lake — majestic, magnificent, and unparalleled in beauty. This area is the source of three rivers: the Songhua, Tumen, and Yalu.

/// 02

Volcanic Crater Lake Wonder

Heavenly Lake on Changbaishan is the highest volcanic crater lake in the world, as well as the deepest and highest lake in China. Its deep, serene blue waters are crystal clear like a mirror, surrounded by 16 peaks — like a giant sapphire set atop the mountain crest.

Heavenly Lake sits within China's best-preserved Cenozoic multi-genetic composite volcano. To its north, Changbai Waterfall drops 68 meters, thundering like a river of stars cascading from the sky. Underground forests, Small Heavenly Lake, hot spring clusters, and other wonders are scattered throughout, forming a unique volcanic landscape belt.

Ecology Primitive Wilderness

Changbaishan boasts a complete temperate mountain vertical vegetation spectrum. From the foot to the summit, it features broad-leaved forests, mixed coniferous and broad-leaved forests, coniferous forests, Erman's birch forests, and alpine tundra — creating a magnificent landscape where "one mountain experiences four seasons, and every few miles the sky changes."

This area serves as a vital ecological barrier in Northeast Asia, home to rare animals such as the Siberian tiger, black bear, and sable. The underground forest lies deep within the canyon, with ancient towering trees shrouded in mystery. Lush green in summer, ablaze with color in autumn, and cloaked in silver in winter — beautiful in every season.
