Jilin's Eye of the Forest

Jingyuetan National Forest Park is located in the southeast of Changchun City. It gets its name "Jingyue" (Clear Moon) from its crescent-moon shape formed by damming water. As the sister lake of Taiwan's Sun Moon Lake, it is the largest man-made forest sea in Asia, covering a total area of 96 km².

Jingyuetan was first built in 1934, originally serving as Changchun's water source. After more than 80 years of afforestation and ecological restoration, it has become a vast forest sea with over 30 tree species. With a forest coverage rate of 96%, it is known as "Changchun's Natural Oxygen Bar."

Scenic Beauty in All Seasons

Enjoy apricot blossoms in spring, row boats on clear waves in summer, admire red leaves in autumn, and view rime ice in winter — Jingyuetan has distinct seasons, each with its own charm. The winter wonder of blue ice bubbles attracts countless photography enthusiasts.

The scenic area features a ring road around the lake, forest trails, a ski resort, and a golf course. The Jingyue Goddess sculpture stands by the lake, becoming an iconic landmark. The Vasaloppet Ski Museum showcases the historical heritage of winter sports in China.

Wandering Deep in the Forest

The tall black pines stand in serene stillness after snowfall, with stone paths winding through the dense forest. In winter, sunlight filters through the snow-covered pine branches, casting dappled light and shadow — a rare tranquil corner within the city.

The metasequoia forest by the lake and the wooden boardwalk complement each other. The Apricot Blossom Slope in spring, Lotus Pond in summer, and Red Leaf Valley in autumn — each spot is worth a pause. Walk through the forest, take a deep breath of air rich in negative oxygen ions, and let the forest heal all your fatigue.
