The Meandering Wetland Wonder of Nenjiang

Da'an Nenjiang Bay Tourist Area is located in Da'an City, Baicheng City, Jilin Province, situated in the heart of the Songnen Plain. The Nenjiang River winds through here, creating vast natural wetland landscapes, making it one of the best-preserved river wetland ecosystems in Northeast China.

The Nenjiang Bay wetland boasts lush water plants, swaying reeds, and flocks of water birds. In late spring and early summer, both banks of the bay are vibrant with greenery; in deep autumn, reed catkins fall like snow as migratory birds fly south. In 2024, Da'an Nenjiang Bay was officially designated a national 5A-level tourist attraction.

A Life Station for Migratory Birds

Da'an Nenjiang Bay is a key passage and stopover for migratory birds in Northeast Asia. Each spring and autumn, tens of thousands of migratory birds rest and forage here. Rare species such as red-crowned cranes, white cranes, and oriental storks are frequent visitors, making Nenjiang Bay a true "bird paradise."

The scenic area features professional birdwatching platforms and ecological boardwalks, allowing visitors to observe wetland birds up close. Walking along the wooden pathways through the reed marshes, with birds chirping around you and the distant horizon where water meets sky, it feels like stepping into a utopia.

Fishing & Hunting Memories on the Songnen Plain

Da'an Nenjiang Bay not only boasts magnificent natural scenery but also carries a deep cultural heritage of fishing and hunting. Laokanzi Wharf has been an important water transport hub on the Nenjiang River since the Liao Dynasty, where Liao emperors once held the "Spring Nabo" ceremony, witnessing the rise and fall of a thousand years of fishing and hunting civilization on the Songnen Plain.

The Fishing and Hunting Culture Village within the scenic area recreates the life scenes of traditional fishermen, allowing visitors to cast nets, catch fish, and taste fresh river delicacies. The best travel season is from May to October, with cool summer wetlands and snowy autumn reeds — each season has its own beauty.
