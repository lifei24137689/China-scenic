Jilin's Last Imperial Court

The Manchukuo Imperial Palace Museum is located in Kuancheng District, Changchun City, Jilin Province. It was the former court of Aisin-Gioro Puyi, the last emperor of the Qing dynasty, when he served as the puppet emperor of Manchukuo. Its predecessor was the Salt Administration Bureau of the Republic of China era. In 1932, Puyi was installed here as the "Chief Executive" of Manchukuo.

This site bears witness to Japanese imperialist aggression against China. The architectural style blends Chinese and Western elements, combining the solemnity of traditional Chinese palaces with Japanese and Western architectural influences. As a significant site of modern Chinese history, it carries the memory of humiliation and resistance.

/// 02

Sino-Western Architectural Fusion

The Manchukuo Palace complex includes major buildings such as Tongde Hall, Jixi Tower, Qinmin Tower, and Huaiyuan Tower. Tongde Hall is the largest building, featuring glazed tile roofs and a Sino-Western style. Jixi Tower was Puyi's residential palace. Qinmin Tower served as Puyi's office and ceremonial venue.

The East Imperial Garden blends traditional Chinese gardens with Japanese dry landscape style, featuring rockeries, water features, and pavilions scattered in harmony. The museum displays a vast collection of precious historical artifacts, documents, and photographs, authentically recreating the political, cultural, and daily life scenes of the Manchukuo period.

Living Historical Memory

The museum houses a vast collection of precious artifacts, including Puyi's personal belongings, palace furniture, documents, archives, photographs, and videos. Exhibits such as Puyi's "dragon robe," edicts from the Manchukuo period, and gifts from Japan all tell the story of that unique era.

As a National Demonstration Base for Patriotic Education, the Manchukuo Imperial Palace Museum is not only an important window into modern Chinese history but also a "living history textbook." Every visitor can feel the weight of history and the awakening of a nation here.
