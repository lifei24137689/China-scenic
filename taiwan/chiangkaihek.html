A Solemn Hall of White Walls and Blue Roof

Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall is located in Zhongzheng District, Taipei. It is a grand memorial building built in honor of former President Chiang Kai-shek. The main structure adopts a Chinese palace-style design with white walls, blue roof tiles, and an octagonal roof. Its sapphire-blue glazed tile roof glistens under the sunlight, making it one of Taipei's most iconic landmarks.

The memorial hall faces west toward the Chinese mainland, covering an area of approximately 250,000 square meters with a height of 70 meters. The main gate faces Zhongshan South Road, with the spacious Liberty Square in front, flanked by the National Theater and the National Concert Hall. These three buildings together form Taipei's most magnificent cultural landscape axis. The entire complex is laid out along the axis of "Chiang Kai-shek's spirit," blending traditional Chinese architectural aesthetics with modern urban spatial planning.

A Cultural Symphony on Liberty Square

Liberty Square, located within the Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall complex, is the largest cultural square in Taipei, covering a total area of approximately 250,000 square meters. At the front of the square stands the "Liberty Square" archway, 30 meters high and 80 meters wide, one of the most magnificent archway structures in Taiwan.

The exhibition halls and artifact rooms on the first floor of the memorial hall showcase Chiang Kai-shek's life stories and artifacts from modern Taiwanese history. A bronze statue of Chiang Kai-shek stands in the main hall, 6.3 meters tall and weighing approximately 21.25 tons. The daily honor guard changing ceremony at the top of every hour is a must-see traditional performance for visitors, both solemn and breathtaking. The National Theater and the National Concert Hall represent the pinnacle of performing arts in Taiwan, hosting classic plays and concerts year-round. Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall is not only a tourist attraction but also an important venue for daily leisure and cultural activities for the people of Taiwan.
