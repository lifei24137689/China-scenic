A Treasure Hall of Chinese Civilization

Located in Waishuangxi, Shilin District, Taipei City, the National Palace Museum is a museum built in the architectural style of a Chinese palace. Nestled against the mountains, it presents a magnificent presence. The museum houses nearly 700,000 exquisite Chinese cultural artifacts, making it a treasured repository of ancient Chinese art and culture. Most of these artifacts were originally part of the precious collections from the former Beijing Palace Museum, the Shenyang Imperial Palace, and the Chengde Mountain Resort.

The National Palace Museum was established in 1965, with its main building designed in the style of the Beijing Imperial Palace. Its light blue glazed tile roof rests atop cream-colored walls, appearing solemn and elegant amidst the surrounding green mountains. The Jadeite Cabbage, Meat-shaped Stone, and Mao Gong Ding are hailed as the "Three Treasures of the Museum," must-see national treasures for every visitor.

A Dialogue with National Treasures Across Millennia

The collections of the National Palace Museum span seven thousand years of Chinese civilization, from the Neolithic Age to the Ming and Qing dynasties. They include bronze vessels, jade artifacts, ceramics, calligraphy and paintings, ancient books, enamelware, and various other categories. The museum's holdings of Ru kiln ceramics from the Song dynasty, official kiln ceramics from the Ming and Qing dynasties, ancient calligraphy and paintings, and rare ancient books rank among the finest in the world.

The museum employs a rotation exhibition system, displaying approximately 5,000 artifacts at a time. Even with intensive visits, it would take three months to see one full rotation. The museum regularly hosts special exhibitions and collaborates with world-renowned museums, bringing Chinese culture to the international stage. Nearby, the Zhishan Garden and Zhide Garden, two traditional Chinese gardens, offer visitors an ideal place to appreciate the beauty of Chinese garden art after viewing the artifacts.
