KENTING · 01

A Tropical Paradise Under Blue Skies

Kenting National Park is located in the southern part of the Hengchun Peninsula in Pingtung County, Taiwan. Surrounded by sea on three sides, it was Taiwan's first national park (established in 1984), covering an area of approximately 332.7 km² with a coastline of 58 km. Kenting is renowned for its warm climate, crystal-clear waters, and rich marine ecology.

With an average annual temperature of about 25°C, it remains warm and pleasant even in winter, making it Taiwan's most popular winter escape. Kenting boasts iconic landmarks such as Maobitou, Eluanbi, Sailing Rock, and Longpan Park. The Eluanbi Lighthouse, built in 1883, is the southernmost lighthouse in Taiwan and one of East Asia's renowned historic lighthouses.

The coral reef coastline, white sandy beaches, and azure waters create a breathtaking tropical landscape.

KENTING · 02

Marine Paradise Among Coral Reefs

Kenting National Park boasts abundant ecological resources and is the richest coral reef ecosystem in Taiwan. Its waters are home to over 300 species of coral and more than 1,000 species of fish, making it a paradise for diving enthusiasts. Snorkeling at the Houbihu Marine Resources Protected Area, beach activities at South Bay, and surfing at Jialeshui attract countless watersports lovers.

Kenting's terrestrial ecology is equally fascinating. Sheding Natural Park features rich limestone terrain and tropical plant communities, while Longluan Lake is an important habitat for migratory birds. The annual Spring Scream music festival around April draws hundreds of thousands of young people, turning this southern town into an ocean of music.

In recent years, Kenting has also been actively developing eco-tourism and promoting marine conservation to ensure the sustainable development of this tropical marine paradise.
