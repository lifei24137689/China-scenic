The Serene Realm of Lalu Island

Sun Moon Lake, located in Yuchi Township, Nantou County, Taiwan, is the largest natural lake in Taiwan with a surface elevation of 748 meters. Bounded by Lalu Island (Guanghua Island), the northern half of the lake resembles a round sun and the southern half a crescent moon, hence the name "Sun Moon Lake." The water is clear and emerald green, surrounded by layered mountains, creating a picturesque landscape of lake and mountain reflections, hailed as the foremost of Taiwan's "Eight Scenic Wonders."

The area features classic attractions including Wenwu Temple, Xuanzang Temple, Ci'en Pagoda, and Hanbi Tower. Sun Moon Lake is also one of Taiwan's largest freshwater lakes, with excellent water quality and rich ecological environment. A 37-kilometer lakeside road encircles the lake, offering cyclists breathtaking views of the lake and mountains. The annual Sun Moon Lake Cross-Lake Swimming Event held each autumn attracts tens of thousands of participants, making it one of the largest water events in the world.

Thao Culture & Lake Mountain Legends

Sun Moon Lake is the main settlement area of Taiwan's indigenous Thao people, with a population of only about 700, making them one of the smallest indigenous groups in Taiwan. Legend has it that the Thao ancestors, while hunting, chased a white deer and discovered this beautiful lake, where they settled. Sun Moon Lake's rich natural landscapes and cultural customs complement each other, creating a unique tourist charm.

The Ita Thao village on the lakeshore preserves a rich Thao culture, including pestle music and dance, traditional weaving, and wood carving crafts. With a lake surface elevation of 748 meters and surrounding peaks mostly above 1,000 meters, Sun Moon Lake has an average annual temperature of about 20°C, making it suitable for travel year-round. Taking a boat tour, cycling around the lake, or riding the cable car to Formosa Aboriginal Culture Village are all excellent ways to experience the beauty of Sun Moon Lake.
