Golden Mountain City Between Sea and Mountains

Jiufen is a mountain town located in Ruifang District, New Taipei City, Taiwan. Built along the hillside, it flourished during the early gold mining era. Jiufen Old Street is one of Taiwan's most representative mountain town streets, lined with quaint tea houses, snack shops, and handicraft stores along narrow stone-step alleys. The old street stretches about 200 meters, winding up the mountain, offering magnificent views of the sea and mountains along the way.

Jiufen's golden history dates back to 1893, when placer gold was discovered along the Keelung River, sparking a gold rush. During the Japanese occupation, Jiufen's gold production peaked, earning it the reputation of "Asia's Golden Capital." Although gold mining has ceased, Jiufen still retains a strong mining history atmosphere and its unique mountain town charm.

The Mountain Town That Inspired Spirited Away

Jiufen's unique charm has attracted countless artists and filmmakers. The bathhouse street scene in Hayao Miyazaki's classic film "Spirited Away" is said to have been inspired by Jiufen's old street landscape. As night falls, red lanterns in Jiufen's mountain town light up one by one, and the winding stone-step alleys appear dreamy and enchanting under the dim yellow glow, like a sleepless city floating on the sea.

Jiufen's tea house culture is also highly distinctive. Grandma A-Gan's Taro Balls and Grandma Lai's Taro Balls are must-try classic snacks. Shengping Theater is the oldest mountain town cinema in northern Taiwan, still preserving its Japanese colonial-era architectural style. Looking down from Jiufen's mountain town, Keelung Island appears faintly on the distant sea, and the beautiful blend of mountains and sea makes visitors linger. Jiufen also features mining heritage sites such as the Jinguashi Gold Museum, which fully presents the mining history of northern Taiwan.
