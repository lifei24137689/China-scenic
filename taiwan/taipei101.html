City Landmark Above the Clouds

Taipei 101 is located in Xinyi District, Taipei City. Standing at 509.2 meters (including its spire) with 101 floors above ground and 5 underground levels, it was the tallest building in the world when completed in 2004, and remains Taiwan's most iconic modern structure. Designed by architect C.Y. Lee, its form is inspired by the segmented growth of bamboo — each cluster of eight floors expands outward, rising like a tall, resilient stalk.

Taipei 101 blends Eastern cultural traditions with cutting-edge building technology, making it the most dazzling landmark on the Taipei skyline. The tower houses the world's largest and heaviest tuned mass damper (660 tons), suspended between the 87th and 92nd floors to ensure stability during strong winds and earthquakes. Taipei 101 has earned LEED v4 Platinum certification, making it one of the tallest green buildings in the world.

Brilliant Jewel of Bustling Xinyi

Taipei 101 is more than a skyscraper — it is a comprehensive landmark integrating shopping, dining, offices, and sightseeing. The 89th-floor observatory offers a 360-degree panoramic view of the Greater Taipei Basin, and on clear days, extends to Yangmingshan and Guanyinshan. The tower is located in Xinyi District, Taipei's most vibrant commercial area, surrounded by key city landmarks including Taipei City Hall, Taipei Dome, Sun Yat-sen Memorial Hall, and Eslite Xinyi Store.

The annual Taipei 101 New Year's Eve fireworks show is a globally celebrated event, drawing hundreds of thousands of spectators in person. The tower also houses the TAIPEI 101 MALL, a high-end shopping center featuring top international brands. A super-high-speed elevator reaches the 89th-floor observatory in just 37 seconds — one of the fastest elevators in the world.
