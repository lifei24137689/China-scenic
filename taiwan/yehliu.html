GEO · Geological Wonder

"Nature's Sculpture Garden Carved by Sea Breeze"

Yehliu Geopark is located in Wanli District, New Taipei City, Taiwan, and is the most renowned geological wonder on Taiwan's northern coast. Covering an area of about 0.7 km² with a coastline of approximately 1.7 km, it has been shaped by long-term wave erosion, weathering, and crustal movements, creating a variety of rare landforms including mushroom rocks, sea erosion gullies, candle-shaped rocks, and tofu rocks.

The most famous of these is the "Queen's Head" mushroom rock, whose neck circumference is only 62 cm and is thinning year by year, making it the most iconic landmark of Yehliu. The geological formation history of Yehliu can be traced back to about 20 million years ago. Through long-term marine sedimentation, orogenic movements, and weathering erosion, the breathtaking coastal landscape we see today was formed. Yehliu has more than 180 mushroom rocks, making it the coastal area with the highest density of mushroom rocks in the world.

YELHIU · NATURE'S SCULPTURE GARDEN
GUIDE · Geology Classroom

"The North Coast's Geology Classroom"

Yehliu Geopark is not only a tourist attraction but also a natural geology classroom. The park is divided into three zones: Zone 1 features mainly mushroom rocks and ginger stones; Zone 2 is home to iconic landmarks such as the Queen's Head, Fairy's Shoe, and Peanut Stone; Zone 3 presents landforms such as sea erosion platforms and weathering windows. The park also houses the Yehliu Ocean World, showcasing the rich marine biological resources of Taiwan's northern waters.

Surrounding Yehliu are attractions such as Jinshan Hot Springs and the Juming Museum, forming the golden tourism belt of the North Coast. In recent years, due to typhoons and weathering, the neck of the Queen's Head has continued to thin, prompting experts to call for enhanced protection measures. To preserve this geological wonder, the park management has formulated corresponding protection plans and uses 3D scanning technology to record precise data on the Queen's Head. Visiting Yehliu feels like stepping into a natural sculpture garden carved by sea breeze and time.
