Five Wonders

A Forest Wonderland Above the Clouds

Located in northeastern Chiayi County, Taiwan, Alishan is one of Taiwan's most famous mountain tourist destinations. The Alishan Forest Recreation Area sits at an elevation of approximately 2,216 meters, covering a total area of about 1,400 hectares. It is renowned for its "Five Wonders": sunrise, sea of clouds, sunset glow, forests, and the railway.

The area boasts extremely rich forest resources, with millennium-old red cypress giant trees reaching over 50 meters in height. The air is filled with refreshing phytoncides, making it a natural forest oxygen bar. During the cherry blossom season from March to April each year, thousands of cherry trees—including Yoshino cherry, mountain cherry, and double-flowered cherry—bloom in succession, painting the entire mountain in a sea of pink and white. It is Taiwan's most iconic cherry blossom viewing destination.

Alishan is also home to one of only three remaining alpine forest railways in the world. Along the journey, you can experience the changing landscapes of four types of forest vegetation: tropical, subtropical, temperate, and alpine.

Century-Old Railway

A Timeless Journey on the Century-Old Railway

The Alishan Forest Railway stretches approximately 71.4 kilometers. Built in 1912, it was originally constructed for timber transportation and has since become a world-renowned scenic railway. The line climbs from Chiayi City at an elevation of 30 meters all the way up to Alishan Station at 2,216 meters, passing through 49 tunnels, 77 bridges, and spiral routes along the way. It is known as the "Railway in the Clouds."

The Zhushan branch line is Taiwan's most famous sunrise-viewing railway. Every morning, it carries visitors to the Zhushan sunrise platform to welcome the first rays of sunlight over Taiwan. The Alishan Scenic Area also includes attractions such as Sister Ponds, Shouzhen Temple, Ciyun Temple, and the Giant Tree Boardwalk.

Sister Ponds consists of two natural alpine lakes of different sizes, with water as still as a mirror, reflecting the surrounding ancient trees in a scene full of poetic charm. Alishan offers distinct scenery across all four seasons—spring and summer for flower viewing and firefly watching, autumn and winter for maple leaves and cloud vistas—making it a year-round tourist destination.
