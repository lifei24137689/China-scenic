「The Marvel of Marble」

Taroko Gorge is located in Hualien County, Taiwan, and is one of the most magnificent natural wonders in Taiwan. Taroko National Park covers a total area of 920 square kilometers, spanning across Hualien, Taichung, and Nantou counties. It is world-famous for its spectacular canyons, cliffs, and valley landscapes. Over millions of years, the Liwu River has continuously cut downward through the hard marble rock layers, carving out a canyon nearly 1,000 meters deep and forming the approximately 19-kilometer-long essence section of Taroko Gorge.

The cliffs on both sides rise almost vertically, and the marble textures display ever-changing colors and patterns under sunlight, truly a masterpiece of nature's craftsmanship. The park features numerous iconic attractions including the Eternal Spring Shrine, Swallow Grotto, Nine Turns Tunnel, Tianxiang, and Baiyang Trail, each showcasing the unique charm of the marble canyon.

HISTORY · Rock-Cutting Legend

「The Rock-Cutting Legend of Liwu River」

The Central Cross-Island Highway in Taroko is one of Taiwan's most magnificent mountain roads and a legend in Taiwan's engineering history. Construction began in 1956 and the highway was opened to traffic in 1960. The entire project cut through solid marble mountains, excavating countless tunnels and cliff-side sections. In an era lacking heavy machinery, road builders used hammers, steel bars, and explosives to carve a passage out of sheer cliffs — a modern-day equivalent of "moving mountains."

Memorial structures such as the Eternal Spring Shrine and Jinheng Park were built in honor of the workers who sacrificed their lives during construction. Taroko is also rich in ecological resources, hosting over 1,100 species of vascular plants and more than 144 species of birds. The annual Taroko Gorge Music Festival, held in summer, uses the canyon as a natural stage, with melodies echoing through the gorge to create a unique artistic experience. With its majestic geological landscapes and profound cultural heritage, Taroko stands as one of Taiwan's most iconic natural attractions.
