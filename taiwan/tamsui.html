A Century-old Port on the Tamsui River

Tamsui Old Street is located in the Tamsui District of New Taipei City, Taiwan, nestled along the banks of the Tamsui River. It is one of Taipei's most historically rich old streets. The development of Tamsui began in 1628 during the Spanish occupation, nearly 400 years ago. Built along the river, Tamsui Old Street stretches about 1 kilometer, with both sides preserving a large number of architectural styles from the late Qing Dynasty and early Republic of China era, blending Minnan, Western-style, and Baroque architectural elements.

Tamsui was once the most important international commercial port in northern Taiwan. After the mid-19th century, it became a major export port for commodities such as tea and camphor. Today, the old street is lined with a dazzling array of traditional snacks, including Tamsui A-gei, fish ball soup, A-po iron eggs, and sour plum soup, making it the best place for visitors to experience authentic northern Taiwanese cuisine. The sunset at Tamsui Old Street is particularly enchanting. When the sun sets, the Tamsui River shimmers with golden ripples, and the silhouette of Guanyin Mountain in the distance creates a picturesque scene, famously known as "Tamsui Sunset".

Historical Imprints from Spanish Rule to Japanese Occupation

Tamsui boasts a rich historical and cultural heritage, with numerous historic sites including Fort San Domingo, Hobe Fort, Tamsui Church, and Mackay's Residence. Fort San Domingo was built by the Spanish in 1628 as San Domingo Fort, and was rebuilt by the Dutch in 1644. It is one of the oldest intact buildings in Taiwan. Hobe Fort was built in 1886 and was a product of the Qing court's efforts to strengthen coastal defense after the Sino-French War (1884-1885), bearing witness to important moments in Taiwan's modern history.

Tamsui is also where Canadian Dr. George Leslie Mackay began his missionary and medical work in Taiwan. Tamsui Church and Mackay Hospital record his outstanding contributions to medical education in Taiwan. The food culture of Tamsui Old Street is equally rich and diverse. Tamsui A-gei is a unique snack made by hollowing out deep-fried tofu and filling it with minced meat and vermicelli before steaming. Fish ball soup is known for its fresh and bouncy texture. Tamsui still retains its traditional old street character and slow-paced lifestyle, making it the best weekend getaway for Taipei residents.
