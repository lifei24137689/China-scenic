/// 01

Riverside Life

Wuzhen is located in Tongxiang, Jiaxing, Zhejiang Province. It ranks first among the six major ancient towns south of the Yangtze River, with a history of over 1,300 years. A cross-shaped inland water system divides the town into four sections — east, south, west, and north — known locally as "East Gate, South Gate, West Gate, and North Gate."

Wuzhen has preserved the look and layout of a water town from the late Qing Dynasty and Republican era. Streets follow the rivers, bridges connect the lanes, houses nestle along the waterways — water and town blend into one. It embodies the serenity and simplicity of a classic Jiangnan water town, long celebrated as the "Land of Fish and Rice, Home of Silk."

/// 02

East & West Gates

The East Gate Scenic Area is known for its authentic water town scenery and profound cultural heritage. The former residence of Mao Dun, the literary master, is located here, offering a glimpse into the life of this literary giant. Ancient opera stages, pawnshops, and dye houses showcase Wuzhen's historic prosperity.

The West Gate Scenic Area consists of twelve islands surrounded by clear waters, perfectly presenting the classic Jiangnan water town landscape. Its night views are especially enchanting — under the shimmering lights, bridges, white walls, and dark tiles reflect on the water like a dream. The Mu Xin Art Museum is a pilgrimage site for art lovers.

/// 03

Cultural Wuzhen

The Wuzhen Theatre Festival is one of the most influential theatre events in China, held every October. Theatre troupes from around the world gather here, turning every corner of the ancient town into a stage. Wuzhen is also the permanent venue for the World Internet Conference, where tradition and modernity blend seamlessly.

Blue calico is Wuzhen's traditional handicraft. The blue fabrics drying on the tall racks of Hongyuan Tai Dyehouse have become one of Wuzhen's most iconic sights. The local cuisine is equally memorable — sister-in-law cakes, Sanbai liquor, braised lamb, and other specialties will linger in your memory.
