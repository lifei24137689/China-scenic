/// 01

Root Carving Buddha Land

Gen Gong Fo Guo Cultural Tourism Zone is located in Kaihua County, Quzhou City, Zhejiang Province. Themed around root carving art and Buddhist culture, it features the world's largest root carving art exposition park. Masterfully crafted by artist Xu Guqing, the scenic area perfectly integrates root carving art with Buddhist culture.

Thousands of exquisite root carving works are displayed throughout the park. Among them, the Five Hundred Arhats root carving group is majestic, with each arhat vividly lifelike, representing the pinnacle of world root carving art.

/// 02

Five Hundred Arhats

The Five Hundred Arhats root carving group is the crown jewel of Gen Gong Fo Guo. Each arhat is carved from thousand-year-old tree roots, meticulously crafted by master artisans. The arhats display a variety of expressions — some benevolent, some fierce — showcasing the ultimate beauty of root carving art.

The Zuigen Tiangong Museum houses a vast collection of precious root carving artworks. The Mahavira Hall features root carving Buddha statues, and the Gen Gong Fo Pagoda rises into the clouds, making the entire scenic area a true Buddha land of root carving art.

Art Hall

Gen Gong Fo Guo is not only a window into Buddhist culture but also a hall of traditional Chinese root carving art. The Zuigen Tiangong Museum within the park collects exquisite root carving pieces from across the country, showcasing the profound depth of Chinese root carving art.

The scenic area is open year-round and suitable for visits in all seasons. Stroll through the ocean of root carving art, feel the magic of "turning decay into wonder," and experience the solemnity and tranquility of Buddhist culture.
