Xixi · A Lasting Impression

Xixi National Wetland Park, located in the western part of Hangzhou, is China's first national wetland park that integrates urban wetland, agricultural wetland, and cultural wetland. "Xixi, a place worth staying forever" — Emperor Gaozong of Song was so captivated by Xixi's beauty during his southward journey that he considered building his capital here, leaving behind this timeless remark.

Covering approximately 11.5 square kilometers, Xixi Wetland features a maze of waterways, interconnected ponds, and scattered islets. It is not only a pristine sanctuary within the city but also a paradise for hundreds of bird species. The area gained even greater fame after being featured in the film "If You Are the One".

Wetland Ecology

Xixi Wetland boasts a complete ecosystem with abundant flora and fauna. Hundreds of bird species, including egrets, herons, and kingfishers, inhabit this area, making it a paradise for birdwatchers. The crisscrossing waterways and dense reed beds create a unique wetland landscape.

Qiuxue Temple is one of the most scenic spots in Xixi. In late autumn, reed catkins bloom like snow, and drifting through them by boat feels like being immersed in a vast white ocean. With its distinctive urban wetland landscape, Xixi has become the top choice for Hangzhou residents seeking leisure, and an exceptional destination for visitors to experience the charm of Jiangnan water towns.
