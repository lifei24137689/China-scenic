/// 01

Lake & Mountain Sanctuary

Nestled in Hangzhou, Zhejiang, West Lake is among China's first National Key Scenic Areas and one of China's Top Ten Scenic Wonders. Surrounded by mountains on three sides with emerald waters at its heart, its landscape of one hill, two pagodas, three islets, three causeways, and five lake sections creates a scene of ever-changing beauty.

In 2011, the "West Lake Cultural Landscape of Hangzhou" was inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List. The Ten Scenes of West Lake — Spring Dawn at Su Causeway, Melting Snow at Broken Bridge, Sunset Glow at Leifeng Pagoda, Three Pools Mirroring the Moon — are renowned worldwide, while the legend of the White Snake adds a romantic aura to the lake.

Ten Classic Scenes

The Ten Scenes of West Lake represent the finest landscape ensemble, including Spring Dawn at Su Causeway, Lotus in the Breeze at Crooked Courtyard, Autumn Moon over the Calm Lake, Melting Snow at Broken Bridge, Viewing Fish at Flower Harbor, Orioles Singing in the Willows, Three Pools Mirroring the Moon, Twin Peaks Piercing the Clouds, Sunset Glow at Leifeng Pagoda, and Evening Bell at Nanping Hill. Each scene offers a unique seasonal charm and viewing perspective.

Leifeng Pagoda stands atop Sunset Hill. As the sun sets, the pagoda's silhouette stretches across the lake as golden light bathes the water, creating the breathtaking "Sunset Glow at Leifeng Pagoda." Broken Bridge is where Lady White Snake met Xu Xian — at its most touching in winter when lingering snow dusts the span.

Millennium Heritage

West Lake is more than a natural wonder — it is a spiritual landmark of Chinese culture. From Bai Juyi to Su Shi, from Lin Hejing to Zhang Dai, countless poets and scholars have left immortal verses in its honor. Bai Juyi's line "Most loved is the east lake — every step a delight, with white sand causeway shaded by green willows" is still recited today.

Cultural sites around West Lake — Lingyin Temple, Yue Fei's Temple, Xiling Seal Engravers' Society — complement the lake and mountain scenery. The aroma of Longjing tea, the elegance of silk, and classic Jiangnan gardens together embody West Lake's profound heritage, making it a paragon of Eastern aesthetics.
