/// 01

Buddha's Realm

Putuo Mountain, located in the eastern part of the Zhoushan Islands in Zhejiang Province, is one of the Four Sacred Buddhist Mountains of China, renowned as the "Buddha's Realm by Sea & Sky." The island resembles a green dragon lying across the sea, standing alongside Mount Wutai in Shanxi, Mount Emei in Sichuan, and Mount Jiuhua in Anhui as one of China's four great Buddhist mountains.

As the bodhimanda where Avalokiteshvara Bodhisattva teaches and transforms sentient beings, Putuo Mountain has long been honored as the "Buddha's Realm by Sea & Sky" and the "Sacred Land of the South Sea." Mountains meet the sea, ancient temples stand in abundance, and the sounds of chanting fill the air, presenting a majestic scene of a Buddhist paradise. The mountain can be visited year-round, with incense burning for a thousand years without ceasing.

Three Great Temples

Puji Temple, also known as the Front Temple, is the main shrine dedicated to Avalokiteshvara on Putuo Mountain, covering an area of 37,019 square meters. In front of the temple lies the Sea Seal Pond, where lotus flowers bloom and the ancient temple's reflection creates a dreamlike scene. The Great Round Hall enshrines an 8.8-meter-tall Vairocana Avalokiteshvara statue, resplendent in gold.

Fayu Temple is built against the mountain in ascending tiers. Its Nine-Dragon Hall was constructed during the Kangxi reign of the Qing Dynasty by relocating an old palace hall from the Nanjing Forbidden City, making it the highest-specification temple building in China. Huiji Temple sits atop the Buddha's Summit Peak, hidden deep within a sea of trees — "If you haven't been to the Buddha's Summit, you haven't truly visited Putuo Mountain."

/// 03

South Sea Guanyin

The South Sea Guanyin standing statue is located at the southern end of Shuangfeng Mountain, with a total height of 33 meters — the pedestal is 13 meters high and the bronze statue is 20 meters tall. Guanyin's countenance is compassionate and solemn, holding a Dharma wheel in the left hand and forming the Abhaya mudra with the right, shining with golden light that illuminates the sea and sky.

Thousand-Step Sand is the longest beach on the eastern coast of Putuo Mountain, with golden sand that is wide and soft. At Chaoyin Cave, the crashing waves against the rocks thunder like a thousand monks chanting sutras. The South Heavenly Gate stands tall on the sea cliffs, offering an unparalleled vantage point for watching the sea and listening to the tides.
