/// 01

The Finest Water Under Heaven

Qiandao Lake is located in Chun'an County, Hangzhou, Zhejiang Province. It is a man-made reservoir created by damming the upper reaches of the Xin'an River for the Xin'an River Hydroelectric Station. Named for its 1,078 emerald islands, the lake has crystal-clear waters with visibility reaching 7–12 meters, earning it the reputation of "the finest water under heaven."

Covering an area of 573 square kilometers, Qiandao Lake is 108 times the size of Hangzhou's West Lake. Its islands vary in size and shape, scattered across the blue-green waters like a natural landscape painting. Guo Moruo once praised it in verse: "Three thousand West Lakes gathered here, the mountains have lost their height."

/// 02

A Thousand Islands

Mei Peak Observatory is the best vantage point for a panoramic view of Qiandao Lake. From the summit, visitors can take in over 300 islands of various sizes. As the saying goes, "Without climbing Mei Peak to see the islands, you cannot truly know Qiandao Lake." Huangshan Point is famous for its "All Under Heaven Is for the Public" island cluster — from above, the islands naturally arrange themselves into the four characters of that phrase.

Dragon Mountain Island is a landmark attraction featuring the Hairui Temple, a memorial to the upright Ming Dynasty official Hai Rui. Moonlight Island, themed around romance, connects five small islets with floating bridges. Fisherman's Isle offers dining and entertainment, making it a popular leisure destination for visitors.

The Submerged Ancient City

Beneath Qiandao Lake lie two ancient cities — Lion City and He City. In 1959, when the Xin'an River Dam was completed and the reservoir filled, both cities were swallowed by the rising waters. Recent underwater archaeological surveys reveal that the city walls, memorial arches, and residential buildings remain remarkably well-preserved, earning the site the title of "the Atlantis of the East."

Qiandao Lake boasts exceptional water quality, classified as a National Class-I water body, and serves as one of the source sites for Nongfu Spring bottled water. With a forest coverage rate of 95%, the area offers crisp, clean air rich in negative oxygen ions, making it an important ecological barrier and leisure destination in the Yangtze River Delta region.
