Jiangnan Great Wall

The Taizhou Prefecture City Wall was first built in the Eastern Jin Dynasty, with a history of over 1,600 years. Stretching more than 6,000 meters along the terrain, it is renowned as the "Jiangnan Great Wall" and "Jiangnan Badaling."

The wall served both military defense and flood control, and is considered a prototype for the northern Ming Great Wall. From atop, the Lingjiang River flows like a ribbon and the ancient city unfolds in full view. The watchtowers and barbicans are well preserved.

Ziyang Ancient Street

Ziyang Ancient Street is the best-preserved historic district within Taizhou Fucheng, stretching 1,080 meters. It was named after Zhang Boduan (also known as Ziyang), the founder of the Southern School of Taoism. Numerous Ming and Qing dynasty buildings line both sides of the street.

The street is filled with century-old shops, traditional craft workshops, and local snacks. Strolling along the blue stone pavement, you can feel the vibrant everyday life of this thousand-year-old city.

Lingjiang & East Lake

The Lingjiang River surrounds Taizhou Fucheng, serving as the ancient city's natural moat. East Lake lies east of the city, nestled beside the city wall, where lake and mountain views complement each other beautifully—one of the finest scenic spots in the ancient city.

The Taizhou Fucheng area also includes Mount Jin and Longxing Temple. The twin pagodas on Mount Jin are iconic landmarks, and Longxing Temple is an important site for Sino-Japanese Buddhist cultural exchange.
