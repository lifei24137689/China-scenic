Tianyi Pavilion

Tianyi Pavilion is located in Haishu District, Ningbo. Built in 1561 (the 40th year of the Jiajing reign of the Ming Dynasty) by Fan Qin, Vice Minister of War, it is China's oldest surviving private library. Its collection once exceeded 70,000 volumes, renowned especially for local chronicles and imperial examination records.

The name "Tianyi" comes from the I Ching's phrase "Heaven generates Water," symbolizing using water to conquer fire and protect the books. A pond was dug in front of the pavilion to store water, surrounded by gardens with rockeries and pavilions — a living fossil of China's bibliophile culture.

Yuehu Lake

Yuehu Lake is Ningbo's Mother Lake, excavated during the Zhenguan reign of the Tang Dynasty. Its round yet elongated crescent-moon shape gives it its name. The lakeshore has long been a gathering place for scholars and literati since the Song Dynasty, leaving behind a wealth of poetry, inscriptions, and historic architecture.

Within Yuehu Lake Park, pavilions and towers are nestled among green trees and blue waters. The Yuehu Bridge spans the lake, making it Ningbo's city living room. Tianyi Pavilion and Yuehu Lake complement each other, creating a unique cultural landscape where books and lake harmoniously blend.

Scholarly Gardens

Tianyi Pavilion is not only a library but also a exquisite Jiangnan-style garden. Buildings such as Dongming Thatched Hall, Zunjing Scripture Hall, and Mingzhou Stele Forest are scattered throughout the grounds. Rockeries, winding paths, and covered corridors reflect the elegance and craftsmanship of ancient scholars.

Today, the Tianyi Pavilion Museum houses over 300,000 volumes of ancient texts, serving as an important base for the study of China's bibliophile culture. Ancient books meet a global port — Tianyi Pavilion and Yuehu Lake are the root and soul of Ningbo culture.
