Shuanglong Cave

Shuanglong Cave is the centerpiece of the Shuanglong Scenic Area, located on Jinhua's Beishan Mountain. Composed of an outer and inner cave, the outer chamber is tall and luminous while the inner cave requires entering by boat—a classic karst cave landscape.

Shuanglong Cave gets its name from stalactites flanking the entrance that resemble dragon heads. Inside, stalactites and stalagmites take myriad forms, illuminated by colored lights into an underground palace. The renowned writer Ye Shengtao once penned the famous essay "A Visit to Shuanglong Cave in Jinhua."

Binghu Cave

Binghu Cave connects to Shuanglong Cave and houses a 20-meter-plus underground waterfall—Binghu Waterfall—one of China's largest cave waterfalls. The water cascades from the cave ceiling with thunderous roar and majestic force.

The Shuanglong area also includes Chaozhen Cave, Taoyuan Cave, and other karst formations, along with Taoist cultural sites such as Jinhua Temple and the Huang Daxian Ancestral Palace. This is the 36th Taoist Grotto Heaven, rich in cultural heritage.

Taoist Grotto

The Shuanglong area is home to the 36th Taoist Grotto Heaven—Jinhua Dongyuan Grotto. The Huang Daxian Ancestral Palace is an important Taoist cultural site, and the worship of Huang Daxian has a profound influence in Hong Kong, Macau, and Southeast Asia.

The area also includes historical sites such as Lutian Academy and the Temple of the Wise. Beishan Mountain boasts dense forest cover and fresh air, making it an ideal destination for city dwellers to reconnect with nature and explore underground wonders.
