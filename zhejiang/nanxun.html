/// 01

Baijian Lou

Nanxun Ancient Town, located in Huzhou's Nanxun District, is one of the six great ancient towns south of the Yangtze River. Baijianlou (Hundred-Building Tower) is Nanxun's most iconic architectural cluster — rows of white-walled, dark-tiled residences stretching for hundreds of meters along the river, exemplifying classic Jiangnan waterside architecture.

Nanxun is famed for its wealthy merchant clans known as the "Four Elephants, Eight Oxen, Seventy-Two Golden Dogs." During the late Qing and early Republic eras, Nanxun's merchants blended Western architectural elements into traditional Jiangnan homes, giving rise to a distinctive East-meets-West style.

/// 02

Xiaolian Zhuang

Xiaolianzhuang (Little Lotus Manor) was the private garden of Liu Yong, the foremost of Nanxun's "Four Elephants" merchant families. Construction began in 1885 and took 40 years to complete. The garden features pavilions, terraces, rockeries, and ponds — blending the refinement of classical Jiangnan landscaping with Western architectural accents.

Jiaye Hall Library is the largest private library of modern China, housing over 600,000 volumes of ancient texts. Zhang Shiming's Former Residence epitomizes the East-meets-West style, while Liu's Ti Mansion with its red-brick Western villa reflects the open-mindedness of Nanxun's wealthy merchants.

/// 03

Scholarly Jiangnan

Nanxun was not only a gathering place of wealthy merchants but also a scholarly hub steeped in cultural heritage. Jiaye Hall Library, the Western-style ballroom inside Zhang Shiming's Former Residence, and the red-brick villa of Liu's Ti Mansion — all bear witness to Nanxun's unique fusion of Chinese and Western cultures.

Strolling along Nanxun's flagstone paths, you will see Baijianlou's reflection rippling in the water and Xiaolianzhuang's lotus blossoms blooming in summer. Unlike the bustling crowds of Wuzhen, Nanxun offers a tranquil and unhurried experience — the finest place to savor the culture of a Jiangnan water town.
