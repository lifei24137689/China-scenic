Baizhang Waterfall

Liu Boweng's Hometown scenic area is located in Wencheng County, Wenzhou City, centered around the Baizhangji Waterfall and Liu Ji's ancestral home. With a drop of 207 meters, Baizhangji is known as "China's Highest Waterfall." Its three-tiered cascade is magnificent, and the misty spray makes it feel like a fairyland.

Baizhangji has three tiers: the first is famous for its height, the second for its depth, and the third for its width. The waterfall plunges down the sheer cliff with a thunderous roar, sending up sparkling spray. Under sunlight, rainbows bridge across the scene, creating a spectacular sight.

Liu Ji's Hometown

Liu Ji (Liu Bowen) was a founding hero of the Ming Dynasty, hailed by Emperor Zhu Yuanzhang as "my Zifang." Liu Ji's Hometown preserves the Liu Ji Temple, Liu Ji's Tomb, and other historical sites, making it the best place to learn about the life of this legendary strategist.

The Liu Ji Temple was first built in the third year of the Tianshun era of the Ming Dynasty, with simple yet dignified architecture. Inside stands a statue of Liu Ji, where incense burns year-round. Every year on the 15th day of the 6th lunar month, Liu Ji's birthday, grand ceremonies are held locally.

Wencheng Landscape

Wencheng County, named after Liu Bowen's posthumous title "Wencheng," is a typical mountainous county in southern Zhejiang. With lush mountains, clear waters, and a forest coverage rate exceeding 70%, it is an ideal escape for city dwellers seeking tranquility and nature.

Beyond Baizhangji Waterfall and Liu Ji's Hometown, Wencheng also boasts natural attractions such as Tongling Mountain and Longqi Valley. Spring brings blooming mountain flowers, summer offers abundant waterfall flow, and autumn paints the forests in vibrant colors, making it a year-round travel destination.
