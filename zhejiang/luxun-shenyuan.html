/// 01

National Soul

Lu Xun's Hometown, located in Yuecheng District, Shaoxing, is where the great writer Lu Xun was born and spent his youth. The area preserves historic sites associated with Lu Xun, including his ancestral residence, former home, the Hundred-Plant Garden, and the Three-Flavor Study.

"Fierce-browed, I coolly defy a thousand pointing fingers; head bowed, like a willing ox I serve the children" — Lu Xun used his pen as a weapon and is revered as the "national soul." Walking through the Hundred-Plant Garden, one can almost hear the laughter of young Lu Xun; stepping into the Three-Flavor Study, the teachings of his mentor Shou Jingwu still echo in the air.

/// 02

Eternal Love

Shen Garden is renowned for the tragic love story of Lu You and Tang Wan. The Southern Song poet Lu You married his cousin Tang Wan in deep affection, but was forced to separate by his mother. Years later, the two reunited at Shen Garden, where Lu You inscribed the immortal poem "Phoenix Hairpin" on the wall.

With its pavilions, rockeries, and ponds, Shen Garden exudes ancient elegance. The "Phoenix Hairpin" stele is a must-see for visitors. Lu You revisited the garden many times throughout his life, leaving behind poignant verses that have made Shen Garden an eternal shrine of love.

Literary Sanctuary

Lu Xun's Hometown is not only a historic site but also a sanctuary of modern Chinese literature. The Lu Xun Memorial Hall houses a vast collection of Lu Xun's manuscripts, works, and biographical materials, offering a comprehensive view of his literary achievements and revolutionary spirit.

The village stage within the scenic area recreates the setting of Lu Xun's story "Village Opera," allowing visitors to experience the folk culture of Shaoxing's water towns. Lu Xun's Hometown and Shen Garden are close to each other, making it possible to explore both themes of literature and love in a single day.
