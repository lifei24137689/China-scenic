Guoqing Temple

Located in Tiantai County, Taizhou, Tiantai Mountain is the birthplace of the Tiantai school of Buddhism. Guoqing Temple, first built in 598 AD during the Sui Dynasty, serves as the ancestral temple of both Chinese Tiantai Buddhism and Japanese Tendai Buddhism, holding a profoundly important place in East Asian Buddhist history.

Set against the mountain, Guoqing Temple is surrounded by ancient trees and dominated by the towering Sui Dynasty pagoda. The temple's Sui Dynasty plum tree, over 1,400 years old, is one of China's oldest. Every spring when the plum blossoms bloom, the ancient temple is filled with fragrance and Zen spirit.

/// 02

Shiliang Waterfall

The Shiliang Waterfall is Tiantai Mountain's most spectacular natural wonder. A natural stone bridge spans between two peaks, with water thundering down beneath it — a breathtaking sight known as "Shiliang Feipu" (Stone Bridge Flying Waterfall). The famed Ming Dynasty traveler Xu Xiake visited Tiantai Mountain six times and marveled at this extraordinary scene.

Tiantai Mountain is renowned as a "Sacred Land of Buddhist and Taoist Heritage." Beyond the Buddhist sanctuary of Guoqing Temple, it is also home to Tongbai Palace, the ancestral temple of the Southern School of Taoism. Huading National Forest Park offers stunning sunrise and sea-of-clouds views, while the Danxia landforms of Chicheng Mountain are truly awe-inspiring.

Qiongtai Fairy Valley

Qiongtai Fairy Valley is the jewel of Tiantai Mountain, with its deep gorge, towering peaks, and the magnificent Baizhang Cliff Waterfall cascading from great heights. The great Tang poet Li Bai wrote his immortal lines here: "Dragon towers and phoenix pavilions could not keep me — I soar straight to Tiantai."

Tiantai Mountain offers distinct beauty across all four seasons: spring wildflowers, cool summer retreats, autumn's vibrant foliage, and winter's snow-covered landscapes. Whether on a pilgrimage or a scenic hike, Tiantai Mountain brings peace to the soul and awe at the wonders of nature.
