Zhongyuan Great Buddha

The Zhongyuan Great Buddha is located at the foot of Yaoshan Mountain in Lushan County, Pingdingshan City, Henan Province. Standing 208 meters tall, it is the tallest Buddhist statue in the world. The statue consists of three parts: the solemn Buddha body, the Sumeru throne, and the lotus pedestal, with a total investment of over 1.2 billion yuan, using 3,300 tons of copper, 108 kilograms of gold, and over 15,000 tons of special steel.

The Buddha's face is solemn and compassionate, with the right hand forming the "Abhaya Mudra" (fearless gesture), symbolizing the removal of suffering for all beings, and the left hand forming the "Varada Mudra" (wish-granting gesture), symbolizing the bestowal of happiness upon all beings. On September 29, 2008, the grand consecration ceremony of the Great Buddha was held, witnessed by 108 eminent monks from around the world. The completion of the Great Buddha marks the continuation and flourishing of Buddhist culture on Chinese soil.

Yaoshan Peaks

Yaoshan is the main peak of the eastern Funiu Mountain range, with an altitude of 2,153 meters. It is named after Liu Lei, the grandson of Emperor Yao, who built an Yao Temple here. Yaoshan is renowned for its spectacular peaks, strange rocks, waterfalls, and hot springs, integrating Buddhist culture, natural scenery, and hot spring wellness. With its towering peaks and misty clouds, it is known as the "Unique Beauty of Central China."

The main attractions of Yaoshan include the Yuhuang Summit, Shiren Mountain, and Bailongtan Waterfall. The Yuhuang Summit, at 2,153.1 meters, is the highest peak of Yaoshan, offering a panoramic view of the Funiu Mountain range. Shiren Mountain, named for its human-like shape, is one of Yaoshan's iconic landmarks. The mountain is filled with towering peaks and rugged rocks, creating a breathtaking landscape.

Hot Spring Wellness

Yaoshan is rich in hot spring resources with excellent water quality, rich in a variety of minerals and trace elements beneficial to the human body. The Yaoshan Hot Spring Resort is a comprehensive hot spring tourism destination integrating hot spring bathing, leisure wellness, and vacation travel. The spring water temperature reaches up to 62°C, classified as high-quality fluoride hot springs.

Built along the mountain and integrated into the natural forest, the hot spring area is suitable for all seasons. In spring and autumn, soak in the hot springs while enjoying the misty mountain scenery; in winter, the steaming hot springs create a wonderful contrast with the surrounding ice and snow. The Yaoshan hot springs complement the Buddhist culture, becoming an ideal place for visitors to cleanse their body and mind and experience zen tranquility.
