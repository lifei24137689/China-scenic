Journey to the West Sacred Mountain · Filming Landmark

Chaya Sacred Mountain · The Great Sage's Hometown

Chaya Mountain served as the main filming location for the TV series Journey to the West. Traces of the classic tale can be found among its unique peaks and peculiar rocks, earning it the reputation of the "Journey to the West Sacred Mountain".

Journey to the West Filming Location · A Screen Legend

Chaya Mountain was chosen as the main filming location for the TV series Journey to the West because its remarkable granite peaks and peculiar rocks closely match the mythical realm described in the story. Attractions such as the Stone Monkey Courtyard and the Journey to the West Sculpture Garden recreate classic scenes from the tale.

Strolling through the mountain feels like stepping into the mythical world of Journey to the West. Every unique rock seems enchanted by the Great Sage's magic, and every cave holds legends of immortals. The majesty of South Gate, the tranquility of Tianmo Lake, and the serenity of Pipa Lake together compose this painting of a Journey to the West wonderland.

Granite Peaks · Geological Wonder

Steep, Perilous, Mysterious, Serene · Nature's Masterpiece

The mountain is composed of granite, with unique peaks and strange rocks forming a natural bonsai, renowned worldwide for its "steepness, peril, mystery, and serenity".

Hundreds of Millions of Years in the Making · Granite Marvel

Chaya Mountain is composed of granite dating back approximately 120 million years. After hundreds of millions of years of weathering and erosion, it formed a unique landscape of towering peaks and jagged rocks. The South Mountain features unique rock formations, while the North Mountain is steep and precipitous — each with its own distinct character.

The unique rocks take various forms — some resemble a divine turtle gazing at the moon, others a giant dragon soaring through the sky, and still others an immortal pointing the way. These naturally formed rock features, complemented by surrounding green trees and clear streams, create a vast natural bonsai that leaves visitors in awe.

Tianmo Lakeside · Picturesque Landscape

Tianmo & Pipa · Lake and Mountain Scenery

Tianmo Lake and Pipa Lake are like two pearls set amidst Chaya Mountain, with emerald waters reflecting the unique peaks — a breathtaking sight.

Emerald Waters Mirroring Peaks · Lake and Mountain in Harmony

Tianmo Lake is one of Chaya Mountain's core attractions, with crystal-clear waters surrounded by unique peaks on all sides. The lake surface is like a mirror, reflecting the unique rocks and the blue sky with white clouds — a perfect harmony of mountain and water resembling a natural landscape painting. Shaded by green trees along the shore, winding paths lead to secluded spots, making it an ideal place for visitors to rest and enjoy the scenery.

Pipa Lake, adjacent to Tianmo Lake, is named for its pipa-like shape. The mountain and water complement each other beautifully. When morning mist envelops the area, gentle ripples on the lake surface resemble the soft strumming of a pipa, playing a melody of landscape. A path connects the two lakes, offering enchanting scenery every step of the way.
