The Human Ancestor

Fuxi is a legendary figure in ancient Chinese history, regarded as the human ancestor of the Chinese nation and the earliest king recorded in ancient Chinese texts. He was born in Chengji (present-day Tianshui, Gansu), established his capital in Chen (present-day Huaiyang, Henan), and was buried in Chen. Taihao Fuxi Mausoleum is located in Huaiyang District, Zhoukou City, Henan Province, and serves as the burial site and temple of Fuxi.

Fuxi is honored as "Foremost of the Three Sovereigns" and "First Among Hundred Kings," the founder who led the Chinese nation from ignorance to civilization. By observing the heavens above and the earth below, he created the Eight Trigrams system that has influenced Chinese civilization for millennia, opening the gateway to Chinese cultural wisdom. Taihao Fuxi Mausoleum was first built during the Spring and Autumn period, with a history spanning thousands of years.

Fuxi's Achievements
/// 02

Fuxi's Achievements

Fuxi made groundbreaking contributions to Chinese civilization. He created the Eight Trigrams, using eight symbols to represent the fundamental laws of heaven, earth, and all things. This became the core of the I Ching (Book of Changes), profoundly influencing Chinese philosophy, science, medicine, and various other fields. "In change there is the Supreme Ultimate, which generates the Two Modes; the Two Modes generate the Four Phenomena; the Four Phenomena generate the Eight Trigrams" — this cosmological view still guides Chinese thinking today.

He also "established marriage rites and rectified surnames," creating the marriage system and surname system, transitioning human society from group marriage to paired marriage, laying the foundation for family ethics. Fuxi also "invented writing to replace knot-tying," creating the earliest recording symbols and initiating the dawn of writing. He taught people to weave nets, fish, and domesticate animals, driving a great transformation in production methods.

Temple Fair
/// 03

Temple Fair

The Taihao Mausoleum Temple Fair is one of the largest folk temple fairs in China and around the world. Every year from the 2nd day of the 2nd lunar month to the 3rd day of the 3rd lunar month, millions of pilgrims and tourists from across the country gather here to pay homage to their ancestors, pray for blessings, and receive good fortune. The scene during the fair is spectacular — a sea of people with incense smoke swirling, earning it the reputation of "China's Most Amazing Temple Fair."

In 2016, the Taihao Mausoleum Temple Fair set a Guinness World Record for the number of pilgrims in a single day, demonstrating the profound influence of Fuxi culture among Chinese people at home and abroad. The fair also features a variety of folk art activities such as pole dancing, dragon and lion dances, and acrobatic performances, offering an excellent window into Central Plains folk culture. Ancient architectural complexes such as the Tongtian Hall and Xianren Hall stand in solemn grandeur, bearing witness to millennia of sacrificial traditions.
