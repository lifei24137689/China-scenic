Taihang Celestial River

Baligou is the crown jewel of the southern Taihang Mountains, renowned for its "Taihang Celestial River" waterfall cluster. The Celestial Waterfall drops 180 meters, making it the tallest waterfall in North China. The water cascades down red rock cliffs like a silver river pouring from the heavens, with thundering momentum that echoes through the valley. During the peak summer flow, the waterfall reaches over 20 meters in width, with mist filling the air and rainbows often appearing in the sunlight 鈥?a truly magnificent sight.

The Celestial Waterfall originates from the 1,700-meter summit of the Taihang Mountains, gathering water from multiple mountain streams along the way as it rushes down the red stone riverbed. A deep pool forms at the base of the waterfall, its crystal-clear emerald waters contrasting vividly with the surrounding red rock cliffs. Visitors can follow the plank road to experience the waterfall up close, feeling the spray of water and the refreshing coolness in the air.

Red Rock Cliffs

The red rock cliffs of Baligou are among the most representative geological wonders of the Taihang Mountains. These red quartz sandstones, formed hundreds of millions of years ago, were uplifted and fractured through prolonged tectonic activity, creating vertical cliff faces hundreds of meters high. Under sunlight, the red rocks display a brilliant ochre hue, forming a colorful natural tapestry against the blue sky, white clouds, and green vegetation.

The "Thread of Sky" is the most breathtaking spectacle within the red rock cliffs 鈥?both walls are steep as if carved by a knife, with the narrowest section barely allowing one person to pass, offering only a sliver of sky above. The Red Stone River winds through the red rock canyon, its waters displaying a unique pale red color due to mineral content, creating a delightful interplay with the red rock cliffs on both sides 鈥?a perfect fusion of geological wonder and mountain scenery.

/// 03

Primeval Forest

Baligou preserves vast expanses of primeval forest, with vegetation coverage exceeding 90%. The forest is home to many rare plant species, including the Taihang-endemic yew tree and cercidiphyllum. Ancient trees tower overhead, intertwined with vines, creating a richly layered forest ecosystem. The area also features a macaque sanctuary, housing hundreds of Taihang macaques, adding vitality and delight to the primeval forest.

Walking along the mountain trails through the primeval forest, breathing in the fresh air rich in negative oxygen ions, listening to the songs of birds and insects, you can feel the tranquility and mystery of nature. Mountain flowers bloom brilliantly in spring, and the forest is painted in vibrant autumn colors 鈥?the scenery changes beautifully with each season. The primeval forest is not only a paradise for wildlife but also an ideal retreat for visitors seeking to escape the hustle and bustle and return to nature.
