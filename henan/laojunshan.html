Taoist Sacred Land · Laozi's Retreat

Supreme Lord Laozi · Purple Mist from the East

Laojun Mountain is the sacred ground where Laozi, the founder of Taoism, retreated for cultivation. For two millennia, incense has never ceased, earning it the title "Peerless Sacred Land, the Foremost Immortal Mountain."

Taoist Ancestral Court · Millennia of Heritage

Originally named Jingshi Mountain, Laojun Mountain was renamed after Laozi (Li Er), the founder of Taoism, who retreated here for cultivation. During the Spring and Autumn period, Laozi resigned from his official post and journeyed westward. Passing through this area, he was captivated by the majestic peaks shrouded in purple mist, and chose to dwell in seclusion, composing the five-thousand-word "Tao Te Ching."

Since the Northern Wei dynasty, Taoist followers have built temples and shrines here, with incense burning continuously through the ages. Emperor Taizong of Tang bestowed the name "Laojun Mountain," and it became a Taoist sacred land, renowned alongside Wudang Mountain as one of the two great Taoist mountains of the north and south.

Above the Golden Summit · The Celestial Palace

Golden Summit Wonderland · Cloud Sea Celestial Palace

Ten-Mile Painting Screen · Purple Twilight

The Golden Summit temple complex, perched atop the 2200m peak, looks like a celestial palace when clouds roll in, earning it the name "Cloud Summit Celestial Palace."

Golden Summit Temples · Sanctuaries Above the Clouds

The Laojun Mountain Golden Summit temple complex comprises the Five-Mother Golden Hall, Morality Temple, Laojun Shrine, Jade Emperor Peak, and other structures, all built atop the 2200-meter summit. The architecture follows the Ming-Qing official style, with golden roofs and richly ornamented beams, appearing as a heavenly palace amid the sea of clouds.

Mazong Ridge is the highest peak of Laojun Mountain at 2217 meters. From its summit, the vast Funiu Mountain range unfolds before your eyes. The Ten-Mile Painting Screen is the most spectacular scenic zone along the ascent, featuring peculiar peaks, ancient pines, and verdant cypresses — a landscape scroll gradually revealed with every step.

Eons of Creation · Underground Wonder

Jiguan Cave · Nature's Masterpiece

Jiguan Cave is the largest karst cave in northern China, filled with stalactites of countless forms, shaped by geological movements over hundreds of millions of years.

North's Finest Cave · Carved by Eons

Located at the northern foot of Laojun Mountain, Jiguan Cave is the largest and best-preserved karst cave in northern China. Stretching approximately 1800 meters in length, it spans five levels from top to bottom, composed of stalagmites, stalactite curtains, cave flowers, stone waterfalls, and various other formations.

The cave maintains a constant temperature of 18°C year-round, cool in summer and warm in winter. Illuminated by colorful lights, the stalactites glisten with iridescent splendor, creating a dreamlike atmosphere. Major attractions include "Jade Pillars Holding the Sky," "Jade Pool Fairyland," "Dragon Palace Treasure Hall," and "Sky-Scraping Flying Waterfall" — each a masterpiece sculpted by nature over eons.
