Han Culture Sacred Site

Liu Bang's Serpent-Slaying Uprising Site

Mangdangshan is where Emperor Gaozu Liu Bang slew the serpent and began his uprising, and it also houses the royal tombs of the Western Han Liang Kingdom. Precious relics unearthed here include the jade burial suit and the Four Spirits Cloud Chart.

The Mangdangshan Han tomb complex is the largest Han-dynasty cliff burial site in China, hailed as "First Stone Chamber Under Heaven." It bears witness to the grandeur and glory of the Han dynasty.

/// L I A N G R O Y A L T O M B S

First Stone Chamber · Under Heaven

A vast Han-dynasty cliff-tomb complex, concealing the splendor and secrets of the Western Han Liang Kingdom.

Cliff Tomb Marvel

Western Han Liang Royal Tombs

The Mangdangshan Han tomb complex is China's largest Han-dynasty cliff-tomb group, known as "First Stone Chamber Under Heaven." Carved into the mountain, the Western Han Liang royal tombs are grand in scale and intricate in structure.

Precious artifacts unearthed here include the jade burial suit and the Four Spirits Cloud Chart. The Four Spirits Cloud Chart is one of the earliest surviving murals in China, holding immense historical and artistic value.

Sages' Footsteps · Millennial Echoes

Mangdangshan is not only the cradle of the Han dynasty but also a stage for countless towering historical figures.

Cultural Convergence

Chen Sheng's Tomb · Confucius Cliff

Beyond the Han Liang royal tombs and Liu Bang's Serpent-Slaying Stele, Mangdangshan also features Chen Sheng's Tomb, Confucius Cliff, and Mangshan Temple—each bearing profound historical memories.

Confucius Cliff is said to be where Confucius once taught, while Chen Sheng's Tomb is the resting place of China's first peasant-revolt leader. Mangshan Temple sits among the hills, its bell tolling through the millennia.
