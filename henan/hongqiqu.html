MAN-MADE RIVER

Man-Made River
Hongqiqu

Built in the 1960s by the people of Lin County (now Linzhou City) along the cliffs of the Taihang Mountains, the Hongqiqu is a 1,500 km water diversion project that brings water from the Zhang River to Lin County. Known as the "Man-Made River" and the "Eighth Wonder of the World," 100,000 mountain excavators worked for ten years, leveling 1,250 peaks and digging 211 tunnels, forging a monument to life on sheer cliffs that moves heaven and earth.

TAIHANG CANYON

Taihang Grand Canyon
Majestic Landscape

The Taihang Grand Canyon is the most magnificent section of the Taihang Mountains, combining grandeur, peril, steepness, and beauty. The canyon is deep and long, flanked by towering cliffs and layered peaks, with babbling streams. In spring, the mountains bloom with flowers; in autumn, the forests are dyed in vibrant colors — each season has its own charm. This is not only a natural museum of geological wonders but also a paradise for photography enthusiasts and an excellent destination for outdoor hiking.

SPIRIT

Hongqiqu Spirit

Self-reliance, hard work, solidarity, and selfless dedication — the Hongqiqu Spirit is a precious spiritual treasure of the Chinese nation, inspiring generation after generation to forge ahead.

01

Self-Reliance

In an era without large machinery, the people of Lin County relied on hammers, drills, and their bare hands to carve a thousand-li canal along the Taihang mountainside, demonstrating an indomitable spirit of self-improvement.

02

Hard Work

Ten years of arduous struggle, with 100,000 workers camping on sheer cliffs, created a miracle in the history of water conservancy with the spirit of the Foolish Old Man who moved mountains.

03

Solidarity

The entire county worked in unison, with neighboring counties providing strong support, composing a magnificent ode to united struggle in those difficult years.
