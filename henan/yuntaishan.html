1.4 Billion Years of Geological Wonder

Yuntai Mountain is recognized as a World Geopark for its unique "Northern Karst Landform." The seabed crustal movements of 1.4 billion years ago created this extraordinary landscape of crimson cliffs and emerald waters.

As one of China's rare representatives of northern karst landforms, Yuntai Mountain preserves a complete record of hundreds of millions of years of Earth's evolution, serving as a natural museum for geological science.

/// 02

Crimson Cliffs & Emerald Gorge

Red Stone Gorge is the crown jewel of Yuntai Mountain. Within the 2,000-meter-long canyon, crimson Danxia cliffs reflect off crystal-clear emerald pools, creating a series of breathtaking natural paintings.

Waterfalls cascade through the gorge, and pools link one to another, with every step revealing a new vista. The gorge's unique beauty comes from iron-rich rocks that oxidize into magnificent red hues — a rare sight in northern China's landscape.

The Peak Immortalized in Poetry

Cornus Peak is the main summit of Yuntai Mountain, standing at 1,308 meters. Tang Dynasty poet Wang Wei once climbed this peak, leaving behind the timeless verse: "Alone, a lonely stranger in a foreign land, I doubly pine for my kinsfolk on holiday."

From the summit, you can gaze upon endless mountain ranges and rolling seas of clouds. Dense vegetation blankets the slopes; in autumn, dogwood berries blanket the hillsides in red, perfectly echoing the poet's imagery.
