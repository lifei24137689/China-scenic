Millennia of Grottoes

Longmen Grottoes are one of China's great stone carving treasure houses, together with Mogao Caves and Yungang Grottoes known as China's Three Great Grottoes. Construction began during the Northern Wei Dynasty when Emperor Xiaowen moved the capital to Luoyang (493 AD), and continued through the Eastern Wei, Western Wei, Northern Qi, Sui, Tang, Five Dynasties, and Song Dynasties, spanning over 400 years of continuous creation.

There are 2,345 extant niches, over 100,000 statues, and more than 2,890 inscriptions. Northern Wei statues account for 30%, while Tang Dynasty statues account for 60%. In November 2000, Longmen Grottoes were inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List.

Stone Carving Art

The Vairocana Buddha is the most representative work of Longmen Grottoes, built in the third year of Xianheng of Emperor Gaozong of Tang (672 AD), taking three years and nine months to complete. The Buddha stands 17.14 meters tall, with a head height of 4 meters and ear length of 1.9 meters. Its plump and elegant face has earned it the reputation of the "Mona Lisa of the East."

Fengxian Temple is the largest and most artistically exquisite cliff-side niche at Longmen, featuring a rigorously composed group of statues with clear hierarchy, embodying the glorious achievements of Buddhist art during the High Tang period. Guyang Cave and Binyang Cave, among other Northern Wei niches, are early treasures of Chinese Buddhist cave art.

Yi River Scenery

Longmen Grottoes are situated on Longmen Mountain and Xiangshan Mountain on both banks of the Yi River in Luoyang. The Yi River meanders between the two mountains, creating a natural spectacle of "two peaks facing each other with a river flowing between them." Longmen has been a natural gateway to the southern outskirts of Luoyang since ancient times, renowned for its scenic beauty and known as the "Splendid View of Longmen."

Xiangshan Temple was first built during the Northern Wei Dynasty. During the Tang Dynasty, Empress Wu Zetian left behind the celebrated story of "composing poetry at Xiangshan to win a brocade robe." Bai Juyi, the great poet, retired here in his later years and styled himself "Hermit of Xiangshan." Ascending Xiangshan Mountain, one can take in the panoramic view of the grottoes on both banks of the Yi River a magnificent spectacle where landscape and stone carvings complement each other.
