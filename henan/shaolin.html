I · Millennial Temple

Zen Buddhism's Cradle

Shaolin Temple, founded in 495 AD during the Northern Wei Dynasty, is the cradle of Chinese Zen Buddhism and the birthplace of Chinese Kung Fu, renowned as "The Number One Temple Under Heaven". It takes its name from its location deep within the forests of Shaoshi Mountain on Mount Song, with a history spanning over 1,500 years.

The Shaolin Temple complex is grand in scale, comprising seven courtyards from the Mountain Gate to the Thousand Buddha Hall, covering a total area of 57,600 square meters. The temple houses a vast collection of precious cultural relics, including murals, stone inscriptions, and Buddhist scriptures, all of immense historical and cultural value.

II · Shaolin Kung Fu

All Martial Arts from Shaolin

Shaolin martial arts are famed throughout the world "All Kung Fu Originates from Shaolin" has long been etched into the collective memory of the Chinese people. Shaolin Kung Fu is the most comprehensive school in Chinese martial arts, with over 700 distinct routines, integrating meditation, internal energy cultivation, and external physical training into one system.

The Pagoda Forest is a cluster of burial stupas for eminent Shaolin monks through the ages. Over 240 brick and stone pagodas from the Tang, Song, Jin, Yuan, Ming, and Qing dynasties survive today, making it the largest existing pagoda forest in China. Known as the "Museum of Ancient Pagodas," it bears witness to Shaolin's millennium-long legacy.

III · Mount Song Scenery

The Lofty Central Peak

Mount Song, anciently known as the "Central Peak," is the central of China's Five Great Mountains, stretching over 60 kilometers from east to west. It comprises Taishi Mountain and Shaoshi Mountain, with its highest peak reaching 1,512 meters. The geology of Mount Song primarily features ancient Archean, Proterozoic, and Paleozoic strata, celebrated as a "Five Generations Under One Roof" geological wonder.

Shaolin Temple sits nestled in the dense forests at the northern foot of Mount Song's Shaoshi Mountain, surrounded by layered peaks and rolling ranges. Historical sites such as Songyang Academy and Zhongyue Temple are scattered throughout the area, together forming Mount Song's rich tapestry of natural and cultural landscapes. Visiting in spring or autumn offers picturesque mountain scenery and pleasant weather.
