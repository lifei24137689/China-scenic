China's Four Great Summer Resorts

Jigongshan is one of China's four great summer resorts, alongside Lushan, Moganshan, and Beidaihe. Known as "Dividing Chu and Yu by its green, Outshining Song and Heng by its grandeur," it takes its name from its mountain shape resembling a rooster crowing skyward.

The average summer temperature on the mountain is only 23.7掳C, offering a cool and pleasant climate. For over a century, Jigongshan has been an ideal retreat for both Chinese and international visitors, earning the name "Park in the Clouds." With a dense forest coverage of 87% and extremely high levels of negative oxygen ions, it is a natural oxygen bar.

International Architecture Museum

Jigongshan preserves a collection of villas from the late Qing and early Republican eras in various national styles, earning it the title "Museum of International Architecture." These villas blend architectural styles from over ten countries including Britain, the United States, France, Germany, Russia, Japan, and Italy, making them living fossils of modern Chinese architectural history.

Over 300 villas remain standing, the most famous being Yilu, General's Villa, and the Sister Villas. Each villa carries a unique historical memory, bearing witness to the dramatic changes of modern Chinese history. Nestled among green mountains and lush trees, these buildings form a unique "International Architecture Exhibition" landscape.

Eight Great Natural Wonders

Jigongshan is renowned for its eight natural wonders: "Buddha's Light, Sea of Clouds, Rime, Glaze Ice, Dawn Glow, Exotic Flora, Strange Peaks and Rocks, and Waterfalls and Streams." Among them, Baoxiao Peak (Crowing Rooster Peak) is the iconic landmark, shaped like a rooster crowing at dawn, standing proudly above the surrounding peaks.

After rain clears, the sea of clouds surges like thousands of galloping horses, while Buddha's Light appears and disappears like a fairyland. In winter, rime and glaze ice transform the entire mountain into a crystal world. Changsheng Valley murmurs with mountain streams, Moon Lake ripples with clear waters, and Borden Forest Park towers with ancient trees, painting a moving landscape scroll.
