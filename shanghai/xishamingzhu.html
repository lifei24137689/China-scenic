Ecological Pearl

Ecological Pearl

Xisha Mingzhu Lake Scenic Area is located at the southwestern tip of Chongming Island, Shanghai — the world's largest estuary alluvial island. Covering a total area of 7.8 square kilometers, it integrates diverse natural landscapes including wetlands, lakes, forests, and marshes. On February 6, 2024, Xisha Mingzhu Lake was officially designated as a national 5A-level tourist attraction, becoming Shanghai's first national 5A scenic area defined by its ecological character.

The exceptional ecological environment keeps the air quality at the national Level 1 standard year-round, with negative oxygen ion concentrations exceeding 2,700 per cubic centimeter — making it a true natural oxygen bar.

Wetland Wonder

Wetland Wonder

Xisha Wetland is currently the only Yangtze River estuary tidal flat wetland in Shanghai with natural tidal phenomena, featuring unique tidal creek geological landscapes. With 2 major categories and 17 typical types of tidal creek landform relics, it bears witness to thousands of years of Chongming Island's formation history. The layered water forests, endless reed marshes, and well-arranged cedar groves create a unique visual tapestry at Xisha Wetland.

Visitors can walk along the ecological boardwalk through the landscape, experiencing the poetry of traveling within a painting. A total of 149 bird species and 67 fish species have been observed in the wetland, including the nationally protected Yangtze finless porpoise and the rare Reed Parrotbill, often called the "giant panda of the bird world."

Lake & Leisure

Lake & Leisure

Mingzhu Lake is Chongming's largest natural inland freshwater lake, stretching 3,000 meters from north to south and about 600 meters from east to west, with a maximum depth of 8 meters. The lake water is as clear as a mirror, reflecting the blue sky, white clouds, and verdant shores. The scenic area combines water recreation, eco-agricultural sightseeing, and competitive leisure sports, having hosted major international events such as the IRONMAN 70.3 World Triathlon and the UCI Women's World Tour.

Films such as "1911 Revolution" and the TV series "Boss & Me" were shot on location here. It has become the "backyard garden" for Shanghai residents' weekend getaways.
