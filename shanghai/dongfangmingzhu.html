01

Pearl of the Huangpu

The Oriental Pearl Radio & TV Tower stands at the tip of Lujiazui in Pudong New Area, Shanghai. Rising 468 meters, it was completed in 1994 and was among the first batch of National 5A Tourist Attractions in 2007. Eleven spheres of varying sizes cascade from the blue sky down to the green lawn, with two ruby-like spheres shining brilliantly. The design was inspired by the poetic imagery of "large and small pearls falling onto a jade plate" from Bai Juyi's "Song of the Pipa."

As Shanghai's iconic landmark, the Oriental Pearl has long transcended its original function as a broadcast tower, becoming the first stop for visitors worldwide to experience the charm of the Magic City.

02

Walking in the Clouds

The 259-meter fully transparent skywalk observation corridor is approximately 150 meters long and 2.15 meters wide, composed of 24 pieces of tempered glass. Standing on the transparent glass floor, you can see the bustling traffic and the waves of the Huangpu River beneath your feet, delivering a thrilling "cloud walk" experience.

The Oriental Pearl also complements the Bund's historic architecture across the Huangpu River, creating Shanghai's most iconic visual impression. Ascending the Oriental Pearl at night, you can overlook the dazzling lights along both sides of the river and feel the prosperity and romance of the Magic City.

03

Urban Living Room

The Shanghai Urban History Development Museum at the tower's base encapsulates the six-century transformation of Shanghai from a small fishing village into an international metropolis. The Shanghai International Convention Center, the Riverside Promenade, and the Bund Sightseeing Tunnel at the foot of the Oriental Pearl together form the core cultural tourism circle of Lujiazui.

The Oriental Pearl has become an important venue for Shanghai's cultural and creative industries, hosting various cultural exhibitions and art events each year, attracting over 5.2 million visitors annually.
