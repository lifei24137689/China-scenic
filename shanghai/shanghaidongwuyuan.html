Kingdom of Beasts

Shanghai Wild Animal Park is located in Xuanqiao Town, Pudong New Area, covering an area of 153 hectares. Officially opened to the public in November 1995 and rated as a National 5A Tourist Attraction in 2007, it is China's first national-level wildlife park. The park is home to over 200 species and more than 10,000 rare wild animals from both China and abroad, including giant pandas, golden snub-nosed monkeys, golden takins, crested ibises, giraffes, and cheetahs. It is a comprehensive themed park integrating animal exhibition, science education, and leisure entertainment.

Wild Encounters

Predator Crossing

The drive-through zone is one of the most distinctive areas of Shanghai Wild Animal Park. Visitors board specially designed sightseeing buses to traverse the lion zone, tiger zone, bear zone, and cheetah zone, experiencing the thrill of passing predators at close range. Here, lions lounge lazily on the grassland, tigers patrol under the shade of trees, and black bears stand up to beg for food. Through the bus windows, visitors can take in the real life of the wild kingdom, experiencing an unprecedented sense of immersion.

Cute Animal Interaction

The walking area is more gentle and fun. In the Giant Panda Hall, the national treasures leisurely munch on bamboo; on the Golden Snub-Nosed Monkey Island, agile monkeys dart and leap through the trees; on the Lemur Island, ring-tailed lemurs actively approach visitors with their adorable charm. In the Water Exploration area, visitors can take a boat to watch flamingos, pelicans, and other water birds. At the Hundred Beasts Mountain Performance Arena, animal stars deliver exciting athletic shows. This is not only a paradise for animals but also an excellent destination for family trips, receiving over 2.8 million visitors annually.
