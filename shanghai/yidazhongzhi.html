Shikumen Memories

The site of the First CPC Congress is located at 76 Xingye Road, Shanghai, in a typical Shikumen building. In 1921, delegates from across the country gathered here, witnessing a pivotal moment in modern Chinese history. The memorial hall was completed in 1952, making it one of the earliest revolutionary memorials established after the founding of New China, and was rated a national 5A tourist attraction in 2021. This Shikumen building, with its grey bricks, dark tiles, and red lintels, carries profound historical memories, drawing visitors from all over the country to pay homage.

Red Footprints

The site of the Second CPC Congress is located at No. 30, Lane 7, North Laochengdu Road, Shanghai, where the congress was held in 1922. The site of the Fourth CPC Congress is located on Dongbaoxing Road, Hongkou District, where it was held in 1925. These three sites together form an important thread in Shanghai's red culture. Each site preserves its original architectural style and, through carefully designed exhibitions, tells visitors the story of those momentous years. The Shikumen building complex of the Second Congress site and the neighborhood of the Fourth Congress site are both vital parts of Shanghai's urban history.

Precious Relics

The three memorial halls feature rich exhibitions of cultural relics, photographs, charts, and multimedia interactive displays. The collections include over a hundred precious artifacts, including the first complete Chinese translation of The Communist Manifesto and other historically significant documents. Using modern exhibition technology, the memorials recreate the meeting scenes and everyday details of a century ago, offering visitors an immersive experience of historic moments inside Shanghai's Shikumen buildings. The memorials receive over a million visitors annually and are a key destination for Shanghai's red culture tourism.
