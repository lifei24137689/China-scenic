Temple of Science

Located on Century Avenue in Pudong New Area, the Shanghai Science & Technology Museum covers a floor area of 100,600 square meters. Opened in 2001 and designated a National 5A Tourist Attraction in 2010, it is one of China's largest comprehensive science museums. Guided by the core philosophy of "Nature · Humanity · Technology," it offers rich interactive exhibits and immersive experiences that let visitors feel the charm of science through exploration. With an annual visitor volume exceeding 2.2 million, it stands as a benchmark for science popularization in China.

SCIENCE & WONDER

Exploration Without Boundaries

The museum houses 11 permanent exhibition halls, including the World of Biology, Earth Exploration, Light of Wisdom, Robot World, and Aerospace Universe, with over 90% of exhibits being interactive experiences. In Robot World, visitors can watch robots dance and play chess; in the Aerospace Universe, they can simulate space flight and experience weightlessness; in the Light of Wisdom hall, fun experiments reveal the mysteries of sound, light, electricity, and magnetism. The IMAX giant screen theater and dome theater deliver stunning audiovisual impact, making science accessible and tangible.

Learning Through Play

The Shanghai Science & Technology Museum is not only a base for science education but also a top choice for family outings. The Animal World exhibition area vividly recreates ecological scenes such as the African savanna and tropical rainforests; the Earth Exploration area showcases dinosaur fossils and the mysteries of geological change. The museum regularly hosts various science events and special exhibitions, allowing visitors of all ages to find their own scientific joy here. In 2025, its "Family Science" related topics across the internet garnered over 3.5 billion reads.
