Millennium of the Great Pass

Jiayuguan, first built in the fifth year of Hongwu reign of the Ming Dynasty (1372 AD), stands as the western terminus of the Ming Great Wall and the best-preserved, most magnificent military fortress along its entire length. Situated at the narrowest point of the Hexi Corridor, flanked by the Qilian Mountains to the south and the Black Mountain to the north, its strategic location has made it a coveted military stronghold since ancient times.

After more than six centuries of wind and rain, Jiayuguan still stands majestically. From the pass, one can gaze upon the snow-capped Qilian Mountains, the vast Gobi Desert, and almost hear the camel bells of the Silk Road echoing through time. This "First Pass Under Heaven" bears witness to the resilience and wisdom of the Chinese nation.

Great Wall Wonder

The Jiayuguan Scenic Area comprises the Pass itself, the First Mound of the Great Wall, and the Overhanging Great Wall. Built along the ridge of the Black Mountain, the Overhanging Great Wall is steep and precipitous, a remarkable example of Ming Dynasty engineering. The First Mound stands by the Taolai River as the westernmost beacon tower of the Great Wall.

Each of these three sites has its unique character, together forming Jiayuguan's complete military defense system. In 2007, it was rated a National 5A Tourist Attraction, and in 1987, it was inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List as a precious treasure of human civilization.

Silk Road Legacy

Jiayuguan was not only a military stronghold but also a vital hub on the ancient Silk Road. Westward beyond the pass lay the vast expanse of the Western Regions; eastward through the pass led to the prosperous heartland of the Central Plains. Merchants, envoys, and monks from all directions once gathered here, where Eastern and Western civilizations converged and blended.

Today, Jiayuguan shines as a brilliant gem on the Silk Road tourism route. In 2024, it received 3.80 million visitors from around the world, all drawn to experience the grandeur of this great pass and the depth of its history. Within and beyond the fortress, camel bells and train whistles resonate together — a dialogue between ancient and modern.
