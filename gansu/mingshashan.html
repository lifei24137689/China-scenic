Where Sand Meets Water — A Desert Wonder

Located in Dunhuang, Gansu Province, Mingshashan and Crescent Moon Spring are world-renowned for the unique spectacle of "sand and water coexisting." Mingshashan gets its name from the sound of shifting sands, while Crescent Moon Spring lies embedded in the desert like a crescent moon, never drying up for millennia.

This site merges the majesty of the desert with the tenderness of a spring, shining as a brilliant pearl on the Silk Road, celebrated as "the finest scenery beyond the Great Wall."

/// 02

A Brilliant Jewel of the Silk Road

Crescent Moon Spring stretches nearly 100 meters north-south and about 25 meters east-west, with water deepening from east to west, reaching about 5 meters at its deepest. It is home to unique species such as iron-back fish and seven-star grass, surrounded by picturesque poplars and tamarisk shrubs.

Designated a National Scenic Area in 1994, Mingshashan and Crescent Moon Spring attained 5A-level scenic status in 2015, welcoming over 4.2 million visitors in 2024.

Ecological Protection of Sand & Spring

The core scenic area covers 12.79 km², with the main peak of Mingshashan reaching 1,715 meters. Multiple ecological protection measures have been implemented to maintain stable water levels at Crescent Moon Spring and preserve the integrity of the sand dunes.

Through scientific ecological restoration and water resource management, this marvel of "sand and water in harmony" continues to endure, leaving a precious natural and cultural legacy for future generations.
