Millennia-Old Grottoes · Silk Road Treasure

Bingling Temple Grottoes were first constructed in the first year of the Jianhong era of the Western Qin (420 AD), serving as an important Buddhist cultural heritage site along the Silk Road. There are 216 existing caves, over 800 statues, and more than 1,000 square meters of murals, earning it the reputation of "The Encyclopedia of Chinese Grottoes."

The grottoes are distributed along the red sandstone cliffs on the banks of the Yellow River. They underwent continuous excavation and restoration through more than ten dynasties including Western Qin, Northern Wei, Northern Zhou, Sui, Tang, Song, Yuan, Ming, and Qing, forming a magnificent grotto art treasury of grand scale, rich content, and diverse styles.

Stone Forest Wonder · Nature's Masterpiece

The Bingling Stone Forest coexists with the grottoes, forming a spectacular Danxia landform composed of red conglomerate sandstone. With thousands of varied peaks, stone pillars, and stone spires stretching along both sides of the Yellow River, it resembles a natural sculpture art palace.

The stone forest area and the grotto cluster together form the core landscape of the Bingling Temple World Cultural Heritage Tourist Area. Unique peaks and strange rocks complement the Buddhist grottoes, achieving a perfect fusion of natural beauty and cultural heritage.

#StoneForest #DanxiaLandform #NaturalWonder

Bingling Lake · Pearl of the Yellow River

Bingling Lake is a brilliant pearl on the upper reaches of the Yellow River, a highland plain lake formed by the impoundment of the Liujiaxia Reservoir. Its crystal clear blue waters reflect the Danxia peaks on both sides, creating a magnificent picture of "a high gorge yielding a peaceful lake."

Taking a boat tour of Bingling Lake, visitors can view the grotto cliffs from afar and observe the rippling blue waves up close, experiencing the profound grandeur of Yellow River civilization amidst mountains and waters. Lake, mountains, and grottoes form a trinity, creating this unique world heritage site.
