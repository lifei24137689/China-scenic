/// 01

Nature's Way, a Sacred Mountain Wonderland

Located in Pingliang City, Gansu Province, Kongtong Mountain is one of the birthplaces of Taoism and is honored as "The Foremost Taoist Mountain of China." According to legend, the Yellow Emperor once came here to seek the Way from Guangchengzi, initiating the millennia-long heritage of Chinese Taoist culture. With its layered peaks and swirling clouds, it has been celebrated since ancient times as the "First Mountain from the West."

Covering an area of 84 square kilometers, the main peak rises to an altitude of 2,123 meters, with a forest coverage rate of over 95%. Ancient temples stand proudly among the mountains, where natural beauty and cultural heritage complement each other, making it an ideal place for spiritual cultivation and the pursuit of the Tao.

/// 02

A Surging Sea of Clouds, a Spectacular Wonder

Kongtong Mountain sits at the junction of Shaanxi, Gansu, and Ningxia provinces, a unique geographical location that produces an ever-changing sea of clouds. In early mornings or after rain, mist rises and clouds surge through the mountains, with peaks appearing and disappearing among the cloud waves like a fairyland.

The mountain offers stunning scenery in all seasons: blooming flowers in spring, lush green canopies in summer, colorful forests in autumn, and a silver-clad world in winter. The best time to visit is at the turn of summer and autumn, when the sea of clouds is at its most spectacular, and the sunrise paints the sky with countless rays of golden light — a breathtaking sight.

/// 03

Millennium Heritage, a Journey of Seeking Tao

Kongtong Mountain is renowned not only for its natural scenery but also as a mountain of culture. Since the Yellow Emperor's quest for the Tao, generations of literati, scholars, and Taoist masters have journeyed here. The mountain preserves numerous Taoist temples, cliffside inscriptions, and ancient architectural complexes, with the Kongtong Mountain ancient building complex being the most spectacular.

From January to September 2025, Kongtong Mountain received a total of 1,048,800 visitors, with tourism revenue continuing to climb. The scenic area's smart management and services have been continuously improving, offering visitors a more convenient and comfortable experience.
