Unique Peaks & Strange Rocks · Crystal Waters & Quiet Forests

Located in Qionglai City, Chengdu, Sichuan, Tiantai Mountain is an important part of the Sichuan Giant Panda Sanctuaries World Natural Heritage. Known for "unique peaks, strange rocks, crystal waters, and quiet forests," the area features layered mountains, grotesque rocks, babbling streams, and lush woodlands, creating a natural landscape painting.

With an average summer temperature of only 23°C, it is an excellent summer retreat. Tiantai Mountain has an elevation of 1,812 meters, a total scenic area of 29.1 km², a forest coverage rate of over 95%, and extremely high negative oxygen ion levels in the air, earning it the reputation of "Chengdu's Backyard Garden."

Xiangshuitan · Panlong Waterfall

The Tiantai Mountain waterfall cluster is one of the most magnificent natural wonders in the area. Xiangshuitan Waterfall drops over 60 meters with a width of about 15 meters, like the Milky Way cascading down, its roar echoing through the valley and audible from miles away — hence its name "Resounding Waters Beach." Panlong Waterfall winds and twists, with water surging between rocks, resembling a coiled dragon leaping forth in majestic splendor.

The two waterfalls — one bold, one gentle, one tall, one wide — complement each other beautifully. After rain, the waterfalls are even more spectacular, with mist filling the air and rainbows often arching across in sunlight, a breathtaking sight. There are also Xiaomofang Waterfall, Huashihai Waterfall, and others, forming a rare waterfall cluster landscape.

Asia's Largest Firefly Viewing Base

Tiantai Mountain is Asia's largest firefly viewing base. From April to October each year, tens of thousands of fireflies dance under the night sky, transforming the forest into a dreamlike fairy-tale world. With over 20 species of fireflies, Tiantai Mountain boasts such abundance and variety that it is known as the "Kingdom of Fireflies."

The best viewing period is summer, from June to August. After nightfall on Tiantai Mountain, streams and grasslands sparkle with firefly lights like falling stars dancing in the air. Strolling along the firefly viewing trail, surrounded by countless twinkling lights, feels like wandering through the Milky Way — an intoxicating and enchanting experience.

Monk's Temple

A Buddhist monastery first built in the Tang Dynasty, it is one of the oldest temples on Tiantai Mountain. With ancient trees reaching skyward and solemn halls within, it was once a place where eminent monks preached the Dharma. After a thousand years of history, its incense still burns continuously.

Leiyin Temple

A thousand-year-old temple perched atop Tiantai Mountain's main peak, named for the frequent thunderclaps at the summit. Surrounded by rolling seas of clouds and magnificent sunrises, visitors can overlook the endless mountain ranges and feel the vastness of heaven and earth — an excellent destination for pilgrimage and sightseeing.

Panda Habitat

As part of the Sichuan Giant Panda Sanctuaries World Natural Heritage, Tiantai Mountain is an important habitat for giant pandas and other rare flora and fauna. The dense bamboo groves and primeval forests provide an ideal living environment for wild giant pandas.
