Low-Altitude Glacial Wonder

Nestled at the foot of Mount Gongga in Garze Tibetan Autonomous Prefecture, Sichuan, Hailuogou sits at an elevation of approximately 2,850 meters and covers a scenic area of 350 square kilometers. It is world-renowned for its low-altitude modern glacier, which stretches 14.7 kilometers long and extends 6 kilometers into the primeval forest, creating a rare wonder where glacier and forest coexist.

The Great Ice Waterfall measures 1,100 meters wide and 1,080 meters high, making it the tallest ice waterfall in China. Here, glaciers, hot springs, and primeval forests blend in perfect harmony, earning it the title "Glacial Wonder at the Foot of Mount Gongga" — an unparalleled destination for experiencing a modern glacier up close.

Three Wonders · Hailuogou

Hailuogou's Three Wonders — hot springs, Golden Sunset Peaks, and glacier cascade — each offer breathtaking beauty. The Gongga Hot Springs have vent temperatures reaching 92°C, offering an unforgettable experience of soaking in thermal waters amidst snow and ice — an ultimate contrast of fire and ice. When the first rays of dawn fall upon the snowy peaks of Mount Gongga, the entire mountain range is bathed in gold — this is the famous "Golden Sunset Peaks".

The Great Ice Waterfall is China's tallest ice waterfall, measuring 1,100 meters wide with a drop of 1,080 meters. From afar, it resembles the Milky Way cascading down; up close, crisscrossing ice crevices glow with an ethereal blue under sunlight. The glacier tongue extends deep into the forest, its surface covered with moraine debris, creating a unique glacial landscape.

Glacier-Forest Symbiosis

Hailuogou boasts a complete vertical natural spectrum, from subtropical evergreen broadleaf forests in river valleys at 1,500 meters above sea level to alpine scree slopes at 4,800 meters — a vertical drop of 3,300 meters. The area harbors a wealth of rare flora and fauna, with ancient relict plants such as yew and katsura thriving here, while rare animals like giant pandas and takins are occasionally spotted.

Winter is the best season for glacier viewing, when the ice is at its most spectacular, with snowscapes and glaciers complementing each other. In summer, Hailuogou becomes a summer retreat, with lush primeval forests, cascading waterfalls, and cool, refreshing air. Each season has its own charm, and Hailuogou draws countless visitors with its rich and diverse natural landscapes.
