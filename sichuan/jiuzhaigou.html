/// 01

Earthly Paradise · Fairy Tale World

Jiuzhaigou is located in Zhangzha Town, Jiuzhaigou County, Aba Tibetan and Qiang Autonomous Prefecture, at the southern section of the Minshan Mountains in northwestern Sichuan, approximately 400 km from Chengdu. Covering a total area of about 651 km², it is named after the nine Tibetan villages scattered throughout the valley. Situated in the transition zone between the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau and the Sichuan Basin, with elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,500 meters, the distinct four-season plateau humid climate nurtures rare and unparalleled natural wonders.

In 1992, Jiuzhaigou was inscribed on the UNESCO World Natural Heritage List. In 2007, it was rated as a National 5A-level Tourist Attraction. It is renowned for its "Six Wonders" — turquoise lakes, layered waterfalls, colorful forests, snow-capped peaks, blue ice, and Tibetan culture — and is hailed as the "King of Waterscapes" and "Paradise on Earth."

Y-Shaped Three Valleys · Each with Its Own Charm

Jiuzhaigou Scenic Area is laid out in a "Y" shape, consisting of three main valleys: Shuzheng Valley, Rize Valley, and Zechawa Valley. Rize Valley stretches 18 km and is the most densely scenic area, featuring world-class attractions such as Five Flower Lake, Pearl Shoal Waterfall, Mirror Lake, and Panda Lake. Zechawa Valley has the highest elevation and is home to Jiuzhaigou's largest lake — Long Lake (elevation 3,101 m) — along with the most brilliantly colored Five Color Pond.

Shuzheng Valley is the first valley entered. Nuorilang Waterfall, 320 meters wide, is the widest high-altitude calcified waterfall in China, cascading like a river of stars. Shuzheng Cluster Lakes consists of 19 interconnected terraced lakes, presenting a unique landscape of "trees growing in water, water flowing through forests."

/// 03

Water in Splendor · Unrivaled Beauty

Five Flower Lake is the crown jewel of Jiuzhaigou. With calcified deposits, algae, and minerals interwoven in the water, it displays a gradient of blue, green, yellow, and orange — as brilliant as a peacock in full display. Pearl Shoal Waterfall, 270 m wide with an average drop of 21 m, sends water cascading over calcified terraces, splashing countless droplets like scattered pearls. It was the filming location for the ending scene of the 1986 classic TV series "Journey to the West."

Long Lake is Jiuzhaigou's largest and deepest alpine lake, stretching 4.3 km. Surrounded by snow-capped peaks and forests, it has no outlet yet never overflows or dries up, earning it the nickname "the ever-full, never-leaking gourd." Mirror Lake, perfectly still on windless mornings, reflects the surrounding snowy peaks and forests with stunning clarity and is hailed as the "King of Reflections in Jiuzhaigou."
