Qiang City Reborn

Beichuan Qiang City Tourist Area is located in Beichuan Qiang Autonomous County, Mianyang City, Sichuan Province. It is a national-level memorial scenic area rebuilt after the Great Wenchuan Earthquake. The scenic area consists of the old county town earthquake ruins, the May 12 Wenchuan Earthquake Memorial Museum, the new Beichuan county town, the Qiang Folk Museum, and the Banaqia Commercial Street, combining remembrance, cultural heritage, tourism, and leisure.

This is the only Qiang Autonomous County in China and an important center for Qiang cultural heritage. The melodious Qiang flute, vibrant Qiang embroidery, and majestic watchtowers the ancient Qiang civilization radiates new vitality and brilliance on this reborn land, showcasing to the world the indomitable spirit of the people of Beichuan.

Remember Rebirth

The old Beichuan county town earthquake ruins preserve the original appearance of the Great Wenchuan Earthquake, making it one of the most complete and largest earthquake disaster sites in the world. The May 12 Wenchuan Earthquake Memorial Museum is located beside the ruins, displaying a vast collection of earthquake artifacts and materials, recreating the tragedy of that disaster and the great human love that emerged.

From earthquake relief to post-disaster reconstruction, from shattered mountains and rivers to a beautiful new homeland, every step of Beichuan embodies the profound friendship of people across the nation. This place is not only a deep remembrance of the departed but also a supreme tribute to the unyielding spirit of humanity in the face of disaster.

Qiang Soul Eternal

The Qiang Folk Museum is an excellent window into Qiang culture. It houses a large collection of precious Qiang artifacts, from ancient Qiang flutes and exquisite Qiang embroidery to unique watchtower architectural models, comprehensively showcasing the long history and splendid civilization of the Qiang people. The Qiang flute has been listed as a national intangible cultural heritage, its tone desolate and far-reaching, carrying the thousand-year memory of the Qiang people.

Banaqia Commercial Street is a landmark district of the new Beichuan county town, featuring traditional Qiang architectural style, bringing together Qiang embroidery, local specialties, and distinctive dining. Strolling through it, the rich Qiang ethnic atmosphere greets you at every turn, with every detail telling the cultural confidence and contemporary rebirth of this ancient people.
