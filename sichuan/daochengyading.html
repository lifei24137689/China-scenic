Sacred Peaks & Holy Lakes

Xiannairi (6,032m), the highest peak in Daocheng Yading, means "Guan Yin Bodhisattva" in Tibetan towering, majestic, and eternally snow-covered. Yangmaiyong (5,958m) resembles Manjushri's sword of wisdom, with ridges as sharp as blades. Xianuoduoji (5,958m) takes the form of Vajrapani Bodhisattva, radiating immense power.

Milk Lake's waters appear milky white due to its rich mineral content, nestled among snowy peaks like a lustrous pearl. Five-Color Lake is famous for reflecting five hues as sunlight refracts through underwater plants and minerals. Pearl Lake glows like emerald jade, mirroring the majestic form of Xiannairi.

This is also a sacred land of Tibetan culture, where Tibetan people have guarded these holy peaks and lakes for generations. Mountain circumambulation, incense burning, and prayer flag hanging ancient traditions of faith that have endured for millennia on this high plateau adding profound cultural depth to this pure land.
