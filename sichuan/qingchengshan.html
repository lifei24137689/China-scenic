The Quietest Mountain Under Heaven

Mount Qingcheng is located in Dujiangyan City, Chengdu, Sichuan Province. With its surrounding peaks rising in succession and lush, verdant forests, it has been renowned since ancient times as "the quietest mountain under heaven." The entire mountain is covered with emerald greenery that lasts all year round, with peaks standing in a circle like a city wall — hence the name Qingcheng (Green City).

Mount Qingcheng is one of the birthplaces of Chinese Taoism and the ancestral seat of the Way of the Celestial Masters. During the Eastern Han Dynasty, Zhang Daoling built a thatched hut here to preach and founded the earliest Taoist organization — the Way of the Celestial Masters. Since then, Mount Qingcheng has become a sacred Taoist site, with incense burning for thousands of years and the Taoist lineage continuing unbroken.

Dujiangyan · A Thousand Years of Water

Dujiangyan is located on the Min River west of the Chengdu Plain. First built in 256 BC, it is a large-scale irrigation project constructed by Li Bing, the governor of Shu Commandery of the Qin State during the Warring States period, together with his son. For over two thousand years, Dujiangyan has served flood control and irrigation purposes, turning the Chengdu Plain into the bountiful "Land of Abundance."

The Dujiangyan irrigation system is characterized by its "diversion without a dam" design, consisting of three major components: the Fish Mouth, the Flying Sand Weir, and the Precious Bottleneck Channel. Its ingenious design and scientific layout continue to astonish water experts from around the world. In 2000, Mount Qingcheng-Dujiangyan was inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List.

Ancient Temples & Timeless Charm

The Celestial Masters Cave, also known as Changdao Temple, is the most important Taoist temple on Mount Qingcheng, located halfway up the mountain. Legend has it that this was the cultivation site of Celestial Master Zhang. Inside the cave are statues of Zhang Daoling and his 30th-generation descendant Zhang Jixian. The temple is built into the mountainside with upturned eaves, nestled among ancient pines and emerald bamboos, exuding an air of antiquity and tranquility.

Shangqing Temple stands at the highest peak of Mount Qingcheng, at an elevation of approximately 1,260 meters. It is the best place to witness three major natural wonders: sunrise, a sea of clouds, and sacred lights. Within the temple are historic sites such as Magu Pond and Yuanyang Well. In front of the temple stands the Sun-Gazing Pavilion, where visitors can look out over the dark blue mountain ranges and the ribbon-like Min River — a scene both magnificent and serene.
