Hometown of the Marshal

Zhu De's hometown is located in Yilong County, Nanchong City, Sichuan Province. It is the birthplace and childhood home of Marshal Zhu De, one of the founding fathers of the People's Republic of China. The scenic area includes his former residence, his birth site, and the Zhu De Memorial Hall, fully presenting his legendary journey from a farm boy to a Marshal of the Republic.

The former residence is a typical Qing-dynasty residential building of northern Sichuan style, constructed with timber and earth, featuring black-tiled roofs. Surrounded by lush green hills and picturesque rural landscapes, the site was officially designated a National 5A Tourist Attraction in 2016, becoming an important revolutionary tourism destination and patriotic education base.

Zhu De Memorial Hall

Located to the south of Zhu De's former residence, the Zhu De Memorial Hall houses a vast collection of precious historical artifacts, photographs, and archival documents, vividly recreating the glorious life of Marshal Zhu De. From the Xinhai Revolution and the Nanchang Uprising to the War of Resistance Against Japan and the War of Liberation, every chapter of history bears the wisdom and courage of this great military strategist.

The hall employs modern multimedia presentation techniques, combining scene reenactments, lighting, and sound effects to create an immersive experience that transports visitors back in time to witness those magnificent revolutionary years. The memorial hall is not only a place to pay tribute to a great leader but also a spiritual high ground for passing on the red legacy.

Northern Sichuan Residence · Ding's Manor

Ding's Manor is the best-preserved cluster of northern Sichuan residential buildings within the Zhu De hometown scenic area. Built during the Qing dynasty, it covers several acres and comprises dozens of rooms. Built against the mountain, the manor features a rigorous layout with richly carved beams and painted rafters, showcasing the exquisite craftsmanship and unique style of traditional architecture in northern Sichuan.

Walking through the manor, one encounters blue stone paths, wooden doors and windows, and flying eaves with upturned corners — every detail exuding antiquity and solemnity. This site is not only a treasure of architectural art but also an excellent window into the folk culture and traditional lifestyle of northern Sichuan.

✦ The Great Man's Journey ✦

1886

Zhu De was born into a poor tenant farmer family in Lijiawan, Ma'an Town, Yilong County, Sichuan Province. From childhood, he worked in the fields and experienced the hardships of the common people, cultivating a resilient and down-to-earth character.

1909

Zhu De was admitted to the Yunnan Military Academy and joined the Tongmenghui (Chinese United League) the same year, embarking on the revolutionary path. He participated in the Xinhai Revolution, the National Protection War, and the Constitution Protection War, distinguishing himself in the Yunnan Army.

1928

Zhu De joined forces with Mao Zedong on Jinggangshan, forming the Fourth Army of the Chinese Workers' and Peasants' Red Army, with Zhu De serving as its commander. Together with Mao, he explored the revolutionary path of "encircling the cities from the countryside and seizing power by armed force," and their forces became famously known as the "Zhu-Mao Red Army."

1949

After the founding of the People's Republic of China, Zhu De served successively as Vice Chairman of the Central People's Government, Commander-in-Chief of the People's Liberation Army, and Chairman of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress. In 1955, he was conferred the rank of Marshal of the People's Republic of China, ranking first among the Ten Marshals.
