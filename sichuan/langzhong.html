Langyuan Fairyland

Langzhong Ancient City, located in Nanchong, Sichuan, is one of China's four great ancient cities, with over 2,300 years of history. Surrounded by mountains on four sides and water on three, the Jialing River winds around the city in a "U" shape, creating a unique landscape where mountains, water, and city coexist in harmony — hailed by ancients as "Langyuan Fairyland on Earth".

Since ancient times, Langzhong has been regarded as a blessed fengshui site. Its city site was chosen following the fengshui principle of "Azure Dragon on the left, White Tiger on the right, Vermilion Bird in front, Black Tortoise behind," making it a model of fengshui theory in ancient Chinese urban planning. It is renowned as the "Fengshui Ancient City." The Tang Dynasty painting sage Wu Daozi once painted "Langzhong Scroll," leaving behind a masterpiece for the ages.

Historical Relics

Zhang Fei Temple, also known as Han Huanhou Shrine, is a memorial temple dedicated to Zhang Fei, the famed general of the Three Kingdoms. Zhang Fei guarded Langzhong for seven years and was deeply loved by the people. Built during the Ming Dynasty and well-preserved, the temple features magnificent halls, a statue of Zhang Fei, and a wealth of Three Kingdoms cultural relics, making it one of Langzhong's most representative historical sites.

The Examination Hall, also known as Sichuan Examination Hall or Langzhong Examination Hall, was the venue for provincial civil service examinations during the Qing Dynasty. It is one of the best-preserved imperial examination sites in China. The complex retains buildings such as the Mingyuan Tower, Zhigong Hall, and Jianlin Hall, allowing visitors to travel back in time to the imperial examination era and experience the legacy of millennia of scholarly culture.

Towers and Elegance

Huaguang Tower, the iconic landmark of Langzhong Ancient City, was first built during the Tang Dynasty and stands on the southern street of the old city. The three-story tower features soaring eaves and a majestic presence. From the top, the Jialing River appears like a silk ribbon, the ancient city's blue-tiled roofs stretch in neat rows, and the distant mountains loom in misty blue — it is the best vantage point for panoramic photos of the ancient city.

Zhongtian Tower sits at the center of the ancient city's cross-shaped intersection, echoing Huaguang Tower from afar. With its three-tiered eaves and exquisite design, it is one of the highest points in the old city. The area around Zhongtian Tower is open in all directions, lined with shops, making it an excellent spot to experience the vibrant local life of Langzhong. From its top, the full splendor of the ancient city unfolds before your eyes.

Langzhong is the birthplace of Spring Festival culture. The Western Han astronomer Luo Xiahong created the "Taichu Calendar" here, establishing the first calendar system that designated the first lunar month as the beginning of the year, giving birth to the most important traditional Chinese festival — the Spring Festival. The Spring Festival Culture Theme Park, centered on Luo Xiahong, showcases the historical origins and folk heritage of Spring Festival culture.
