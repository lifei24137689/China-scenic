Lingyun Giant Buddha

Leshan Giant Buddha is located in the central district of Leshan City, Sichuan Province, at the confluence of the Min River, Qingyi River, and Dadu River. It was carved into the cliff face of Qixia Peak on Mount Lingyun. The Buddha stands 71 meters tall, with a head height of 14.7 meters, head width of 10 meters, ear length of 7 meters, and instep width of 8.5 meters and length of 11 meters, making it the largest extant stone seated Maitreya Buddha in the world.

The construction of the Great Buddha began in the first year of the Kaiyuan era of the Tang Dynasty (713 AD). It was initiated by the eminent monk Haitong to tame the floodwaters and protect sentient beings. The project spanned three generations of craftsmen and took approximately 90 years, finally completed in the nineteenth year of the Zhenyuan era (803 AD). It stands as a masterpiece of ancient Chinese stone carving art.

Millennium Buddhist Chant

The Leshan Giant Buddha sits majestically by the river in a full "bhadrasana" (seated with feet on the ground) posture, hands resting on its knees, with a dignified and solemn appearance. Its design is exquisite — the entire statue features a drainage system hidden within the garment folds and hair bun, allowing rainwater to flow down naturally, effectively reducing the erosion of the stone statue over millennia of wind and rain, showcasing the extraordinary architectural wisdom of Tang Dynasty craftsmen.

After completion, a thirteen-story wooden pavilion (the Great Image Pavilion) was built to shelter the Buddha. It was called "Lingyun Pavilion" in the Tang Dynasty and "Tianning Pavilion" in the Song Dynasty, but was unfortunately destroyed at the end of the Ming Dynasty. Today, the Great Buddha sits in the open air, blending seamlessly with Mount Lingyun and the blue waters of the three rivers, becoming an eternal symbol of the perfect fusion of Chinese Buddhist culture and natural landscapes.

Buddhist Sacred Realm

Lingyun Temple, also known as "Great Buddha Temple," stands beside the Giant Buddha. Built during the Tang Dynasty, it is one of the most famous ancient temples in Leshan. The temple grounds are filled with towering ancient trees and the resonant sound of Buddhist chanting, together forming a sacred Buddhist site on Mount Lingyun alongside the Great Buddha. The Nine-Turn Plank Path spirals down from the Buddha's head to its feet, with over 250 steps, offering views of the Buddha's full grandeur and the magnificent confluence of the three rivers along the way.

The Mahao Cliff Tombs are outstanding representatives of Leshan's Han Dynasty cliff burial complex, rich in exquisite stone carvings and regarded as the "Han Dynasty Stone Carving Art Corridor." The Giant Sleeping Buddha is a natural wonder discovered in 1989 — viewed from the Binjiang Road in Leshan city, Mount Wuyou, Mount Lingyun, and Mount Guicheng form a colossal sleeping Buddha approximately 4,000 meters long, with the Leshan Giant Buddha seated precisely at the heart of the sleeping Buddha — a unity of heaven and man that inspires awe.
