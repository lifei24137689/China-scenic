Fairy Pool on Earth

Huanglong is located in the Aba Tibetan and Qiang Autonomous Prefecture of Sichuan Province, at an elevation of approximately 3,500 meters, covering an area of 700 square kilometers. It is world-famous for its magnificent and uniquely structured travertine color pool clusters, earning the title "Fairy Pool on Earth."

The layered pools spread across the valley like terraced fields, their waters shimmering in shades of emerald green, sapphire blue, and golden yellow under the sunlight, as if nature's own palette. In 1992, Huanglong was inscribed on the UNESCO World Natural Heritage List.

Five Color Ponds

The Five Color Ponds are the core attraction of Huanglong, consisting of hundreds of pools of varying sizes with brilliantly colored, ever-changing waters. Due to differences in mineral content and water depth, the pools display a spectrum of blue, green, yellow, and orange hues under sunlight.

The pools are interconnected layer by layer, their winding rims resembling scattered jade beads. In autumn, the waters reflect brilliantly against the surrounding colorful forests, creating a breathtaking landscape painting.

Golden Sand on Earth

The Golden Sand on Earth is Huanglong's most spectacular travertine flow landscape, stretching 1,300 meters in length and up to 170 meters wide, making it one of the world's largest. Calcium-carbonate-rich water flows down the slope, shimmering with a golden luster under the sun, like a golden dragon lying across the valley.

The water forms countless delicate ripples and scale-like patterns on the travertine surface, creating a striking contrast with the surrounding green forests and blue sky. Scenic spots such as Yingbin Pool, Huanglong Temple, and Xuebaoding Peak are scattered throughout the area, each with its unique charm.
