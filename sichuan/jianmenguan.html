Jianmen the World's Peril

Located in Jiange County, Guangyuan City, Sichuan Province, Jianmen Pass guards the Golden Ox Ancient Road and stands as the most treacherous fortress on the Shu Road system. The cliffs on both sides are as if carved by knives and axes, with seventy-two peaks thrusting skyward like sharp swords, forming a natural defensive barrier. The Tang dynasty poet Li Bai lamented in his renowned poem "The Difficulty of Shu Roads": "The Sword Pavilion towers steep and sheer — one man defends the pass, ten thousand cannot break it." This timeless verse captures the essence of Jianmen's peril.

Jianmen Pass sits at the break of the Great Sword Mountain. The pass is about 20 meters wide and 50 meters long, with rock walls on both sides piercing the clouds. The pass tower stands proudly in between, commanding a sweeping vantage — truly a heavenly barrier where "one general guards the pass, ten thousand cannot cross." Ascending the pass and gazing into the distance, the mountains roll like ocean waves, mist and clouds stretch endlessly, and the full peril of the Shu Road unfolds before your eyes.

Three Kingdoms Era

The history of Jianmen Pass is inseparable from the Three Kingdoms period. Zhuge Liang, Chancellor of Shu Han, recognized the precipitous terrain and established a garrison here, making it a vital stronghold for Shu Han to defend against Cao Wei from the north. Zhuge Liang launched five Northern Expeditions, all using Jianmen Pass as his headquarters, with provisions and military supplies transported through this route.

In 263 AD (the sixth year of Jingyao), Wei general Zhong Hui led a hundred-thousand-strong army to invade Shu. Shu general Jiang Wei retreated to Jianmen Pass and relied on the natural defenses to repel the enemy. Zhong Hui's elite troops were halted before the pass, unable to breach it after prolonged siege, and were on the verge of retreat. Had Deng Ai not secretly crossed the Yinping Trail and launched a surprise attack on Chengdu, Jianmen Pass would never have fallen. This chapter of history imbues Jianmen Pass with a heroic and solemn legacy.

Cliffside Cloud Terrace

Suspended on the sheer cliff, the glass observation platform offers the most thrilling experience at Jianmen Pass. The platform extends beyond the precipice, with a bottomless abyss beneath your feet — through the transparent glass you can see straight down to the valley floor, an exhilarating and breathtaking sensation. Standing on the platform, you face the howling mountain wind, surrounded by the undulating seventy-two peaks, as if floating in midair, merging with heaven and earth.

Green Cloud Corridor is the most famous ancient cypress avenue along the Jianmen Shu Road, said to have been planted by Shu Han soldiers. The ancient cypress trees lining the road tower skyward, verdant as clouds, stretching for hundreds of miles, hence the name "Green Cloud Corridor." These millennium-old cypresses have witnessed countless merchants and travelers passing through, as well as battles of sword and horse — they are living monuments of history. Strolling through, a gentle breeze brushes your face and the fragrance of cypress soothes your heart, evoking a nostalgic longing for antiquity.
