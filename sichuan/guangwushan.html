Guangwu Under Heaven

Located in Bazhong, Sichuan, Guangwushan is China's largest autumn leaf viewing area, renowned as "Asia's Longest Red Carpet." Every autumn, 680 square kilometers of crimson foliage blanket the mountains in layers of color, creating a breathtaking spectacle of red across countless peaks. The autumn leaves here are extraordinarily diverse, with rich gradations from deep crimson to golden yellow, from amber to purple — each mountain and slope displaying its own unique palette, like nature's own paintbox.

Guangwushan brings together elegant peaks, sheer cliffs, secluded valleys, streams, and waterfalls. It is famed for its remarkable karst peak cluster terrain, pristine primitive vegetation, and enchanting waterscapes. The mountains are perpetually shrouded in mist and shifting light — this very phenomenon inspired the name "Guangwushan" (Light Mist Mountain). It is not only a kingdom of autumn leaves but also a natural gallery where mountains and water merge in perfect harmony.

Autumn Five Sights

The Natural Gallery is the crown jewel of Guangwushan, where layered peaks and colorful forests complement each other — strolling through feels like walking inside a living oil painting. Black Bear Gully is known for its deep canyon and crystal-clear streams; in autumn, the water reflects the red leaves lining both banks, creating an unforgettable scene. Great & Small Lan Gully is a paradise for photographers, with incredibly dense foliage and intense colors — every shot is a masterpiece.

Incense Burner Peak is Guangwushan's highest summit. From the top, you can overlook a sea of peaks and rolling clouds — in autumn, it becomes a surging ocean of red. The Eighteen Moon Pools gather waterfalls, deep pools, streams, and shoals in one place. Eighteen uniquely shaped pools are scattered like pearls along the valley, with autumn leaves drifting on the water's surface, poetic and picturesque. Each of the five main attractions has its own character, together weaving a magnificent autumn symphony.

Mountains & Water Spirit

Guangwushan boasts exceptionally rich natural ecology, with a forest coverage rate exceeding 98% and abundant rare flora and fauna. The landscape features layered peaks and crisscrossing ravines, with typical karst landforms creating numerous caves, sinkholes, and geological fissures. Streams crisscross the mountains, waterfalls and springs can be seen everywhere, and the clear waters shimmer with golden light reflected by the red leaves.

The mountains are shrouded in mist year-round. The interplay of light and fog creates Guangwushan's signature spectacle — the "Light Mist" wonder. At sunrise, sunlight pierces through the mist and falls upon the red leaves, as if the entire mountain is ablaze. In late autumn, climbing to a high vantage point reveals rolling red waves and steaming clouds, a sight that refreshes the soul and makes visitors reluctant to leave.
