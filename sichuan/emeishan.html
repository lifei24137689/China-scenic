Golden Summit · Samantabhadra

Golden Summit is the highest peak of Mount Emei at 3,099 meters, where Samantabhadra Bodhisattva manifests. The Samantabhadra Golden Statue stands 48 meters tall and weighs approximately 660 tons, making it the tallest golden Buddha statue in the world. The statue blends Buddhist art with contemporary craftsmanship, appearing solemn and radiant as its golden brilliance illuminates all directions.

Standing atop the Golden Summit, the Samantabhadra statue shines brilliantly above the sea of clouds, as if a pure Buddhist land has descended upon the mortal world. At dawn or dusk, the golden statue reflects against the sky and clouds, stirring a sense of reverence in all who behold it.

Four Great Wonders

The Golden Summit is renowned for four wonders: the Sea of Clouds, Sunrise, Buddha's Halo, and Sacred Lanterns. The sea of clouds stretches like vast white waves rolling between the peaks, with Golden Summit floating like an isolated island above the cloud tide. At sunrise, the sky shifts from deep purple to glowing orange, as thousands of golden rays pierce through the clouds in a spectacle of indescribable grandeur.

The Buddha's Halo is the most mysterious wonder of Mount Emei. When sunlight shines from behind, visitors standing at the cliff's edge can see their own shadow within a seven-colored halo, the shadow moving with the viewer in a miraculous dance. The Sacred Lanterns appear on dark nights as drifting specks of light floating through the valleys, traditionally regarded as a sign of Samantabhadra's presence.

Ancient Temples · Zen Echoes

Mount Emei is one of the Four Sacred Buddhist Mountains and the primary sacred site of Samantabhadra Bodhisattva, adorned with numerous temples and monastic halls. Wannian Temple is among the oldest temples on the mountain, housing a bronze statue of Samantabhadra riding a white elephant cast during the Northern Song Dynasty, weighing 62 tons and designated as a national treasure. Below Qingyin Pavilion, the Black and White Waters converge, their flowing sound as melodious as a zither, giving the pavilion its name.

Baoguo Temple stands as the first temple when ascending the mountain, originally built during the Wanli era of the Ming Dynasty. Its plaque bearing the characters "Baoguo Si" was inscribed by Emperor Kangxi of the Qing Dynasty. The mountain's wild monkeys roam freely, adding a touch of liveliness and rustic charm to Mount Emei. Literary figures and artists throughout the ages who have visited this mountain have all been awestruck by its beauty.
