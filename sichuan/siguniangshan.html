Queen of Sichuan Mountains

Siguniangshan (Four Sisters Mountain) is located in Xiaojin County, Aba Tibetan and Qiang Autonomous Prefecture, Sichuan Province. It consists of four connected peaks. The main peak, Yaomei Peak, stands at 6,250 meters above sea level, making it the highest peak of the Qionglai Mountains and the second highest peak in Sichuan Province after Mount Gongga. It is honored as the "Queen of Sichuan Mountains."

The four peaks line up from north to south: Big Sister Peak, Second Sister Peak, Third Sister Peak, and Yaomei Peak. They are covered in snow year-round, presenting a majestic spectacle. Known for its magnificent snow-capped mountain scenery and abundant outdoor resources, it is also called the "Oriental Alps."

Three Valleys

The Siguniangshan Scenic Area consists of three distinctive valleys. Shuangqiao Valley (Double Bridge Valley) stretches 34.8 kilometers, offering the richest scenery among the three. Visitors can take sightseeing buses throughout the entire valley, where snow-capped mountains, forests, meadows, and alpine lakes complement each other — a top choice for first-time visitors.

Changping Valley (Long Flat Valley) is renowned for hiking and horseback riding experiences, with attractions such as the ancient cypress path, Lama Temple, and Dead Tree Beach scattered along the way. Haizi Valley (Lake Valley) preserves the most pristine alpine lake clusters, making it a hidden paradise for mountaineers and photographers.

Four Seasons Scenery

Siguniangshan offers stunning views in every season, with autumn being the most enchanting. From September to October, the colorful forests at the foot of the snow mountains are dyed in layers of gold, orange-red, and deep purple, weaving a brilliant tapestry that contrasts dramatically with the white snow peaks and azure sky.

In late spring and early summer, hundreds of flowers bloom across the alpine meadows, covering them in lush green carpets. In winter, the mountains are cloaked in silver, resembling a fairy-tale world of ice and snow. Maobiliang Viewing Platform is the best spot to capture the panoramic view of Siguniangshan, where all four peaks can be seen in a single frame.
