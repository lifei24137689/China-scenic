SECTOR 01

Lungs of Tianfu · Bifengxia Wonderland

Bifengxia is located in Ya'an, Sichuan, on the western edge of the Sichuan Basin bordering the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau. Known as the "Lungs of Tianfu," its two V-shaped gorges wind deep into the landscape with dense forests and numerous waterfalls, creating a natural green tapestry.

The gorge is rich in negative oxygen ions, with the air filled with the fresh scent of vegetation. Its unique natural environment makes it an ideal habitat for rare animals such as giant pandas and golden monkeys, truly an ecological treasure trove.

SECTOR 02

Panda Base · National Treasure Sanctuary

Bifengxia Giant Panda Base is China's largest semi-wild panda breeding base and a key part of the China Conservation and Research Center for the Giant Panda. The pandas live in a near-natural environment, freely roaming, foraging, and breeding in semi-wild conditions.

Visitors can observe the daily lives of giant pandas up close, watching these adorable national treasures play and leisurely feed among the bamboo groves. Bifengxia's panda breeding program has achieved remarkable results, contributing significantly to the protection of this endangered species.

SECTOR 03

Flying Waterfalls · Nature's Harmony

Bifengxia's waterfall cluster is one of the most magnificent sights in the gorge. Thousand-Layer Rock Waterfall cascades like silver silk over layered rocks, spraying droplets that refract rainbow light under the sun. White Dragon Pool Waterfall surges like a white dragon emerging from the sea, with thunderous power echoing through the valley.

Azure Cloud Stairway is built along the cliff face. Ascending the steps offers a panoramic view of the gorge's grandeur. Below, rushing streams; around, the roar of waterfalls; ahead, an emerald sea of forest — every step unfolds a stunning landscape painting.

SECTOR 04

Wildlife Park · Living in Harmony

Bifengxia Wildlife Park is one of the largest wildlife parks in Southwest China, divided into the Predator Zone and the Gentle Animal Zone. Visitors can take a safari vehicle through the Predator Zone to observe tigers, lions, black bears, and other predators up close in their natural behavior.

In the Gentle Animal Zone, sika deer, antelopes, peacocks, and other animals roam freely across vast meadows. The park employs an eco-ranging model, striving to create a living environment as close to natural habitats as possible, offering visitors the most authentic wildlife experience.
