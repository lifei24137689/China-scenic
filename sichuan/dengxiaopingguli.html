Deng Xiaoping's Former Residence

Located in Paifang Village, Xiexing Town, Guang'an City, Sichuan Province, Deng Xiaoping's former residence is a typical eastern Sichuan farmhouse courtyard. With blue tiles, white walls, and lush greenery, it exudes simplicity and dignity. On August 22, 1904, Deng Xiaoping was born here and spent his childhood and youth in this very place.

The residence covers about 830 square meters with 17 rooms of various sizes, divided into three sections: the main hall, the left wing, and the right wing. The courtyard features lush bamboos and flourishing flowers and trees, with a clear stream flowing gently in front of the house, exuding the idyllic charm unique to eastern Sichuan farmhouses.

Bronze Statue Square

The Bronze Statue Square is located in the core area of Deng Xiaoping's Former Residence. At the center stands a 6-meter-tall bronze statue of Deng Xiaoping, meticulously crafted by renowned sculptors. The statue captures the great man striding forward, symbolizing the monumental strides of China's reform and opening-up.

Surrounded by verdant pines and cypresses with clusters of blooming flowers, the square is both solemn and vibrant. Millions of domestic and international visitors come each year to pay homage, lay floral baskets, and remember the chief architect of reform, opening-up, and modernization.

Memorial Exhibition Hall

The Deng Xiaoping Memorial Exhibition Hall covers about 10 mu (0.67 hectares) with a building area of nearly 4,000 square meters. Themed "I am the son of the Chinese people," the hall presents the magnificent life of Deng Xiaoping through a vast collection of precious historical photos, cultural relics, and multimedia displays.

The architectural design is unique, blending the style of eastern Sichuan folk houses with modern exhibition concepts. The hall features exhibition areas such as "Leaving Guang'an," "Military Career," and "Years of Reform," vividly recreating Deng Xiaoping's glorious journey from his student years to his revolutionary struggles and his leadership of reform and opening-up.
