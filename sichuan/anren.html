Museum Town

Located in Dayi County, Chengdu, Sichuan Province, Anren Ancient Town is China's only museum town, known as the "Cradle of Chinese Museums." The town brings together 27 historic mansions blending Chinese and Western styles and over 30 modern museums, forming a unique cultural landscape. With bluestone-paved roads, gray bricks and tiles, and a rich Republic-era atmosphere, every building tells a story of a century of change.

The core appeal of Anren Ancient Town lies in its profound cultural heritage. It is not only a treasure trove of architectural art but also a microcosm of modern Chinese history. From the grandeur of Liu's Manor to the awe-inspiring Jianchuan Museum, from the romance of Republic-era streets to the elegance of historic mansions, Anren attracts visitors from around the world with its unique cultural character.

Liu's Manor

Liu's Manor is the most iconic historic architectural complex in Anren Ancient Town and an important witness to modern Chinese history. Built in the late Qing Dynasty, this manor complex blends traditional Chinese architecture with Western elements, featuring a grand scale and exquisite layout. The manor preserves a wealth of precious historical artifacts and original displays, vividly recreating the social life of modern western Sichuan.

As a National Key Cultural Heritage Protection Unit, Liu's Manor attracts countless history enthusiasts and architecture researchers with its unique historical and architectural value. Strolling through it, the mottled walls, exquisite carvings, and deep courtyards all convey a profound sense of history and the passage of time.

Jianchuan Museum Cluster

The Jianchuan Museum Cluster is China's largest private museum complex, founded by Mr. Fan Jianchuan. It houses over 30 themed museums including the Anti-Japanese War series, Red Era series, Earthquake series, and Folk Culture series, with a collection of over 10 million artifacts. Each museum tells the story of the Chinese nation's arduous journey and enduring spirit through its unique theme and rich exhibitions.

The Jianchuan Museum is not only a treasure trove of history but also a three-dimensional textbook of modern Chinese history. From war relics to red memories, from folk customs to post-disaster reconstruction, every exhibit here carries the nation's memories and emotions. Visiting the Jianchuan Museum is like traveling through time, engaging in a profound dialogue with history.
