/// 01

The Last Canyon of the Upper Yellow River

Located in Wuzhong City, Ningxia Hui Autonomous Region, the Qingtongxia Yellow River Grand Canyon is the last canyon on the upper reaches of the Yellow River, known as the "Lesser Three Gorges of the Yellow River." Stretching over ten li in length, with layered mountains on both sides and the surging Yellow River flowing through, it forms a magnificent canyon landscape. In 2024, the Qingtongxia Yellow River Grand Canyon was officially rated as a national 5A-level tourist attraction.

The canyon is rich in natural resources and profound in cultural heritage. Here you can find not only the spectacular Yellow River canyon scenery but also the largest existing Lamaist pagoda group in China — the 108 Pagodas, as well as the first hydroelectric station on the Yellow River — the Qingtongxia Water Conservancy Hub. The ancient Yellow River civilization, Western Xia culture, and the legend of Yu the Great converge here, forming a unique cultural tourism landscape belt.

/// 02

China's Largest Lamaist Pagoda Group

The 108 Pagodas are one of the largest and best-preserved Lamaist pagoda groups in China. Built along the western bank of the Yellow River at Qingtongxia, the pagodas are arranged in an isosceles triangle from top to bottom, consisting of 108 stupa-style Lamaist pagodas. Their construction dates back to the Western Xia period, nearly a thousand years ago, making them an important heritage for the study of Western Xia Buddhist culture.

The 108 pagodas are arranged in 12 rows by odd numbers, with a larger pagoda at the very top and increasing numbers descending downward, forming an overall isosceles triangle layout. Facing the Yellow River and backed by the mountains, the pagodas blend seamlessly with the canyon scenery. Climbing to the top offers a panoramic view of the surging Yellow River and the winding canyon.

/// 03

The First Hydroelectric Station on the Yellow River

The Qingtongxia Water Conservancy Hub is the first hydroelectric station built on the main stream of the Yellow River, known as the "First Dam of the Yellow River." Construction began in 1958, and the first generator unit started operation in 1967. It is a large-scale multipurpose water conservancy project primarily for irrigation and power generation, also serving flood control and ice prevention. The dam cuts the Yellow River in two, creating the magnificent spectacle of a high gorge with a calm lake.

The completion of the Qingtongxia Dam not only provided a stable water source for agricultural irrigation in the Ningxia Plain but also sustained the reputation of the "Oasis Beyond the Great Wall." The reservoir formed upstream of the dam stretches about 40 kilometers, complementing the canyon scenery on both banks and becoming a key stop for tourists taking boat tours of the Yellow River canyon. During flood discharge, the roaring Yellow River bursts forth from the dam in a magnificent display of power.
