The Last Mountain of the Long March's Red Monument

Mount Liupan was the last great mountain crossed by the Chinese Workers' and Peasants' Red Army during the Long March, standing at an elevation of approximately 2,942 meters. In October 1935, after leading the Central Red Army over Mount Liupan, Mao Zedong wrote the stirring poem "Pure Serene Music · Mount Liupan": "The sky is high, the clouds are pale, / We watch the wild geese disappear southward. / If we fail to reach the Great Wall we are not men, / We who have already measured twenty thousand li." This poem has inspired countless generations.

The Liupanshan Red Army Long March Memorial Hall sits atop the mountain, vividly recreating the arduous years of the Long March through a vast collection of precious historical relics, photographs, and scene reconstructions. On the memorial square stands the Red Army Long March Monument, bearing Mao Zedong's handwritten inscription "If we fail to reach the Great Wall we are not men," making it an important landmark for red education.

Red Army Trail & Revolutionary Footprints

The scenic area preserves the precious "Red Army Trail," a winding stone path through the mountains that bears witness to the arduous journey of the Red Army soldiers. Walking the Red Army Trail has become the most evocative experience in the scenic area, allowing visitors to personally feel the revolutionary spirit of the Red Army — "If we fail to reach the Great Wall we are not men." Along the trail, there are recreations of major Long March events, from the Battle of Xiangjiang to the Zunyi Conference, from the Four Crossings of Chishui River to the Capture of Luding Bridge — every step is steeped in history.

The main peak of Mount Liupan, Migangshan, reaches an elevation of 2,942 meters, making it the highest peak of the Liupan Mountains. From the summit, the endless rolling mountains and surging sea of clouds unfold in a magnificent panorama. Standing here, the Red Army once looked out over the land of northern Shaanxi and saw the dawn of victory. Today, it stands as a sacred red site for commemorating martyrs and receiving revolutionary traditional education.

Plateau Green Island · Ecological Wonderland

Liupanshan National Forest Park is a dazzling green gem on the Loess Plateau, with a forest coverage rate exceeding 80%, earning it the reputation of "Plateau Green Island." Coniferous forests, broad-leaved forests, and shrubbery layer upon layer, and the area is rich in wildlife resources. The average summer temperature is only around 18°C, making it a renowned summer retreat in Northwest China.

The park is crisscrossed by streams, with several rivers such as the Jinghe, Qingshuihe, and Huluhe originating here. The Jingheyuan scenic area features crystal-clear waters, flying waterfalls, and flowing springs, serving as an important water conservation area for the Shaanxi-Gansu-Ningxia region. Attractions such as Yehe Valley, Xiaonanchuan, and Liangdian Gorge each have their own unique charm, blending the elegance of Jiangnan water towns with the grandeur of Northwest landscapes.
