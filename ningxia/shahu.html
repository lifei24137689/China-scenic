Sand and Water in Harmony — A Wonder Beyond the Great Wall

Shahu Lake is located in Shizuishan City, Ningxia Hui Autonomous Region, and is one of China's Top Ten Charming Wetlands. The unique landscape where desert and lake coexist is a rare sight nationwide. Thousands of acres of lake water glisten with rippling blue waves, while rolling sand dunes stretch endlessly. The scene of sand and water blending, reeds and birds coexisting, is like a flowing painting of an oasis beyond the Great Wall.

Here, the majesty of the vast Gobi desert meets the gentle elegance of Jiangnan water towns. The expansive lake mirrors the blue sky, white clouds, and golden dunes. Drifting through the reed marshes by boat, water birds glide past, while camel bells echo in the distance — as if stepping into a tranquil paradise far from the hustle and bustle of the world.

Bird Paradise — Shahu Lake Bird Island

Shahu Lake is a paradise for birds, home to over 216 species, including nationally protected first and second-class animals. Every spring and autumn, tens of thousands of migratory birds settle and breed here. The spectacular sight of thousands of birds taking flight over the island is breathtaking. Rare species such as egrets, herons, cormorants, and whooper swans make their home here.

Bird Island is a prime destination for birdwatching enthusiasts. Taking a boat slowly toward the island, you can glimpse the heartwarming scenes of birds nesting and raising their young among the reeds. Dawn and dusk are the best times for birdwatching, as sunlight spills across the water and flocks soar through golden light, composing a vivid natural tableau.

Wandering Through the Reed Maze

The reed marshes of Shahu Lake are among the most poetic landscapes in the area. Thousands of acres of reeds grow scattered across the lake, forming a natural labyrinth of waterways. Gliding through on a small boat, the reeds sway in the breeze on either side, rustling softly. Sunlight filters through the gaps, casting dappled shadows — as if stepping into a green wonderland.

The reed marshes are not only a scenic highlight but also a haven for birds and fish. Various waterfowl nest among the reeds, while beneath the surface lies a paradise for fish. In summer and autumn, the reeds are lush and verdant, complementing the emerald lake waters. Come winter, reed catkins drift across the lake, turning it golden — a scene of unique charm.
