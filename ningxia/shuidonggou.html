Echoes of Civilization from 40,000 Years Ago

Shuidonggou is one of the earliest discovered and excavated Paleolithic sites in China, dating from 40,000 to 5,000 years ago, spanning the late Paleolithic to Neolithic periods. In 1923, French paleontologists Pierre Teilhard de Chardin and Émile Licent conducted the first excavations here, unveiling the prelude to Chinese prehistoric archaeology, earning Shuidonggou the title "Cradle of Chinese Prehistoric Archaeology".

The site has yielded a large number of stone artifacts, bone tools, and animal fossils, including typical microblades produced by blade technology, representing the advanced technical level of late Paleolithic humans. These discoveries prove that as early as 40,000 years ago, humans were thriving on this land, creating a brilliant prehistoric civilization.

Ming Dynasty Military Underground Great Wall

The Hidden Soldier Cave is one of the most distinctive military defense structures in the Shuidonggou area, an important part of the Ming Dynasty Great Wall defense system. These underground caves crisscross in a complex layout, featuring conference halls, storage rooms, armories, and trap facilities, serving as hiding, ambush, and supply storage facilities for Ming Dynasty soldiers.

The Hidden Soldier Caves work in coordination with above-ground military facilities such as the Ming Great Wall, beacon towers, and fortresses, forming a complete three-dimensional military defense system. These caves wind and twist, some extending hundreds of meters. Though not tall, their interior spaces are ingeniously laid out, fully demonstrating the wisdom and craftsmanship of ancient military architects.

Frontier Wall by Hongshan Lake

The Ming Great Wall within Shuidonggou is one of the best-preserved sections of the Great Wall in Ningxia. This wall winds across the Loess Plateau, complemented by the blue waves of Hongshan Lake, creating a unique "Great Wall embracing the lake" landscape. Standing on the Great Wall and looking into the distance, the vast Loess Plateau and the blue lake waters form a magnificent frontier tableau.

Hongshan Lake is another highlight of Shuidonggou. Its clear waters and swaying lakeside reeds contrast strikingly with the distant Great Wall, beacon towers, and loess cliffs. Taking a boat ride across the lake, visitors are surrounded by steep loess cliffs and ancient Great Wall ruins on both sides, as if traveling through time to an era of clashing swords and galloping horses.
