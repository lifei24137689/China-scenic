Mysterious Royal Tombs at the Foot of Helan Mountains

The Western Xia Imperial Tombs, located at the eastern foothills of the Helan Mountains on the western outskirts of Yinchuan City, are the royal mausoleum complex of the Western Xia Dynasty (1038-1227). Spanning approximately 50 km², the site contains 9 imperial tombs and over 250 accompanying burial sites, distinguished by its grand scale and meticulous layout among ancient Chinese mausoleum architecture.

Nestled against the majestic Helan Mountains and facing the vast Yinchuan Plain, the site embodies the imperial feng shui principle of "mountains at the back, water at the front." Each imperial tomb forms a complete architectural complex comprising watchtowers, stele pavilions, crescent walls, inner city walls, offering halls, and tomb towers. Despite millennia of wind and rain erosion, the magnificent tomb towers still stand proudly, earning the title "Eastern Pyramids."

The Lost Western Xia Civilization

The Western Xia Museum is China's largest museum dedicated to Western Xia culture, housing over 10,000 artifacts that comprehensively showcase the dynasty's politics, economy, military, culture, and religion. Its most famous collections include the gilded bronze ox, Western Xia script steles, ceramics, and Buddhist statues — all precious cultural relics.

The Western Xia Dynasty created its own unique script, commissioned by founding Emperor Li Yuanhao, comprising approximately 6,000 characters — one of the most complex ethnic scripts in Chinese history. Western Xia inscribed steles, Buddhist sutras, and official seals provide invaluable written materials for studying this civilization. Through its rich exhibitions, the museum offers visitors an in-depth understanding of the glorious history of this dynasty that once dominated the northwest.

Tomb Towers & Helan Mountain Petroglyphs

No. 3 Mausoleum is one of the best-preserved and most magnificent imperial tombs in the Western Xia Imperial Tombs complex. It is believed to be the burial site of Li Yuanhao, the founding emperor of the Western Xia Dynasty. The tomb tower stands over 20 meters tall in an octagonal stepped structure. Despite millennia of weathering, it remains majestically imposing, earning the site its "Eastern Pyramids" reputation.

The Helan Mountain petroglyphs complement the imperial tombs as another important cultural heritage site in Ningxia. Dating from the Neolithic Age to the Western Xia period, these rock carvings document the production, daily life, religious beliefs, and aesthetic pursuits of ancient nomadic peoples. Face motifs, animal patterns, and hunting scenes carved into the rock add profound historical and cultural depth to the Helan Mountains.
