/// 01

Chinese Cinema Goes Global

Located in Yinchuan, Ningxia Hui Autonomous Region, Zhenbeibu Western Film City was founded in 1993 by renowned writer Zhang Xianliang. Known as the "Cradle of Chinese Cinema," this film base rose from the desolate northwestern desert, serving as the shooting location for over 200 films and TV dramas including Red Sorghum, A Chinese Odyssey, New Dragon Gate Inn, and The Treasure Hunter.

The film city sits atop two ancient fortresses from the Ming and Qing dynasties — Ming Fortress and Qing Fortress. With his unique cultural vision, Zhang Xianliang transformed this barren ruins into a world-renowned film sanctuary. "Chinese cinema goes global from here" — this is the film city's proudest label and a testament to Chinese cinema's journey onto the international stage.

/// 02

Two Fortresses Centuries Old

Ming Fortress was built during the Hongzhi reign of the Ming Dynasty (circa 1500), serving as a key military stronghold on the northwestern frontier. Its walls were rammed from yellow earth and have stood firm for over 500 years through wind and rain. Qing Fortress, built during the Qianlong reign of the Qing Dynasty, is even grander in scale, with towering walls and majestic gates — it is the most iconic architectural complex in the film city.

The raw, rugged character of both fortresses has been perfectly preserved — the rammed-earth walls, the desolate Gobi landscape, the ancient weapon racks... every corner exudes the rich frontier atmosphere of the northwest. It is this unadorned, primal beauty that has drawn countless directors to film here. The winery from Red Sorghum and the gate tower from A Chinese Odyssey have become iconic scenes in the hearts of film fans.

/// 03

A Chinese Odyssey Eternal Classic

In 1994, Stephen Chow's A Chinese Odyssey was filmed at Zhenbeibu Western Film City. The gate tower, the Spider Spirit's Cave, the Bull Demon King's Palace — all these scenes were shot here. The iconic dialogue "There was a sincere love placed before me..." between Joker and the Fairy Zixia was filmed on the film city's gate tower, which has since become a pilgrimage site for countless fans.

Beyond A Chinese Odyssey, Zhang Yimou's Red Sorghum also gave birth to its classic scene here — the winery and the Moon Gate remain the most popular photo spots. In addition, New Dragon Gate Inn, The Treasure Hunter, 14 Blades, and many other films have left their brilliant moments on this yellow earth.
