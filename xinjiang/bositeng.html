Pearl of the Western Sea · China's Largest Inland Freshwater Lake

Bosten Lake, known as the Western Sea in ancient times, is located in Bohu County, Bayingolin Mongol Autonomous Prefecture, Xinjiang. It is the largest inland freshwater lake in China. The lake is as vast as an ocean, with a water area of 1,646 square kilometers, equivalent to twice the land area of Singapore.

As a brilliant pearl at the southern foot of the Tianshan Mountains, Bosten Lake gathers melted snow from the Tianshan range, nourishing the vast Yanqi Basin. With its clear blue waters, swaying reeds, and fragrant lotus flowers, it is renowned as a "Water Town Above the Desert." It serves not only as an important ecological barrier for Xinjiang but also as a world-famous tourist destination.

600,000 Mu of Reeds and Lotus

Bosten Lake boasts 600,000 mu of reed marshes, making it one of China's four major reed-producing regions. In midsummer, the reeds are as lush as an ocean, undulating with the wind across thousands of hectares. Thousands of acres of water lilies bloom in the lake, pink and white intermingling like stars scattered across the water, earning it the name "Water Garden."

Lotus Lake is one of the most famous scenic spots in the area. From June to August each year, lotus leaves spread across the water and lotus flowers glow under the sun. Boating through them feels like being in a water town south of the Yangtze River. The Ahongkou Birdwatching Tower offers a panoramic view of the entire lake area, where flocks of water birds dart through the reeds, painting a vivid natural picture.

Bountiful Gifts of the Desert Oasis

Bosten Lake is rich in over 20 species of fish, including pond smelt, crucian carp, and common carp. It is the largest fishery base in Xinjiang, with an annual catch of thousands of tons. The wetlands surrounding the lake are ecologically rich and serve as an important stopover for many migratory birds. Each spring and autumn, tens of thousands of birds rest and feed here.

Scenic spots such as Dahekou, Bailuzhou, and Golden Beach each have their own unique charm, offering both vast blue waters and exotic golden sandy shores. The desert and lake converge here, creating a unique landscape of "sand and lake in harmony" that leaves visitors in awe.
