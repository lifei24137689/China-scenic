Border Reclamation · Chapter I

359th Brigade Reclaims the Tianshan

The 359th Brigade Reclamation Memorial Hall chronicles the glorious journey of the Corps soldiers — born in Jinggangshan, raised in Nanniwan, fighting across thousands of miles, and reclaiming the Tianshan. This memorial hall is a symbol of the Corps spirit, witnessing the magnificent epic of generations of Corps soldiers rooted in the frontier, dedicating themselves selflessly.

From the Nanniwan mass production campaign to reclaiming wasteland along the Tarim River, the banner of the 359th Brigade has always flown wherever the motherland needed it most. The precious historical artifacts and visual materials displayed in the hall vividly recreate the arduous years of the Corps soldiers reclaiming and defending the border.

Desert Solitude · Chapter II

World's Second Largest Shifting Desert

The Taklamakan Desert is the world's second-largest shifting desert, and the Desert Gate scenic area is precisely the starting point for entering this mysterious sea of sand. The vast undulating dunes stretch endlessly, presenting a magnificent tableau of gold and ochre under the sunlight, like a desert landscape straight out of a Dunhuang mural.

Here you can experience special activities such as desert off-roading and camel riding, immersing yourself in the boundless desolate beauty of the desert. The Desert Gate is not only a paradise for adventurers but also an excellent place to witness the breathtaking wonders of nature.

Populus Euphratica Standing Tall · Chapter III

Reclamation Culture Populus Euphratica Spirit

The Reclamation Culture Park showcases the historical culture of the Corps' border reclamation and defense. The Populus euphratica forest is the most representative ecological landscape of the Tarim River basin. These millennia-old poplars stand proudly, symbolizing the tenacious and unyielding Populus euphratica spirit of the Corps soldiers rooted in the frontier.

The Tarim River is the mother river of Xinjiang, nourishing this magical land. In autumn, the poplar forests along the river transform into a golden ocean, composing a magnificent ecological tableau together with the desert and river, making it a paradise for photography enthusiasts.
