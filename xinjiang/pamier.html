Ancient Congling · Roof of the World

Known in ancient times as Congling, the Pamir Plateau was a must-pass place on the ancient Silk Road and has long been called the "Roof of the World." With an average elevation of over 4,000 meters and a total area of approximately 3,000 square kilometers, it is the convergence point of several major Asian mountain ranges — the Tianshan, Kunlun, Karakoram, and Hindu Kush.

In 2019, the Pamir Tourist Area was officially designated a National 5A Scenic Area. With its majestic snow-capped peaks and glaciers, serene highland lakes, and unique Tajik ethnic culture, it attracts travelers from all over the world. It is a masterpiece of nature and a crossroads of human civilization.

Muztagh Ata · Father of Icebergs

Muztagh Ata, standing at 7,546 meters, is known as the "Father of Icebergs" and is the iconic peak of the Pamir Plateau. Its summit is covered in perpetual snow, with massive glaciers winding down like silver dragons, shimmering with an icy blue glow under the sun. At its foot, Lake Karakul nestles quietly, its waters shifting colors with the light — sometimes deep blue, sometimes emerald green, like a dream.

White Sand Mountain and White Sand Lake are equally ethereal. The white sand dunes are mirrored in the turquoise lake, creating a striking visual contrast like a natural ink-wash painting. It is also a paradise for photographers, with every frame offering a stunning landscape.

Melodious Eagle Flute · Soul of the Tajik

The Tajik people are China's only Caucasian ethnic minority, having lived on the Pamir Plateau for generations. They revere the eagle as their totem, and the eagle dance and eagle flute are recognized as national intangible cultural heritage. The eagle dance mimics the soaring of an eagle, with vigorous and powerful movements; the eagle flute is crafted from eagle bones, producing a resounding, melodious tone that echoes through the snowy mountains.

The Stone Fortress ruins bear witness to the Pamir's millennia of history. Built during the Han Dynasty, this ancient castle once served as a garrison post at Congling and was an important relay station on the ancient Silk Road. Though only crumbling walls remain today, they still tell tales of past glory. The Khunjerab Pass is one of the highest border crossings in the world, connecting China and Pakistan.
