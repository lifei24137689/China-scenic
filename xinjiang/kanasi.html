God's Private Reserve

Kanas is located in the Altay Prefecture of Xinjiang, renowned as a "paradise on earth and the garden of gods", one of China's most beautiful lakes. The Kanas Lake changes colors with the seasons, with Moon Bay, Immortal Bay, and Wolong Bay embedded like emeralds among the mountains.

The mysterious legend of the Kanas Lake Monster adds a touch of fantasy. Tuvan villages preserve the original nomadic culture, and the golden birch forests in autumn look like a breathing oil painting.

/// 02

Alpine Lake

Kanas Lake is the core of the scenic area, with a surface elevation of 1,374 meters, shaped like a crescent moon. The lake water changes colors with seasons and weather, from emerald green to azure blue, from jade to amber, earning it the name "Color-Changing Lake".

The three famous bays — Moon Bay, Immortal Bay, and Wolong Bay — each have their own charm. Moon Bay is like a new moon set in the canyon, Immortal Bay is shrouded in morning mist like a fairyland, and Wolong Bay resembles a coiled dragon. Fish Viewing Platform offers a panoramic view of the lake, a paradise for photography enthusiasts.

/// 03

Golden Fairytale

From September to October each year, Kanas enters its most splendid season. The birch forests turn golden, Siberian larches blaze into fiery red, reflecting against the turquoise lake waters to create breathtaking autumn scenes.

Autumn is the best time to visit Kanas, with pleasant temperatures and rich colors. Immortal Bay in morning mist, Fish Viewing Platform at sunset, Tuvan villages under the starry sky — every frame is a gift from nature.
