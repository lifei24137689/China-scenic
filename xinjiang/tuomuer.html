Summit of Tianshan · Tomur Peak

Tomur Peak, at an elevation of 7,443 meters, is the highest peak of the Tianshan Mountains, located in Wensu County, Aksu Prefecture, Xinjiang. "Tomur" means "Iron Mountain" in Uyghur, named for its iron-gray rock formations. The summit is covered with snow year-round, surrounded by hundreds of modern glaciers, making it a vital water source for the Tarim Basin.

As the highest peak of the Tianshan, Tomur Peak, along with neighboring peaks such as Khan Tengri, forms the most spectacular snow mountain cluster in the range. The peak is majestic, often shrouded in clouds and mist, with tremendous vertical elevation differences that create a powerful visual impact, earning it the title "Soul of the Tianshan Mountains."

/// 02

Canyon Secret Realm · Wensu Grand Canyon

The Wensu Grand Canyon, located at the southern foot of Tomur Peak, is a spectacular canyon complex formed by glacial meltwater erosion over hundreds of millions of years. The canyon walls display vibrant colors — layers of red, yellow, brown, and white stacked together like a massive geological tapestry spread across the earth. The deepest sections reach hundreds of meters, creating a majestic scene.

The canyon contains numerous uniquely shaped Danxia and Yardang landforms, some resembling castles, some like giant towers, and others like thousands of horses galloping — a breathtaking sight. This place is not only a research paradise for geologists but also a creative sanctuary for photography enthusiasts.

Snow Kingdom · Tianshan Wonderland

The Mount Tomur Scenic Area boasts the most spectacular glacier cluster in the Tianshan range, with hundreds of modern glaciers surrounding Tomur Peak, covering thousands of square kilometers. These glaciers are the most important freshwater resource for the Tarim Basin, known as the "solid reservoir." The glaciers take various forms — ice towers, ice caves, and crevasses create a breathtaking landscape.

The scenic area brings together diverse landscapes including snow mountains, glaciers, canyons, grasslands, and forests — the essence of Tianshan's natural beauty. The Divine Tree Garden features towering ancient trees, the Tash Village grassland echoes with pastoral songs, and the Paklek grassland is covered with wildflowers, each showcasing the unique charm of the Tianshan Mountains.
