Sky Prairie · Tianshan Wonderland

Nalati Grassland is one of the world's four largest grasslands, located at the eastern end of the Ili River Valley in Xinjiang, at an elevation of over 1,800 meters, renowned as the "Sky Prairie." Here, majestic snow-capped mountains, lush forests, vast grasslands, and winding rivers blend perfectly, creating a magnificent natural landscape.

In Kazakh, "Nalati" means "where the sun rises." Legend has it that when Genghis Khan was on his western expedition, a Mongol army crossed the Tianshan Mountains and saw this sun-drenched grassland, they couldn't help but exclaim "Nalati! Nalati!" — The sun! From then on, this grassland was given this beautiful name.

Valley Prairie · Nomadic Paradise

Nalati Grassland consists of three major areas: the Sky Prairie, the Valley Prairie, and the Panlong Valley Trail. The Valley Prairie stretches along the Gongnaisi River with abundant water and lush grass, serving as the ancestral homeland of Kazakh herders. White yurts dot the green grasslands like scattered clouds, with wispy smoke rising and herds of cattle and sheep grazing.

Every summer, Kazakh herders migrate to the high mountain summer pastures, continuing a nomadic tradition that has endured for millennia. Visitors can step into Kazakh yurts, savor aromatic milk tea and hand-grabbed meat, listen to the melodious sounds of the dombra, and experience the most authentic nomadic culture.

Panlong Valley · Forest Wonderland

The Panlong Valley Trail is the most enchanting forest canyon in Nalati Grassland, with winding mountain paths coiling like a giant dragon through the snowy spruce forests. Here, ancient trees tower skyward, dappling the sunlight, while streams babble through the woods and wildflowers blanket the ground. Strolling through, you feel as if you've stepped into a fairytale world, where every step reveals a scenic view.

Woltajiaota is the highest viewing platform in Nalati Grassland, offering a panoramic view of the entire Valley Prairie. In the distance, snow-capped mountains stretch endlessly; up close, the grassland spreads like a carpet, and the Gongnaisi River winds like a silver ribbon, opening up a vast and breathtaking panorama.
