Wind-Eroded Yardang · Devil's Howl

World Devil City is a typical yardang landform shaped by wind erosion. Over hundreds of millions of years, wind erosion has created unique mound formations. When fierce winds suddenly rise, sand and stones fly, producing howling sounds like the roar of demons, sending chills down one's spine — hence the name "Devil City."

Located at the northwestern edge of the Junggar Basin, the scenic area covers approximately 260 square kilometers, making it one of China's most spectacular yardang landforms. The diverse terrain, sculpted by wind, features countless uniquely shaped mounds and ravines, forming a majestic and desolate Gobi landscape.

Nature's Masterpiece · Wind Erosion Wonder

The scenic area features numerous lifelike wonders such as Peacock Greeting Guests, the Sphinx, Devil Couple, and Broken Bridge, each breathtakingly vivid. These uniquely shaped mounds are the result of hundreds of millions of years of wind and water erosion, each one like a meticulous carving by nature.

When strong winds arrive, they collide and weave through the mounds, producing eerie sounds akin to wailing ghosts and howling wolves. This unique natural phenomenon adds an air of mystery and awe to Devil City. During golden autumn, as the setting sun casts its glow upon the mounds, the entire Devil City presents a magnificent golden vista.

Desert Light & Shadow · Famed Film Location

The unique landscape of World Devil City has made it a filming location for many famous movies. Classics such as Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon, Seven Swords, and Warriors of Heaven and Earth were all shot here. The desolate grandeur of Devil City adds stunning visual impact to the silver screen.

The terrain resembles abandoned ancient castles, presenting different colors and forms under changing light. The softness of morning, the intensity of noon, the warmth of dusk — each moment reveals a different charm of Devil City. Well-developed walking paths and observation decks allow visitors to venture deep in and experience this wondrous land up close.
