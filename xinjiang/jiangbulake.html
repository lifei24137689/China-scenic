01

Source of Holy Water · Earth Art

Jiangbulake means "Source of Holy Water" in Kazakh. The myriad acres of dryland rise and fall with the mountain terrain, like earth art. On the Tianshan Strange Slope, water flows uphill — it is the longest strange slope in the world. In summer, wheat waves roll; in autumn, golden wheat fields contrast with the snowy mountains; in winter, everything is wrapped in silver.

The tulip flower sea and the Tianshan wheat sea are the two hallmarks of Jiangbulake. Here, there are not only magnificent natural landscapes but also rich Kazakh ethnic culture, making it a paradise for photography enthusiasts and nature travelers.

Tianshan Strange Slope · Myriad-acre Wheat Fields

The Tianshan Strange Slope stretches 290 meters, making it the longest strange slope in the world. Here, water flows uphill and cars slide upward, overturning people's perception of gravity. This unique geological phenomenon attracts countless visitors to experience it.

The myriad-acre dryland is Jiangbulake's most magnificent earth landscape. The wheat fields undulate with the mountain terrain, like the fingerprints of the earth. In summer, wheat waves roll with vibrant greenery; in autumn, golden wheat ears shine in harmony with the distant snowy mountains, creating a stunning pastoral tableau.

Flower Sea · Kazakh Culture

Every year at the turn of spring and summer, the tulip gardens of Jiangbulake are in full bloom. The colorful tulip flower sea, set against the distant snowy mountains, creates a picturesque scene that has become a paradise for photography enthusiasts. Scenic spots like Flower Sea and Heilaoba each have their own unique charm, with different beauty across all four seasons.

Jiangbulake is a Kazakh settlement where rich Kazakh ethnic culture is preserved. Visitors can experience Kazakh yurts, taste hand-grabbed meat and milk tea, and feel the hospitality of the grassland people. The ancient nomadic culture and the magnificent natural landscape are perfectly integrated here.
