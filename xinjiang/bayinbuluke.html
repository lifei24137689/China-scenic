China's Second Largest Grassland · Home of Swans

Bayanbulak is China's second-largest grassland, located on the southern slopes of the central Tianshan Mountains, with an average elevation of 2,500 meters and a total area of approximately 23,835 square kilometers. In Mongolian, "Bayanbulak" means "abundant spring." With lush water and grass and herds of cattle and sheep, it is a typical alpine grassland ecosystem.

The Kaidu River's Nine Bends wind like a silver ribbon across the grassland. At sunset, the spectacular sight of nine suns can be seen in the Nine Bends. Swan Lake is China's largest swan nature reserve, where tens of thousands of swans inhabit and breed each year, earning it the reputation of "Home of Swans."

/// 02

Nine Bends · Legend of Nine Suns

The Kaidu River meanders across the Bayanbulak Grassland, forming the famous "Nine Bends" spectacle. The river winds across the grassland like a silver ribbon. When the sun sets, golden sunlight spills onto the river, and nine bends simultaneously reflect the sun's image, creating the magnificent "Nine Suns" scene.

The Basilik Observation Deck is the best vantage point for viewing the Nine Bends. Standing on the deck, you can take in the entire grassland and the winding river. At sunset, countless photography enthusiasts gather here to capture the miraculous moment of nine suns shining together.

Swan Lake · Home of Highland Spirits

Bayanbulak Swan Lake is China's largest swan nature reserve and one of the world's largest breeding grounds for whooper swans. The lake area is covered with marshes and wetlands with abundant aquatic plants. From March to October each year, tens of thousands of swans, greylag geese, wild ducks, and other waterfowl inhabit and breed here, creating a spectacular scene.

Swan Lake is composed of many interconnected small lakes with crystal-clear waters, surrounded by grassland and snow-capped mountains. It is not only a paradise for birds but also a summer pasture for Mongolian herders. White yurts dot the green grassland, creating a picture of harmony between humans and nature.
