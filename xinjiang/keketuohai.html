Green Jungle · Irtysh Grand Canyon

Keketuohai is located in Fuyun County, Altay Prefecture, Xinjiang. Its name means "green jungle" in Kazakh. Though not a sea, it boasts geological wonders more magnificent than the ocean. The Irtysh Grand Canyon stretches for dozens of kilometers, with granite peaks of various shapes on both sides. After billions of years of wind and water erosion, unique formations such as Shenzhong Mountain, Camel Peak, and Elephant Trunk Peak have taken shape.

The Irtysh River surges through the canyon with clear blue waters — it is the only river in China that flows into the Arctic Ocean. In autumn, the birch forests on both banks turn golden and brilliant, creating a striking contrast with the gray-white granite peaks, like a vivid oil painting.

Shenzhong Mountain · Granite Peak

Shenzhong Mountain is the iconic landmark of Keketuohai — a giant granite monolith rising 365 meters high, shaped like an inverted bell, standing majestically by the Irtysh River. The surface is covered with vertical grooves, the masterpiece of glacial meltwater erosion over billions of years.

Legend has it that Shenzhong Mountain is a divine bell bestowed upon the earth, ringing melodiously at each hour. Standing at its foot and looking up, the giant rock soars into the clouds with overwhelming grandeur. In autumn, the golden birch forests at the mountain's base create a delightful contrast with the gray-white granite peak, making it a paradise for photography enthusiasts.

Meritorious Mine · Backbone of the Republic

Keketuohai's Pit No. 3 is a world-class large rare metal deposit, known as the "Natural Geological Museum." It contains 86 types of minerals, with reserves of rare metals such as beryllium, lithium, niobium, and tantalum ranking among the highest in China. In the 1960s, Pit No. 3 contributed greatly to the nation by repaying about 47% of its foreign debt to the former Soviet Union.

Today, the pit has formed a massive site about 500 meters in diameter and 200 meters deep, with spiral vehicle tracks clearly visible on its walls, recording those momentous years. Ore specimen displays around the pit showcase various colorful mineral crystals, attracting countless geology enthusiasts and history seekers on pilgrimage.
