Layered Grassland in the Heart of Tianshan

KalaJun Grassland lies at the core of the Tianshan World Natural Heritage site, renowned as a "layered grassland" — five distinct landforms stand in perfect harmony: towering snow-capped peaks, verdant spruce forests, rolling grassland expanses, deep canyons, and winding rivers, all woven together into a magnificent three-dimensional natural tapestry.

Covering 2,848 km² at an elevation of around 2,000 m, this is no ordinary flat expanse. The terrain rolls and undulates in rich layers, like a colossal green staircase descending from the snowy peaks to the river valleys. In summer, the five-flower meadows burst into a riot of color, with bloom-covered terraces spreading like a natural palette at the foot of the Tianshan Mountains.

World Natural Heritage Core Reserve

In 2013, KalaJun Grassland was inscribed as a core component of "Xinjiang Tianshan," a UNESCO World Natural Heritage site. It preserves a complete vertical ecosystem unique to the Tianshan range, spanning from snow-capped glaciers at 5,000 m down to river valleys at 1,000 m, creating a magnificent spectrum of vertical landforms.

The Kuokesu Grand Canyon winds through the landscape in majestic splendor, its meandering rivers flowing like the earth's veins across the grassland. The "Body Grassland" is famed for its graceful terrain curves — rolling hills that under shifting light reveal silhouettes as soft as the human form, earning the name "Sculpture of the Earth." Generations of Kazakh herders have roamed these lands, preserving the purest grassland culture.

Kazakh Millennium Heritage

KalaJun Grassland has been the ancestral home of Kazakh herders for generations. "KalaJun" means "Black Wilderness" in Kazakh, referring to the fertile dark soil that nurtures abundant pasture. Each summer, herders drive their flocks to these grounds, where yurts dot the green ocean like stars in the night sky.

Here, the purest nomadic grassland culture endures: eagle hunting is a traditional Kazakh craft, giving Falcon Terrace its name. Horse racing, sheep-grabbing, and "girl-chasing" festivities thrive with timeless vitality. Summer is the most beautiful season in KalaJun, when wildflowers blanket the meadows and the cool climate offers the perfect setting to experience grassland culture.
