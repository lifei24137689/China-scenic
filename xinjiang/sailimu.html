The Last Tear of the Atlantic — Blue Tears

Sayram Lake is the last place touched by the warm, moist air currents of the Atlantic, earning it the name "The Last Tear of the Atlantic." The lake water is as blue as a jewel, with visibility up to 12 meters. It is the highest-altitude and largest alpine cold-water lake in Xinjiang.

In 2021, it was rated as a National 5A-level Scenic Spot. The lake sits at an elevation of 2,073 meters and covers an area of approximately 458 square kilometers. The 92-kilometer lakeside highway is known as one of "China's Most Beautiful Lakeside Highways," a paradise for self-driving and cycling enthusiasts.

Azure as Gem — Sacred Alpine Lake

The waters of Sayram Lake are crystal clear and azure, with visibility reaching up to 12 meters. Under sunlight, the lake displays varying shades of blue, gradually shifting from light to deep blue, like a massive sapphire embedded within the Tianshan Mountains.

No major rivers flow into the lake; its water source relies mainly on precipitation and groundwater recharge, keeping the water pure. The lake is surrounded by mountains on all sides — snow-capped peaks on the south, vast grasslands on the north, and dense forests on the west — creating rich, layered scenery that changes with each season.

Flower Seas & Blue Ice — Seasonal Wonderland

In summer, wildflowers bloom across the lakeside — golden lotuses, wild poppies, pedicularis, and more — creating a magnificent sea of flowers. Against the blue sky and white clouds, the lake takes on a dreamlike blue hue, with swans gracefully gliding, painting a picture of paradise on earth.

In winter, the lake surface freezes over, creating a spectacular blue ice wonderland. The ice is crystal clear, emitting an ethereal blue glow under sunlight. Natural formations like ice cracks and ice bubbles appear like works of art. Sayram Lake's winter scenery is hailed as "China's Lake Baikal," attracting countless photography enthusiasts.
