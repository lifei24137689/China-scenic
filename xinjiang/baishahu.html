Desert Pearl · Baishahu

Baishahu is a pearl in the desert, embraced by white sand mountains, with lake water that never dries up all year round. Reeds grow densely in the lake, lotuses bloom, and wild ducks gather in flocks. Baishahu is a border scenic area of the 185th Regiment, where the red culture of the First Company of the Northwest Border blends with the natural scenery, hailed as a desert wonder.

The lake water is as clear as a mirror, reflecting the blue sky, white clouds, and the surrounding white sand mountains, like a natural painting. Every summer, lotuses in the lake bloom in competition, creating a delightful contrast with the green reeds, attracting countless visitors.

Sand Meets Lake · Natural Wonder

Baishahu is surrounded by white sand mountains with sand as fine as snow, shimmering under the sunlight. The white sand mountains and the blue lake water create a striking visual contrast, forming a unique desert lake landscape.

Dense reeds grow in the lake. In summer, lotuses bloom, and water birds such as wild ducks and egrets inhabit and breed here. The ecosystem of Baishahu is unique yet fragile, with the lake relying on groundwater and precipitation for replenishment, never drying up all year round — truly a miracle.

Bingtuan Spirit · Red Borderland

Baishahu Scenic Area is located within the 185th Regiment of the Xinjiang Production and Construction Corps (Bingtuan), where the First Company of the Northwest Border is situated. The Bingtuan people cultivate land and guard the border, building homes in the desolate Gobi desert, creating the miracle of "turning desert into oasis."

Landmarks such as the Singing Sand Dune and Eye Mountain bear witness to the hard work and selfless dedication of the Bingtuan people. The Red Culture Education Base of the First Company of the Northwest Border is located within the scenic area, where visitors can learn about the history of the Bingtuan and border defense culture.
