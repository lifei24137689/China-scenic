/// 01 Geography

A Green Corridor at the Foot of Flaming Mountain

Located in the Flaming Mountain gorge of Turpan, Xinjiang, Grape Valley is a green corridor stretching about 8 kilometers from north to south and 2 kilometers from east to west. Meltwater from the Tianshan snow peaks forms streams that flow through the valley, nurturing a lush oasis amid the scorching Flaming Mountain.

Turpan is one of the hottest and driest regions in China, with summer temperatures reaching up to 47.8°C. It is precisely this unique climate, combined with nourishment from Tianshan snowmelt, that makes Grape Valley one of the world's most suitable areas for grape cultivation. Abundant sunshine and large temperature differences between day and night create exceptional grape quality.

/// 02 Culture

A Millennia-Old Charm Beneath the Grape Trellises

Grape Valley is not only a kingdom of grapes but also a living museum of Uyghur culture. The valley preserves traditional Uyghur dwellings — adobe houses that stay cool in summer and warm in winter, with grape trellises shading their doorways. Afanti's former residence, the Centenarian Village, and the Wang Luobin Music Hall are scattered throughout, telling the legendary stories of this land.

Every late August, the Turpan Grape Festival grandly opens, attracting visitors from around the world to Grape Valley to taste hundreds of grape varieties, enjoy Uyghur song and dance, and immerse themselves in the rich ethnic culture. The craft of making raisins in drying houses has been passed down for millennia, producing incomparable culinary treasures.

/// 03 Ecology

A Miracle of Life Deep in the Gobi

At the foot of the barren Flaming Mountain, Grape Valley is like an emerald embedded in the Gobi Desert. Meltwater from the Tianshan Mountains nourishes this land through the ancient Karez well irrigation system, forming a unique and precious oasis agricultural ecosystem. The grape trellises block out the sky, streams babble, creating a sharp contrast with the surrounding desert.

The ecological value of Grape Valley lies not only in its agricultural output but also in serving as a valuable ecological model for arid regions — a testament to harmonious coexistence between humans and nature. Every grapevine here represents a tenacious struggle against extreme conditions and embodies the wisdom of the Turpan people.
