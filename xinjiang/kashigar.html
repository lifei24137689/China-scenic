Millennium Maze · Pearl of the Silk Road

Kashgar Ancient City is located in the Kashgar Prefecture of Xinjiang. It is China's only maze-like urban district characterized by Islamic culture, with a history spanning over 2,100 years. The ancient city features a crisscross network of streets and alleys, colorful Uyghur residential houses, and earthy yellow building walls that glow with warm golden hues under the sunlight.

Strolling through it feels like traveling through time, stepping into a fairy tale from One Thousand and One Nights. Every alley holds a story, and every carved wooden door reveals a unique scene behind it. The labyrinthine street layout ensures that every turn is filled with surprise — this is a living ancient city of a thousand years.

Islamic Architecture · Id Kah Mosque

The Id Kah Mosque is the largest mosque in China, located in the heart of Kashgar Ancient City. Built in 1442, its architectural style blends Islamic traditions with local Uyghur characteristics — towering arches, exquisite geometric ornamentation, and a green dome all showcase the solemnity and splendor of Islamic architecture.

During Eid al-Adha and Eid al-Fitr, tens of thousands of Muslims gather here to pray. The sea of white prayer caps surging like waves creates a magnificent spectacle. The carved wooden pillars, painted caisson ceilings, and intricate brickwork — every detail is a perfect embodiment of craftsmanship.

Silk Road Commerce · Millennia-old Crafts

Kashgar is a brilliant pearl on the ancient Silk Road, a key confluence where Eastern and Western civilizations met. The ancient city is lined with handicraft workshops, where traditional crafts such as copperware, carpets, Atlas silk, wood carving, and pottery have been passed down through generations. Each piece embodies the wisdom and dedication of Uyghur artisans.

The Kashgar Bazaar is one of the largest markets in Asia. With its dazzling array of goods, the rising and falling cries of vendors, and the scent of spices drifting through the air, it continues the bustling legacy of the ancient Silk Road. Here, the old Silk Road has never closed — the fusion of commerce and culture endures to this day.
