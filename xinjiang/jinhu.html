/// 01

Waterland Poplar · Golden Corridor by the Yarkant River

Zepu Jinhu Poplar National Forest Park is located in Zepu County, Kashgar Prefecture, and is the largest poplar forest scenic area in southern Xinjiang. The Yarkant River meanders through the land, nourishing the natural poplar forest that stretches for dozens of miles along both banks, creating a unique "waterland poplar" landscape.

Poplar trees live a thousand years without dying, stand a thousand years after death without falling, and endure a thousand years after falling without decaying. This poplar forest is hailed as a "three-thousand-year ode to life." Every golden October, the poplar leaves turn from green to gold, shining brilliantly against the clear waters, blue skies, and snow-capped mountains, like a richly colored oil painting.

/// 02

Millennium Vigil · The Life Legend of the Poplar King

Within the Jinhu Poplar scenic area stands a thousand-year-old "Poplar King" — over ten meters tall with a trunk nearly two meters in diameter, still thriving after a millennium of wind and frost. This Poplar King is the area's most precious natural heritage, having witnessed the great changes of the Western Regions, and is hailed as a "living fossil."

Strolling along Jinhu Poplar Avenue, towering poplars line both sides in varied postures — some tall and sturdy, others with twisted, gnarled branches. Each poplar tells a story of resilience and tenacity through its unique form. Deep within the forest, Russian olive groves and tamarisk bushes interweave, creating a rich and diverse desert vegetation landscape.

/// 03

Deep in the Desert Ecological Sanctuary

Jinhu Poplar scenic area lies on the western edge of the Taklamakan Desert, on the alluvial fan of the Yarkant River. This unique location creates a remarkable landscape where "water and sand converge" — on one side the vast, boundless desert, on the other a vibrant oasis. The poplar forest acts as a green barrier, holding back the desert's advance.

The Russian olive corridor within the park is another scenic highlight. When the Russian olive trees bloom, their fragrance fills the air, refreshing the spirit. The Longevity Village nestles among the woods, where generations of villagers have lived alongside the poplars, embodying harmonious coexistence between humans and nature through their simple way of life. The area's exceptionally high concentration of negative oxygen ions makes it a true natural oxygen bar.
