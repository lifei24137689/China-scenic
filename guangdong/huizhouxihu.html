/// 01

Pearl of Lingnan · Mountain & Water Paradise

Huizhou West Lake Scenic Area is located in Huicheng District, Huizhou City, Guangdong Province. Comprising the West Lake area and the Honghua Lake area, it covers a total area of 20.91 km² with a water area of 3.13 km², making it one of the most beautiful urban ecological lakes in China.

Characterized by its elegant natural beauty and serene, winding landscapes, the scenic area boasts a profound history and rich cultural heritage as a national-level scenic spot with a distinctive Lingnan garden style. In 2018, it was designated a national 5A-level tourist attraction.

Six Lakes & Nine Bridges · Watery Grace

Huizhou West Lake is renowned for its "Six Lakes and Nine Bridges" — Ping Lake, Feng Lake, Nan Lake, Ling Lake, E Lake, and Honghua Lake are set like six pearls in the city, while nine ancient bridges including Xixin Bridge, Gongbei Bridge, Yingxian Bridge, and Jiuqu Bridge connect them, creating a unique landscape of "lakes within lakes, bridges beyond bridges."

Honghua Lake is known as the "Lung of Huizhou City." Its 18-km lakeside greenway, celebrated as a "green corridor of blooming splendor," is a perfect destination for recreation and fitness. Iconic landmarks such as Sizhou Pagoda on Ping Lake, Su Causeway, and Diancui Islet carry a millennium of cultural heritage.

Su Dongpo's Enduring Legacy

In the first year of the Shaosheng era (1094) of the Northern Song Dynasty, Su Dongpo was exiled to Huizhou, where he lived for three years. He not only left behind enduring masterpieces such as "At the first watch, the moon rises over the mountain; the jade pagoda lies in gentle ripples," but also led the donation-funded construction of Su Causeway and Xixin Bridge, benefiting the local people.

Su Dongpo's beloved concubine Wang Chaoyun rests forever beneath Gushan Hill by West Lake. Her tomb and Liuru Pavilion remain sites of pilgrimage for visitors. Generations of literary figures, including Wen Tianxiang and Zhu Zhishan, have also set foot on West Lake, enriching its landscape with profound cultural depth.
