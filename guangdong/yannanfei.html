◊ Yannanfei Brief

Yannanfei Tea Plantation is China's first 5A-level scenic area themed around tea culture. The terraced tea gardens cascade like green waves, with Hakka Weilong Houses dotted throughout. Here you can experience the entire process of tea picking, processing, and tasting, and immerse yourself in the perfect fusion of Hakka culture and tea culture.

/// 01

Thousand Mu Tea Fields Green Waves

Yannanfei Tea Plantation boasts thousands of mu of premium tea gardens, with terraced tea plants undulating like green waves across the mountains. In the early morning, mist lingers as tea farmers move through the fields, creating a moving pastoral tableau. This is the core production area of Dancong tea, yielding leaves with a rich aroma and a lingering sweet aftertaste.

Stroll along the tea garden boardwalk, breathe in the fresh fragrance of tea, and gaze at the rolling mountains in the distance. During the picking season, visitors can hand-pick tender tea buds and experience the poetic life of “picking tea beneath the eastern fence, quietly gazing at the southern mountains.”

/// 02

Hakka Weilong Houses Cultural Treasures

Weilong Houses —— the quintessential Hakka architectural form, renowned for their semicircular enclosed structure. Yannanfei has transformed traditional Weilong Houses into boutique hotels, allowing visitors to experience the unique charm of Hakka architecture in modern comfort. Green bricks, black tiles, carved beams and painted rafters — every detail tells the story of Hakka wisdom and craftsmanship.

Inside the Weilong Houses, tea rooms and Hakka cultural exhibition areas offer visitors the chance to enjoy tea with a view and learn about the Hakka people's five great migrations. Hakka folk songs, Hakka cuisine, and Hakka customs all converge here, making it an exceptional window into Hakka culture.

/// 03

Tea Art Experience Zen Lifestyle

Yannanfei offers a full-circle experience from tea picking and processing to tasting. Under the guidance of professional tea artisans, you can hand-fry your own batch of tea leaves and witness the wondrous transformation from fresh leaf to finished product. The tea art performances incorporate traditional Hakka rituals, with every gesture showcasing the beauty of Eastern tea ceremony.

The Yannanfei Divine Stone is the scenic area's iconic landmark, with the three characters “Yannanfei” engraved upon the massive rock. Hakka cuisine is also a must-try experience — salt-baked chicken, stuffed tofu, and Mei Cai Kou Rou (pork belly with preserved mustard greens), each dish an authentic taste of Hakka flavor.
