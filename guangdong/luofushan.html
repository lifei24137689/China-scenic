◈ Luofushan Brief

Luofushan is the Premier Mountain of Lingnan and one of China's Ten Great Taoist Sacred Mountains. During the Eastern Jin dynasty, Ge Hong once lived here practicing alchemy and Taoist cultivation, leaving behind Taoist relics such as the Chongxu Temple. With 432 peaks and over 980 waterfalls and famous springs, Luofushan is renowned as the "Premier Mountain of Lingnan."

Premier Mountain of Lingnan Taoist Sacred Land

Luofushan — the Premier Mountain of Lingnan, one of China's Ten Great Taoist Sacred Mountains. Luofushan is composed of Mount Luo and Mount Fu, featuring 432 peaks, over 980 waterfalls and famous springs, and 18 grotto wonders, offering exceptionally rich natural landscapes.

Luofushan is known as the "Seventh Grotto Heaven" and the "Thirty-Fourth Blessed Land" of Taoism. Ge Hong, the Eastern Jin Taoist scholar, once lived here in seclusion practicing alchemy and authored classics such as the Baopuzi. The Chongxu Temple on the mountain has a history of over 1,600 years and is the ancestral temple of Lingnan Taoism.

Chongxu Temple Millennium Sanctuary

Chongxu Temple is the most important Taoist structure on Luofushan. First built during the Xianhe era of the Eastern Jin dynasty (327 AD), it has a history of over 1,600 years. Built against the mountain, the temple is grand in scale and is the largest and oldest Taoist temple in the Lingnan region.

The temple enshrines the Three Pure Ones and the Immortal Ge Hong, attracting a steady stream of worshippers. Every year on the third day of the third lunar month, Ge Hong's birthday, Luofushan holds a grand Taoist ceremony that draws devotees from home and abroad. Chongxu Temple is also a National Key Cultural Relics Protection Unit.

Waterfalls & Springs Grotto Paradise

Luofushan boasts over 980 waterfalls and famous springs. Among them, Baishuimen Waterfall drops more than 100 meters, making it one of the largest waterfalls in Lingnan. Mountain streams gurgle, and springs are clear and sweet, providing a natural source of mountain spring water.

Luofushan also features 18 grotto wonders including Zhuming Cave and Penglai Cave, as well as summit hikes like Feiyun Peak (elevation 1,296 m). The mountain is covered with dense vegetation and fresh air, with extremely high concentrations of negative oxygen ions, making it a natural oxygen bar and summer retreat for the Pearl River Delta.
