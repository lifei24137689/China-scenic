◈ Lianzhou Underground River Brief

Lianzhou Underground River is the largest underground river karst cave in South China, featuring rivers, waterfalls, lakes, and spectacular stalactites. The underground river stretches 1,500 meters. Taking a boat through the cave, you will see stalactites of myriad shapes, like an underground palace. The cave maintains a constant temperature of 18-22°C year-round, making it a natural summer retreat.

Underground River Largest in South China

Lianzhou Underground River — the largest underground river karst cave in South China, stretching 1,500 meters. Taking a boat through the dark channels, you will see stalactites of myriad shapes on both sides, illuminated by colorful lights, creating a dreamlike scene that feels like entering a mysterious underground palace.

The underground river winds and twists, sometimes opening into grand halls and sometimes narrowing to just enough for one boat to pass. Along the way, you can see stalagmites, stone pillars, stone curtains, and various other karst landform wonders — the uncanny workmanship of nature is truly breathtaking.

Stalactite Wonders Eons in the Making

The stalactites inside the cave take on myriad forms — some cascade like waterfalls, some stand tall like bamboo shoots, and some hang like curtains. These stalactites were shaped over hundreds of millions of years by dripping water, with every centimeter of growth taking hundreds of years.

Illuminated by lights, the stalactites display a dazzling array of colors. An underground waterfall plunges from the cave ceiling, its sound echoing through the cavern, creating a mysterious and magnificent atmosphere. The cave also contains several underground lakes with mirror-like surfaces, reflecting the fantastical scenes of the stalactites.

Huangchuan Three Gorges Landscape Gallery

The Lianzhou Underground River scenic area not only includes the underground karst cave but also encompasses the magnificent landscapes of the Huangchuan Three Gorges. Comprising Longquan Gorge, Lengjia Gorge, and Yangtiao Gorge, the area features verdant mountains and jade-green river waters, earning it the reputation of being a "Lingnan Landscape Gallery."

The cave maintains a constant temperature of 18-22°C year-round, making it an ideal summer escape. The scenic area also features a geological science exhibition, introducing visitors to the formation of karst landforms and the underground river ecosystem — combining education with entertainment.
