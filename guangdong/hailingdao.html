China's Top Ten Most Beautiful Islands

Hailing Island Dajiao Bay — located in Yangjiang, Guangdong Province, is one of China's top ten most beautiful islands. In 2015, it was rated as a National 5A Tourist Attraction, world-renowned for its blue sea, silver beaches, coconut palms, and ocean breeze.

Dajiao Bay's beach stretches 2.5 kilometers, with fine white sand and crystal-clear waters. It is not only a paradise for coastal vacations but also an important node of the Maritime Silk Road, carrying a thousand years of maritime civilization.

Nanhai No.1 Shipwreck Museum

The Guangdong Maritime Silk Road Museum is located on Ten-Mile Silver Beach of Hailing Island, centered on the excavation, preservation, and research of the "Nanhai No.1" Song Dynasty shipwreck. It is the earliest, largest, and most intact ocean-going merchant ship discovered among shipwrecks worldwide.

Over 180,000 artifacts have been recovered from the "Nanhai No.1" shipwreck, including exquisite porcelain, gold, silverware, and copper coins, bearing witness to the prosperity of the Maritime Silk Road during the Song Dynasty. The museum employs a world-first integral salvage technique, moving the entire ship into a "Crystal Palace."

Ten-Mile Silver Beach Coastal Paradise

Ten-Mile Silver Beach is the most famous beach on Hailing Island, with fine white sand that has been recorded in the Guinness World Records. Attractions such as Dajiao Bay, Mawei Island, and Zhapo Fishing Port each offer unique characteristics, creating a rich and colorful coastal tourism tapestry on Hailing Island.

The best travel season on Hailing Island is summer (May-October), when the sea water is warm, ideal for swimming, surfing, diving, and other water activities. The seafood cuisine at Zhapo Fishing Port is also an unmissable experience.
