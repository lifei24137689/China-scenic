/// I · Mountain & Sea

Sea & Mountain Wonder

The Sanya Nanshan Cave Heaven Scenic Area has a history of over 800 years. It is a comprehensive tourist area centered on Taoist culture, longevity culture, and the South China Sea Dragon culture. The sea views, mountain views, rock formations, and cave landscapes complement each other, earning it the historical praise as "the finest landscape of Qiongya in eight hundred years, the best sea and mountain wonder in the southern seas."

Located in Nanshan Village, Yazhou District, Sanya City, the scenic area borders the South China Sea with a winding and extended coastline. Strange rocks, deep caves, and the sea merging with the sky form a magnificent southern landscape scroll. Since 2024, the scenic area has offered free admission to the public, allowing more people to experience this wonderful blend of nature and culture.

/// II · Longevity

Nanshan Evergreen Pine

The scenic area is home to the Dragon Blood Tree grove, known as the "Nanshan Evergreen Pine," one of the longest-living plants in the world, with some trees reaching thousands of years old. The Dragon Blood Tree, shaped like a giant umbrella, stands tall and sturdy with lush foliage. It is a living fossil among tropical plants and a unique natural cultural landscape of Cave Heaven.

The Evergreen Pine perfectly complements the Taoist culture of longevity. The ancient saying "May your longevity match the Nanshan Evergreen Pine" originates from this very place. For thousands of years, countless visitors have come here to pray for blessings and longevity under the ancient trees, experiencing the Taoist philosophy of harmony between heaven, earth, and humanity. This ancient grove has become a sacred site for visitors seeking blessings and longevity, carrying people's best wishes for health and a long life.

/// III · Culture

The Way of Nature

The area preserves cliff inscriptions left by literati throughout history. Inscriptions such as "Little Cave Heaven," "Fishing Terrace," "Sea & Mountain Wonder," "Immortal Footprint," and "Sword Testing Peak" bear witness to eight hundred years of cultural accumulation. Some of these carvings are bold and vigorous, while others are elegant and free, representing a perfect fusion of calligraphic art and natural landscape.

Today, Cave Heaven has become a famous wedding photography base in Sanya, with mountains and waters as witnesses and the sea and sky as matchmakers. Newlyweds make their vows between the sea and mountains, allowing this ancient Taoist blessed land to witness the romance of modern love. The blend of traditional culture and contemporary lifestyle has infused new vitality into this millennium-old blessed land.
