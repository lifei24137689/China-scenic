Discover the Water Town Charm

Zhouzhuang Ancient Town is located in Kunshan, Suzhou. First built in 1086 during the Northern Song Dynasty, it boasts over 900 years of history. The town is laid out along rivers, with streets connected by bridges, deep courtyards, high-gabled roofs, riverside galleries, covered walkways, bamboo railings, and waterside pavilions — all exuding an ancient tranquility.

Zhouzhuang is known as "China's No.1 Water Town" and is one of the six great ancient towns of Jiangnan. Over 60% of its residences still retain Ming and Qing architectural styles. Within just 0.47 square kilometers, there are nearly a hundred classical residences and over 60 brick-carved gate towers.

Twin Bridges Dream

Zhouzhuang's most famous attraction is the "Twin Bridges" — Shide Bridge and Yong'an Bridge. One runs horizontally, the other vertically; one has a square arch, the other a round one, resembling an ancient key, hence also called "Key Bridge". In 1984, painter Chen Yifei created an oil painting "Memories of Hometown" featuring the Twin Bridges, which was purchased by American oil tycoon Armand Hammer and gifted to Deng Xiaoping, bringing Zhouzhuang worldwide fame.

Beyond the Twin Bridges, Zhouzhuang has 14 ancient bridges from the Yuan, Ming, and Qing dynasties, including Fu'an Bridge and Zhenfeng Bridge. Each bridge has weathered centuries of wind and rain, their stone slabs polished smooth as mirrors by time.

/// 03

Waterside Living

Zhouzhuang's charm lies not only in its ancient architecture but also in its vibrant water town life. In the morning mist, boatwomen row past; in the afternoon sun, elders play chess by the bridges; at dusk, lanterns glow and red reflections dance on the water. This is not a museum — it's a living thousand-year-old town.

Take a black-canopied boat through the water alleys, listen to a boatwoman sing a Jiangnan folk tune, sip a cup of Granny Tea, and taste a bite of Wansan Pig Trotter — this is the most authentic way to experience Zhouzhuang. Through the seasons: spring's misty rain, summer's lotus-scented breeze, autumn's bright moon, and winter's light snow.
