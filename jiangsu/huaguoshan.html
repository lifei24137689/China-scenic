/// 01

Sun Wukong's Hometown

Located in Lianyungang, Huaguoshan is the legendary hometown of Sun Wukong from Journey to the West, and the inspiration for Wu Cheng'en's timeless masterpiece. Scattered across the mountain are countless attractions tied to the tale. Yunu Peak, at 624 meters, is the highest peak in Jiangsu.

"Not a single step of Journey to the West leaves this mountain," it is said. Huaguoshan holds the deepest cultural memory of the classic tale. Ancient trees, flowing springs, and cascading waterfalls adorn the mountain year-round — a mythical sea-island that blends rock formations, ocean views, historic sites, and legends.

/// 02

Water Curtain Cave · Sanyuan Palace

The Water Curtain Cave is Huaguoshan's most famous attraction. The waterfall at its entrance cascades like a curtain — it is said to be where Sun Wukong led the monkey tribe to dwell. Passing through the curtain reveals a hidden world within the cave, as if stepping into a mythical realm.

Sanyuan Palace, first built in the Tang Dynasty, is the largest ancient architectural complex on Huaguoshan. The mountainside also features Seventy-Two Caves, Bajie Stone, Tang Seng Cliff, and many other Journey to the West attractions — each holding a story from the epic.

/// 03

Sea Fairy Mountain

Huaguoshan blends mountain, sea, and stone into one. Ascend Yunu Peak and gaze into the distance — the entire Lianyungang port unfolds beneath you, with the sea and peaks complementing each other. The Ashoka Pagoda, a key national heritage site, has stood tall for over a millennium.

Every April, peach blossoms blanket Huaguoshan in a sea of pink, filling the air with fragrance. Summer and autumn turn the mountain into a cool retreat. After winter snow, Huaguoshan is cloaked in silver, adding to its mythical allure — each season offers a unique charm.
