01 / Taoist Ancestral Court

Jiuxiao Wanfu Palace — Millennial Temple of the Shangqing School

Maoshan is the birthplace of the Shangqing School of Taoism and ranks first among China's sacred Taoist mountains. Jiuxiao Wanfu Palace crowns the summit of Damao Peak, first built in the Han Dynasty over two thousand years ago. Yuanfu Wanning Palace houses the world's largest statue of Laozi, showcasing the profound depth of Taoist culture.

02 / Grotto Heaven & Blessed Land

Maoshan Taoists — Mystical Legends of the Foremost Blessed Land

Maoshan is hailed as the Taoist "Foremost Blessed Land, Eighth Grotto Heaven." Natural karst caves like Huayang Cave and Immortal Cave are sacred sites for Taoist cultivation. "Maoshan Taoist priests" became household names through films and folklore — tales of talisman drawing, incantation chanting, and spirit exorcism shroud this mountain in mystery. Mist and clouds swirl among the peaks, creating an ethereal, otherworldly realm.

03 / Red Memories

New Fourth Army Memorial — Historical Witness to the War Years

Maoshan is not only a sacred Taoist mountain but also one of China's six major anti-Japanese base areas. The New Fourth Army Memorial, nestled at the foot of Maoshan, chronicles the heroic resistance of the New Fourth Army in the Maoshan region during the war-torn years. Here, Taoist culture and red memories intertwine, revealing the profound historical depth of Maoshan.
