/// 01

World-Rare Three Cities Three Rivers

Chunqiu Yancheng is located in Wujin District, Changzhou City. It is one of China's oldest and most well-preserved ground city sites. Its unique "Three Cities, Three Rivers" layout — consisting of an outer city, inner city, and royal city with three encircling moats — is extremely rare nationwide and even worldwide.

Numerous precious artifacts have been unearthed during archaeological excavations, including dugout canoes and bronze vessels, proving this was an important city during the Spring and Autumn period. For 2,500 years, the three-city, three-river layout of Yancheng has remained largely intact, earning it the title "China's First City South of the Yangtze."

/// 02

A Journey Through Spring-Autumn Time

Yancheng Spring-Autumn Theme Park celebrates the culture of the Spring and Autumn period, recreating the historical scenes of the Hundred Schools of Thought and the Warring States. The "Hundred Schools Contend" themed area showcases Confucian, Daoist, Mohist, Legalist, and other philosophies, while the "Spring-Autumn Palace" reconstructs the court life of the era.

Visitors can take a dugout canoe cruise along the moat, traveling through 2,500 years of history. The park also features a large-scale live performance, "Spring-Autumn Grand Ceremony," which brings the stories of the Spring and Autumn period to life with stunning audiovisual effects.

Archaeological Treasures

The Yancheng site has yielded 4 dugout canoes (one of which is among the oldest in the world), a large number of bronze vessels, primitive celadon ware, and other precious artifacts, proving that this was already a prosperous city during the Spring and Autumn period.

The Yancheng Museum, located next to the ruins, systematically showcases the archaeological achievements of Yancheng and the culture of the Spring and Autumn period. The annual Spring-Autumn Culture Tourism Festival attracts a large number of visitors, breathing new life into this 2,500-year-old ancient city.
