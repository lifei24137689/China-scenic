SHAJIABANG · RED CLASSIC & WETLAND PARADISE

Shajiabang became a household name through the Peking opera "Shajiabang." Its reed marshes hide revolutionary memories. The Yushan Shanghu Scenic Area features mountains and lakes intertwined — Yushan is the foremost mountain of Wu culture, and Shanghu is named after Jiang Taigong's fishing legend. A unique blend of revolutionary heritage and wetland ecology, it is a perfect union of Jiangnan culture and natural beauty.

01 Red Classic

Reed Maze · Revolutionary Memories

Shajiabang is located on the shores of Yangcheng Lake in southern Changshu City. It gained nationwide fame through the modern Peking opera "Shajiabang." During the Second Sino-Japanese War, it served as a base for the New Fourth Army's Jiangnan Anti-Japanese Volunteer Force, and the story of the "Eighteen Pines" is still widely told today. The Shajiabang Revolutionary History Memorial Hall is a national patriotism education base.

Taking a sculling boat through the reed marshes, with dense reeds on both sides, feels like traveling back to those war-torn years. The reeds cover over 2,000 mu (about 133 hectares), with winding waterways forming a natural aquatic maze.

02 Mountains and Waters

Yushan Shanghu · Jiangnan Secret Realm

Yushan is an important cradle of Wu culture, historically known as Wumu Mountain. It was named after Yu Zhong, son of King Tai of Zhou, who ceded the throne and retired here. The mountain features historical relics such as Yanzi Tomb and Zhongyong Tomb, and the Yushan School of Painting is an important school in Chinese art history.

Shanghu is named after Jiang Taigong (Jiang Shang), who lived in seclusion fishing here during the late Shang dynasty. The lake is wide and crystal clear, featuring attractions such as Peach Blossom Island and Peony Garden. Each spring, hundreds of peony varieties bloom in splendor at the garden.

03 Ecological Wetland

Yangcheng Ecology · Wetland Paradise

Shajiabang Wetland Park covers a total area of 30 square kilometers, making it an important wetland ecosystem in eastern China. It is not only a vital habitat for numerous migratory birds but also the famous origin of Yangcheng Lake hairy crabs. In golden autumn, when the reeds turn yellow and crabs are at their prime with fragrant rice, it is the most beautiful season at Shajiabang.

The wetland also preserves historical districts such as Hengjing Old Street, where classic scenes from the Peking opera "Shajiabang" are frequently performed on ancient stages, allowing visitors to relive red classics while experiencing the charm of the water town.
