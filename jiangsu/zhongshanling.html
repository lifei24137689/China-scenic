/// 01

All Under Heaven

The Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum is located on the southern slope of Purple Mountain in Xuanwu District, Nanjing. It serves as the final resting place of Dr. Sun Yat-sen, the great pioneer of China's democratic revolution, along with its surrounding memorial structures. The mausoleum grounds cover over 80,000 square meters, with construction beginning in the spring of 1926 and completing in the summer of 1929.

The mausoleum faces a plain in front and is backed by verdant peaks, bordered by Linggu Temple to the east and Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum to the west. The entire complex is built along the mountain terrain, gradually ascending along a central axis from south to north. Key structures include the Bo'ai Archway, the Sacred Way, the Mausoleum Gate, the Stone Steps, the Stele Pavilion, the Memorial Hall, and the Tomb Chamber, all aligned along the central axis.

/// 02

Architectural Epic

The Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum was designed by renowned architect Lü Yanzhi, blending the essence of ancient Chinese architecture with Western architectural styles. The Bo'ai Archway at the mausoleum entrance is a granite archway inscribed with the characters "Bo Ai" (Universal Love) in Dr. Sun Yat-sen's calligraphy. From the archway to the Memorial Hall, there are 392 stone steps, symbolizing the 392 million Chinese people of that era.

The Memorial Hall is a palace-style building, with horizontal plaques inscribed "Nation," "Livelihood," and "People's Rights" on the lintels, and a vertical plaque bearing Dr. Sun Yat-sen's calligraphy "Heaven and Earth Upright Spirit" on the central door. Inside the hall stands a marble seated statue of Dr. Sun Yat-sen, with the full text of his "Fundamentals of National Reconstruction" engraved on the walls.

Zhongshan Sublime

The Zhongshan Scenic Area, where the Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum is situated, is Nanjing's most celebrated scenic region, covering approximately 31 square kilometers. Known in ancient times as Jinling Mountain, it gained its name during the Three Kingdoms period when Zhuge Liang praised it as "Zhongshan, the coiled dragon." Here, mountains, waters, cityscapes, and forests merge seamlessly, with natural and cultural landscapes complementing each other.

Standing at the highest point of the mausoleum and gazing into the distance, the entire city of Nanjing unfolds before your eyes. Plum blossoms bloom vibrantly in spring, while golden plane trees adorn the autumn scenery — each season offers a unique vista. The Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum is not only a sacred site for remembering a great leader but also an exceptional place to experience the spirit of Nanjing.
