/// 01

Water Town Heritage

Located in Wujiang District, Suzhou, 18 km from downtown Suzhou, Tongli Ancient Town is one of the best-preserved water towns in Jiangsu Province. The town is divided into seven islets by 15 small rivers forming a "#" shape, all connected by 49 ancient bridges.

Famous for its "small bridges, flowing water, and homes" layout, Tongli's buildings stand along the water, creating streets, markets, and gardens shaped by the waterways. Walking on the cobblestone paths, with white-walled, gray-tiled roofs reflecting in the water and dark-canopied boats gliding by, feels like traveling back through a thousand years.

Tuisi Garden Charm

Tuisi Garden is the most famous garden in Tongli Ancient Town. Built in the 11th year of the Guangxu reign (1885), its name derives from the Zuo Commentary: "In office, devote oneself to loyalty; in retirement, reflect and mend one's ways." It was designated a World Cultural Heritage site in 2001.

Covering only 9.8 mu (about 1.6 acres), Tuisi Garden encapsulates the grandeur of Qing Dynasty garden design. Pavilions, terraces, towers, corridors, boats, bridges, and waterside pavilions are all built against the water, creating a uniquely elegant style. Its "three marvels" — the Dry Boat, the Sky Bridge, and the Mianyun Pavilion — are truly breathtaking.

Three Bridges Charm

Tongli has a folk tradition of "Walking the Three Bridges." Taiping Bridge, Jili Bridge, and Changqing Bridge form a triangular layout. Local residents, during weddings and celebrations, carry bridal sedan chairs across these three bridges to the sound of drums and music, praying for peace, good fortune, and longevity.

Beyond the Three Bridges, Tongli is home to many other ancient bridges such as Siben Bridge, Fuguan Bridge, and Pu'an Bridge, each with its own unique story and design. Bridges are the soul of this water town, connecting life on both banks as well as the past with the present.
