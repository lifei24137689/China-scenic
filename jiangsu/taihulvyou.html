LAKE TAI · THE BOUNDLESS MAJESTY OF WATER AND SKY

Lake Tai is the third-largest freshwater lake in China, spanning a vast and misty expanse of 36,000 hectares. The Suzhou Lake Tai Scenic Area encompasses Dongshan, Xishan, and the lakeside areas — a landscape of islands within lakes, mountains within islands, and temples within mountains, forming the most magnificent scroll of Jiangnan scenery.

01 Lake Peninsula

Dongshan · The Land of Biluochun Tea

Dongshan is a peninsula extending into Lake Tai, surrounded by water on three sides. Since ancient times, it has been a fertile land of fish and rice, a mountain of flowers and fruits. Dongshan is the birthplace of Biluochun tea, one of China's top ten teas. Every year around the Qingming Festival, tea leaves sprout across the mountainsides, filling the air with fragrance.

On the mountain lie historic sites such as the Southern Song painted clay arhats at Zijin Temple and the Carved Flower Building. The Carved Flower Building is hailed as the "First Building of Jiangnan" for its exquisite brick and wood carvings. Ascending Moli Peak offers a panoramic view of the misty waters of Lake Tai.

"The vast Lake Tai spans thirty-six thousand hectares — how many gentle breezes and bright moons has it seen? The seventy-two peaks stretch endlessly green, white waves like silk ribbons winding around Wu and Yue."

02 Isle in the Mist

Xishan · The Ethereal Wonderland

Xishan is the largest island in Lake Tai, surrounded by water on all sides, like a pearl embedded in azure waves. The island's Piaomiao Peak stands as the highest among Lake Tai's seventy-two peaks. From its summit, the vast expanse of Lake Tai unfolds beneath your feet — the name "Piaomiao" (ethereal) is by no means undeserved.

Xishan is renowned for its lake delicacies and fruits, producing loquats, bayberries, and citrus in abundance. Linwu Cave is celebrated as the "Ninth Grotto-Heaven Under Heaven," a famed Taoist sacred site. Stone Man Mountain (Shigong Shan) stands by the lake with its striking rock formations and breathtaking scenery.

03 Lake & Mountain Views

Taihu Landscape · A Painterly Year-Round Beauty

The most enchanting aspect of Lake Tai lies in the ever-changing interplay of light and landscape. In spring, the lake is veiled in mist; in summer, lotus flowers stretch to the horizon; in autumn, reeds turn golden; and in winter, the lake lies still as a mirror. At dawn, a light mist drapes over Taihu; at dusk, the setting sun casts molten gold across the waves — each season offers its own unique beauty and endless delight.

Lake Tai's "Three Whites" — whitefish, white shrimp, and silverfish — are the lake's freshest delicacies. The lake cuisine, represented by Taihu boat dishes, offers a true feast for visitors. The ancient towns of Guangfu and Mudu along the lakeshore preserve the authentic charm of Jiangnan's water towns.

BOUNDLESS HORIZON

Sail Lake Tai, Feel the Boundless Horizon

The misty vastness of 36,000 hectares, the verdant stretch of seventy-two peaks — Lake Tai, with its grandeur and beauty, stands as the most magnificent landscape painting in the land of Jiangnan. Whether savoring tea in Dongshan, exploring the wonders of Xishan, or boating on the lake, it is a poetic sanctuary for the soul.
