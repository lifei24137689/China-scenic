YUANTOUZHU · THE SUPREME SPOT ON LAKE TAI

Yuantouzhu is a peninsula lying on the northwest shore of Lake Tai. Its name comes from a giant rock protruding into the lake that resembles a divine turtle raising its head. Mr. Guo Moruo praised it as "Taihu's finest spot, indeed at Yuantouzhu." Every March, over 30,000 cherry blossom trees bloom, making it one of the world's top three cherry blossom destinations.

01 World Cherry Blossom Destination

Cherry Blossom Valley · Ocean of Pink

Yuantouzhu is home to over 30,000 cherry blossom trees spanning nearly a hundred varieties, making it China's largest and most diverse cherry blossom destination. From late March to early April, Cherry Blossom Valley and the banks of Changchun Bridge are draped in clouds of pink, creating the breathtaking scene that inspired the saying "Taihu's finest spot, indeed at Yuantouzhu."

Night cherry blossom viewing is a signature experience at Yuantouzhu. After dark, lights illuminate the blossoms, and the lake surface mirrors the pink floral reflections in a dreamlike display. Changchun Bridge offers the best vantage point, surrounded by cherry trees along what is known as "Cherry Embankment."

"Taihu's finest spot, indeed at Yuantouzhu. Across thirty-six thousand acres, cherry blossoms in glorious bloom."

02 Ascend for a Panoramic View

Luding Greeting Dawn · Lake Tai Panorama

Luding Mountain is the highest point in the Yuantouzhu scenic area, standing at about 80 meters above sea level. At its summit stands Shutian Pavilion, the best vantage point for overlooking the full expanse of Lake Tai and witnessing the sunrise. "Luding Greeting Dawn" is counted among Wuxi's Fifteen Scenic Views of Lake Tai.

From Shutian Pavilion, the Seventy-Two Peaks of Lake Tai scatter faintly across the vast blue waters, with Sanshan Island floating like a celestial mountain in the lake. On clear days, the mountain contours of Suzhou on the opposite shore are visible on the horizon.

03 Immortal Isle Penglai

Taihu Immortal Isle · Penglai on the Lake

Sanshan Island, also known as Taihu Immortal Isle, is composed of four small islets — East Duck, West Duck, Daji, and Xiaoji — floating like the mythical Penglai paradise upon the emerald waves of Lake Tai. A ferry ride from Yuantouzhu to the isle offers stunning lake and mountain scenery along the way.

The island features Taoist cultural landmarks including the Taihu Immortal Isle Archway, Meeting Immortals Bridge, and Lingxiao Palace, blending natural beauty with spiritual heritage. Every autumn, the island's red leaves blaze like fire against the azure lake waters, creating an intoxicating vista.
