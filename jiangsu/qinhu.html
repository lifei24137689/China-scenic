QIN LAKE · THE WATER FAIR OF CHINA

Qin Lake National Wetland Park is the largest wetland park in Jiangsu Province. The annual Qingming Qintong Boat Festival is a national intangible cultural heritage, renowned as the "Water Fair of China" with thousands of boats racing across the lake. The wetland is home to herds of elk and flocks of migratory birds, an ecological paradise where humans and nature coexist in harmony.

01 Green Lung of Jiangsu

Wetland Ecology · Bird Sanctuary

Covering a total area of 26 square kilometers, Qin Lake National Wetland Park is the largest wetland ecosystem in Jiangsu Province. With its dense water networks and thriving reeds, it provides an ideal habitat for hundreds of bird species. Every autumn and winter, tens of thousands of migratory birds stop here to rest and overwinter, creating a spectacular sight.

Taking a rowboat through the reed maze, you will be greeted by rippling blue waters and a gentle breeze, with reeds rustling on both banks. Occasionally, an egret startled into flight breaks the tranquil water surface. The water quality of Qin Lake maintains the national Class II standard year-round, so clear that you can see the bottom.

"A thousand boats set sail, hundreds of vessels race; a water fair unlike any other in the world."

02 Water Fair

Qintong Boat Gathering · Thousand Boats Set Sail

The Qintong Boat Festival is a unique folk celebration exclusive to the Qin Lake area, with a history spanning over 800 years, recognized as a national intangible cultural heritage. Every Qingming Festival, hundreds of boats from surrounding towns gather on Qin Lake — pole boats, rowboats, flower boats, dragon boats — a magnificent race of a thousand vessels.

During the festival, hundreds of thousands of visitors flock to Qin Lake. The lake surface is adorned with colorful flags, the air filled with the sound of drums and gongs, while the shores are packed with bustling crowds. This unique water folk event is hailed as the "Water Fair" and stands as one of China's most distinctive folk celebrations.

03 Elk & Ancient Town

Elk Homeland · Qintong Ancient Town

Hundreds of elk live freely within the Qin Lake wetland. This unique Chinese species, known as the "four不像" (four unlikes), roams and breeds here in its natural habitat. Visitors can take a sightseeing vehicle deep into the elk park to observe these gentle creatures foraging and playing in the wetlands up close.

On the lakeshore, the ancient town of Qintong preserves a wealth of Ming and Qing architecture, with gray bricks, black tiles, small bridges, and flowing streams. The town is famous for the "Eight Fresh Delicacies of Qin Lake" — including Qin Lake crab, Qin Lake shrimp, and Qin Lake turtle — offering a culinary experience not to be missed.
