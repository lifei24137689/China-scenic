Jin Shan · Jiao Shan · Bei Gu Shan Zhenjiang, Jiangsu 5A

THREE HILLS OF ZHENJIANG · THE FINEST LANDSCAPE UNDER HEAVEN

The three hills of Zhenjiang — Jin Shan, Jiao Shan, and Bei Gu Shan — are the most renowned scenic attractions along the lower reaches of the Yangtze River. Jin Shan is famous for Jinshan Temple and the legend of "The Flooding of Jinshan"; Jiao Shan floats on the river like a piece of jade, rich with stele inscriptions; Bei Gu Shan is celebrated for its Three Kingdoms culture, where Xin Qiji left behind timeless poetic masterpieces.

01 The Flooding of Jinshan

Jinshan Temple · Millennial Monastery

Located northwest of downtown Zhenjiang, Jin Shan was originally an island in the Yangtze River, later connected to the south bank by sediment deposition. Jinshan Temple was first built during the Eastern Jin Dynasty, with a history spanning over 1,600 years. The temple is built along the mountain, with halls and pavilions stacked layer upon layer, forming a unique layout known as "the temple enveloping the mountain" from the foot to the summit.

Jinshan Temple is widely known for the legend of "The Flooding of Jinshan" from the tale of the White Snake. Relics such as Fahai Cave and White Dragon Cave still remain, attracting countless visitors to explore this millennium-old legend. Ascending the Cishou Pagoda, one can gaze into the distance at the Yangtze River stretching like a silk ribbon, where the river and sky merge into one.

"Where to gaze upon our sacred land? The view from Beigu Tower fills the eye. How many dynasties have risen and fallen? Endless. The Yangtze rolls on endlessly."

— Xin Qiji, "Song of the South: Ascending Beigu Pavilion at Jingkou" —
02 Emerald Jade Floating on the River

Jiaoshan Stele Forest · Calligraphy Sanctuary

Jiao Shan stands proudly in the midst of the Yangtze River, surrounded by water on all sides, like a piece of emerald jade floating on the river surface. The Dinghui Temple on the mountain is a renowned ancient monastery south of the Yangtze, while the Jiaoshan Stele Forest is hailed as the "Number One Stele Forest South of the Yangtze," housing over 400 historical stone inscriptions. Among them, the "Yihe Ming" is revered as the "Ancestor of Large Characters," holding an extremely lofty position in the history of Chinese calligraphy.

Climbing to the Xijiang Tower on Jiao Shan, one can overlook the mighty Yangtze rolling eastward, with the river breeze blowing freely, bringing immense joy and serenity. The ancient artillery fort on Jiao Shan bears witness to the heroic resistance of Zhenjiang's soldiers and civilians against the British during the Opium War, serving as an important base for patriotic education.

03 Three Kingdoms Epic

Bei Gu Mountain · The Finest Landscape Under Heaven

Bei Gu Mountain is renowned for its precipitous terrain, with cliffs on three sides plunging straight into the river's heart, forming a strategically formidable landscape. Emperor Wu of the Liang Dynasty once inscribed the words "The Finest Landscape Under Heaven" in praise of its majesty. The Ganlu Temple on the mountain is famous for the story of "Liu Bei's Marriage Alliance" from the Romance of the Three Kingdoms, and the Duojing Tower was praised by Mi Fu as the "Number One Tower Under Heaven."

Xin Qiji ascended Beigu Pavilion twice, writing the timeless masterpieces "Yong Yu Le" and "Song of the South." Today, standing atop Beigu Tower and gazing into the distance, the Yangtze River still surges mightily, while Jin Shan and Jiao Shan face each other from afar — a millennium of cultural heritage flowing endlessly through the landscape.

TIMELESS LANDSCAPE

Ascend the Three Hills, Feel the Millennial Heritage

The Flooding of Jinshan, the stele inscriptions of Jiao Shan, the Three Kingdoms saga of Bei Gu Shan — the three hills of Zhenjiang carry some of the most brilliant chapters of Chinese culture. The Yangtze River surges endlessly here, history layers upon layer, and every ascent is a dialogue across a thousand years.
