HOMETOWN OF ZHOU ENLAI · THE PEOPLE'S PREMIER

Located in Huai'an District, Huai'an City, Jiangsu Province, the Hometown of Zhou Enlai is where Premier Zhou Enlai was born and spent his childhood. The scenic area includes Zhou Enlai's Former Residence, Zhou Enlai Memorial Hall, and the Grand Canal Governor's Office, serving as a National Patriotic Education Demonstration Base and a Major Historical and Cultural Site Protected at the National Level.

01 Childhood Home

Zhou Enlai's Former Residence · Cradle of a Great Man

Zhou Enlai's Former Residence is located at No. 7 Fuma Lane, Huai'an District, Huai'an City. It is a modest residential courtyard built during the Ming and Qing dynasties. On March 5, 1898, Zhou Enlai was born here and spent his childhood years. The residence preserves its original late-Qing and early-Republican architectural style, divided into eastern and western courtyards.

The eastern courtyard is where Zhou Enlai was born, while the western courtyard served as a study and reception area for the Zhou family. The residence features Zhou Enlai's childhood study room, a kitchen, a well, and other relics. A stone slab beside the well bears the inscription "When you drink the water, remember its source," evoking deep reverence for the great man.

02 Great Achievements

Zhou Enlai Memorial Hall · Eternal Legacy

The Zhou Enlai Memorial Hall covers an area of approximately 1.6 square kilometers and consists of the main hall, auxiliary halls, and a bronze statue square. The main hall's architecture carries profound symbolism — viewed from above, it resembles a crabapple blossom, Premier Zhou's favorite flower; viewed from the side, it forms a "Ren" (person) shape, symbolizing "the People's Premier loves the people." The memorial hall is situated on an island surrounded by water.

The hall features large-scale photographic and artifact exhibitions of Zhou Enlai's life, including historical photographs and precious relics from his work in diplomacy and domestic affairs. A full-body bronze statue of Premier Zhou Enlai stands in the bronze statue square, commanding solemn respect.

03 Millennium Old Town

Huai'an Government Office · Canal Hub

The Hometown of Zhou Enlai scenic area also includes historical and cultural relics closely tied to the ancient city of Huai'an. The Grand Canal Governor's Office site is an important part of China's Grand Canal culture, bearing witness to Huai'an's glorious history as the "Canal Capital." Wu Cheng'en's Former Residence is also nearby, where the classic novel Journey to the West was born in this very land.

Huai'an is the birthplace of Huaiyang cuisine, one of China's four great culinary traditions. While visiting the Hometown of Zhou Enlai, savoring authentic Huaiyang delicacies offers a unique experience. This is a meeting point of revolutionary culture and canal culture, where the spirit of a great man coexists with the charm of a waterside town.
