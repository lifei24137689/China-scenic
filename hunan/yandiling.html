The First Mausoleum in China

The Yandiling (Emperor Yan Mausoleum) is the resting place of Yan Emperor Shennong, the legendary ancestor of Chinese civilization, renowned as "The First Mausoleum in China." Emperor Yan Shennong tasted hundreds of herbs to heal the people, cultivated five grains to nourish the nation, and crafted farming tools to advance agriculture, ushering in the dawn of Chinese agricultural civilization.

Five thousand years ago, Emperor Yan Shennong ignited the spark of Chinese civilization on this very land. Today, the Yandiling stands majestically in Zhuzhou, Hunan, becoming a spiritual pilgrimage site for descendants of Yan and Huang around the world to trace their roots and pay homage to their ancestors.

/// 02

Ancestral Worship Sanctuary

The Shennong Grand Hall, magnificent with its golden tiles and vermilion walls, is the core venue for worshipping Emperor Yan Shennong. The Imperial Stele Garden houses stone inscriptions of sacrificial odes by emperors throughout the dynasties, recording millennia of ancestral worship traditions. On the Sacred Flame Terrace, the torch of Chinese civilization is passed down from generation to generation, never to be extinguished.

During the Double Ninth Festival each year, descendants of Yan and Huang from home and abroad gather here for a grand ancestral worship ceremony at the Yandiling. Drums and music resound, ten thousand people pay homage together, the scene solemn and dignified, embodying the deep bloodline bond of the Chinese nation sharing the same roots.

/// 03

Cradle of Civilization

The Yandiling is not only a historical site but also a vital symbol of Chinese civilization. As a pilgrimage destination for Chinese people worldwide to trace their roots, the Yandiling embodies the boundless reverence of the Chinese nation for its humanities ancestor and unites the bloodline identity of descendants of Yan and Huang across the globe.

This is the birthplace of agricultural civilization and the cradle of traditional Chinese medicine and herbal culture. Emperor Yan Shennong's great achievements — tasting hundreds of herbs, cultivating five grains, crafting farming tools, and establishing markets — continue to influence the lives of the Chinese people to this day. Spring and autumn offer the clearest skies and the most pleasant weather, making them the best seasons to pay homage to the ancestor.
