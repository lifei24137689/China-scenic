/// 01

Hometown of the Great

Shaoshan Chong, a small mountain village in central Hunan, is world-famous for being the birthplace of the great leader Mao Zedong. Nestled in green hills, Mao's former residence stands quietly at Shangwuchang—a farmhouse built of earth and wood that witnessed the beginning of the Chinese revolution.

In front of the residence, lotus ponds reflect the sunlight and green bamboos cast their shade. On December 26, 1893, Mao Zedong was born here, spending his childhood and youth in this very place. Today, it has become a sacred destination for China's red tourism.

/// 02

Red Memories

The Mao Zedong Bronze Statue Square is solemn and majestic, with the bronze statue standing tall. Every day, countless visitors come to pay their respects and lay flowers. The square is the spiritual heart of Shaoshan, carrying the deep reverence of the Chinese people for the great leader.

The Mao Zedong Memorial Museum houses a vast collection of precious artifacts and historical materials, comprehensively presenting the magnificent life of the great man. Dishui Cave—the mysterious "Western Cave"—was where Mao Zedong stayed and worked during his returns to his hometown.

Shaofeng Peak

Shaofeng Peak, one of the seventy-two peaks of Mount Heng in Nanyue, stands at 519.1 meters above sea level, making it the highest peak in Shaoshan. From the summit, rolling mountains stretch into the mist—"Shaofeng Peaks Embraced in Verdure" has been celebrated as one of the "Eight Scenic Wonders of Xiangtan" since ancient times.

Natural beauty and red culture blend perfectly here. "The East is Red, the Sun Rises"—as the morning sun rises over Shaofeng Peak, golden light bathes the hometown of the great leader, awakening this sacred land that carries the memories of the Chinese revolution.
