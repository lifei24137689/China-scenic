/// Chapter I

Millennia-old Academy

Yuelu Academy is one of the four great ancient academies of China, founded in 976 AD during the Northern Song dynasty. Through the Song, Yuan, Ming, and Qing dynasties, its lectures never ceased and its academic lineage continued unbroken. The couplet at its main gate — "Chu produces talent, flourishing here" — fully expresses the profound influence of this millennia-old academy on Huxiang culture.

As one of the oldest academies in the world, Yuelu Academy still continues its thousand-year educational tradition as a teaching institution of Hunan University. The academy's architectural complex is elegantly classical, with lecture halls, library buildings, and the Confucian Temple arranged in picturesque order, every corner exuding a rich academic atmosphere and cultural heritage.

/// Chapter II

Aiwanting in Autumn

Aiwanting Pavilion is located in Qingfeng Gorge on Yuelu Mountain. Built in 1792 during the Qing dynasty, it is one of China's four great pavilions, alongside the Drunkard Old Pavilion in Chuzhou, Anhui, the Mid-Lake Pavilion at West Lake in Hangzhou, and the Taoran Pavilion in Beijing. The pavilion's name derives from Du Mu's famous verse: "I stop my carriage to sit and admire the evening-tinged maple grove; its frosted leaves are redder than the February flowers."

In autumn, Yuelu Mountain is covered in a blaze of red leaves — the most beautiful season in Changsha. Aiwanting Pavilion is nestled among the crimson maples, the ancient Lushan Temple's bells echo through the distance, and millennia-old towering trees stand tall, where countless literati have left behind moving poems.

/// Chapter III

Orange Isle Head

Orange Isle is a narrow sandbar in the Xiang River, about five kilometers long, like an emerald embedded in the river's blue waves. In 1925, a young Mao Zedong visited Orange Isle and wrote "Changsha · To the Tune of Spring in Pleasure Garden," whose heroic lines — "Who rules over the vast earth's fate?" — still stir the heart today.

The youthful Mao Zedong art sculpture standing at Orange Isle Head, 32 meters tall and 83 meters long, has become Changsha's most iconic urban landmark. Every autumn, thousands of orange trees on the isle are heavy with golden fruit, their fragrance mingling with the blue waters of the Xiang River and the high-rises on both banks, creating a unique urban landscape painting.
