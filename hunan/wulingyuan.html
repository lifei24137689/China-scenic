/// 01 · Wulingyuan

Three Thousand Peaks

Wulingyuan is world-renowned for its unique quartz sandstone peak forest landform. 380 million years of geological transformation have shaped over three thousand towering peaks, rising like swords and pillars piercing the sky.

It is China's first national forest park and the inspiration for the floating Hallelujah Mountains in the film Avatar. Amid swirling clouds, the stone peaks loom in and out of view, as if transporting you to an alien world.

/// 02 · Wulingyuan

Fairyland Exploration

Yuanjiajie is the core area of Wulingyuan, featuring breathtaking sights such as the Pillar of Heaven (Hallelujah Mountain), the Lost Soul Terrace, and the First Bridge Under Heaven. Tianzi Mountain is famous for its sea of clouds, winter snow, and sunrises.

The Golden Whip Stream meanders among the peaks with crystal-clear water. Huangshi Village offers panoramic views of Wulingyuan, while Yangjiajie is known for its precipitous terrain. Every spot is worth exploring in depth.

/// 03 · Wulingyuan

World Heritage

In 1992, Wulingyuan was inscribed on the UNESCO World Natural Heritage List. The World Heritage Committee described it as "a rare natural wonder."

It is renowned for its extraordinary quartz sandstone peak forest, possessing immeasurable scientific and aesthetic value. Clouds, peaks, streams, and forests together create a breathtaking natural tableau.

/// 04 · Tianmen Mountain

Heaven's Gate Opens

Known in ancient times as Yunmeng Mountain, Tianmen Mountain is the highest peak in Zhangjiajie, named after the natural wonder of Heaven's Gate Cave. This cave penetrates through the mountain from north to south, standing 131.5 m high, 57 m wide, and 60 m deep, like a gateway to heaven — the world's highest natural穿-through mountain cave.

"Heaven's Gate breaks apart and the Chu River opens" — the magnificent scene described by Tang dynasty poet Li Bai finds its most perfect expression in this famed western Hunan mountain. Geologists believe Heaven's Gate Cave is a karst wonder formed by millions of years of dissolution and collapse.

/// 05 · Tianmen Mountain

The Heavenly Road

The Heavenly Road winds around the mountainside of Tianmen Mountain, stretching 10.77 km with 99 sharp turns, coiling like a giant dragon. Known as the "No. 1 Highway Wonder Under Heaven," each turn is thrilling and the scenery along the way is magnificent.

Driving on the Heavenly Road, cliffs and rolling seas of clouds pass by the window, as if heading toward the horizon. The 99 turns symbolize the philosophical concept of "all things return to one," making it one of the most challenging experiences at Tianmen Mountain.

/// 06 · Tianmen Mountain

Cloud Summit Realm

The Tianmen Mountain Glass Skywalk hangs on a cliff at 1,430 m above sea level, with a bottomless abyss below — walking on it feels like strolling through the clouds. The Ghost Valley Plank Path winds along the sheer cliffs, adding even more thrill and excitement.

Tianmen Mountain Temple, first built in the Tang Dynasty, sits atop the peak and is a renowned Buddhist sacred site in western Hunan. Amid morning bells and evening drums, the sea of clouds rises, and each season offers its own wonders: spring flowers, summer clouds, autumn red leaves, and winter snowscapes.
