/// Chapter I

The Most Elegant of the Five

Mount Hengshan, the southern great mountain among China's Five Great Mountains, is renowned as "the most elegant of all." Its 72 peaks meander for hundreds of miles, with layered green ridges and towering summits a landscape that has inspired endless poetry and prose from literati across the ages.

As an outstanding representative of southern China's mountain scenery, Hengshan attracts countless visitors with its exquisite natural beauty and profound cultural heritage. Though less majestic than Mount Tai, it surpasses all five great mountains in elegance, presenting a picturesque scene in every season, shrouded in mist and clouds.

/// Chapter II

Buddhist & Taoist Sacred Site

Mount Hengshan is one of the rare sacred mountains in China where Buddhism and Taoism coexist in harmony. Taoist temples stand alongside Buddhist monasteries, their chants intertwining for over a millennium, creating a unique landscape of religious culture.

The Grand Temple of Mount Heng is the largest ancient architectural complex south of the Yangtze River, known as "the finest temple in Jiangnan." The Zhurong Hall atop Zhurong Peak draws pilgrims in great numbers, while Fangguang Temple rests deep in a secluded valley, its Buddhist chants echoing through the ages every site is a confluence of faith and culture.

/// Chapter III

Sea of Clouds & Sunrise

The sea of clouds and sunrise at Mount Hengshan are truly a breathtaking spectacle. At dawn, ascending to the top of Zhurong Peak, one witnesses rolling clouds illuminated by radiant sunshine, with the red sun bursting forth from the sea of clouds in magnificent splendor. After winter snowfall, the mountain is cloaked in silver white, adding an ink-wash poetic charm.

Spring brings blooming mountain flowers, summer offers lush green canopies, autumn paints the forests in vibrant colors, and winter drapes everything in pure white snow. Through the cycle of seasons, Hengshan reveals its unique beauty, with spring and autumn being the most pleasant times for visiting and sightseeing.
