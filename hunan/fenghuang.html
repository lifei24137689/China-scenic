/// Chapter I

Tuo River Ancient Town

Fenghuang Ancient Town was built along the Tuo River, with clear, rippling waters flowing through the city. Rows of stilted houses line both banks, and blue stone paths wind their way, connecting every corner of the old town into a long, lyrical ink painting. The Rainbow Bridge spans the Tuo River like a rainbow lying across the water, one of the town's most iconic landmarks.

The ancient town was first built during the Tang Dynasty. After thousands of years of weathering, it still retains its Ming and Qing architectural character. Stilted houses rise by the water, clinging to the hillside, while ancient city towers, ancestral halls, and temples are scattered throughout—everywhere bearing the marks of time.

/// Chapter II

Wanming Pagoda

The Wanming Pagoda stands on the eastern bank of the Tuo River, complementing the stilted houses on both sides. It is one of the most iconic sights of Fenghuang Ancient Town. The six-cornered, seven-story pagoda is reflected in the emerald waters of the Tuo River, and as the sun sets, the shimmering pagoda silhouette creates an unforgettable scene.

The pagoda is built entirely of blue brick, with flying eaves and upturned corners on each level, wind chimes gently swaying. Climbing to the top offers a panoramic view of the ancient town—the Tuo River like a ribbon, stilted houses like a painting, distant mountains like brows—a sight that refreshes the mind and makes visitors linger.

/// Chapter III

Ancient Streets

The flagstone streets of Fenghuang Ancient Town crisscross in a labyrinth, with historic sites like the Tianhou Temple and the City God Temple scattered throughout. The Tianhou Temple, first built during the Ming Dynasty, is dedicated to Mazu and features exquisite architecture. The City God Temple draws crowds of worshippers, bearing witness to the town's faith and traditions.

Strolling along the blue stone-paved lanes, well-preserved Ming and Qing buildings line both sides, with carved wooden window lattices and sweeping eaves showcasing the masterful craftsmanship of Xiangxi artisans. Old shops, tea houses, and silver stores stand along the streets, telling tales of past prosperity and bustle.
