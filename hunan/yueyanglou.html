/// Chapter I

Famous Tower Under Heaven

Yueyang Tower was first built in the late Eastern Han Dynasty. After a thousand years of wind and rain, it stands as the foremost of the three great towers south of the Yangtze River. Fan Zhongyan's timeless essay "Record of Yueyang Tower," with its immortal lines "Be the first to worry about the world's troubles, and the last to rejoice in its joys," has immortalized Yueyang Tower as an important symbol of the Chinese literati spirit.

Overlooking Dongting Lake, Yueyang Tower features three stories with flying eaves and dougong brackets, constructed entirely of wood. From its heights, visitors can take in the magnificent vista of "Dongting's waters under heaven, Yueyang's tower above the world." For centuries, countless poets and scholars have left their timeless verses here.

/// Chapter II

Dongting's Splendid Scenery

"Dongting's waters under heaven"——the vast Eight-Hundred-Li Dongting Lake stretches endlessly, embracing distant mountains and swallowing the Yangtze River. From Yueyang Tower, the lake and mountain vistas unfold in all their grandeur, lifting the spirit and refreshing the mind.

Dongting Lake is one of China's largest freshwater lakes, rich in historical culture and ecological value. Junshan Island lies within the lake like a gleaming pearl, echoing Yueyang Tower from afar, forming a unique landscape of "tower and island as one."

/// Chapter III

Junshan Jewel

Junshan Island is a dazzling pearl in Dongting Lake. Once known as "Dongting Mountain," it is famous for its mottled bamboo (Xiangfei bamboo) and Silver Needle tea. Bamboo groves sway in the breeze, tea plantations fill the air with fragrance, and the legend of the Xiang River maidens' tear-stained bamboo adds a touch of poignant romance to Junshan.

Junshan Silver Needle is one of China's top ten famous teas. The tea buds stand straight like needles, standing upright after brewing, with a clear apricot-yellow liquor. Spring outings and autumn tea-tasting make Junshan Island a delightful destination year-round, especially during spring and autumn.
