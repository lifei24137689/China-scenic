Canyon Sky Bridge

Aizhai Bridge is located in Aizhai Town, Jishou City, Xiangxi Prefecture, Hunan Province, spanning the Dehang Grand Canyon. It is a key control project of the Baomao Expressway. With a main span of 1,176 meters and a deck height of 355 meters above the valley floor, it is one of the world's longest-spanning steel truss suspension bridges across a canyon.

Completed in 2012, the bridge connects east and west, turning a natural chasm into a thoroughfare, fundamentally transforming Xiangxi's historically isolated transportation network. Viewed from afar, the bridge appears as a silver rainbow floating above the misty canyon, complementing the lush green mountains, earning it the title "World's No.1 Canyon Bridge."

Dehang Grand Canyon

Dehang means "beautiful grand canyon" in the Miao language. With towering peaks, babbling streams, and cascading waterfalls, it features classic karst landforms. The canyon has exceptionally high levels of negative oxygen ions, making it a natural forest oxygen bar. Walking along the canyon trails, you will find waterfalls, springs, and ancient trees everywhere.

Aizhai Canyon also includes the famous "Shibadong" — a cluster of eighteen traditional Miao villages. Here, intact Miao cultural traditions and architectural styles are preserved, with intangible cultural heritage such as Miao drum dance, batik, and silver ornaments passed down through generations. It was here that the important principle of "targeted poverty alleviation — seeking truth from facts and adapting measures to local conditions" was first proposed.
