/// 01

Misty Wonderland

Xiaodongjiang River is world-famous for its dreamlike morning mist. From April to October each year, layers of mist rise over the river in the early morning and at dusk, creating a surreal, ethereal landscape. A small black-canopied boat glides through the fog, and the moment a fisherman casts his net has been captured by countless photographers, becoming the most iconic image of Dongjiang Lake.

Known as one of China's most poetic rivers and lakes, every wisp of mist seems to carry the spirit of ink-wash painting, making it a photographer's lifelong dream destination.

/// 02

Switzerland of the East

The water quality of Dongjiang Lake meets national Grade 1 drinking water standards. Its crystal-clear, emerald-green waters resemble a giant jade inlaid in the landscape of southern Hunan. With its expansive lake surface, scattered islands, and stunning interplay of mountains and water, it has earned the title "Switzerland of the East."

Doushuai Island at the heart of the lake is the largest island on Dongjiang Lake. The Doushuai Lingyan Karst Cave on the island is one of the largest karst caves in southern China, featuring spectacular stalactites, stalagmites, and an underground river — a true marvel of nature.

/// 03

Lakeside Splendor

The Dongjiang Dam is China's first double-curvature thin-shell arch dam designed and built independently. It is a magnificent structure that stretches across the Dongjiang River like a giant arc. From the top of the dam, the vast misty lake is surrounded by rolling mountains — a truly breathtaking sight.

Hougu Mountain Waterfall plunges from a hundred-meter-high cliff, cascading down like the Milky Way, with mist and rainbows appearing from time to time. The Dongjiang Lake Ring Road winds through the landscape, offering picturesque views along every mile, making it a year-round destination.
