Wangxiangtai Waterfall China's Highest Waterfall

Standing 158 meters tall and 48 meters wide, Wangxiangtai Waterfall is the most iconic attraction of Simianshan, known as "China's Highest Waterfall." The water cascades down the Danxia cliff, creating a misty spray that thunders through the valley.

Behind the waterfall lies a natural cave where visitors can walk through and experience the wonder of a water-curtain cave. Looking up from the foot of the falls, you see a white silk suspended in the air with droplets flying, and when sunlight hits, a rainbow arcs across — a breathtaking sight.

Danxia Landform A Geological Wonder

Simianshan is a classic Danxia landform scenic area. Over hundreds of millions of years, wind and erosion have shaped the red sandstone into striking pillar-like and tower-like formations. Red cliffs and unique peaks form a magnificent geological tapestry.

The Tudi Rock Mural stretches 376 meters long and 127 meters high, making it the largest natural mural in Asia. On this massive red cliff face, natural textures and colors intertwine, resembling a giant painting carved by a divine craftsman — a truly awe-inspiring sight.

The Love Ladder A Testament to True Love

The Love Ladder is the most touching legend of Simianshan — an elderly man named Liu Guojiang hand-chiseled 6,000 stone steps on a steep cliff for his wife Xu Chaoqing. This "Love Ladder" winds its way upward, bearing witness to half a century of deep affection.

From youth to old age, Liu Guojiang spent 50 years, hammer and chisel in hand, carving a path out of the mountains for his beloved. Today, the site has become a pilgrimage destination for couples, who come here to make their vows of love.
