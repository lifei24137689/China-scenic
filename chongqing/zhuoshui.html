Zhuoshui Ancient Town Millennial Charm

Zhuoshui Ancient Town originated in the Tang Dynasty and flourished in the Ming and Qing Dynasties. It is one of the most famous ancient towns in southeastern Chongqing. With blue bricks, black tiles, and winding streets, Tujia stilted houses line the river.

The stone-paved streets still bear traces of the former "Salt and Oil Ancient Road" bustling with merchants, exuding a long-lasting ancient charm. Strolling through, it feels as if you have traveled through a thousand years of time, with every bluestone slab telling the long history of the ancient town.

/// 02

Wind-Rain Covered Bridge World Wonder

Zhuoshui Wind-Rain Covered Bridge spans across the Apeng River, with a total length of 658 meters, making it one of the world's longest asymmetrical wind-rain covered bridges. The wooden structure exudes antique charm, with railings and benches on both sides, providing shelter from wind and rain while offering scenic views.

From the bridge, one can see the sparkling river waters and the distant blue-green mountains. This covered bridge is not only a passage connecting both banks but also a landmark of Zhuoshui Ancient Town, carrying countless memories and stories of travelers.

Apeng River Crystal Waters

The Apeng River winds through the ancient town, its waters emerald green and crystal clear. The surrounding gorges are serene, with towering peaks forming a natural landscape painting.

The Tujia and Miao ethnic cultures are rich and vibrant, with traditions such as the welcoming-door wine ceremony, hand-waving dance, and Miao songs and embroidery passed down through generations. Human culture and natural scenery blend perfectly here.
