Longgang Tiankeng The World's No.1 Tiankeng

With a depth of 335 meters, the Longgang Tiankeng is known as the "World's No.1 Tiankeng." Peering down from the rim, the sheer cliffs plunge into unfathomable depths, inspiring awe and reverence.

This giant tiankeng is the centerpiece of the karst landscape, showcasing hundreds of millions of years of geological evolution. The walls appear carved by blades, while the lush vegetation at the bottom forms a unique underground ecosystem.

Cloud Corridor Skywalk

Suspended 700 meters above the ground, the glass-bottomed Cloud Corridor is longer than the Grand Canyon Skywalk in the USA. Standing on the transparent glass, you face a bottomless chasm beneath your feet, with mist and clouds swirling around — thrilling and exhilarating.

This is a must-visit for the brave — every step challenges your heartbeat. Built with high-strength laminated tempered glass, the corridor can accommodate hundreds of visitors simultaneously, letting you experience ultimate thrill in complete safety.

Landscape Painting Sea of Clouds

The Shisun River and Qingshui Lake shimmer like mirrors, while the canyon streams murmur and towering peaks stand in splendor. The sea of clouds shifts unpredictably; at sunrise, golden rays pierce through the rolling mist, creating a celestial wonderland.

Longgang combines majesty, peril, wonder, and tranquility — a paradise for photographers. No matter the season, there is always a different natural beauty waiting to be discovered.
