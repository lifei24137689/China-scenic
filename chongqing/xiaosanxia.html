Little Three Gorges Hundred-Li Gallery

The Daning River's Little Three Gorges consist of Longmen Gorge, Bawu Gorge, and Dicui Gorge, stretching about 50 km. Towering cliffs line both banks, peaks are layered in green, and the river is as jade-green as gemstone.

Ancient plank road relics hide among the cliffs, and Ba people's hanging coffins are suspended high on the precipices — a breathtaking sight. Mist and clouds shroud the gorge year-round, making it feel like a flowing ink-wash landscape painting.

Mini Three Gorges Secret Deep Valley

The Madu River's Mini Three Gorges are even more secluded and tranquil, consisting of Changtan Gorge, Qinwang Gorge, and Sancheng Gorge. Riding a canopy boat through them, you hear the babbling streams and birds singing in the valley, with lush virgin vegetation on both sides.

This is one of the best-preserved primitive gorges in the Three Gorges region. The narrowest sections barely allow a single boat to pass. Precipitous cliffs rise like knife cuts, with vines hanging down, as if entering a green world cut off from the outside.

Ba Culture Millennium Heritage

The Little Three Gorges area was an important settlement of the ancient Ba people. The hanging coffins on the cliffs remain an unsolved mystery to this day. Along the way, precious cultural relics such as ancient plank road ruins and cliff-side inscriptions can be seen.

The landscape here gave birth to a unique Bayu folk culture, with folk songs and boatmen's chants passed down through generations. The Ba people were valiant warriors. Their distinctive funeral practice — placing coffins on sheer cliffs, enduring millennia of wind and rain without decay — continues to amaze the world.
