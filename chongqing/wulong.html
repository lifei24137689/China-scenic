Three Natural Bridges Asia's Largest Natural Bridge Cluster

The Three Natural Bridges Tianlong Bridge, Qinglong Bridge, and Heilong Bridge form the largest natural bridge cluster in Asia. These three natural stone arch bridges span across the giant sinkhole, each standing over 200 meters high, creating a breathtaking spectacle.

This site served as a filming location for Transformers 4 and Curse of the Golden Flower. Looking up from beneath the bridges, the three colossal spans appear like giant dragons lying across the sky. At the bottom of the sinkhole, an ancient post station and cascading waterfalls complement each other, composing a stunning karst geological panorama.

Fairy Mountain Oriental Switzerland

Fairy Mountain boasts 100,000 mu (6,667 hectares) of alpine grassland and 330,000 mu (22,000 hectares) of primeval forest at an elevation of 2,033 meters. Known as the "Oriental Switzerland," it enjoys an average summer temperature of 24°C, with vast meadows and boundless forest seas, making it the most popular summer resort around Chongqing.

Here you'll find a rare alpine grassland landscape in southern China — horseback riding in spring and summer, snow sports in autumn and winter. Under clear blue skies, herds of cattle and horses graze while windmills turn, as if you've been transported to the European Alps.

Furong Cave Underground Art Palace

Furong Cave is one of the world's three largest caves, with a main tunnel stretching 2,700 meters. Its stalactites are crystal clear and the underground wonders are resplendent. The cave features the "Five Wonders of Furong" Giant Curtain Waterfall, Coral Jade Pool, Fountain of Life, King of Stone Flowers, and Dogtooth Crystal Flowers earning it the title "Underground Art Palace".

Inside, over 70 types of secondary chemical deposits dazzle the eye. Stalagmites, stone pillars, stone drapes, and stone flowers shimmer under the lights like a dreamy fantasy, as if entering a sci-fi world deep within the earth a truly awe-inspiring sight.
