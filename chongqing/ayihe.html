Canyon Rafting

Ayi River stretches 21 kilometers through deep, steep canyons with crystal-clear emerald waters. As Chongqing's most beloved rafting destination, it lets you ride the rapids downstream while marveling at towering cliffs, unique peaks, waterfalls, and springs along both banks.

Summer rafting is Pengshui's hottest tourist attraction. The canyon narrows to just a few meters wide in places, alternating between raging rapids and placid pools, flipping you between screams and serenity as you experience nature's rawest wildness and gentlest touch.

Wujiang Gallery

Ayi River is a major tributary of the Wujiang River, with scenery along the way as poetic as a painting. "Boats glide on the river, people wander through the painting" — unique peaks stand tall on both sides, emerald waters mirror the sky, and mist wraps around like a landscape scroll.

The Wujiang Gallery is hailed as the most magnificent section of the "Thousand-li Wujiang, Hundred-li Gallery." The river gleams like jade, lush greenery clings to sheer cliffs, and troops of monkeys leap through the forests, unrolling a vivid landscape painting before your eyes.

Miao Village

"Jiao'ayi" means "beautiful girl" in the Miao language. Niujiao Village is a quintessential traditional Miao settlement with elegant stilted houses. Miao song and dance are spectacular, the welcome-door wine is exuberant, and Miao embroidery is a masterpiece of craftsmanship.

Here you can deeply experience the warmth and hospitality of the Miao people. As night falls and bonfires blaze, Miao girls and boys sing and dance, the lusheng flute lingers, silver ornaments jingle — every visitor is enchanted by this mysterious and passionate Miao culture.
