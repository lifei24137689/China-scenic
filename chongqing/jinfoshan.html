Jinfo Mountain Platform Karst

Jinfo Mountain is a quintessential example of world platform karst. Its main peak, Fenghuangling, rises 2,238 meters above sea level, and the 1,300 km² scenic area integrates mountains, water, forests, springs, and caves.

Inscribed as a World Natural Heritage site in 2014, it is the nearest World Heritage site to downtown Chongqing. UNESCO highly praised its karst landform for its integrity and typicality, earning it the title "Oriental Alps."

Four Seasons One Mountain, Four Seasons

In spring, alpine rhododendrons bloom across the mountains, weaving pink, white, and purple into clouds of color. Midsummer brings emerald valleys and clear pools, with an average temperature of just 20°C. In late autumn, maple leaves turn the hills ablaze with crimson. In winter, the southern snowland is cloaked in silver white.

Jinfo Mountain is Chongqing's nearest ski paradise. With four months of ice and snow, it is a rare snow destination in the south — skiing, playing, and admiring snow, all the joy of a northern winter wonderland.

Gufo Cave Finest Buddhist Cave

Gufo Cave is the largest karst cave on Jinfo Mountain, with an entrance about 80 meters high that can accommodate ten thousand people. Inside, stalactites, stalagmites, and cave flowers take on myriad forms, earning it the title "Finest Buddhist Cave Under Heaven."

Ancient temples such as Jinfo Temple and Xilai Temple are hidden among the forests, with incense burning for over a thousand years. Buddhist statues within the cave complement the natural wonders, making it a dual sanctuary of nature and culture.
