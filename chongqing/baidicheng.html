/// 01

Baidicheng Liu Bei's Last Entrustment

Baidicheng guards the entrance of Qutang Gorge, the historic site where Liu Bei entrusted his son to Zhuge Liang. Li Bai wrote "Leaving Baidicheng at dawn amidst colorful clouds" here, and Du Fu left "The boundless forest sheds its leaves shower by shower" here, alongside countless other literary masters.

Baidicheng is the first city of the Three Gorges, carrying over two thousand years of historical memory. It not only preserves relics from the Three Kingdoms period but has also been celebrated by poets throughout the ages, earning the title "Poetry City."

/// 02

Qutang Gorge Kuimen Natural Barrier

Qutang Gorge is the first of the Yangtze Three Gorges, stretching 8 kilometers. "Kuimen, the mightiest pass under heaven" — sheer cliffs on both sides face each other like gates, with the river surging and roaring through.

This is the iconic scene featured on the 10-yuan banknote and one of the most spectacular canyon entrances in the world. The perilous Kuimen Pass and the breathtaking gorge scenery captivate countless visitors.

Poetry City Culture Millennium Legacy

Li Bai, Du Fu, Bai Juyi, Su Shi, Lu You and other poets throughout the dynasties all left immortal poems here. According to statistics, over 1,000 poems are associated with Fengjie.

The Baidicheng Museum houses a large collection of precious cultural relics, showcasing the rich history of the Three Gorges region. From the Three Kingdoms to the Tang and Song dynasties, from Ming and Qing to modern times, the cultural legacy of the Poetry City has never been interrupted.
