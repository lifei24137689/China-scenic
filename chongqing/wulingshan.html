Wulingshan Fissure Wonder

The Wulingshan Grand Rift stretches 10 kilometers, a classic karst canyon. The fissure is deep and narrow, flanked by 300-meter-high cliffs as sheer as an axe cut.

Looking up from the valley floor, the sky is reduced to a thin slit — a breathtaking visual spectacle. It is known as “The Oldest Scar on Earth,” a masterpiece of nature’s geological artistry.

Azure Canyon Thread of Sky

Azure Canyon is the core attraction of the rift, with its narrowest section measuring just 2 meters wide. Underground rivers surge, strange rocks stand tall, and plank paths traverse the sheer cliffs for an exhilarating experience.

The canyon is rich in negative oxygen ions, with fresh, sweet air — a natural forest oxygen bar. Stroll through and feel the cool tranquility within the fissure.

Primeval Ecology Natural Treasure

The rift’s forest coverage rate reaches 92.7%, preserving intact secondary virgin forests. Rare animals such as macaques, golden pheasants, and musk deer inhabit the area, while precious plants like dove trees and yew trees can be seen everywhere.

In spring, mountain flowers bloom; in summer, it offers a cool retreat; in autumn, the forests blaze with color — a natural ecological treasure with distinct beauty in every season.
