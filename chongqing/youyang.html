Taigu Cave Entrance to Paradise

Taigu Cave is the entrance to Peach Blossom Paradise. Only by passing through the deep karst cave can one enter this hidden paradise. Inside, stalactites take on myriad forms, resembling an underground palace. Exiting the cave, the view suddenly opens up as if entering a different world, perfectly recreating Tao Yuanming's description: "At first it was very narrow, barely allowing passage. After walking several dozen steps, it suddenly opened up into a bright and broad space."

Taigu Cave stretches approximately 3,000 meters, filled with karst wonders such as stalagmites, curtain stone, and stone flowers. Under the illumination of lights, the various stalactites present a colorful spectacle, like an underground corridor leading to a fairyland.

Peach Blossom Paradise Earthly Pure Land

Covering an area of 50 square kilometers, the scenic area features towering ancient trees and forests of peach blossoms. The clear green waters of Qin Creek meander through the landscape, and boating upon it feels like drifting through a dreamland. The pastoral scenery brings peace and joy, far from the hustle and bustle, perfectly recreating the reclusive atmosphere of Tao Yuanming's "The Story of Peach Blossom Spring."

Peach trees line both banks of the creek. In spring when flowers bloom, the mountains are covered with peach blossoms like clouds and rosy mist, with fallen petals dancing in the air. Strolling along forest paths, listening to birdsong and babbling brooks, with a gentle breeze on your face, you will forget worldly worries and immerse yourself in this earthly paradise.

Youzhou Ancient Town Tujia & Miao Culture

Youzhou Ancient Town features gray bricks, black tiles, and stilted buildings arranged in picturesque disorder, rich with Tujia and Miao ethnic charm. Built along the creek, the town's flagstone streets wind and twist. Here you can find authentic Baishou Dance, Tujia folk songs, Miao embroidery, and other intangible cultural heritage, making it an excellent place to experience the ethnic culture of the Wuling Mountain region.

The ancient town preserves a large number of architectural features from the Ming and Qing dynasties. Strolling along the blue stone slab paths, shops on both sides exude antique charm. Cultural treasures such as the Tujia traditional Baishou Dance, Miao silver crafts, and Xilankapu brocade are passed down and continued here.
