Chapter One

Ancient Kiln Folk Custom Expo Area

Jingdezhen Ancient Kiln Folk Custom Expo Area is the only ceramic culture-themed 5A scenic area in China, completely recreating the kilns from the Song to the Qing dynasties — Song Dynasty Dragon Kiln, Yuan Dynasty Mantou Kiln, Ming Dynasty Gourd Kiln, Qing Dynasty Town Kiln — making it a living history of kiln development.

Here, intangible cultural heritage inheritors demonstrate the 72 steps of handmade porcelain production on site, from kneading clay and throwing to painting and glazing — each step embodies a millennium of craftsmanship.

Chapter Two

Blue-and-White Elegance

Blue-and-white porcelain is Jingdezhen's soul work. Cobalt pigment is used to paint patterns on the raw body, covered with a transparent glaze, and fired at 1300°C in a single firing. The blue and white contrast elegantly, bright and vivid, earning it the title of "King of Porcelain".

The Yuan Dynasty blue-and-white jar depicting Guiguzi descending the mountain sold for a record 230 million yuan, while Ming Dynasty Yongle and Xuande blue-and-white wares are unmatched through the ages. Jingdezhen blue-and-white porcelain has been exported overseas, becoming the most elegant ambassador of Chinese culture.

Chapter Three

Artisan Heritage

Jingdezhen's handmade porcelain techniques are listed as National Intangible Cultural Heritage. From quarrying and refining clay to throwing, trimming, and underglaze painting, the 72 steps are interlinked, with each piece embodying the craftsman's dedication and wisdom.

Inside the Ancient Kiln scenic area, intangible cultural heritage inheritors demonstrate throwing, painting, and other master techniques on site. Visitors can also experience the joy of making porcelain with their own hands. The millennium-old kiln fire burns endlessly, and the spirit of craftsmanship is passed down through generations.
