Sanqingshan

Taoist Sacred Mountain · Floating Wonderland

A masterpiece of granite peak forest landforms and a UNESCO World Natural Heritage site. The Giant Python Emerging from the Mountain and the Goddess of Spring — nature's uncanny craftsmanship has crystallized into eternal poetry here.

Chapter I

Giant Python Emerging from the Mountain

A towering granite pillar of 128 meters rises abruptly from the mountain, like a giant python bursting forth and piercing the clouds. It is the most iconic landmark of Sanqingshan and a unique wonder among the world's granite landforms.

After hundreds of millions of years of weathering and erosion, Python Peak stands firm with a flat top, a slightly slender neck, and a thick body — its narrowest point measures less than 7 meters in diameter. When mist and clouds swirl around it, the python appears and disappears, as if swimming through a sea of clouds.

"Having toured the Five Sacred Mountains, the finest scenery lies at Sanqing"
— Su Shi
Chapter II

Goddess of Spring

The Goddess of Spring Peak rises 86 meters high, shaped like an elegant and graceful maiden seated among the surrounding peaks. With her hands holding two ancient pines, she appears to be sowing the seeds of spring, guarding this sacred mountain paradise.

Legend has it that the Goddess of Spring is a Taoist deity who oversees the beauty of spring on earth. When the first rays of morning sunlight bathe the peak in golden glow, the entire mountain seems draped in a sacred halo, inspiring awe and reverence.

Chapter III

Sanqing Holy Land

The Sanqing Temple was first built during the Shengping era of the Eastern Jin Dynasty (357–361 AD), with a history spanning over 1,600 years. It serves as the sacred site for the Three Pure Ones (Yuqing, Shangqing, Taiqing) of Taoism.

The ancient architectural complex is built along the mountain, centered around Sanqing Temple and laid out according to the Eight Trigrams of the I Ching, embodying the Taoist philosophy of "unity of heaven and humanity." The West Coast Plank Road clings to thousand-meter-high cliffs — walking along it, with the sea of clouds surging beneath your feet, feels like treading through a fairyland.
