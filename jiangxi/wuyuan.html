Wuyuan Jiangwan

China's Most Beautiful Village · Huizhou Elegance

"Half an acre of square pond opens like a mirror, where skylight and cloud shadows wander together" — Wuyuan Jiangwan, a thousand-year-old ancient town where Huizhou architecture and pastoral landscapes complement each other, known as "China's Most Beautiful Village."

Chapter One

Ancient Town

Jiangwan was founded in the late Sui and early Tang dynasties, with a history of over 1,300 years. Surrounded by mountains and rivers with picturesque scenery, it is the only 5A-level scenic area in Wuyuan County and a representative of China's most beautiful villages.

The ancient town preserves a large number of Ming and Qing dynasty buildings — white walls, black tiles, and upturned eaves. The essence of Huizhou architecture is perfectly showcased here. Strolling along the cobblestone paths feels like traveling back through a thousand years.

"Half an acre of square pond opens like a mirror, where skylight and cloud shadows wander together"
— Zhu Xi, "Reflections on Reading"
Chapter Two

Huizhou Architecture

The Huizhou architecture in Jiangwan is marked by white walls, black tiles, and horse-head walls, with layouts emphasizing harmony between nature and humanity. The ancient building complex — including the Xiaojiang Ancestral Hall, Dunlun Hall, and Shanyu Hall — showcases the exquisite craftsmanship of Huizhou's "Three Carvings" (wood carving, stone carving, and brick carving).

Every old residence carries the memory of Huizhou merchant culture, and every brick and tile tells the story of this thousand-year-old town. It is a living museum of Huizhou culture.

Chapter Three

Cultural Heritage

Jiangwan has been a flourishing center of culture since ancient times and is the ancestral home of Zhu Xi, the great Neo-Confucian scholar of the Southern Song dynasty. During the Ming and Qing dynasties, Jiangwan produced dozens of imperial scholars, earning it the reputation of a "Land of Letters." Scenic spots such as the Worthies Garden and Huixian Lake embody its profound cultural heritage.

Every spring, rapeseed flower seas and the Huizhou ancient village create a stunning pastoral tableau. On the ancient stage, the melodies of Hui opera echo, preserving a cultural legacy that spans a thousand years.
