◈ Mountain Brief

Lushan, a World Cultural Landscape Heritage, is renowned worldwide for its majesty, wonder, peril, and beauty. "I cannot see the true face of Lushan, because I am right in the middle of it." Su Shi's timeless verse made Lushan famous across the land. The Sandiequan Waterfall drops 155 meters and is hailed as "Lushan's Most Spectacular Wonder." Lushan also served as the "Summer Capital" of modern Chinese history, where the Meilu Villa witnessed many historical events.

Sandie Waterfall Plunging Torrent

The Sandiequan Waterfall is the most magnificent waterfall on Lushan, dropping 155 meters in three tiers. The upper tier falls like drifting snow, the middle like shattered jade crushing ice, and the lower like a jade dragon plunging into the pool. An ancient saying goes, "If you haven't seen Sandiequan, you haven't truly visited Lushan," underscoring its central place among Lushan's landscapes.

The waterfall originates from Mount Dayue, flows across the back of Five Old Peaks, and pours from the northern cliff into Ninefold Valley. During the wet season, Sandiequan thunders like rolling thunder, mist fills the valley, and rainbows arch across the sunlight — a breathtaking spectacle. It is nature's most astonishing stroke upon Lushan.

/// 02

Five Old Peaks Mist Immortal Realm

Five Old Peaks — five peaks standing side by side, resembling five elders sitting shoulder to shoulder. Viewed from Hankou Pass, the peaks appear and disappear through the sea of clouds like a celestial realm. Li Bai once built a hut here to study, leaving behind the immortal verse: "To the southeast of Lushan stand the Five Old Peaks, carved from the blue sky like golden lotus blossoms."

Hankou Pass — the best spot on Lushan to watch the sunrise. At dawn, clouds and mist over Poyang Lake surge like an ocean, and the crimson sun bursts forth from the sea of clouds, bathing the peaks in golden light — the most enchanting scene on Lushan.

/// 03

Millennium Poetic Mountain

Lushan stands as a monument in Chinese cultural history. From Tao Yuanming's "picking chrysanthemums beneath the eastern fence" to Li Bai's "flying torrent plunging three thousand feet" and Su Shi's "I cannot see the true face of Lushan," generations of literary masters have left behind over 4,000 poems and verses about Lushan. The White Deer Grotto Academy was the foremost of China's four great ancient academies.

Lushan also bears witness to modern Chinese history. The Meilu Villa served as the summer residence of Chiang Kai-shek and Soong Mei-ling, while the Lushan Conference Site records key moments in the history of New China. Nature and culture blend perfectly on Lushan, which was inscribed as a World Cultural Landscape Heritage in 1996.
