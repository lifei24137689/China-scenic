Mount Longhu

Taoist Ancestral Home · Danxia Wonderland

The birthplace of Chinese Taoism and the ancestral home of the Zhengyi School. The Luxi River winds like a jade belt through the red Danxia cliffs, the millennium-old hanging coffin mystery clings to the precipices, and the Heavenly Master Mansion's incense has burned unbroken for a thousand years.

Chapter I

Heavenly Master Mansion

The Heavenly Master Mansion was first built in the fourth year of Chongning of the Northern Song Dynasty (1105 AD). It served as the residence and worship site for successive generations of Celestial Masters Zhang and is hailed as the "First Mansion of Taoism". The compound boasts towering ancient trees, majestic halls, and the Seven-Star Ancient Camphor trees that have remained verdant through a thousand years of wind and rain.

Since Zhang Daoling founded the Zhengyi School during the Eastern Han Dynasty, it has been passed down for over sixty generations. As the ancestral home, the Heavenly Master Mansion carries the two-thousand-year cultural lineage of Chinese Taoism and is a sacred site for Taoist believers at home and abroad.

Chapter II

Xianshui Rock Hanging Coffins

On the Danxia cliffs along both banks of the Luxi River, over 200 cliff tomb hanging coffins from the Spring and Autumn and Warring States periods are distributed. These coffins hang in natural caves dozens of meters above the water, having withstood 2,600 years of wind and rain without decay.

How did ancient people place coffins weighing hundreds of catties onto sheer cliff faces? This eternal mystery continues to attract countless archaeologists and visitors to explore. The daily "Coffin Raising" performance recreates this ancient and mysterious burial custom.

Chapter III

Danxia Cliffs & Emerald Waters

Mount Longhu is a quintessential example of China's Danxia landform. The red cliffs appear as if carved by knives and axes, reflecting off the clear blue waves of the Luxi River, together forming a breathtaking picture of "emerald waters and crimson mountains".

Riding a bamboo raft along the Luxi River, you'll see fantastic peaks lining both banks in myriad forms — some like a lion looking back, others like a fairy descending to earth. The clear river water mirrors the crimson mountains, making you feel as if you are traveling within a painting.
