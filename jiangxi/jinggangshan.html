Jinggangshan

Cradle of Revolution · Red Sacred Land

A single spark can start a prairie fire. The five-hundred-li Jinggangshan, with its layered peaks and vast sea of forests, every inch of land is engraved with the magnificent red memory of that tumultuous era.

Chapter One

Huangyangjie

Huangyangjie stands at an elevation of 1,343 meters, the most famous of Jinggangshan's five major outposts. In 1928, the Red Army, with less than a battalion, repelled an attack by four enemy regiments here, creating the legendary battle of "the roar of cannons at Huangyangjie".

Standing at the Huangyangjie outpost, one can gaze into the distance at the sea of mountains and rolling clouds. The winding Grain-Carrying Trail bears witness to the arduous years when Commander-in-Chief Zhu De shared hardships with the soldiers.

"A single spark can start a prairie fire"
— Mao Zedong
Chapter Two

Wuzhi Peak

Wuzhi Peak is the main peak of Jinggangshan, standing at 1,438 meters. It is named for its five peaks standing side by side like five fingers. It is not only the symbol of Jinggangshan but also the prototype of the 100-yuan banknote from the fourth series of RMB.

Between the peaks, pristine forests thrive with abundant rare flora and fauna. Ascending to the summit, one can take in the panoramic view of the five-hundred-li Jinggangshan, with magnificent sea of clouds and sunrises.

Chapter Three

Red Heritage

Jinggangshan preserves over 100 revolutionary site relics. Longjiang Academy is where Mao Zedong and Zhu De first met, and it was also where the Red Army training corps was founded, earning it the reputation as the "cradle of military and political academies."

Mao Zedong's former residence in Ciping, Jinggangshan Revolutionary Museum, Xiaojing Red Army Hospital... Every site tells the story of those arduous and extraordinary years. Today, Jinggangshan has become a major national base for patriotic education.
