Mingyue Mountain

Jiangxi Province · Yichun City

"Love me, take me to Mingyue Mountain" — the legendary birthplace of Chang'e flying to the moon. The Qingyun Plank Road hangs on thousand-meter cliffs, Moon Lake reflects the starry sky, and selenium-rich hot springs nourish body and soul. This is the closest place to the moon.

Chapter One

Qingyun Plank Road · Walking in the Clouds

Qingyun Plank Road hangs on the cliffs of Mingyue Mountain at an elevation of 1,500 meters, stretching about 3,100 meters like a jade belt wrapped around the precipice. Walking along it, beneath your feet lies a bottomless abyss, above your head the blue sky and white clouds. Mist drifts by as if you are strolling through the clouds.

Along the plank road, several viewing platforms overlook the rolling mountains and surging sea of clouds. At the end of the road lies Xingyue Cave, where stalactites take on myriad shapes. Illuminated by lights, it resembles a starry sky — the most romantic spot in Mingyue Mountain.

"How rare the moon, so round and clear! With cup in hand, I ask of the blue sky: 'I do not know in the celestial palaces above, what season of the year it is tonight.'"
—— Su Shi, "Prelude to Water Melody"
Chapter Two

Moon Lake · A Highland Gem

Moon Lake sits halfway up Mingyue Mountain at an elevation of about 1,000 meters. Its waters are as clear as a mirror, surrounded by mountains on all sides. On nights of the full moon, the moon's reflection shimmers in the lake, creating a stunning scene of "one moon in the sky, one moon in the water," each complementing the other.

By the lakeshore, a Moon Culture Theme Square hosts the grand Moon Culture Festival every Mid-Autumn Festival. Visitors can admire the moon, sip tea, and listen to music, immersing themselves in the romantic legend of Chang'e flying to the moon and the profound heritage of moon culture.

Chapter Three

Cloud Valley Waterfall · Selenium-Rich Hot Springs

Cloud Valley Waterfall plunges 119 meters — the most spectacular waterfall in Mingyue Mountain. The water cascades from the cliff top, filling the air with mist, and rainbows arch across the sunlight. The surrounding area is lush with vegetation and rich in negative oxygen ions, making it a natural forest oxygen bar.

Mingyue Mountain's selenium-rich hot springs are renowned far and wide. The spring water remains at a constant temperature of 52-57°C year-round, rich in selenium, metasilicic acid, and other beneficial trace elements — a rare selenium-rich hot spring in China. Soaking in the hot springs while gazing up at the bright moon, washing away all fatigue, is the most soothing experience Mingyue Mountain has to offer.
