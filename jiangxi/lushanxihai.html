Lushan West Sea

Jiangxi Province · Jiujiang City

Originally named Zhelin Lake, formed by the largest earth-fill dam in Asia. 1,667 islands are scattered across vast emerald waters, earning it the title "Jiangxi Thousand Islands Lake." It is a paradise for water sports and a haven for leisure vacations.

Chapter One

Lake of a Thousand Islands · Vast Emerald Waters

Lushan West Sea, originally named Zhelin Lake, covers a water area of 308 square kilometers. The lake contains 1,667 islands of various shapes scattered like stars. Viewed from above, the islands resemble emeralds scattered across the blue lake, creating a magnificent landscape painting.

The Zhelin Lake Dam is the largest earth-fill dam in Asia, standing 63.5 meters high and 590 meters long. This magnificent hydraulic project not only created the stunning landscape of Lushan West Sea but also provides abundant water resources and electricity to the surrounding areas.

"The lake and mountains blend in harmony, the water surface smooth as a mirror without a ripple — Lushan West Sea, a poetic dwelling of 1,667 islands."
—— Lushan West Sea · Jiangxi Thousand Islands Lake
Chapter Two

Water Sports · Leisure Paradise

Lushan West Sea is a water sports enthusiast's paradise. With its clear waters and open lake surface, it is ideal for sailing, kayaking, jet skiing, flyboarding, and various other water activities. Every summer, water sports lovers from all over the country gather here.

Beyond thrilling water sports, Lushan West Sea also offers hot spring resorts and West Sea Fishing Village for leisure. Soak in a hot spring, savor a table of lake delicacies, and enjoy a slow life amidst the lake and mountains — the ideal escape from urban hustle and bustle.

Chapter Three

Golden Dragon Bridge · Lakeside Stroll

Golden Dragon Bridge is one of the iconic landmarks of Lushan West Sea. Spanning between two islands, it lies like a golden dragon resting upon the emerald waves. Walking on the bridge, the lake breeze caresses your face, distant mountains appear misty blue, and the nearby waters shimmer with haze — as if strolling through a landscape painting.

Lushan West Sea also features numerous viewing platforms such as Haohan Slope. From atop, a thousand islands compete in beauty, and vast emerald waters stretch before your eyes. Whether cruising the lake by boat, exploring islands, fishing by the shore, or soaking in hot springs, Lushan West Sea fulfills every vision of a vacation.
