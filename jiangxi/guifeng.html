Chapter One

Laoren Peak · Danxia Wonder

Laoren Peak is the iconic landscape of Guifeng, at 418 meters above sea level, shaped like a kind elderly figure sitting on the mountaintop. The peak is made of red sandstone conglomerate, shaped by hundreds of millions of years of weathering and erosion into a vivid old man image. At sunrise, golden light spills over the ocher-red rock walls, like a natural oil painting.

Laoren Peak is not only the symbol of Guifeng, but also a classic example of Danxia landform development. Its steep cliffs, rounded summit, and layered rock strata fully record the magnificent geological evolution since the Cretaceous period.

Chapter Two

Three-Stacked Turtle · Nature's Masterpiece

Three-Stacked Turtle is the most legendary landscape of Guifeng. Three huge rocks stacked on top of each other, resembling three giant turtles in a pyramid, incredibly lifelike. Legend has it that three divine turtles, captivated by the Danxia beauty of the mortal world, turned into stone peaks and stayed here forever.

Besides the Three-Stacked Turtle, Guifeng has 36 uniquely shaped peaks including Zhanqi Peak, Jinzhong Peak, and Camel Peak. Each peak has a touching legend, each rock tells a geological story of hundreds of millions of years.

Chapter Three

Danxia Landform · World Heritage

Guifeng is an important part of the World Natural Heritage "China Danxia". The Danxia landform here developed from Cretaceous red sandstone conglomerate layers, through long-term weathering, water erosion, and gravitational collapse, forming typical landscapes such as red cliffs, square peaks, rock caves, and stone pillars.

In 2010, "China Danxia" was inscribed on the World Heritage List. Guifeng, together with Danxiashan in Guangdong, Langshan in Hunan, Taining in Fujian, Chishui in Guizhou, and Jianglangshan in Zhejiang, forms this world-class geological wonder. With its unique "every mountain is a turtle" formations, Guifeng stands out among the Danxia family.
