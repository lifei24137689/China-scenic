Chapter One

Yeping Revolutionary Sites

Yeping is the birthplace of the Provisional Central Government of the Chinese Soviet Republic. On November 7, 1931, the First National Congress of the Chinese Soviet Republic was held here, proclaiming the founding of the Chinese Soviet Republic, with Mao Zedong elected as Chairman.

The Yeping site complex preserves the First Soviet Congress venue, the Red Army Martyrs Memorial Tower, the Red Army Reviewing Stand, and 23 other revolutionary sites and memorial buildings, making it one of the best-preserved revolutionary site clusters in China.

Chapter Two

The Story of the Red Well

The Red Well in Shazhouba is Ruijin's most famous Red tourist attraction. In 1933, Mao Zedong led the locals to dig this well, solving the drinking water shortage for the community. After the Red Army's Long March, Kuomintang troops repeatedly filled in the well, but each time the locals dug it open again.

The stele beside the well bears the inscription: "When you drink water, never forget who dug the well; we will always cherish Chairman Mao." This well bears witness to the deep bond between the Chinese Communist Party and the people, and stands as the most vivid symbol of the Red spirit.

Chapter Three

Cradle of the Republic

Ruijin is one of the starting points of the Central Red Army's Long March and an important birthplace of Mao Zedong Thought. During their time in Ruijin, the Chinese Communist Party established the first nationwide red political power and carried out great practices in land reform, economic construction, and cultural education.

The Ruijin Cradle of the Republic Scenic Area comprises four core sites: Yeping, Red Well, Second Soviet Congress, and the Chinese Soviet Memorial Park. It serves as a national patriotic education demonstration base, attracting millions of visitors each year.
