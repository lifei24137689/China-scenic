◈ Mountain Brief

Mount Dajue, located in Zixi County, Fuzhou City, Jiangxi Province, is world-famous for its Buddhist culture and canyon rafting. The Dajue Rock Temple, first built in the Eastern Jin Dynasty, has a history of over 1,600 years and is a rare natural cave temple in China. The Dajue Mountain rafting course stretches 3.6 kilometers with a drop of 188 meters, dubbed "Asia's No.1 Rafting", making it an excellent summer destination for adventure and escaping the heat.

/// 01

Dajue Rock Temple Cave Buddha Realm

The Dajue Rock Temple was built during the Xianhe period of the Eastern Jin Dynasty, making it a rare natural cave temple in China. Built into the mountainside and embedded within a massive natural cave, the temple covers over 1,800 square meters and can hold thousands of people. The cave entrance is about 20 meters high and 30 meters wide, resembling a giant beast's open mouth in its magnificent scale.

Inside, statues of Shakyamuni Buddha and Guanyin Bodhisattva are enshrined, with incense burning continuously for millennia. The cave stays warm in winter and cool in summer, with hanging stalactites and the sound of dripping springs, earning it the title "Cave Buddha Realm". Every year on the 19th day of the second lunar month, Guanyin's birthday, tens of thousands of devotees gather here in a spectacular scene.

/// 02

Canyon Rafting Asia's No.1 Rafting

Dajue Mountain Rafting — 3.6 kilometers in total length with a 188-meter drop, passing through one valley, three bends, six pools, and nine waterfalls, earning the title "Asia's No.1 Rafting". The course winds through primitive forest canyons, with ancient trees towering on both banks, cascading waterfalls, and stunning scenery — a blend of thrill and beauty.

Riding the Rapids — The entire rafting journey takes about 1.5 hours, offering both the adrenaline-pumping excitement of rushing rapids and the leisurely enjoyment of calm lake drifting. The river channel reaches up to 30 meters wide at its broadest and narrows to just 2 meters, with currents sometimes surging like arrows and other times as still as a mirror, letting you feel the power of nature amidst screams and laughter.

/// 03

Heavenly Lake Alpine Pearl

Heavenly Lake — Located halfway up Mount Dajue at an elevation of about 800 meters, the lake covers an area of over 200 mu (33 acres). The water is crystal clear and emerald green, surrounded by lush forests and towering mountains, like a pearl set in a sea of green. The lake reflects the blue sky, white clouds, and green mountains — a breathtaking sight.

Southern Heavenly Gate — The iconic scenic spot at the highest point of Mount Dajue, at an elevation of 1,300 meters. Ascending to the Southern Heavenly Gate, the mountains bow below, clouds and mist swirl around, and on a clear day you can see the outline of Fuzhou City in the distance. The Dajue Buddha statue, standing 108 meters tall, gazes down upon all beings — a perfect fusion of faith and nature.
