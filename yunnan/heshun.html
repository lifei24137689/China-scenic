Six Centuries of Overseas Chinese Heritage

Heshun Ancient Town, located in Tengchong City, Baoshan, Yunnan, was built during the Ming Dynasty with over 600 years of history. It is a renowned overseas Chinese hometown in Yunnan, where generations of Heshun people traveled afar and to Southeast Asia, bringing the outside world back to this border town.

Nestled against mountains and beside rivers, the town is a living fossil of ancient Chinese architecture. Bridges over flowing water, white walls and black tiles, the sound of water by the washing pavilion — all tell the story of China's #1 Charming Town.

"Heshun in harmony — six centuries of overseas Chinese heritage, every bluestone slab remembers the figures who walked from here to the world."

A World-Class Library in the Countryside

The Heshun Library, built in 1924, is the largest rural library in China with a collection of over 70,000 books. This Sino-Western architectural gem stands at the heart of the ancient town, with a plaque inscribed by Hu Shi witnessing the town's thirst for knowledge.

The town also features the Washing Pavilion, Double Rainbow Bridge, Ai Siqi's Former Residence, Zhongtian Temple, and many other historic sites. With the spirit of "being both merchants and scholars," the people of Heshun nurtured a town filled with literary grace on this hub of the Ancient Tea Horse Road.
