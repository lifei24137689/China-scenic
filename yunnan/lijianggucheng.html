Eight Hundred Years of Flowing Water & Homes

Lijiang Old Town was first built in the late Song Dynasty and early Yuan Dynasty, situated on the Yunnan-Guizhou Plateau at an elevation of about 2,400 meters. The soul of the old town is water — the clear springs of Black Dragon Pool flow into the city through the Jade River, weaving a vibrant water network. Three-well wells, stone arch bridges, the Mu Mansion — every stone slab is soaked in the wisdom of the Naxi ancestors.

"The city exists by water, and water follows the city" is the most touching portrayal of Lijiang. In 1997, Lijiang Old Town was inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List, becoming a treasure guarded by all humanity.

"Small bridge, flowing water, homes; ancient road, west wind, lean horse — in every dusk of Lijiang, time slows by half a beat."

The Big Waterwheel at the Old Town Entrance

The Big Waterwheel is the iconic symbol of Lijiang Old Town, located at the main entrance. This pair of waterwheels, having weathered wind and rain, turns day and night, telling the Naxi people's reverence and gratitude for water — the soul element of the ancient city.

Entering the old town from the Big Waterwheel, Sifang Street, Qiyi Street, and Wuyi Street unfold like veins. The melodious sounds of Naxi ancient music, the faint fragrance of Dongba paper, the hammering from coppersmith shops — this is Lijiang on the Ancient Tea Horse Road, a "living" ancient city.
