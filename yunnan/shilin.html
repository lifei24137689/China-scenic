The World's Most Spectacular Karst

Shilin Scenic Area is located in Shilin Yi Autonomous County, Kunming, Yunnan Province, approximately 78 km from downtown Kunming. It features the world's largest and most historically evolved karst landscape, covering an area of 350 square kilometers.

Formed during the Permian period 270 million years ago, this area was once a vast ocean. Through millennia of weathering and dissolution, the limestone was sculpted into today's fantastical stone forest. In 2007, Shilin was inscribed on the UNESCO World Natural Heritage List as a core component of "South China Karst."

Ashima's Beautiful Legend

In the Lesser Shilin area stands a natural stone peak resembling a Yi ethnic maiden — the renowned "Ashima Stone." Legend has it that the beautiful Yi girl Ashima fell in love with the brave young man Ahei, but was persecuted by a wealthy tyrant and swept away by a flood, ultimately transforming into an eternal stone peak.

Every year on the 24th day of the sixth lunar month, during the Torch Festival, the Yi people of Shilin don their finest attire and celebrate with sanxian dances, bullfighting, and wrestling, commemorating the true love of Ashima and Ahei. Yi embroidery and the oral epic "Ashima" have been inscribed on the National Intangible Cultural Heritage list.

Stone Peaks in Myriad Forms

The Shilin Scenic Area is divided into five main zones: Greater Shilin, Lesser Shilin, Naigu Shilin, Long Lake, and Zhiyun Cave. Greater Shilin is the core area with the highest density of stone peaks, featuring landmarks such as Sword Peak Pond and Wangfeng Pavilion, where winding paths lead through secluded wonders.

Naigu Shilin (Black Pine Rock) is the oldest section of the Shilin area, with dark, majestic stone peaks that stand in striking contrast to the blue-gray tones of Greater and Lesser Shilin. Long Lake is a sapphire embedded in the stone forest, stretching 3 km long and 300 m wide with crystal-clear waters.
