Eternal Bloom of the Century Flower Sea

Kunming Expo Garden was built for the 1999 Kunming World Horticultural Exposition and is the only completely preserved World Expo site in the world today. Covering an area of 218 hectares, it brings together five major pavilions, six themed gardens, and dozens of international outdoor exhibition areas.

The China Pavilion showcases the essence of Chinese gardens with traditional layout, while the International Pavilion gathers exotic horticultural styles from around the world. The Man and Nature Pavilion, Science & Technology Pavilion, and Grand Greenhouse each have their unique features, together composing the eternal theme of "harmonious coexistence between man and nature."

Architectural Beauty of the Five Pavilions

The China Pavilion is inspired by the "Han Que" architectural style, blending classical charm of Qin bricks and Han tiles with modern construction techniques. The International Pavilion, shaped like a giant orchid blooming at the heart of the garden, was designed by a renowned American architect and once won an international architecture award.

The Grand Greenhouse gathers rare plants from tropical, temperate, and alpine regions, making it a paradise for flower enthusiasts. With the annual Tulip Festival in spring, Lotus Exhibition in summer, and Chrysanthemum Exhibition in autumn, Kunming Expo Garden fulfills its promise of a "never-ending" bloom through uninterrupted floral events.
