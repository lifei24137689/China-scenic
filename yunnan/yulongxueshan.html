The Naxi Sacred Mountain

Jade Dragon Snow Mountain is located within Lijiang City, Yunnan Province, and is the closest perennially snow-capped mountain range to the equator in the Northern Hemisphere. Its 13 snow peaks stretch from south to north in a row, resembling a silver-white dragon leaping through the sea of clouds — hence the name "Jade Dragon."

The Naxi people call the mountain "Olu," meaning silver mountain rock. For thousands of years, Jade Dragon Snow Mountain has been a sacred mountain in the hearts of the Naxi people, guarding this highland pure land. The main peak, Shanzidou, at 5,596 meters, remains unclimbed to this day, preserving its primeval solemnity and mystery.

/// 02

Glacial Wonder

Jade Dragon Snow Mountain is home to 19 modern glaciers, covering a total area of 11.61 square kilometers. The main cable car takes visitors directly to the Glacier Park at an elevation of 4,506 meters, offering an up-close encounter with the majestic and ancient glaciers.

The waters of Blue Moon Valley originate from snowmelt and are rich in copper ions, giving them a dreamlike Tiffany blue hue on sunny days. The Baishui River rushes through the valley, while yaks graze leisurely on the meadows of Ganhaizi — ice and fire, snow and grass coexist here in wondrous harmony.

At the Foot of the Mountain

Spruce Meadow is considered the entrance to the "Third Kingdom of Jade Dragon" in Naxi mythology — where lovers who die for love are said to find eternal happiness. Towering spruce forests encircle a high-altitude meadow, with the snow mountain appearing and disappearing through the mist.

Directed by Zhang Yimou, "Impression Lijiang" uses the snow mountain as its natural backdrop, with the theater at 3,100 meters being the highest open-air performance venue in the world. Over 500 local farmers and herders participate in the show, telling the legendary stories of the Ancient Tea Horse Road at the foot of the mountain.
