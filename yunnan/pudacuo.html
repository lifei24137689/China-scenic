China's First National Park

Potatso National Park is located in Diqing, Shangri-La, Yunnan, at an altitude of 3,500 to 4,159 meters, covering a total area of 1,313 km². It preserves a complete virgin forest ecosystem, with fir and spruce towering into the clouds and rhododendron flowers blanketing the mountainsides.

Shudu Lake is the first stop of the park. Its waters are as clear as a mirror, reflecting the surrounding primeval forests and snow-capped peaks. A 2.7 km wooden boardwalk traces the lakeshore, letting you listen to the highland wind from a distance that does not disturb nature.

"Potatso — the lake that ferries all beings to the other shore. At 3,500 meters above sea level, water, grass, forest, and mountain compose a symphony of life."

—— Potatso National Park · Shudu Lake

The Mysterious Legend of Bita Sea

Bita Sea is the soul of Potatso. In Tibetan, "Bita" means a place carpeted with oak trees, and the lakeshore is indeed covered by vast oak forests. Every May when the rhododendrons bloom, petals fall onto the lake surface, creating a wondrous spectacle called "rhododendron drunken fish" — fish that eat the fallen petals briefly faint.

A small temple on the islet in the lake is a sacred site of Tibetan Buddhism. Local herders have grazed their livestock here for generations, and yaks wander leisurely across the meadows, painting a plateau picture of harmony between heaven and humanity.
