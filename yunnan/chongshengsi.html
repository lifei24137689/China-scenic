Three Pagodas Soaring

The Three Pagodas of Chongsheng Temple are located about 1.5 km northwest of Dali Ancient City, backed by Cangshan Mountain and facing Erhai Lake. The main Qianxun Pagoda was first built during the Nanzhao Kingdom period of the Tang Dynasty (824-859 AD), while the two smaller pagodas to the north and south were built during the Dali Kingdom period of the Song Dynasty. The three pagodas stand in a tripod formation, making them one of the oldest and most magnificent brick pagoda clusters in southern China.

Having endured a thousand years of weathering and 33 major earthquakes, the Qianxun Pagoda only tilts slightly without collapsing, hailed as "a miracle in Chinese architectural history." During repairs in 1978, over 680 precious cultural relics from the Nanzhao and Dali Kingdom periods were discovered at the top of the pagoda.

Buddha Capital Chongsheng Temple

Chongsheng Temple was once the royal temple of the Nanzhao and Dali Kingdoms, destroyed by war during the Qing Dynasty. In 2005, it was extensively rebuilt on its original site under the concept of "dialogue with history," restoring core structures including the Mahavira Hall and the Rain Bronze Guanyin Hall, recreating the solemn aura of the "Buddha Capital."

The temple is built along the mountainside, covering nearly 600 mu (about 100 acres), with 891 halls and 11,429 Buddha statues, making it the largest single Han Chinese Buddhist temple complex in China. From the top, the magnificent panorama of Cangshan's nineteen peaks unfolds before you, while the blue waves of Erhai Lake shimmer under the sun like Buddhist light.

Golden-winged Garuda

The uniqueness of the Three Pagodas lies not only in the architecture itself. Artifacts unearthed from the pagodas — gilded copper cases, golden statues of the Golden-winged Garuda, and inscribed scriptures — bear witness to the deep cultural exchange between the Nanzhao civilization and the Central Plains, Tibet, and India.

Standing before the Qianxun Pagoda, gazing up at this millennia-old structure that still stands proud, you can truly feel the weight of time. As the sunset glow over Cangshan casts golden light upon the white pagodas, the radiance seems to travel across a thousand years, perfectly blending the splendor of the ancient Nanzhao and Dali Kingdoms with the tranquility of today.
