Museum of the First Sino-Japanese War

The Museum of the First Sino-Japanese War is located on Liugong Island. It is a memorial museum dedicated to the Beiyang Navy and the First Sino-Japanese War. The museum houses a vast collection of precious historical relics, ship models, and historical photographs, comprehensively presenting the historical background and solemn course of the war.

The museum's main building is solemn and dignified, facing the sea. Stepping inside, artifacts such as the anchor of the battleship Dingyuan and the compass of the Zhiyuan are profoundly striking. This is a national-level patriotic education base for remembering the martyrs and commemorating national history.

Admiralty of the Beiyang Navy

The Admiralty of the Beiyang Navy served as the command center of the Beiyang Fleet. Built in 1887 and covering approximately 10,000 square meters, this architectural complex blending Chinese and Western styles witnessed the rise and fall of modern China's navy — from the official establishment of the Beiyang Fleet in 1888 to the devastating defeat in the 1894 naval battle.

Inside, there are council halls, combat command rooms, and the admiral's living quarters, displaying relics of Beiyang Fleet commanders along with historical charts and telegraph messages. Walking through these halls, one can almost envision the naval strategists of a century ago mapping out their campaigns.

Maritime Forest Park

Liugong Island boasts a forest coverage rate of 87%, living up to its name as a true "Maritime Forest Park." The island features lush vegetation and towering ancient trees, with over a hundred species of trees and shrubs including black pine, black locust, and sawtooth oak, forming a natural green barrier.

Walking up the winding forest trails, the rustling of pines and gentle chirping of birds fill the air, with the fresh breeze carrying the fragrance of grass and trees. Ascending to the Qidingshan Fort and gazing into the distance, the blue sea and sky blend with the verdant island, bringing a sense of tranquility and joy.
