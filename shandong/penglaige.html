Penglai Pavilion

Originally built in the sixth year of the Jiayou era of the Song Dynasty (1061 AD), Penglai Pavilion stands atop Danya Mountain. Along with Yellow Crane Tower, Yueyang Tower, and Prince Teng Pavilion, it is renowned as one of the Four Great Towers of ancient China. Rising abruptly from the earth, the ocher-red cliff supports the pavilion high above, often shrouded in mist and clouds, like a celestial palace suspended in midair.

From atop the pavilion, the vast blue sea and sky unfold before your eyes. Since ancient times, Penglai Pavilion has been celebrated as a "wonderland on earth." Legends of Qin Shi Huang's eastern tour in search of the elixir of life and Emperor Wu of Han's visit in pursuit of immortals have added an air of mystery and romance to this landscape of sea and mountains.

Three Immortal Mountains

The Three Immortal Mountains Scenic Area is themed around Taoist immortal culture, bringing to life the legendary mountains of Penglai, Fangzhang, and Yingzhou atop the shimmering waters. The park's layout is exquisitely designed, with pavilions, towers, and terraces gracefully arranged, their reflections dancing on the water and blending seamlessly with the real structures. This creates a breathtaking vision of "celestial mountains rising above the sea, veiled in mist and mystery."

Within the scenic area, a wealth of artistic treasures is preserved, including paintings, jade carvings, and wood sculptures, embodying the grandeur of classical Chinese gardens. Strolling through this landscape feels like stepping back a thousand years into the abode of the immortals, experiencing the profound depth and richness of traditional Chinese culture.

Eight Immortals Crossing the Sea

Shaped like a magical gourd lying across the vast ocean, the Eight Immortals Crossing the Sea Scenic Area weaves the legendary tale of the Eight Immortals into a stunning coastal landscape. Classical structures such as the Eight Immortals Bridge, Immortal Source Tower, and Wangying Tower stand gracefully by the sea, forming a natural tableau against the blue sky and azure waters.

In late spring and early summer each year, mirages occasionally appear over the Penglai seas — towering towers and city walls shifting and shimmering in the air. The ancients believed these were the gates to the realm of immortals. The great poet Su Shi once immortalized this wonder in his verse: "The eastern sea clouds stretch vast and empty, immortals appear and vanish in the radiant sky."
