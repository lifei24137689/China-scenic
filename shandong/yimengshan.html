Guimeng Peak

Guimeng Peak, the main summit of Mount Meng at 1,156 meters, is the second highest peak in Shandong Province. Standing atop, the mountains of southern Shandong unfold before your eyes. When the sea of clouds surges, it feels like stepping into a fairyland. Confucius once ascended Mount Meng and penned the timeless line, "Upon climbing the Eastern Mountain, I find Lu small."

The peak's massive rock resembles a crouching turtle, giving Guimeng Peak its name. At the summit stands the Jade Emperor Hall, where incense has burned for millennia. Gazing into the distance, the Yi River winds like a ribbon and the fields spread like brocade, unfolding a magnificent Yimeng landscape painting.

Yunmeng Peak

Yunmeng Peak, the second highest summit of Mount Meng at 1,108 meters, features layered peaks shrouded in clouds and mist. In spring and summer, azaleas blanket the mountainside, painting the entire peak in brilliant red. In golden autumn, the forests burst into hues of gold, as if stepping into a fairytale world.

With a forest coverage rate of 98% and an exceptionally high concentration of negative oxygen ions, Yunmeng is known as a "natural oxygen bar." Strolling along forest boardwalks, listening to the rustling pines and babbling brooks, it is the perfect escape for city dwellers seeking tranquility and nature.

Red Soil

Yimeng Mountain is not only a mountain of nature but also a mountain of spirit. It is a nationally renowned revolutionary base and the birthplace of the Yimeng Spirit. During the war years, the people of Yimeng nurtured the revolution with millet and pushed forward victory with wheelbarrows. The Yimeng Spirit of "the last piece of cloth for army uniforms, the last mouthful of grain for army rations" moves heaven and earth.

The Yimeng Red Film Base has preserved the original village appearance, where many classic red-themed films and TV series have been shot. The Menglianggu Battle Memorial stands tall, telling the heroic stories of those fiery years. Come to Yimeng and experience the profound legacy of red culture.
