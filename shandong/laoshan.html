Jufeng · Summit of Lao

Jufeng is the main peak of Laoshan, standing at 1,132.7 meters above sea level, commonly known as the "Summit of Lao." From the top, you can gaze out at the vast Yellow Sea to the east with its boundless blue waves, and to the west, the city of Qingdao with its red tiles and green trees unfolding before your eyes. Where mountains meet the sea and the waters blend with the sky, it is hailed as the highest peak along China's coastline.

The Jufeng area is renowned for its four great wonders: "Sunrise at Jufeng," "Sea of Clouds Spectacle," "Laoshan Fireball," and "Silver Peak Icicles." At dawn, the crimson sun leaps from the horizon, bathing the peaks in golden light as the sea of clouds rolls like a celestial realm.

Taiqing Palace · Taoist Ancestral Temple

Located at the southeastern end of Laoshan, embraced by mountains on three sides and facing the sea on one side, Taiqing Palace was first built in the first year of the Jianyuan era of the Western Han Dynasty (140 BC). With a history spanning over two thousand years, it is the oldest and largest Taoist temple in Laoshan.

The temple grounds are filled with ancient trees — cypress from the Han Dynasty, elm from the Tang Dynasty, and ginkgo from the Song Dynasty — all still lush after a thousand years of wind and rain. With its unique layout of "peaks embracing three sides and tides greeting from one," Taiqing Palace is honored as the "Second Greatest Temple of Quanzhen Taoism Under Heaven," with incense burning unceasingly through the ages.

North Nine-Water · Landscape Gallery

North Nine-Water is the most scenic landscape gallery in Laoshan, named for the nine bends of the mountain and the nine curves of the water. The creek winds down through the canyon, forming nine distinct water features — some cascading as waterfalls, others still as mirrored pools — each step revealing a new vista.

In summer when rain is abundant, the Eighteen Pools ripple with emerald waves, and the Tide Sound Waterfall roars like thunder. In autumn, the layered forests are dyed in color, with red leaves reflected in the clear waters, like a vibrant landscape painting. North Nine-Water is also the source of Laoshan mineral water, known for its crisp, sweet quality.
