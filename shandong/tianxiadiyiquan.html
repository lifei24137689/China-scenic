Baotu Spring

Baotu Spring, the foremost of Jinan's Seventy-Two Famous Springs, is honored as the "Finest Spring Under Heaven." Water gushes from underground limestone caverns through three vents simultaneously, thundering like hidden thunder, boiling with vigor, surging day and night without cease.

During his southern inspection tour, Emperor Qianlong of the Qing Dynasty brewed tea with Baotu Spring water and found it mellow and sweet, thus bestowing upon it the title "Finest Spring Under Heaven." The water is crystal clear and refreshing, maintaining a constant temperature year-round, serving as the very soul and symbol of the spring city of Jinan.

Daming Lake

Daming Lake is formed by the convergence of numerous springs in Jinan. Its waters are as clear and blue as a mirror, with weeping willows gently brushing the embankments. Covering approximately 58 hectares, it is a rare natural lake within a bustling city, celebrated as the "Pearl of the Spring City."

On the lake stands the elegant Lixia Pavilion, surrounded by lotus flowers on four sides and willows on three, with the city's hills reflected across half the lake. This has been a place of poetic inspiration for literati through the ages and a serene retreat for Jinan's residents.

Five Dragon Pool

Five Dragon Pool Park, located just north of Baotu Spring, is dotted with 26 ancient springs of various forms, forming one of Jinan's four major spring groups — the Five Dragon Pool Springs. The pool water is crystal clear and emerald green, with fish visible swimming below, surrounded by towering ancient trees.

Within the park, the former residence of Qin Qiong stands in quiet serenity, while the five dragon sculptures beside the pool are remarkably lifelike. Spring water converges into streams, cascading down stone steps, preserving a haven of tranquility amid the urban clamor.
