Jade Emperor Summit

Ascend to the summit

The Jade Emperor Summit is the highest peak of Mount Tai, standing at 1,532.7 meters above sea level. Named after the Jade Emperor Temple built on its crest, this sacred summit has been the site of imperial Fengshan ceremonies for millennia, bearing the profound weight of Chinese civilization.

From the summit, you gaze upon rolling mountain ranges like ink washes and surging seas of clouds. Countless poets and scholars throughout the ages have left timeless verses here, making it the perfect place to experience the majestic scope of "mountains shrink beneath your feet."

South Heaven Gate

A long whistle at heaven's gate

The South Heaven Gate, known in ancient times as "Heaven Pass," stands at the end of the Eighteen Bends and serves as the main gateway to ascend Mount Tai. This ancient structure, first built in the Yuan Dynasty, features soaring eaves, vermilion walls, and golden tiles, resembling the very portal to the celestial realm.

Passing through the South Heaven Gate, you enter the "Celestial Street" wonderland. As mist swirls around, the gate tower appears and disappears like a heavenly palace leading to the clouds, inspiring both awe and longing.

Eighteen Bends

Towering five thousand zhang

The Eighteen Bends are the steepest section of the Mount Tai climbing route, spanning about 1 kilometer with a vertical rise of over 400 meters and more than 1,600 stone steps, hanging like a heavenly ladder against the cliffside.

"Tight eighteen, loose eighteen, neither tight nor loose another eighteen" — each of the three segments offers a distinct character. Every step is a test of endurance and will, and reaching the summit rewards you with an unparalleled sense of clarity and triumph.
