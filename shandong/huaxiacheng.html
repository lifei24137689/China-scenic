Huaxia's First Memorial Archway

Huaxia's First Memorial Archway is the iconic landmark of Weihai Huaxia City, standing majestically at the scenic area entrance. With grand scale and exquisitely carved beams and painted rafters, it blends the essence of traditional Chinese architecture with modern craftsmanship — a vivid embodiment of Huaxia City's philosophy of "ecological restoration and cultural heritage."

Carved entirely from granite and white marble, the archway bears the inscription "Huaxia City" on its front facade. Passing through the archway feels like stepping into a time tunnel — where the miraculous journey from a ruined quarry to lush mountains and clear waters begins.

"Spiritual Journey of Huaxia" Live Performance

"Spiritual Journey of Huaxia" is a large-scale live landscape performance masterfully produced by Huaxia City. Featuring a 360-degree rotating audience seating as its centerpiece, the stage is set amidst real mountains and waters. The performance weaves together five thousand years of Chinese civilization, integrating sound, light, and electricity with modern technology to deliver a feast for both the eyes and the soul.

The stage nestles against mountains and borders waters, ingeniously utilizing the restored quarry's natural terrain to transform cliffs, water surfaces, and hillsides into natural backdrops. As night falls and light and shadow dance across the scene, the audience feels immersed in the river of history, experiencing the grandeur and majesty of Chinese civilization.

Ecological Restoration — A Model for China

Weihai Huaxia City is hailed as "China's No. 1 Eco-Environmental City." Its predecessor was a barren abandoned quarry scarred by years of mining. After more than a decade of ecological restoration, the once-exposed mountains have been cloaked in green again, and the desolate quarry pits have transformed into crystal-clear lakes — a stunning metamorphosis from an "urban scar" to an "urban lung."

Covering approximately 16 square kilometers with a green coverage rate exceeding 95%, this is not only a paradise for visitors but also a textbook case of ecological restoration — showcasing a Chinese path where economic development and ecological protection go hand in hand.
