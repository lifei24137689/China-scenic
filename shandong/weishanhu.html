L A R G E S T F R E S H W A T E R L A K E

Weishan Lake, the largest freshwater lake in northern China, covers an area of approximately 1,266 square kilometers, spanning the provinces of Shandong and Jiangsu. Its vast, misty waters have long been hailed as the "Pearl of Southern Shandong." The Beijing-Hangzhou Grand Canal flows through the lake, merging northern and southern cultures, while millennia of canal transport have left countless historical relics and legendary tales along its shores.

Weishan Lake is formed by four connected lakes — Weishan, Zhaoyang, Dushan, and Nanyang — dotted with islands, lush reeds, and flocks of waterbirds. It is the most brilliant ecological gem on the Huang-Huai-Hai Plain, serving as a vital reservoir for the South-to-North Water Diversion Eastern Route, nurturing millions of people along its banks.

L O T U S L E A V E S S T R E T C H T O T H E S K Y
Endless lotus leaves stretch to the horizon — a summer dream on Weishan Lake

L O T U S S E A

In the height of summer, Weishan Lake's lotus blossoms burst into bloom across tens of thousands of mu, stretching for dozens of miles in a magnificent tapestry of green and pink. Red lotuses blaze, white lotuses shimmer, and a gentle breeze carries their intoxicating fragrance. Drifting on a small boat through the depths of the lotus fields, watching dragonflies skim the water and fish play among the leaves, you may feel as though you have stepped into a poetic dream of the Jiangnan water towns.

Weishan Lake is home to over a hundred lotus varieties, including unique local species such as "Weishan Red." The best viewing season runs from June to August each year, when all the lotus blooms seem to open at once, creating a breathtaking scene of "ten li of fragrance and a hundred li of gallery." During the Lotus Festival, visitors can enjoy traditional activities such as lotus picking, lotus banquets, and floating lotus lanterns.

R A I L W A Y G U E R R I L L A H O M E L A N D

Weishan Lake is not only a treasure trove of natural ecology but also a land of red revolution. During the War of Resistance against Japan, the Railway Guerrillas used Weishan Lake as their base, launching ambushes along the Tianjin-Pukou Railway, striking at the Japanese invaders and writing heroic chapters of resistance. The 1956 film "Railway Guerrilla" brought the song "The sun is setting in the west, silence falls on Weishan Lake" to households across China.

The Railway Guerrilla Memorial Park is located on Weishan Island, featuring a monument, a museum, and grand sculptural groups that vividly recreate the legendary feats of the guerrillas as they boarded trains, blew up bridges, and fought the enemy. As a national patriotic education base, it attracts countless visitors each year who come to honor the martyrs and relive those stirring times.
