Canal Ancient Street · North-South Thoroughfare

Tai'erzhuang Ancient Town is situated at the central hub of the Beijing-Hangzhou Grand Canal. Since ancient times, it has served as the strategic throat of north-south grain transport. During the Ming and Qing dynasties, merchants gathered here and masts stood like a forest, earning it the reputation of "The First Town Under Heaven"—one of the most brilliant pearls of canal civilization.

Within the ancient town, a 3-kilometer-long historic canal channel and numerous ancient docks have been preserved. Blue stone paths wind along the river, and old bridges arch over the emerald waters. Strolling along the canal ancient street feels like traveling back to a prosperous era bustling with merchant ships, oar sounds, and lantern-lit reflections.

Battle Site · City of Heroes

In the spring of 1938, the Battle of Tai'erzhuang erupted here. The Chinese army fought fiercely with their flesh and blood, achieving the first major victory on the main battlefield since the start of the War of Resistance. This triumph shattered the myth of Japanese invincibility and greatly inspired the confidence of the entire nation in the resistance effort.

Today, the Tai'erzhuang Battle Memorial stands solemn and dignified, preserving a vast collection of precious historical artifacts and archival footage. The scarred ancient city wall still stands tall, silently telling the story of those heroic and unforgettable years of warfare—this is a place where the Chinese nation displayed its undaunted spirit.

Ancient Town Reconstruction · Rebirth from Ashes

Tai'erzhuang Ancient Town is the second city in the world, after Warsaw, to be rebuilt on the ruins of World War II. The reconstruction project began in 2008, guided by the philosophy of "preserving antiquity, restoring antiquity, and creating antiquity." Over five years, historic buildings such as the ancient city wall, the Guandi Temple, and the ancient docks were meticulously restored.

The rebuilt ancient town blends the majestic grandeur of northern courtyards with the elegant charm of southern water towns, creating a unique layout where "every courtyard has water, and every household faces the river." In 2012, it was designated a national 5A-level tourist attraction, becoming the most dazzling cultural icon of the Qilu region.
