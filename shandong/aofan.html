Qingdao Aofan Center

The Qingdao Olympic Sailing Center is located on the shores of Fushan Bay, serving as the core venue for the sailing competitions of the 2008 Beijing Olympic Games. Once a competitive stage where Olympic athletes rode the waves, it has now transformed into a coastal cultural tourism landmark integrating events, tourism, and leisure.

Strolling along the coastal promenade of the Aofan Center, the modern sailing marina complements the blue sky and azure sea. Hundreds of sailboats and yachts are moored in the harbor, with masts standing tall and white sails dotting the view, creating a vibrant picture of a coastal city.

Lovers Pier · International Conference Center

Lovers Pier is a romantic promenade extending into the sea from the Aofan Center, stretching 534 meters with a white lighthouse standing at its end. When night falls, the lighthouse glows with warm light as couples walk hand in hand against the backdrop of Qingdao dazzling city skyline — the epitome of romance.

The Qingdao International Conference Center is built along the sea, with a streamlined architectural appearance that undulates like ocean waves, serving as the main venue for the 2018 SCO Qingdao Summit. Blending coastal architectural aesthetics with modern design concepts, it has become a new window for Qingdao to the world.

Olympic Torch · Marine Museum

The Olympic Torch sculpture within the Aofan Center is an iconic landmark of the scenic area. This colossal torch sculpture, standing over 20 meters tall, symbolizes the eternal Olympic spirit, creating a powerful visual impact against the backdrop of blue sea and sky — a must-visit destination for tourists.

The Marine Museum, with its rich marine culture exhibitions, showcases Qingdao's profound heritage as the "City of Sails". From ancient maritime civilization to modern sailing sports, from marine ecological conservation to the vision of the blue economy, it presents an immersive feast of marine culture.
