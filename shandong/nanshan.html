Nanshan Grand Buddha

The Nanshan Grand Buddha is the world's largest seated tin-bronze Buddha, standing 38.66 meters tall and weighing approximately 380 tons. Composed of 232 body parts, 108 lotus petals, and 302 ushnisha coils — 642 tin-bronze castings in total — it is a rare masterpiece of Buddhist art.

The Buddha sits atop Nanshan Mountain, facing south with eyes gently lowered, right hand in the Abhaya Mudra and left in the Varada Mudra — majestic and compassionate, blessing all beings. Beneath the statue lies the Ten Thousand Buddhas Hall, housing 9,999 small Buddha statues, which together with the grand Buddha above symbolize "all Buddhas returning to the source."

Nanshan Zen Temple

Nanshan Zen Temple was first built in the Tang Dynasty, enduring a thousand years of history through cycles of prosperity and decline. Built along the mountainside, its Heavenly King Hall, Grand Buddha Hall, and Sutra Depository unfold along the central axis, with red walls and golden tiles nestled among ancient pines and cypresses, filled with the sound of chanting and the scent of sandalwood.

The temple houses the Tripitaka and other Buddhist scriptures, with morning bells and evening drums echoing through the centuries. On major events like Buddha's Birthday and the Ullambana Festival, devotees gather from all directions to worship and chant, creating a profoundly solemn atmosphere. It is one of the most important Buddhist centers in the Jiaodong region.

Avatamsaka World

The Avatamsaka World is designed around the Avatamsaka Sutra's philosophy of "a flower contains a world, a leaf reveals a Buddha." It comprises three major areas: the Avatamsaka Dharma Hall, Avatamsaka Pagoda, and Avatamsaka Assembly. The Medicine Buddha Jade Hall enshrines a translucent jade Medicine Buddha — a rare large-scale jade Buddha statue in China.

The Nanshan Medicine Buddha is carved from a single block of Burmese white jade, standing 13.8 meters tall and weighing over 660 tons. Radiant and serene, the jade Buddha is adorned with seven precious gems, shimmering under the light and inspiring awe and tranquility in all who behold it. The Avatamsaka World embodies the essence of Buddhist art and is another highlight of the Nanshan Scenic Area.
