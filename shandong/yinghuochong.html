Firefly Water Cave

The Firefly Water Cave is a rare firefly viewing sanctuary in Asia. The cave maintains a constant temperature of 18℃ year-round, with surging underground rivers and towering stalactites. Taking a boat deep into the cave, you look up to see thousands of fireflies twinkling like stars, dotting the dome like a fallen galaxy, leaving you breathless.

From June to August each year, fireflies enter their active season, and the density of light dots inside the cave reaches its peak. These luminous sprites have extremely strict requirements for their ecological environment. The fact that fireflies thrive and multiply here is nature’s most gentle endorsement of this pure land. Being here feels like a dream.

Underground Grand Canyon

Linyi Underground Grand Canyon stretches 6,100 meters in length, making it one of China’s super-large karst caves, hailed as “China’s No. 1 Cave for Underground River Rafting.” The canyon reaches a maximum width of over 100 meters and a maximum height of 46 meters, creating a majestic and awe-inspiring spectacle. Underground rivers meander through the canyon, with stalactites in countless fascinating forms.

Underground river rafting is the biggest highlight, spanning about 1,000 meters. Riding a rubber raft through the darkness, you experience thrilling rapids and calm, mirror-like waters in turns. The stalactites on both sides glisten under the colorful lights, creating a dazzling underground world known as the “Underground Three Gorges.”

Butterfly Valley & Stalactite Wonders

Butterfly Valley is another major highlight of the Firefly Water Cave scenic area. Flowers bloom year-round in the valley, with butterflies dancing gracefully. Hundreds of butterfly species inhabit and flutter here, complementing the surrounding green mountains and clear waters, creating a paradise on earth. Strolling through, the harmony between man and nature unfolds before your eyes.

The stalactite clusters of the Underground Grand Canyon formed over hundreds of millions of years of geological change. Stalagmites, stalactites, and draperies take on various shapes—some like inverted lotuses, others like cascading waterfalls. Illuminated by colorful lights, the entire cave resembles a natural underground palace, quietly telling the story of Earth’s vicissitudes.
