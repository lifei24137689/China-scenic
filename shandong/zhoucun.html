M A I N S T R E E T

Zhoucun Main Street is the central axis of the ancient commercial city, originally about 1,000 meters (two Chinese miles) long. Both sides retain a large number of Ming and Qing dynasty shop buildings. Blue stone roads, wooden door panels, carved window lattices — every detail tells the story of the bustling "Dry Port" of the past.

As an important birthplace of Shandong merchant culture, this was once one of the largest commercial distribution centers in the north, earning the reputation "Golden Zhoucun." Strolling through, it feels like traveling back to the Ming and Qing dynasties when merchants gathered.

S I L K S T R E E T · S I L V E R M A R K E T

Silk Market Street was once the center of northern silk trade, with merchants gathering from all over, creating a scene of "mulberry fields covering the countryside, every household raising silkworms, the sound of looms everywhere, every family weaving silk." Silver Market was the financial center of the time, with banks and exchange shops lining the streets.

These two streets bear witness to Zhoucun's glorious history as a northern commercial hub, still preserving the former sites of century-old brands such as Ruifuxiang and Qianxiangyi.

G R A N D D Y E H O U S E

The Grand Dye House is one of the most representative industrial heritage sites of Zhoucun Ancient Commercial City. The hit TV series "The Grand Dye House" made it a household name, vividly recreating the prosperity of the national printing and dyeing industry in the late Qing and early Republic periods.

The towering cloth-drying racks, ancient dye vats, and mottled brick walls silently tell the story of China's modern national industry. This is not only a tourist attraction but also a living museum of industrial culture.
