Six Centuries of Timeless Charm

Located in the southern outskirts of Huaxi District, Guiyang City, Guizhou Province, Qingyan Ancient Town was first built during the Hongwu Era of the Ming Dynasty (1378). Named after the Qingyan Mountain outside the town, it is one of the four great ancient towns of Guizhou. Built along the mountain contours with winding city walls, it has weathered over six hundred years of history while preserving its Ming and Qing Dynasty architectural layout and ancient charm.

Qingyan Ancient Town blends Han, Buyi, Miao and other ethnic cultures. Temples, pavilions, ancestral halls, and residential buildings are scattered in harmonious arrangement, with stone alleys, stone steps, and stone walls visible everywhere. Confucianism, Buddhism, and Taoism coexist within the town, while Christianity and Catholicism have also left their traces, creating a unique multicultural landscape. It is hailed as one of "China's Most Charming Towns."

Dingguang Gate · Tracing Historic Relics

Dingguang Gate is the southern entrance of Qingyan Ancient Town and one of its most iconic structures. The gate tower stands tall and magnificent, with the characters "Dingguang" inscribed boldly on the gate plaque. The city walls, built from bluestone, are solid and ancient in style. Ascending the gate tower, you can gaze at the rolling mountains in the distance and overlook the entire town, feeling as if six hundred years of history are flowing beneath your feet.

Zhao Gong Memorial Temple was built in honor of Zhao Yijiong, a renowned official of the Qing Dynasty who was the first Zhuangyuan (top scholar) in Guizhou's history. Wanshou Palace, first built in the Ming Dynasty, was converted from a Jiangxi Guild Hall into a Taoist site, featuring exquisite carved beams, painted rafters, and colorful murals. Ciyun Temple, built during the Kangxi Era of the Qing Dynasty, houses well-preserved Buddhist statues and murals, exuding tranquility and solemnity. Zhuangyuan Mansion, the former residence of Zhao Yijiong, features green tiles and white walls with deep courtyards, bearing witness to the glory of achieving the highest imperial examination honor.

A Taste of Qingyan

Qingyan Ancient Town is one of Guiyang locals' favorite food destinations. Qingyan Braised Pig Trotters (Zhuangyuan Trotter) are famous far and wide — legend has it that Zhao Yijiong, the top scholar of the late Qing Dynasty, ate pig trotters before his imperial exam, giving the dish its name. Slowly simmered over low heat, the trotters turn an appealing golden color with tender, melt-in-your-mouth meat. This traditional dish, steeped in story, has become Qingyan's culinary signature. The flavors of Qingyan tofu and rose cakes — these delicacies are hidden deep in the town's stone alleys, waiting for every visitor to discover and savor.

Strolling along the town's flagstone streets, the air is filled with the aroma of braised meats, the sweetness of pastries, and a medley of other delightful scents intertwined — creating a unique olfactory memory that belongs only to Qingyan Ancient Town. It attracts visitors from all over the country to savor this centuries-old culinary experience, filling this ancient town with the warmth of everyday life and vibrant energy.
