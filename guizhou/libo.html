Emerald on the Earth's Belt

Libo Zhangjiang Scenic Area is located in Libo County, Qiannan Buyi and Miao Autonomous Prefecture, Guizhou Province, covering a total area of 273.1 square kilometers. It is a core component of the UNESCO World Natural Heritage "South China Karst." Featuring classic karst landforms where mountains and waters blend seamlessly in a jade-green landscape, it is renowned as the "Emerald on the Earth's Belt."

The scenic area consists of three parts: Xiaoqikong, Daqikong, and Shuichun River, integrating mountains, waters, forests, caves, lakes, waterfalls, and bridges. It is one of the best-preserved karst forest ecosystems in China and the world. The water here is crystal-clear emerald green, the forests are lush and vibrant — every scene is a living painting.

Xiaoqikong · Landscape Essence

Xiaoqikong is the most renowned core scenic area of Libo Zhangjiang, named after a seven-arch ancient bridge built during the Daoguang era of the Qing Dynasty. Stretching about 7 kilometers, it is celebrated for its exquisite and delicate beauty — every step reveals a scenic wonder, earning it the title "Superb Bonsai." The most breathtaking sight is the 68-tier cascading waterfall: within a valley less than 2 kilometers long, 68 layers of waterfalls flow down the travertine terraces, their murmuring waters sounding like a zither.

Laya Waterfall cascades down from the lush green mountainside, filling the air with mist and a refreshing coolness. The Water Forest is a unique wonder of Libo — clear streams race over rocks while towering ancient trees take root in stone crevices, creating the remarkable scene of "water flowing over stone, trees growing in water." The seven-arch ancient bridge, ancient and elegant, reflects upon the emerald water, creating Libo's most iconic image.

Daqikong · Majestic Gorge

Daqikong Scenic Area is characterized by primitive forests, gorges, subterranean streams, and underground lakes. Unlike the delicate beauty of Xiaoqikong, Daqikong presents a majestic and grand spectacle. The Natural Bridge within the area stands 73 meters high, spans about 22 meters wide, with a thickness of 15 meters — a giant natural limestone arch bridge that stands as a masterpiece of nature's ingenuity.

Shuichun River Scenic Area is the upper section of the Zhangjiang River, stretching about 13 kilometers, famous for its gorge rafting. Precipitous cliffs line both banks, dense forests cloak the mountainsides, and the river water is crystal clear. Drifting downstream by kayak, you will encounter rushing rapids and calm, mirror-like waters in turn, fully experiencing the scenic beauty of Libo Zhangjiang. Buyi and Shui ethnic villages dot the banks along the way, where culture and nature shine together.
