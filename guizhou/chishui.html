Chishui Grand Waterfall

Standing 76 meters tall and 80 meters wide, the Chishui Grand Waterfall is the largest waterfall within China's Danxia landform and one of the most representative Danxia waterfalls in the Yangtze River basin. The surging Chishui River plunges from the red sandstone cliffs, filling the air with mist and thunderous roar.

Unlike the elegant beauty of Huangguoshu Waterfall, Chishui Grand Waterfall takes on a unique ochre-red hue reflected by the Danxia red rock walls. Rainbows often appear in the mist under sunlight, earning it the title "The Premier Waterfall of Danxia." During the rainy season, the waterfall cascades with overwhelming power; in drier months, it hangs like a beaded curtain, offering a different kind of charm.

Buddha Light Rock · Danxia Wonder

Buddha Light Rock is the iconic landmark of Chishui Danxia. A massive curved red rock wall rises abruptly from the green bamboo sea, standing about 300 meters high with an arc stretching over a thousand meters, like a giant red screen. The rock face transitions from ochre-red to golden-red during dawn and dusk, creating a truly spectacular sight.

Multiple slender streams cascade down the rock face like silver threads, with water droplets shimmering under sunlight as if blessed by Buddha's light — hence the name "Buddha Light Rock." Surrounded by primeval forests and bamboo seas, the striking contrast between the red rock wall and green vegetation makes it a paradise for photography enthusiasts.

Four Cave Gully · Bamboo Sea Retreat

Four Cave Gully gets its name from four distinct tiered waterfalls: Water Curtain Cave, Moon Pool, Frog Leap Cliff, and White Dragon Pool. These four cascading waterfalls descend step by step along the Danxia gorge, each with a deep pool beneath, where the water is crystal clear and surrounded by lush bamboo.

Chishui is home to the world's largest bamboo sea at the same latitude — Chishui Bamboo Sea National Forest Park, covering approximately 170,000 mu (about 11,333 hectares). Strolling along the bamboo paths, a gentle breeze rustles the leaves, intertwining with the distant sound of waterfalls into a natural symphony. The green bamboo sea and red Danxia rock walls create a stunning complementary palette.
