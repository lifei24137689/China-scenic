Underground Palace

Zhijindong Cave, located in Zhijin County, Bijie City, Guizhou Province, is one of China's most beautiful tourist caves and a UNESCO Global Geopark. The cave has been surveyed to extend over 12 km, with a tourist route of 6.6 km and a total area exceeding 700,000 square meters, making it the largest and most well-developed karst cave in China.

Inside the cave, stalactites, stalagmites, draperies, and cave flowers take on myriad forms, shimmering dreamily under colorful lights. "After Huangshan, no other mountain is worth seeing; beyond Zhijindong, no other cave exists" — this timeless praise speaks to Zhijindong's extraordinary allure. With its grand chambers, exquisite formations, and abundant deposits, it is hailed as "the No.1 Cave Under Heaven."

Wonders of the Cave

Zhijindong Cave comprises over a dozen grand halls, including the Welcoming Hall, Scripture Lecture Hall, Pagoda Forest Palace, Longevity Palace, Wangshan Lake, Lingxiao Hall, Guanghan Palace, and Snow Fragrance Palace, each with its own distinctive character. Guanghan Palace, towering approximately 70 meters high with an area of tens of thousands of square meters, is one of the most magnificent halls in the cave.

The 17-meter-tall "Silver Rain Tree" is the cave's most treasured formation — a rare palm-shaped stalagmite of pure white, gracefully standing tall, having taken hundreds of thousands of years to achieve its perfect form. Other iconic sights include the "Overlord's Helmet," "Inverted Pipa," and "Sister Jade Trees" — each a masterpiece carved by nature over billions of years.
