The Earth's Ribbon · Baili Flower Corridor

Baili Dujuan Scenic Area is located in Bijie City, Guizhou Province, covering a total area of 125.8 square kilometers. It is a National 5A Tourist Attraction and a National Forest Park. This is the world's largest natural azalea forest belt, stretching over 50 kilometers with a width of 1 to 3 kilometers, renowned as the "Ribbon of the Earth" and the "World's Natural Garden."

The scenic area boasts 64 species of azaleas, covering 4 of the world's 5 major azalea subgenera, accounting for nearly one-third of China's azalea species. From March to May each year, the mountainsides burst into bloom with azaleas in red, white, pink, purple, and yellow, weaving together into a magnificent floral corridor. Rare species such as Ma Ying azalea, Dewdrop azalea, and Tree-form azalea are breathtaking, with the fiery red Ma Ying azalea being the iconic flower of Baili Dujuan.

Pudi Scenic Area · Heart of the Flower Sea

Pudi Scenic Area is the core area of Baili Dujuan, located in the middle section of the azalea sea. With relatively open terrain, it offers the most spectacular floral landscape. The area features famous attractions such as Shuhua Peak, Baihua Plain, and Jinji Valley. Shuhua Peak provides the best panoramic view of Baili Dujuan — from the summit, you can take in the azalea sea stretching for dozens of miles in layered splendor, like brocade spreading across the land.

Baihua Plain is a naturally formed flat meadow surrounded by colorful azaleas. During bloom season, it becomes the liveliest spot in Baili Dujuan. Ma Ying azaleas grow most luxuriantly here, their fiery red clusters like burning flames, weaving together with distant mountains, blue skies, and white clouds into an intoxicating spring landscape. Pudi Scenic Area is also home to Yi and Miao ethnic minorities. During the bloom season, local ethnic dances, songs, and traditional festivals add rich cultural ambiance to the sea of flowers.

Jinpo Scenic Area · King of Azaleas

Jinpo Scenic Area is located in the western section of Baili Dujuan, known for its steep terrain and rich variety of azalea species. Its most notable attraction is the "King of Azaleas" — an ancient Ma Ying azalea tree over a thousand years old, standing about 8 meters tall with a crown spanning over 10 meters. Every spring it blooms with tens of thousands of flowers, blazing with color, earning the title "China's King of Azaleas."

The terrain of Jinpo Scenic Area is quite undulating, with azaleas distributed in layered patterns along the mountain slopes, creating a unique three-dimensional floral landscape. Strolling along the paths, you are surrounded by a canopy of brilliant azalea blossoms overhead, while a carpet of fallen petals covers the ground beneath your feet, and the air is filled with a delicate fragrance. The Dewdrop azalea found here has pinkish-white petals naturally marked with crystal dewdrop-like spots, as if touched by morning dew — one of the most ornamental rare species in Baili Dujuan.
