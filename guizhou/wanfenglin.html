Only Here, Peaks Form a Forest

Wanfenglin is located in Xingyi City, Qianxinan Buyi and Miao Autonomous Prefecture, Guizhou Province. Covering an area of approximately 2,000 square kilometers, it is the largest and most typical karst peak forest landscape in China. The Ming dynasty geographer Xu Xiake once marveled: “There are countless mountains under heaven, but only here do peaks form a forest,” and “Towering peaks stretch for thousands of li, a spectacular wonder of the southwest.”

Wanfenglin is divided into the Eastern and Western peak forest areas. The undulating cone-shaped peaks stand like thousands of troops in mighty formation. Nestled among the peaks, Buyi villages are scattered like stars across a tapestry of farmland, with the Nahui River meandering through. This creates a stunning tableau of “peaks amidst fields, villages amidst fields,” earning it the reputation of “China's Most Beautiful Peak Forest.”

East & West Peaks · A Scroll of Heaven and Earth

The Eastern peak forest is renowned for its grandeur, with steep cliffs and layered peaks that resemble a fairyland when shrouded in clouds and mist. From a high vantage point, thousands of peaks unfold before your eyes in a magnificent display. At dawn, golden sunlight spills over the peaks, creating a dreamlike interplay of light and shadow.

The Western peak forest is celebrated for its elegance and beauty, where peaks harmoniously coexist with farmland and villages. The terrain here is gentler, with Buyi villages scattered across the flatlands between peak clusters. Rice paddies mirror the sky, and the Nahui River winds through like a silk ribbon. In spring, when the rapeseed flowers bloom, the golden sea of blossoms creates a stunning pastoral landscape alongside the emerald peaks.

Bagua Celestial Seal · Buyi Homestead

The Bagua (Eight Trigram) Field is one of the most wondrous natural landscapes in Wanfenglin. On a flat expanse between the peaks, rice paddies naturally form a bagua pattern, with a circular shallow depression at the center and radiating paddies arranged around it like a natural Tai Chi bagua diagram. This remarkable sight is a perfect fusion of karst landforms and agricultural civilization, a breathtaking wonder to behold.

Nahui Village is the most representative ancient Buyi village deep within the peak forest, with a history spanning several hundred years. Stone-walled, gray-tiled traditional houses are scattered in an orderly fashion, ancient banyan trees tower overhead, and intangible cultural heritage such as Buyi embroidery and Bayin Zuochang (eight-tone seated singing) thrives here. Strolling through the village, you can experience the rich ethnic culture and a sense of serenity reminiscent of paradise.
