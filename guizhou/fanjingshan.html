Red Cloud Golden Summit

At an elevation of 2,572 meters, the Red Cloud Golden Summit is the iconic landmark of Fanjingshan. The peak rises abruptly from the ground, with a vertical drop of nearly a hundred meters, standing like a giant pillar piercing the sky. It is named "Red Cloud Golden Summit" because red clouds and auspicious mists often surround the summit in the morning.

The summit is split into two peaks, connected by a sky bridge, with a temple on each side — the Shakyamuni Hall and the Maitreya Hall, enshrining the Buddha of the Present and the Buddha of the Future, symbolizing a dialogue between past and future. The ascent is extraordinarily steep, requiring climbers to pull themselves up by iron chains using both hands and feet. But the breathtaking view of rolling sea of clouds and mountains bowing below makes every effort worthwhile.

Mushroom Stone · A Billion-Year Vigil

The Mushroom Stone is another iconic wonder of Fanjingshan. Standing about ten meters tall, it is wider at the top and narrower at the bottom, resembling a mushroom, and has stood atop the mountain for over a billion years. Formed from metamorphic rock, this breathtaking geological marvel was shaped by hundreds of millions of years of wind and water erosion.

Though it appears precarious, it stands as firm as Mount Tai. The Mushroom Stone has become a symbol of Fanjingshan's indomitable spirit. Around it, a collection of uniquely shaped rocks — such as the "Ten Thousand Scrolls" and the "Heaven-Sealing Stamp" — form a spectacular natural rock museum, showcasing the extraordinary craftsmanship of nature.

Chengen Temple · A Millennium of Buddhist Chants

Chengen Temple is located between the Old and New Golden Summits of Fanjingshan. Built during the Ming Dynasty, it is an important vessel of Fanjingshan's Buddhist culture. Fanjingshan has been one of China's most famous Buddhist mountains since ancient times, standing alongside Mount Wutai, Mount Emei, Mount Putuo, and Mount Jiuhua, and is recognized as the bodhimanda of Maitreya Bodhisattva.

The mountain is dotted with temples, where morning bells and evening drums echo with Buddhist chants. Chengen Temple has endured centuries of wind and rain, witnessing the changes of this "Sacred Land of Purity." The temple houses precious Buddhist relics and stone inscriptions, making it an important heritage site for the study of Buddhist history in Southwest China. In 2018, Fanjingshan was inscribed on the World Natural Heritage List for its unique natural ecology and rich biodiversity.
