Foreword

Tai Chi Ancient Town · Millennium Zhenyuan

Zhenyuan Ancient Town is located on the banks of the Wuyang River in Qiandongnan Miao and Dong Autonomous Prefecture, Guizhou Province, with a history of over 2,200 years. Surrounded by mountains on all sides, the Wuyang River winds through the town in an S-shape, dividing it into northern and southern banks. From a distance, it resembles a natural Tai Chi diagram, earning it the name "Tai Chi Ancient Town."

As a renowned national historic and cultural city, Zhenyuan has long been known as the "Key to Yunnan and Hunan, Gateway to Eastern Guizhou." Historically, it served as an important military stronghold and commercial hub in Southwest China, where Central Plains culture, Jing-Chu culture, and Miao-Dong culture converged and blended, leaving behind a wealth of historical relics and unique cultural landscapes. Ancient alleys, wells, and traditional residences dot the old town, with every blue stone slab bearing the marks of time.

Heritage Exploration

Qinglong Cave · Zhusheng Bridge

The Qinglong Cave ancient architecture complex is the most renowned cultural treasure of Zhenyuan Ancient Town. Located on the east bank of the Wuyang River, it consists of Qinglong Cave, Zhongyuan Cave, Ziyang Cave, and others — an architectural complex integrating Confucianism, Buddhism, and Taoism. Built into the mountainside, the structures rise in layers with striking variety, blending the delicacy of Jiangnan gardens with the grandeur of mountain architecture, earning it the title "Suspended Temple of Southwest China."

Zhusheng Bridge spans the Wuyang River. First built during the Ming Dynasty, it is an iconic landmark of the ancient town. The bridge is constructed from blue stone with seven arched openings, featuring a simple and elegant design. The Kuixing Pavilion at the center rises with flying eaves and carved beams, facing Qinglong Cave across the river. Together, they form a stunning landscape painting. Standing on the bridge, you can take in the verdant mountains, the emerald waters, and the full panorama of the ancient town.

Nature & Culture

Wuyang River · Ancient City Wall

The Wuyang River is the soul of Zhenyuan Ancient Town. It meanders through the town, dividing it in two. The water is clear and emerald green, with lush mountains reflected on both sides, creating a picturesque scene. A boat ride along the Wuyang River reveals overlapping green peaks and ancient buildings lining the waterfront, like a flowing landscape painting. Fishing boats on the river, willows along the banks, and pedestrians on the bridges bring this vibrant waterside tableau to life.

Zhenyuan's ancient city wall was first built during the Ming Dynasty. It winds along the mountain terrain in an imposing manner. Originally featuring four gates, two well-preserved sections remain — the Fu City Wall and the Wei City Wall — stretching approximately 2 kilometers. Climbing the ancient city wall offers a panoramic view of the entire town: the Wuyang River wraps around it like a jade belt, layered residential buildings with green tiles and white walls stretch before you, and distant mountains are shrouded in mist and clouds — a truly refreshing sight.
