Dragon Palace Cave · Underground Realm

Longgong (Dragon Palace) is located in Anshun, Guizhou a national 5A scenic area centered on its water karst cave system. Spanning 60 km², it is known as "China's #1 Water Karst Cave", with a 5,000m underground river a globally rare marvel.

The Dragon Palace cave cluster comprises numerous karst caves of varying sizes, filled with stunning stalactites, stalagmites, curtains, and flowers. Illuminated by colorful lights, the caves transform into a dreamlike wonderland. The winding underground river invites boat rides that feel like a journey through the earth's hidden core.

Dragon Gate Waterfall · Cave Giant

The Dragon Gate Waterfall is the centerpiece of the Dragon Palace area 38m high and 25m wide, it is China's largest cave waterfall. Thundering down from the cave ceiling, its waters crash against the rocks, sending up mist that often spawns brilliant rainbows in the sunlight. Suspended like a giant skylight, the Dragon Gate is a breathtaking sight as water bursts through the rock.

What makes the Dragon Gate Waterfall unique is that it lies entirely within a cave a natural wonder formed when the underground river plummeted from the cave roof during karst evolution. Surrounding the waterfall are abundant cave formations, with falls inside caves and pools below falls a masterpiece of karst geology.

Heavenly Lake · Guanyin Cave

Heavenly Lake is a jewel of the Dragon Palace scenic area, perched atop the mountain with crystal-clear emerald waters mirroring the blue sky, white clouds, and surrounding greenery. Its serene surface echoes the underground river below, creating a remarkable "heaven above, earth below" landscape.

Guanyin Cave is an important Buddhist cultural site within the Dragon Palace area, housing a statue of Guanyin (Goddess of Mercy) with thriving incense offerings. Caves within caves, sights within sights naturally formed stalactites in varied shapes complement theman-made sculpture. Dragon Palace integrates karst caves, underground river, waterfall, and heavenly lake into one visitable year-round, each season with its own charm.
