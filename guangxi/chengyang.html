Millennium Dong Village · Mystical Chengyang

Chengyang Eight Villages is located in Sanjiang Dong Autonomous County, Liuzhou City, Guangxi. It consists of eight Dong villages — Ma'an, Yan, Ping, Dazhai, Dong, Jichang, Pingtan, and Pingpu — making it one of the largest Dong ethnic communities in China. Preserving the most complete traditional Dong culture, it is hailed as the "Living Museum of World Dong Culture."

Chengyang Eight Villages was first built during the Ming Dynasty. After centuries of development, it has formed a grand-scale Dong village cluster with a unique layout. Over 2,000 stilt houses are built along the hillsides in scattered harmony, 13 drum towers stand tall, and 11 wind-and-rain bridges span across streams, composing a magnificent picture of the Dong homeland.

Chengyang Wind-Rain Bridge · World-Famous Bridge

The Chengyang Wind-Rain Bridge is the landmark of Chengyang Eight Villages and one of the world's four great historic bridges. Built in 1912, the bridge has a total length of 77.76 meters and a deck width of 3.75 meters. It was constructed using traditional Dong wooden mortise-and-tenon joinery, without a single nail or rivet, and has stood firm for over a century through wind and rain.

The wind-rain bridge integrates bridge, corridor, and pavilion into one structure. Multiple pavilions are built atop the bridge, featuring soaring eaves and exquisite painted decorations. Long benches are placed along both sides inside the bridge for travelers to rest and shelter from the rain. This bridge is not only a vital thoroughfare but also an important venue for the Dong people to socialize, discuss matters, and welcome guests, carrying the collective memory of the Dong homeland.

Dong Grand Song · Heavenly Sound

Dong Grand Song is a multi-part folk chorus art passed down through generations of the Dong people, inscribed on the UNESCO Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity. In Chengyang Eight Villages, during festivals or when distinguished guests visit, Dong people of all ages gather in front of the drum tower and sing the melodious grand songs, with their voices echoing through the valleys, enchanting all who hear them.

The drum tower is the soul of the Dong village and a symbol of Dong culture. The 13 drum towers in Chengyang Eight Villages each have their own distinctive features, with the tallest being the Ping Village Drum Tower at 23 meters. The drum tower is not only a place for the Dong people to gather, discuss matters, and entertain but also a treasure of Dong architectural art, with layered soaring eaves resembling a phoenix spreading its wings.
