Crocodile Hill · Volcanic Wonderland

The Nanwan Crocodile Hill Scenic Area is located in the southern part of Weizhou Island, Beihai City. It is the core scenic area of Weizhou Island, China's youngest volcanic island. Crocodile Hill gets its name from its shape, which resembles a crocodile crawling on the sea surface. This area features the most concentrated volcanic geological landscapes on the island, where lava remains from volcanic eruptions millions of years ago are still clearly visible.

Strolling along the coastal boardwalk, you can closely admire the unique rock textures formed by volcanic lava and experience the geological wonder carved by the sea and volcano together. The azure blue sea water crashes against the black volcanic rocks, creating a striking and breathtaking contrast of colors — every spot is a masterpiece of nature's extraordinary craftsmanship.

Colorful Beach · Sunrise Spectacle

Colorful Beach is the most famous sunrise viewing spot on Weizhou Island, named for the colorful volcanic rock layers scattered across the beach. After the tide recedes, large flat volcanic lava terraces emerge from the sea, revealing rich layers of red, brown, blue-green, gray, and yellow under the morning light — an incredibly beautiful sight.

At sunrise, golden sunlight spills over the colorful volcanic rocks, reflecting off the blue-green sea water to create Weizhou Island's most enchanting natural tableau. Walking on the beach, exploring the tide pools, and collecting seashells — experiencing the leisurely and pleasurable island life — is an unmissable experience for every visitor to Weizhou Island.

Catholic Church · Century of Guardianship

Weizhou Island Catholic Church was built in 1853 by French missionaries leading local island residents using local volcanic rock and coral stone. It is the most representative cultural building on Weizhou Island. The church features Gothic-style architecture with towering spires, exquisite stained glass windows, and thick volcanic rock walls, still standing tall after a century of wind and rain.

Dripping Water Danxia Screen and Stone Snail Mouth Beach are two other unmissable natural attractions on Weizhou Island. Dripping Water Danxia Screen gets its name from the water droplets that constantly drip from the cliff face, creating a particularly moving silhouette at sunset. Stone Snail Mouth Beach is an excellent spot for watching sunsets and snorkeling, with clear waters where coral and tropical fish are clearly visible.
