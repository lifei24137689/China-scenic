Two Rivers & Four Lakes · Guilin's Iconic Card

Located in the heart of Guilin's urban center, the Two Rivers & Four Lakes · Elephant Trunk Hill Scenic Area is the soul of Guilin's cityscape. The scenic area is composed of the Li River, Peach Blossom River, Shan Lake, Rong Lake, Gui Lake, Mulong Lake, and Elephant Trunk Hill, with vast water surfaces forming a unique urban landscape where "the city lies within the scenery, and the scenery lies within the city."

Elephant Trunk Hill is the icon and symbol of Guilin, named for its striking resemblance to an elephant drinking from the river. Though only slightly over 200 meters in elevation, this stone hill has been the quintessential representation of Guilin's landscape for thousands of years. Since the Song Dynasty, the poetic line "Below the water there is a bright moon, above the water the bright moon floats" has been celebrated to this day.

Sun & Moon Pagodas · Pagodas Reflected on the Lake

The Sun and Moon Pagodas stand on Shan Lake, shining pearls of Guilin's night scenery. The Sun Pagoda, made of copper, stands 41 meters tall with nine stories; the Moon Pagoda, made of glazed tiles, stands 35 meters tall with seven stories. One gold and one silver, one tall and one short, they complement each other as if made by heaven.

When the evening lights come on, the Sun and Moon Pagodas shimmer with colorful reflections on the lake, creating one of the most enchanting scenes in Guilin's nightscape. Visitors can climb the pagodas for a panoramic view of Guilin or take a night cruise along the Two Rivers and Four Lakes to experience the charming allure of this "city on the water."

Rong & Mulong Lakes · Waterfront Charm

Rong Lake and Mulong Lake are two of the most charming water bodies in the Two Rivers and Four Lakes system. Along the shores of Rong Lake, ancient banyan trees reach for the sky, willows sway gently, and pavilions and terraces are scattered across the islets — the perfect place for locals to take a leisurely stroll. The Ancient South Gate stands quietly by the lake, telling the thousand-year history of Guilin.

Mulong Lake is characterized by its Song Dynasty-style architecture. Cluster of ancient-style buildings line the lake, harmonizing beautifully with the emerald waters and green trees. The exquisite Mulong Pagoda by the lakeside reflects gracefully upon the rippling blue waves, like a flowing Song Dynasty painting that captivates every visitor.
