Millennia-old Rock Art · Immortal Civilization

Huashan Rock Art is located along the Zuo River in Ningming County, Chongzuo City, Guangxi. It is a massive rock painting created by the Luoyue people, ancestors of the Zhuang ethnic group, on cliffs during the Warring States period to the Eastern Han Dynasty over 2,000 years ago. In 2016, Huashan Rock Art was inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List, becoming China's first rock art World Heritage site.

Huashan Rock Art is distributed across 38 rock art sites along the Zuo River and its tributary, the Ming River, spanning 105 kilometers. It is the largest, most content-rich, and best-preserved rock art group discovered in China. Painted in ochre-red pigment, the images remain strikingly vivid after thousands of years of weathering, making it a true treasure of world rock art.

Luoyue Imprint · Ancient Code

The most distinctive feature of Huashan Rock Art is its grand human figures and mysterious compositional language. The human figures in the rock art predominantly appear in a squatting frog-like posture, with arms raised and legs spread apart. This pattern, known as the "squatting human figure," is an iconic symbol of Luoyue culture. The figures vary in size, with the tallest reaching over 3 meters, creating an awe-inspiring spectacle.

Beyond human figures, the rock art also features bronze drums, swords, boats, animals, and various other elements, vividly recording the Luoyue people's rituals, battles, fishing, and hunting scenes. These mysterious symbols serve as a wordless history book, telling future generations about the glory and mystery of the ancient Luoyue civilization.

Zuo River Gallery · Landscape & Rock Art

The Zuo River is a major waterway in southwestern Guangxi, flanked by towering peaks and winding emerald waters. The karst landscape and Huashan Rock Art complement each other, forming a unique cultural corridor of natural scenery and ancient art. As you cruise down the Zuo River, ochre-red rock paintings appear intermittently on the sheer cliffs on both banks, like giant scrolls suspended between heaven and earth.

Huashan Rock Art Scenic Area was rated as a National 5A Tourist Attraction in 2024, becoming a world-class cultural tourism destination in Guangxi. The scenic area deeply integrates the Zuo River landscape with the Huashan Rock Art cultural heritage. Visitors can take a boat ride through the green mountains and clear waters to closely observe these millennia-old mysterious rock paintings and experience the awe-inspiring power of Luoyue civilization.
