Baise Uprising · Red Monument

Located in Baise City, Guangxi, the Baise Uprising Memorial Park was built to commemorate the Baise Uprising led by proletarian revolutionaries such as Deng Xiaoping and Zhang Yunyi in 1929. Covering a planned area of approximately 360 hectares, it is a national 5A-level tourist attraction and an important national base for patriotic education and classic red tourism destination.

On December 11, 1929, Deng Xiaoping, Zhang Yunyi, Wei Baqun and others led the Baise Uprising that shook southern China, establishing the Seventh Red Army of the Chinese Workers' and Peasants' Red Army and founding the Youjiang Revolutionary Base. This uprising was a brilliant example of the Chinese Communist Party waging armed struggle in minority regions, leaving a magnificent chapter in the history of the Chinese revolution.

Red Seventh Army HQ · Historical Witness

The former site of the Seventh Red Army headquarters is located inside the Guangdong Guild Hall on Jiefang Street in Baise City. This Lingnan-style architectural complex, built during the Qing Dynasty, served as the command center of the Red Seventh Army during the Baise Uprising. Leaders such as Deng Xiaoping and Zhang Yunyi strategized here, directing the armed struggle of the Youjiang Revolutionary Base.

The site preserves original historical scenes including the meeting room, office, and living quarters, displaying a large collection of precious historical artifacts and photographs. Walking through these halls transports visitors back to those fiery revolutionary days, offering a profound appreciation of the arduous struggle spirit of the revolutionary forebears.

Monument Tower · Eternal Spirit

The Baise Uprising Revolutionary Martyrs Monument stands tall in the center of the park, with golden inscribed characters reading "Eternal Glory to the Revolutionary Martyrs of the Baise Uprising" on its front face. The monument's solemn and dignified design symbolizes the indomitable fighting spirit of the revolutionary martyrs and their noble dedication to the revolutionary cause.

The park also houses the Baise Uprising Memorial Hall, with an exhibition area spanning thousands of square meters. Through rich historical photographs, revolutionary artifacts, and modern multimedia displays, it comprehensively and systematically recreates the historical background, combat history and great significance of the Baise Uprising, serving as an important window into the history of the Chinese revolution.
