Detian Waterfall · Transnational Wonder

Located on the Guichun River along the China-Vietnam border in Daxin County, Chongzuo City, Guangxi, Detian Transnational Waterfall is the largest transnational waterfall in Asia and the fourth largest in the world. Spanning over 200 meters wide with a drop of 70 meters, its average annual water flow is approximately three times that of Huangguoshu Waterfall, presenting a magnificent and awe-inspiring spectacle.

The waterfall cascades in three tiers, each displaying a distinct character with the changing seasons. During the wet season, torrents plunge down with thunderous roar, while misty spray often gives rise to rainbows; in the dry season, gentle streams flow like a sheer curtain of silk, revealing a softer elegance. Straddling the border between China and Vietnam, it creates a unique natural landscape known as "One Waterfall, Two Countries."

China-Vietnam Boundary · Border Charm

Within the Detian Waterfall scenic area stands Boundary Marker No. 53 on the China-Vietnam border. This marker has witnessed the changes of the border between the two countries and the friendly exchanges between their peoples, making it one of the most culturally significant attractions in the Detian area. Standing beside the marker, visitors can experience the unique thrill of having one foot in each country.

At the border market near the marker, Vietnamese vendors paddle small boats along the Guichun River to sell local specialties and handicrafts to Chinese tourists. The rich border culture and magnificent natural scenery complement each other, making a trip to Detian Waterfall a journey filled with unique cultural charm.

Mingshi Tianyuan · Landscape Gallery

Mingshi Tianyuan, located near the Detian Waterfall scenic area, is a perfect fusion of classic karst peak forest landforms and idyllic pastoral scenery. The crystal-clear Mingshi River meanders through the landscape, flanked by lush bamboo groves and waving rice paddies, with uniquely shaped mountain peaks reflected in the water — a poetic tableau known as "Sailing on emerald waves, traveling within a painting."

The Heishui River, another important tributary upstream of the Guichun River, derives its name from its deep jade-green waters. Flanked by layered green mountains and dense vegetation, a boat ride along the river feels like entering a secluded paradise. Together with the Heishui River, Mingshi Tianyuan forms the most enchanting landscape gallery around the Detian Waterfall area.
